Order: No. 23, Rural Development and Panchayat Raj, (C.G.S-II), 13th February 2015.
In the letter first read above, Government of India have launched a new scheme called Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana
on 11-10-2014.
2. Para 12(C) of the Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) Guidelines envisages the constitution of State Level
Empowered Committee headed by the Chief Secretary, consisting of relevant departments and including experts as required,
with at least two Civil Society Representatives.  The Principal  Secretary/Secretary of the Rural Development Department
of the State will be the Member – Convener.  Two full time resource persons on contract may be deployed to support the
work of this State level Committee.
3. In the letter 2nd read above, the Director of Rural Development and Panchayat Raj has stated that the State Level
Empowered Committee on Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana may be constituted with the following composition:

4. He has further stated that the Chairman of the Committee may co-opt Secretary / Head of any other Department as
a Special Invitee for any specific meeting, as per the requirement and necessity that may arise from time to time.  The
Committee will meet at least once a quarter and perform the following tasks:
1. Supplement the central SAGY guidelines and issue state specific instructions taking into account different state
schemes and lay out the roles and responsibilities of functionaries at the Village Panchayat, Block, District and State Level.
2. Review Village Development Plans from across the constituencies and suggest changes, if required, to ensure that
the key outputs are achieved within the stipulated timelines.
3. Review implementation and lay down monitoring mechanisms supplementary to the web-based monitoring system.
4. Identify bottlenecks, and the technical and administrative support required and issue necessary instructions /
Orders from time to time.
5. Coordinate with the Committees at the National level as required.
6. Develop a time table for exposure visits to the model villages and design a State level plan for dissemination of
best practices.
7. Design a Grievance redressal mechanism for the scheme which will be put in place at the level of the Charge
Officer and the District in accordance with scheme guidelines.
5. He has stated that  the Director of State Institute of Rural Development, Maraimalainagar, may be directed to designate
two Resource Persons from among the existing faculty members of the Institute to support the work of the State Level
Committee.
