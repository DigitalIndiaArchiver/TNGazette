BILL INTRODUCED IN THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF THE
STATE OF TAMIL NADU
Under Rule 130 of the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly Rules, the following Bill
which was introduced in the Legislative Assembly of the State of Tamil Nadu on
25th July 2014 is published together with Statement of Objects and Reasons for
general information:—
L.A. Bill No. 9 of 2014
A Bill further to amend the Tamil Nadu Agricultural Produce Marketing
(Regulation) Act, 1987.
BE it enacted by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Tamil Nadu in the
Sixty-fifth Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
1. (1) This Act may be called the Tamil Nadu Agricultural Produce Marketing
(Regulation) Amendment Act, 2014.
(2) It shall be deemed to have come into force on the 31st day of
May 2014.
2. In section 33 of the Tamil Nadu Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation)
Act, 1987 (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act), in the proviso to
sub-section (1), for the expression “three years”, the expression “four years”
shall be substituted.

STATEMENT OF OBJECTS AND REASONS.
Sub-section (1) of section 33 of the Tamil Nadu Agricultural Produce Marketing
(Regulation) Act, 1987 (Tamil Nadu Act 27 of 1989) empowers the Government to
appoint a Special Officer to manage the affairs of the market committee pending
the constitution of a new market committee for a period not exceeding three years
in the aggregate.  By virtue of the said provision, Special Officers were appointed
to the 21 market committees to manage the affairs of the said market committees
initially for a period of one year ending the 30th May 2012 and lastly extended upto
30th May 2014. When the Government were taking earnest efforts to re-constitute
the new market committees by making nominations, the Model Code of Conduct
for election to the Lok-Sabha came into force on the 5th March 2014 and was in
force upto the 18th May 2014.  The reconstitution of the market committees could
not, therefore, be carried out before the 30th May 2014.  Hence, there is compelling
necessity to continue the appointment of the said Special Officers for one more
year beyond the said period of three years or till the reconstitution of the market
committees, whichever is earlier.  The Government have, therefore, decided to
amend the said Tamil Nadu Act 27 of 1989 for the purpose and also to validate
anything done or any action taken by the said Special Officers on or after the
31st May 2014.
2. The Bill seeks to give effect to the above decision.
