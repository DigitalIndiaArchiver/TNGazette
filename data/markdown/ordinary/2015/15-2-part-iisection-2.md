No. II(2)/EF/178/2015.—
[Environment Control - Adjudication of disputes relating to
Environment and Pollution - Appointment of Chairman to
the Appellate Authority - Orders - Issued.]
The following Government Order is republished:—
[G.O. Ms. No. 47, Environment and Forests (EC-1),
28th March 2015, ðƒ°Q 14, üò, F¼õœÀõ˜
Ý‡´-2046.]
Read:
1. G.O. Ms. No. 208, Environment and Forests (EC-1)
Department, dated 15-09-2000.
2. G.O. Ms. No. 23, Environment and Forests (EC-1)
Department, dated 29-01-2012.
3. G.O. Ms. No. 66, Environment and Forests (EC-1)
Department, dated 07-05-2013.
Order: No. 47, Environment and Forests (EC-1),
  28th March 2015.
Section 28 of the Water (Prevention and Control of
Pollution) Act, 1974 and Section 31 of Air (Prevention and
Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 provide that the State
Government may constitute a State Appellate Authority
consisting of one or three persons as it may think fit to be
appointed to help in the adjudication of disputes relating
to Environment and Pollution.
2. In pursuance of the orders of the Supreme Court in
its Judgment in Civil Appeal Nos.368-371 of 1999 of
SLP (Civil) Nos. 10317-10320/98 and Civil Appeal Nos.372,
373/99 in SLP (Civil) Nos.10380 & 13380/98, the Authority
is composed of a retired Judge of High Court as Chairman
and Experts / Scientists in the field of Pollution Control as
Members. Accordingly, the Appellate Authority for Tamil Nadu
was constituted vide Government order 1st cited.
3. In reference 2nd read above, Hon’ble Justice Thiru
T.V. Masilamani, retired Judge of Madras High Court was
appointed as the Chairman, Appellate Authority with
effect from 24-01-2012 and his term expired on 23-01-2015.
4. The Government after careful examination appoint
Hon’ble Justice T. Sudanthiram as Chairman, Appellate
Authority, for adjudication of disputes relating to Environment
and Pollution Control for a period of three years from the
date of issue of order.
5.
The terms and conditions of appointment of the
Chairman of Appellate Authority shall be as per G.O. Ms.
No. 116, Environment and Forests (EC-1) Department, dated
25-10-2007.
(By Order of the Governor)
HANS RAJ VERMA,
Principal Secretary to Government.

Exemption from the Provisions for the Regulation of
Second Master Plan for Chennai Metropolitan Area
relating to construction of Commercial Building at
Egmore Village, Chennai under the Act.
[G.O. (3D) No. 8, Housing and Urban Development (UD-V)

No. II(2)/HOU/179/2015.—In exercise of powers conferred
under Section 113 of the Tamil Nadu Town and Country
Planning Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act 35 of 1972) the Governor
of Tamil Nadu hereby exempt the commercial building at
Door Nos.48/2, 49/2 & 50, Harris Road R.S. Nos. 1389/2,
1389/5, 1389/6, 1389/7, 1389/8, 1389/9 and 1391/2, Block
No.47 of Egmore Village, Chennai from the provisions of
Development Regulations 28(7)A relating to Basement floor
roof above Ground Level (maximum allowable is 1.20m and
provided is 1.52m which is excess of 0.32m); Development
Regulations 2(34) Annexure XVIII(11) relating to service floor
height (maximum allowable is 1.5m whereas the
exiting service floor height is 2.74m which is excess by
0.17m); Development Regulations 28(6) Annexure XVII relating
to corridor width (The required corridor width is 2m whereas
the existing corridor width is 1.40m which is less by 0.60m.);
and Development Regulations 28(2) G relating to setbacks
and car parking lots to the extent required under rule 28(4)
by regularizing the sub-division with disclaimer to residuary
plot, if any, of Development Regulations Second Master Plan
for Chennai Metropolitan Area 2026.
Exemption from the Provisions for the Regulation of
Second Master Plan for Chennai Metropolitan Area
relating to construction of Guest House Building at
Pallikaranai Village, Chennai under the Act.

20th March 2015, ðƒ°Q 6, üò, F¼õœÀõ˜

No. II(2)/HOU/180/2015.—In exercise of powers conferred
under Section 113 of the Tamil Nadu Town and Country
Planning Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act 35 of 1972) the Governor
of Tamil Nadu hereby exempt the construction of the Stilt
Floor (Part) / Ground Floor (part) + 6 Floors electrical room
in terrace floor (Shop in ground floor; Auditorium in 1st and
2nd floor; conference room and office in 3rd floor; guest
rooms in 4th floor to 6th floor) Guest House building for
Odisha Bhavan in S.No.26/3 of Pallikaranai Village, Chennai
from the provisions of violation of Development Regulation
28(2)(A) relating to site extent; Development Regulation
28(2)(B) relating to plot coverage; Development Regulation
28 (2)G relating to set back violation on the eastern side;
Development Regulation 28(2)6 relating to Corridor width; of
Second Master Plan for Chennai Metropolitan Area 2026.
