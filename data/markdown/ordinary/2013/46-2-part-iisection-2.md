READ:
From the Additional Director General of Police, Economic
Offences Wing-II, Chennai Letter No. C1/13420/2012, dated
8-12-2012 and letter even No. dated 18-1-2013 and 8-4-2013.
Order No. 590, Home (Police XIX), 13th  August 2013.
WHEREAS the Additional Director General of Police, economic
Offences Wing has reported that a case in Crime No.168/
2010 under section 457, 380 IPC was registered in Thirunavalur
Police Station based on the complaint of Secretary to
Thirunavalur Farmers Service Co-operative Society and later
it was transferred to Villupuram  Commercial Crime Investigation
Wing – Criminal Investigation Department in Crime No.1/2011
under sections 408, 409, 468, 420, 471, 477 (A) read with
section 109 Indian Penal Code and sections 414 and 461 IPC
and the investigation has revealed that one Veerasundaram,
who is a salesman and incharge of the Society’s properties,
conspired with one Sivakumar to do away with the gold jewels
kept in the society’s locker and the duo executed their operation
and Veerasundaram gained entry into the society, broke open
the bureau and took away 1790 sovereigns of gold jewels and
Rs. 3,00,000/- cash from the locker and concealed them in
the backyard of Sivakumar’s house. Subsequently,
Veerasundaram died and the whole property was inherited by
Sivakumar, who took the jewels one by one and gave it to his
friend Mothibai for disposal and thus Sivakumar got
approximately Rs. 33,00,000/- and Thiru. Mothibai purchased
7 plots.
AND WHEREAS, the offence under section 420 Indian Penal
Code is a scheduled offence under the Criminal Law
Amendment Ordinance, 1944 (Central Ordinance XXXVIII of
1944) as amended by Tamil Nadu Act 47 of 1997;
AND WHEREAS, it is reported that the accused Mothibai at
Farooklal and Sivakumar involved in the above misappropriation
have acquired and are in possession of properties specified
in the Schedule-I below in their names which are believed to
have been procured by means of the said offence;
AND WHEREAS, the Deputy Superintendent of Police,
Commercial Crime Investigation Wing – Crime Investigation
Department, Chennai incharge Villupuram sub-division has
requested the government to authorise, him to make an
application in the Court of District Judge, Villupuram within the
Local limits of whose jurisdiction the said accused ordinarily
resides, for the attachment under the Criminal Law Amendment
Ordinance, 1944, of the properties  as detailed in the Schedule
to this Order, which are believed to have been procured by
means of the above said offence;
NOW, THEREFORE, in exercise of the powers conferred by
sub-section (1) of Section 3 of the Criminal Law Amendment
Ordinance, 1944 (Central Ordinance XXXVIII of 1944), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu, after careful consideration of the
materials placed before him hereby Authorizes the making of
an application by the Deputy Superintendent of Police,
Commercial Crime Investigation Wing – Crime Investigation
Department, Chennai incharge Villupuram sub-division to the
District Court, Villupuram for the attachment of the movable
and immovable properties in the schedule-I, to this Order, which
are procured by means of the aforesaid offence by the accused
Mothibai @ Farooklal and Sivakumar who were involved in the
Misappropriation of funds of Thirunavalur Farmers Service
Co-operative Society, Villupuram District and also appoints the
said Deputy Superintendent of Police, Commercial Crime
Investigation Wing – Crime Investigation Departement to sign
and verify such application.
2.
The Additional Director of Police, Economic Offences
Wing – II, Chennai is directed to file and affidavit before the
Jurisdictional District Judge under section 6 of the Criminal
Law Amendment Ordinance, 1944 (Central Ordinance XXXVIII
of 1944) for the attachment of the movable and immovable
properties of malafide transferees mentioned in Schedule – II
of this order, which are believe to have been procured by
means of the said offence.
3.
The Government Pleader, Villupuram is requested to
conduct the above case for attachment of the properties
mentioned in Schedules to this order.

Explanations.—(1) Composition of Zones is as follows:—
Zone – A : All Corporations and Hill Stations as detailed
below, including works lying in the reserve forest areas and
five kilometre belt areas.
Hill Stations
(1) The Nilgiris Plateau.
(2) Valparai sub-Taluk of Coimbatore District.
(3) The Sheveroys.
(4) Kodaikanal.
(5) Peermedu.
(6) Sangalteri in the Kalakkad reserve of Nanguneri range
in Tirunelveli District.
(7) Mudaliaroothu in the Srivilliputhur reserve of Srivilliputhur
range in Virudhunagar District.
(8) Gudalur Taluk in the Nilgiris District.
(9) Javadu Hills and Elagiri Hills in the Vellore District.
Zone – B : All Grades of Municipalities.
Zone – C : All Other areas not covered under Zones “A”
and “B”.
(2) Classification of employees into class-I and class-II is
based on physical capacity, skill, efficiency and out turn of
work.
(3) In case of piece rated workers, their daily total earning
for 8 hours of work shall not be less than the daily rates of
wages revised for that category of employees. In computing
the above rates, a day’s work is deemed to comprise of 8
working hours. If the employee is employed for less than 8
hours, wages have to be calculated proporitionately.
(4) Dearness Allowance.—(1) In addition to the basic wages
fixed above, the employees shall be paid Dearness Allowance
as indicated below:—
(i) The dearness allowance is linked to the average
Chennai City Consumer Price Index number for the year 2010,
that is 161 points (with base 2001=100) and for every rise of
one point over and above 161 points an increase of 2.20 paise
(Two rupees and twenty paise only) per day shall be paid as
Dearness Allowance.
(ii) The dearness allowance shall be calculated every
yea on the first April on the basis of the average Consumer
Price indices of the preceding twelve months, i.e. from
January to December.

iii) The first calculation shall thus be effective from the date
of publication of this Notification in the Tamil Nadu Government
Gazette based on the average Chennai City Consumer Price

(2) Where the nature of work is the same, no distinction
in the payment of wages shall be made as between men and
women employees.
(3) To arrive at monthly rates of wages, the daily rates of
wages shall be multiplied by 30.
(4) Wherever the existing wages are higher than the
minimum wages fixed herin, the same higher wages shall be
continued to be paid.

PUBLIC DEPARTMENT
(SC)
Order of Detention of certain person and Direct to appear
before Certain Official under Conservation of Foreign
Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act.
[G.O. No. SR. I/49 – 12/2013, Public (SC),
9th November 2013.
No. II(2)/PUSC/897/2013.—Whereas, an order had
been made by the Government of Tamil Nadu in
G.O. No. SRI/49-8/2013. Public (SC) Department, dated
3rd October 2013, under section 3(1)(i) of the Conservation
of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities
Act, 1974 (Central Act 52 of 1974), directing that Thiru Maninder
Singh, s/o. Thiru Preetam Singh, resident of No. A-7, 1st Wing,
7th floor, Appasamy Apartments, No. 114, Inner Ring Road,
Arumbakkam, Chennai-600 106, be detained.
And Whereas, Maninder Singh, s/o. Thiru Preetam Singh,
resident of No. A-7, 1st Wing 7th Floor, Appasamy Apartments,
No. 114, Inner Ring Road, Arumbakkam, Chennai-600 106,
can not be found.
And whereas, the Government of Tamil Nadu have reasons
to believe that Thiru Maninder Singh, in respect of whom the
detention order has been made, has absconded and is
concealing himself so that the said detention order cannot be
executed.
Now Therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred by
section 7(1) (b) of the Conservation of Foreign Exchange and
Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 (Central Act 52
of 1974) the Government of Tamil Nadu hereby direct
Thiru Maninder Singh, son of Thiru Preetam Singh, resident
of No. A-7, 1st Wing, 7th floor, Appasamy Apartments,
No. 114, Inner Ring Road, Arumbakkam, Chennai-600 106 to
appear before the Commissioner of Police Chennai Police,
Chennai, at his office within a period of 15 days (fifteen days)
from the date of publication of this order in the Tamil Nadu
Government Gazette.
JATINDRA NATH SWAIN,
Principal Secretary to Government.
