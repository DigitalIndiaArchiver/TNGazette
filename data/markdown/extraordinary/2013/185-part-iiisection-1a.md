TAMIL NADU SPECIAL POLICE YOUTH BRIGADE RULES, 2013
[G.O. Ms. No. 432, Home (Pol.XIV), 1st July 2013.]
No. SRO A-17(c)/2013.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 12 of the Tamil Nadu Special Police Youth Brigade Act, 2013 (Tamil Nadu Act
3 of 2013), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following rules:-
RULES.
1.
Short title.—These rules may be called the Tamil Nadu Special Police Youth Brigade Rules, 2013.
2.
Definitions.—In these rules, unless there is anything repugnant in the subject or context:-
(a)
“Act” means the Tamil Nadu Special Police Youth  Brigade Act, 2013 (Tamil Nadu Act 3 of 2013);
(b)
“Appellate Authority”  means the Deputy Inspector General of Police in the Ranges, the Joint Commissioner of
Police (Head quarters) in the Chennai Police Commissionerate and the Commissioner of Police in all other
Commissionerates;
(c)
“Authorised Officer”  means Superintendent of Police in the Districts, Deputy Commissioner of Police (Administration)
in the Chennai Police Commissionerate and Deputy Commissioner of Police (Head quarters) or the Deputy
Commissioner of Police (Crime and Traffic) where there is no Deputy Commissioner of Police (Head quarters)
in all other Commissionerates of Police; and
(d)
“Youth Brigade” means the Tamil Nadu Special Police Youth Brigade constituted under Section 3 of the Act.
3. Qualification for appointment to the Youth Brigade.—No person shall be  eligible for appointment as a member of the
Youth Brigade, unless he –
(a)
has passed the 10th  standard examination;
(b)
is of good character and is  physically fit;
(c)
is a resident of Tamil Nadu; and
(d)
has completed 18 years of age and has not completed 25 years of age as on 1st January of the year in which
the selection for appointment is made:

4.Method of Selection.—(1)  The members of the Youth Brigade shall be selected by the  authorised officer concerned
through an open  advertisement to be notified by the authorised officer concerned.
(2)  The members of the Youth Brigade shall be selected through an objective type of examination conducted by the
Tamil Nadu  Uniformed Services Recruitment Board in accordance with the  syllabus and standard specified in
Annexure I to these Rules.
(3)  The short listed candidates based on their performance in the objective type examination will be called for
Physical Measurement and Physical Endurance Test of a qualifying  nature in the events specified in  Annexure II to these
rules.
(4)  Every candidate must possess the following minimum standards of physical measurements:-

170 cms / 167 cms

(5) The Communal reservation followed in appointments in the services under the State as per the law in force shall
apply for appointment to the Youth Brigade.
(6) There shall be no special reservation such as ex-servicemen, differently abled persons, sports persons etc., for
appointment to Youth Brigade.
5.Areas of training and duration.—On selection and appointment, the candidate  shall become a member of
the Youth Brigade.  During the  1st year  of the appointment each member shall receive training in the areas specified in

6.Absorption in the Tamil Nadu Police Force.—Members of the Youth Brigade who have completed one year of service
in the Youth Brigade and who have successfully completed training in all the areas will be eligible to appear at a common
state-level examination to be conducted by the Tamil Nadu Uniformed Services Recruitment Board, separately. Successful
candidates who qualify in that   examination will be absorbed in the  Tamil Nadu Police Force, based on  merits.
7.Terms  and conditions of Appointment.—(1) Every person appointed as a member of the Youth Brigade shall be
eligible to continue till the attainment of the age of forty  years.
(2)
Every member of the Youth Brigade shall be liable to serve in  any part of the State of Tamil Nadu.
(3)
A member of the Youth Brigade shall not be entitled for any leave except casual leave for twelve days in a
year.

8. Disciplinary action.—(1) Any member of the Youth Brigade shall be subjected to disciplinary action by the authorized
officer.  The authorized officer shall for good and sufficient reasons suspend a member of the Youth Brigade, if he neglects
or refuses to discharge his duty without reasonable cause or commits any breach of discipline or is found guilty of
misconduct or is physically unfit to continue or whose continuance is detrimental to the good order, welfare, or discipline
of the brigade or is prejudicial to the security of the State or any part thereof.
(2) Any member of the Youth Brigade, against whom disciplinary action is initiated shall be given an opportunity to explain
the charges against him. The authorized officer may by an order in writing, terminate the appointment of any member of the Youth
Brigade.
9. Appeal.—Any member of the Youth Brigade aggrieved by an order of the authorised officer under sub-section (2) of
Section 9 of the Act, may prefer an appeal to the appellate authority and the  decision of such authority shall be final.
10. Uniform / Accoutrements.—(1) All  members of the Youth Brigade shall be supplied with two sets of uniforms at
Government cost on enrolment.
(2) A member of the Youth Brigade shall, while on duty, wear the following uniform:
(i)
A half sleeved khaki colour shirt and full khaki colour trousers:
Provided that, for those who work in  Hill Stations, the uniform shall be either those prescribed above or the items
prescribed below,  whichever is convenient for the season;
(ii)
Khaki beret;
(iii)
Black leather shoes;
(iv)
Woollen socks;
(v)
Whistle with Chain;
(vi)
Black leather Belt; and
(vii) Members of the Youth Brigade shall wear the letters  TNSPYB on their shoulder straps.
In Hill Stations, warm clothing will be supplied, in addition, on the same scale as for policemen and two sets of  uniforms
will be supplied every year.
11. Honorarium.—(1)  Every member of the Youth Brigade shall be paid a consolidated monthly honorarium of
Rs.7,500/- (Rupees seven thousand five hundred only)   and shall not be entitled for any other allowances except Travelling
Allowance which shall be admissible, while serving away from the head quarters.
(2)
For days of unauthorized absence, deduction will be made from honorarium on a pro rata basis.
(3)
They are also eligible for the  facilities available in the police canteens and for supply of essential commodities at
subsidized prices and also to use bus warrants and railway warrants.
