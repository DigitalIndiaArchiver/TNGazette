No. VI(2)/473(a-1)/2011.
In exercise of the powers conferred under Section 43-D of the Tamil Nadu District Municipalities  Act, 1920 (Tamil Nadu Act
V of 1920), Section 59A of the Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919 (Tamil Nadu Act IV of 1919), Section 66A of  Madurai
City Municipal Corporation Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act XV of 1971), Section 68-A of the Coimbatore City Municipal Corporation Act,
1981 (Tamil Nadu Act 25 of 1981) read with Section 8 of the Tirunelveli City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act  28
of 1994) Section 8 of the Salem City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 29 of 1994),  Section 8 of the Tiruppur City
Municipal Corporation Act, 2008 (Tamil Nadu Act 7 of 2008), Section 8 of the Erode City Municipal Corporation Act, 2008 (Tamil
Nadu Act 8 of 2008), Section 8 of the Vellore City Municipal Corporation Act, 2008 (Tamil Nadu Act 26 of 2008) and Section 8 of
the Thoothukudi City Municipal Corporation Act, 2008 (Tamil Nadu Act 27 of 2008),  the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission
hereby specifies that in the Ordinary Elections to fill up the Ordinary vacancies in the offices of the Mayors / Chairmen and Ward
Members / Councillors of Town Panchayats, Municipalities and Corporations notified  in the S.O. No. 45/2011/TNSEC/ME1, dated
21-9-2011, the giving and recording of votes by electronic voting machine shall be adopted.
(By Order of the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission)

III. There shall be one block of lines or shades of 1 em below the perforated rule/disjointed straight line on the ballot paper.
IV. The front face of the ballot paper will contain the name and particulars of the Panchayat, Ward and month and year of
election and whether it is ordinary of casual. The ballot papers shall contain the names of the candidates in Tamil along with the
symbols allotted to them.
V. Serial Number of the ballot paper shall be printed on the left-hand side either on the front or on the back, as may be
convenient and also in the counterfoil.
VI. The width of the ballot paper will be between 3" and 4" as may be considered convenient by the Returing Officer for printing
the ballot paper.
VII. In the case of the ballot papers for the elections listed in Serial Nos. 3 and 4 of the said Table, the name of the candidate,
in Tamil, will appear on the left side and his symbol on the right side within a panel, in the ballot papers.
VIII.The width of the space allotted to each candidate will be 6½  ems and the width of the shaded area between the space
allotted to any two candidates will be 3 ems. There will be a thick black border of 1 em width at the bottom.
IX. The size of each symbol will be not more than 9 ems x 5½ ems.
X. (a) In the case of the ballot papers for the elections listed in Serial Nos. 1 and 2 of the said Table, the symbols may be
arranged in one or more columns as may be convenient.
(b) In the case of elections listed in Serial Nos. 3 and 4 of the said Table the number of candidates in one column should
not ordinarily exceed nine. Where the number of contesting candidates exceeds nine, the ballot paper shall be printed in two or
more columns depending upon the number of contesting candidates.

(c) The width of the ballot paper and its counterfoil shall be dependent upon the number of columns which are provided
on the ballot paper. Each column shall be separated from the other by a shaded area of 2½ ems width from top to bottom.
The names of the contesting candidates and/or their symbols will appear in the correct order one below the other in those columns,
starting with the name/symbol of the candidate at serial number 1 in the list of contesting candidates appearing at
serial number 1 in the first column, candidate at serial number 10 in the list of contesting candidates as the first candidate in the
second column, and so on. If the number of contesting candidates is not exactly divisible by the number of candidates in each
column, the end panels on the right side of the ballot paper which do not contain the name of any candidate will be completely
shaded.
XI. The ballot papers will be stitched into bundles of fifty, with consecutive serial numbers. It should be ensured that the serial
numbers on each ballot paper and its counterfoil are identical.
