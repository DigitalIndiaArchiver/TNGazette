CHENNAI PORT TRUST
Chennai Port Trust Employees’ (Grant of Advances for Festival and Natural Calamities)
(Amendment) Regulations, 2011.
(RRC/11812/2008/GA.)
No. SRO C-2/2012.
The Notification GSR No. 825 (E) dated the 9th November 2011 issued by the Government of India, Ministry of
Shipping and published in Part II—Section 3, sub-section (i) of the Extraordinary issue of the Gazette of India, dated
19th November 2011 is republished hereunder as required under Section 132(2) of the Major Port Trusts Act, 1963:—
GSR No. 825 (E)—In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (i) of Section 124, read with sub-section (i)
of Section 132 of the Major Port Trusts Act, 1963 (38 of 1963), the Central Government hereby approves the Chennai
Port Trust Employees’ (Grant of Advances for Festival and Natural Calamities) (Amendment) Regulations, 2011, made by
the Board of Trustees for the Port of Chennai Port further to amend the Chennai Port Trust Employee's (Grant of
Advances for Festival and National Calamities) regulations, 1994 and set out in the Schedule annexed to this Notification.
SCHEDULE
Chennai Port Trust Employees’ (Grant of Advances for Festival and Natural Calamities)
(Amendment) Regulations, 2011.
In exercise of the powers conferred under Section 28 of the Major Port Trusts Act, 1963 (38 of 1963), the Board of
Trustees of the Port of Chennai hereby makes the following regulations further to amend the Chennai Port Trust
Employees’ (Grant of Advances for Festival and Natural Calamities) Regulations, 1994, namely:—
1. Short Title and Commencement:—
(a) These Regulations may be called the Chennai Port Trust Employees’ (Grant of Advances for Festival and
Natural Calamities) (Amendment) Regulations, 2011.
(b) The said amendment Regulations shall come into force from the date of publication in the Official Gazette.
2.
In the Chennai Post Trust Employees’ (Grant of Advances for Festival and Natural Calamities) Regulations, 1994
the figure Rs. 3,000/- appearing in sub-Regulation (1) of Regulation 4 shall be substituted by the figure Rs. 10,000/-.
Note:
The Principal regulations were published vide number G.S.R. 56 (E), dated 2nd February, 1994 and amended vide
number G.S.R. 14 (E), dated 9th  January, 1996 and number G.S.R. 192 (E), dated 16th March, 2001.

CONDITION OF SALE
1. Lots are to be examined by the intending purchasers bidding and to be taken with faults and errors to description.
2. The highest bidder to be the purchaser and in the event of any dispute arising between two or more bidders the
lots so disputed should be put up again and resold.
3. The bid amount shall be paid in full immediately after the bid is knocked down. If this not possible a deposit of
25 per cent of bidden amount should be paid on the spot to the Auctioneers and a receipt shall be obtained for the
amount paid in case only a deposit of 25 per cent is paid. The balance amount should be paid to the Trust Financial
Adviser and Chief Accounts Officer on or before 04.00 p.m. on the third day calculating from the Date of confirmation
of sale, failing which the EMD paid will be forfeited and the Trust will resale the lot either in sale by Tender or by Public
Auction at the risk and responsibility of the defaulters.
4. Goods advertised for sale will not be withdrawn by the Trust except on payment of sufficient deposit to cover the
Harbour dues, Demurrage charges and other charges due thereon.
5. The goods sold in Auction will not be delivered to the purchaser thereof or his authorized agent unless the
purchaser produced Delivery Order from the Auctioneers for having paid the amount in full.
6. Goods sold in Auction shall be allowed three days excluding Sundays and Board’s Holidays from the date of
confirmation of sale and there after shall be charged at daily Rate as per Scale of Rates up to and including the date
of clearance.
7. The lots sold will be lying at the risk of the purchaser and must be removed within the free period of Three days
from the date of confirmation of sale at the purchaser’s expenses.
8. The purchasers are distinctly warned that if the goods are not cleared within Eight days from the date of expiry
of Free period they have no claim whatsoever over the materials and the same will be sold whether in Sale by Tender
or Public Auction. The first purchaser to be held liable for any loss arising from such resale and to forfeit any advances
together with auctioned commission.
