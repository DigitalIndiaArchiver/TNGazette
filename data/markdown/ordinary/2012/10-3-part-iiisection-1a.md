Draft Amendments to the Tamil Nadu Motor Transport Workers’ Rules, 1965
[G.O. Ms.No.18, Labour and Employment (H1), 27th January 2012, Thai 13, Thiruvalluvar Aandu — 2043.]
No.SRO A-9/2012.—The following draft of amendments, to the Tamil Nadu Motor Transport Workers’ Rules, 1965, which it
is proposed to make in exercise of the powers conferred by sub-sections (1) and (2) of Section 40 of the Motor Transport
Workers’ Act, 1961  (Central Act 27 of 1961), is hereby published for information of all persons likely to be affected as
required by sub-section (1) of Section 40 of the said Act.
2.
Notice is hereby given that the draft amendments will be taken into consideration on or after the expiry of three
months from the date of publication of the Notification in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette. Any objection or suggestion,
if any, may be addressed to the Principal Secretary to Government, Labour and Employment Department, Secretariat,
Chennai-600 009 through Commissioner of Labour Chennai-600 006.
DRAFT AMENDMENTS
In the Said Rules,—
(1) after rule 25, the following rule shall be inserted, namely:—
“25-A. Compulsory Medical Checkup for HIV/AIDS.—(1) Notwithstanding anything contained in  rules 24 and 25, the
employer of every motor transport undertaking shall ensure that all the workers employed in the motor transport undertaking
shall subject themselves for medical checkup for testing for HIV/AIDS, within a period of three months from the date of effect
of this rule, in any of the Integrated Counseling and Testing Centers attached to Tamil Nadu State Aids Control Society and
that they shall continue the checkup periodically once in a period of every three months. At any point of testing, if any worker
is found to be HIV/AIDS positive, then that worker shall be subjected to treatment and care in the Anti-Retroviral Therapy
Centers attached to the hospitals. The employer shall ensure that the workers present themselves for treatment periodically
on the dates specified without fail.
(2) The expenses for testing and treatment shall be borne by the employer.
(3) The worker found to have HIV/AIDS shall not be discharged from service by the employer.
(4) Every employer shall maintain a Register in Form No. XXII with update entries and  produce the same with
relevant records to the inspector on demand”.;
