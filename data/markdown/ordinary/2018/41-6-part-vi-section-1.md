No.VI(1)/371/2018. 
The Tamil Nadu Medical Council at its Special Business Meeting held on 11th April,2018 has passed the 
following Resolutions. 
3) “THE CME CREDIT REGISTRATION”:  
3.1Brief: As per the Code of Medical Ethics Regulations 2002 (amended upto 8th October 2016) and 
published in Part Ill, Section 4 of the Gazette of India dated 6th April 2002. Section 1 and clause 1.2.3 and vide 
the directions of the Medical Council of India in their letter No. MCI-7(9) /2011 Med./58885 dt 24.01.2012 to do 
crediting of the hours of participation in the CME programmes once in 5 years. The CME crediting process was 
announced by Medical Council of India (MCI) by incorporating the mandatory 30 hours of CME every 5 years in the 
Code of Medical Ethics Regulations in 2012. Subsequently the Tamil nadu Medical Council in its resolution dated 
21.03.2012 have started the process.  
3.2The TNMC has been carrying out the certification of CMEs held by various organizations in the state complying 
with the directions of the Medical Council of India.   
3.3 The Council discussed the same elaborately and resolved to start the “CME  Credit Registration” for the period 
2012-2017 as per the following guidelines.  
3.3.1. The “CME Credit Registration” should be done by all Medical  graduates who have registered before 2013. 
(once in 5 years from the  date of their M.B.B.S. individual registration.)  
3.3.2. The “CME Credit Registration” will be done for all Registered Medical  Practitioners.  
3.3.3. The fees for “CME Credit Registration” will be fixed and collected by  TNMC  
3.3.4. The process will be started shortly after completing the preparatory  work, tentatively in November-December,2018.

3.3.5. Registration / accreditation of reputed professional academic bodies and organizations for holding TNMC 
approved CME will be updated first. 
3.3.6. Subsequently, these organizations / institutions will be allowed to get a one-time retrospective approval for 
crediting of CME held by these organizations during the period 2012-2017. (after verification of the records produced) 
3.3.7. For those who have not acquired 30 credit hours since 2012, s/he will have to pay a Fees based on the 
shortage of Credit hours. The practitioners will be permitted to carry-over the credit hours short this term to the next 
block-5 years. 
3.3.8. The “Carry-Over Fees” will be Rs.50/- for carrying over the shortage of one Credit hour. The fees has been

3.3.9. For defaulters, not doing the ‘CME Credit Registration” before the stipulated time the late fee will be 5 fold 
and further they shall be considered to have violated the Code of Medical Ethics Regulations Section / Clause 1.2.3. 
3.3.10. The “CME Credit Registration” will be intimated to all practitioners through a paper advertisement and 
communication letter to Heads of  Directorates and all medical Colleges, I.M.A, all Specialty Associations, TNGDA. 
3.3.11. A letter will written to MCI seeking clarifications on CME hours for crediting on the discrepancy in 
their letter with that of the Code para 1.2.3 on the calculation of CME hours. The further decision will be taken 
based on the reply from MCI and legal opinions on the calculation of CME hours and after publishing the same in 
Tamil nadu Government Gazette.

PROCEDURES FOR CME ACCREDITATION:- 
1.	
Medical Councils shall consider the applications for accreditation of following organization for holding CME 
Programmes and convey their approval if the organization fulfills all requirements. 
	
(a) 	All recognised Medical Colleges. 
	
(b) 	Indian Medical Association or the State/District/Tehsil/City Chapters of the Indian Medical Association. 
	
(c) 	 National Academy of Medical Sciences. 
	
(d) 	Specialists Associations (only National level Associations or their state Chapters) 
	
(e)	 All Recognised Postgraduate Medical Institutions. 
	
(f) 	 Central Govt. and State Govt. Hospitals(including Districts Hospitals) and training centers in health field 
including Ministry of Health and family welfare, Defence, Railways. 
	
(g) 	International Conference of Professional bodies 
	
(h) 	National Conference of Professional bodies 
	
(i)	
State level conference of National Organizations. 
	
(j)	
Professional bodies of repute functional at District, City, State level ego Physician Forum / Surgeons forum  / 
Doctors Forum etc. in their areas I specialities and super specialties. 
	
(k)	 Educational Foundations setup for specific super speciality subjects 
	
(l) 	 Private medical institutions with multi specialty departments. 
	
(m)	 Private Medical Institutions registered with the State authorities as trusts. 
(2)	 Any professional organization or body or institution which wants to hold CME should apply for accreditation to 
the state Medical Council. The Council on verifying the credentials of the organization will give certificate of accreditations 
to those bodies to hold CMEs.
(3) 	Accreditation will be for the period of 5 years subject to renewal on  review.  	
(4) 	(A) In case of accredited institutions/associations / bodies, every CME  will not require separate permission.

of CME system. The State  Medical Council will have right to revoke accreditation of any  institution/association/body 
if it finds that CME is not held  as per the guidelines.

22. PENALTY FOR USING UN-RECOGNISED DEGREES, TITLES BY  REGISTERED MEDICAL PRACTITIONERS.  
According to Regulation 1.4.2 of the Tamil Nadu Medical Council Code of Ethics Regulations 2002,  
“Physician shall display as suffix to their names only recognized medical  degrees or such certificates/diplomas and 
memberships/honours which confer  professional knowledge or recognizes any exemplary qualification / achievements”  
After discussion, the Council opined that there are many violations in the  display of unrecognized degrees, false 
issuing and using medical titles by Registered Medical Practitioners, and we have to levy the penalty to them.  
The Council approved to levy the penalty amount of Rs. 10,000/- for the first offence and Rs. 25,000/ - for the 
second offence.
