ENVIRONMENT AND FORESTS DEPARTMENT
Declaration of Sengurichi Block-I in Dindigul Taluk,
Dindigul District as Reserved Forest under Tamil Nadu
Forest Act.
[G.O.Ms.No.13, Environment and Forest (FR14),
3rd February 2010.]
No.II(2)/EF/284/2010.—In exercise of the powers conferred
by Section 16 of the Tamil Nadu Forest Act, 1882 (Tamil Nadu
Act V of 1882), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby declares
the area, the boundaries of which are specified in the Schedule
below, as reserved forest with effect on and from 18th November
2010.

BOUNDARIES
North—The boundary starting from the trijunction point of
Survey Field Nmubers 87/1, 88 and 92 of No. 80. Sengurichi
Village, the boundary runs generally towards east and North-
East and runs along the south and South-East boundary
line of Survey Field Numbers 88, 89 and 90 till it reaches
the trijunction point of Survey Field Numbers 87/1, 90 and
91. Then it runs towards east and runs along the southern
side boundary line of Survey Field Numbers 91 and 69, till
it reaches the trijunction point of Survey Field Numbers 69,
87/1 and 87/2 of 80. Sengurichi village.
East.—Thence the boundary turns generally towards South
and runs along the West and South Boundary line of Survey
Field Number 87/2 and it reaches the trijunction point of Survey
Field Numbers 71, 87/1 and 87/2. Then the boundary turns
towards South and runs along the west north and west side
boundary line of Survey Field Number 71 till it reaches the
trijunction point of Survey Field Numbers 71, 84 and 87/1 of
80. Sengurichi Village.
South.—Thence the boundary turns generally towards west
and runs along the North boundary line of Survey Field Numbers
84 and 86 till it reaches the trijunction point of Survey Field
Numbers 86, 87/1 and 92 of 80. Sengurichi Village.
West.—Thence the boundary turns generally towards north
west and runs along with eastern boundary line of Survey Field
Number 92 of 80. Sengurichi Village and reaches the starting
point.
ADMITTED RIGHT
Right is admitted for the pathway running inside the Forest
Block for the usage of pattadars in Survey Field Numbers 89,90
and others. The Pathway starts from south-west corner of
Survey Field Number 87/1 and runs towards north and reaches
the South-East corner of Survey Field Number 89. The length
of pathway is 168 metres and width is one metre. The bearing
and distances are given below:

23rd April 2010, CˆF¬ó 10, F¼õœÀõ˜ Ý‡´-2041.]
No.II(2)/HWMP/285/2010.—The Governor of Tamil Nadu
having been satisfied that the lands specified in the Schedule
below are required for the purpose of construction of Road Over
Bridge in lieu of LC 45A and 45B located at Kolatheri Village
in Wallajah Taluk and it having already been decided that the
entire amount of compensation to be awarded for the lands
to be paid out of the funds controlled and managed by the
Chief Engineer (H) Projects, Highways Department and after
considering the cause shown by the owners or other persons
having interest in the said lands as the case may be do hereby
publish the following notice under sub-section (1) of Section
15 of the Tamil Nadu Highways Act, 2001 (Tamil Nadu Act
34 of 2002):—
NOTICE
Under sub-section (1) of Section 15 of the Tamil Nadu
Highways Act, 2001 (Tamil Nadu Act 34 of 2002) the Governor
of Tamil Nadu hereby acquires the lands specified in the
schedule below admeasuring 0.04.5 Hec. to the same, a little
more or less needed for the purpose of construction of Road
Over Bridge in lieu of LC 45A and 45B located at Kolatheri
Village in Wallajah Taluk, Vellore District.
The plan of the lands are kept in the office of the Tahsildar,
Wallajah, Vellore District and may be inspected at any time
during officer hours.
