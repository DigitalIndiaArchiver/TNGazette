The following Government Order is Published:-
  Read:
1.
From the Joint Secretary to Government of India, Ministry
of Agriculture, Department of Animal Husbandry, Dairying
and Fisheries, New Delhi, Do. No. 2-121/2013-AHT/FF/
PT dated 20-3-2014.
2.
From the Under Secretary to Government of India, Ministry
of Agriculture, Department, of Animal Husbandry, Dairying
and Fisheries, New Delhi, F.No.2-47/ 2009-AHT(FF)/
NLM Vol III, dated 21.05.2014.
Order: No. 147, Animal Husbandry, Dairying and Fisheries
(AH2), 21st July 2014.
Livestock Sector plays an important and vital role in
providing  nutrition, income generation, employment and poverty
alleviation for the rural poor. Livestock and poultry rearing is
an imperative factor for improving the Indian economy and
food security. The National Livestock Mission to be implemented
by the Government of India intends to achieve sustainable
growth and development of livestock sector including poultry,
increasing the fodder production and promoting skill
development.
2. The Government of India, in its letter first read above
has informed that it has formulated a National Livestock Mission
(NLM) to be implemented from 2014-2015 by subsuming and
modifying seven Centrally Sponsored Schemes and seven
Central Sector Schemes and the National Livestock Mission
is organised into the following four Sub-Missions:-
(a) Sub-Mission on Livestock Development,
(b) Sub-Mission on Pig Development for the
North
Eastern Region,
(c) Sub-Mission on Feed and Fodder Development and
(d) Sub-Mission on Skill Development, Technology
Transfer and extension.
3.
The  Government  of  India in  their National Livestock
Mission Operational Guidelines 2014-2015 at Para 4.2.1 has
envisaged that the respective State Government / Union
Territories are required to notify the State Level Executive
Committee  (SLEC) under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary,
having representatives from other concerned Departments of
State Government, the State Agricultural Universities (SAU),
Institute under Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR),
Dairy Cooperatives, Farmers’ / Animal Rearers’ / Poultry
Associations, etc. Which will oversee implementation of the
Mission in respective State. The Central Government will
nominate its representative who will be a member in the State
Level Executive Committee (SLEC). At operational level, State
Governments shall constitute and notify a State Livestock
Mission (SLM) which would be an empowered body for
implementing the Mission Programmes at State and District
Levels, The Principal Secretary/ Secretary, Animal Husbandry
will be the State Mission Director. He will also function as  the
Member-Secretary of SLEC. The Director of Animal Husbandry
will service the SLEC and the SLM, Panchayat Raj Institutions
(PRI) existing in the State will be fully involved implementation
of the Mission, for forwarding the above proposal to Government
of India for Financial assistance. Further at District Level, the
NLM Operational Guidelines stipulates the District Livestock
Mission Committee (DLMC) will be responsible for carrying
forward the objectives of the Mission for project formulation,
implementation and monitoring. Accordingly,  State Government /
UT are required to issue necessary notification for constitution
of DLMC which will be headed by the District Collector, having
as members the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Zila Parished
or Zila Panchayat / Chief Development Officer/ District
Development Officer / PD of District Rural Development Agency
(DRDA), representatives from concerned line Departments,
Dairy Cooperatives, Farmers / Animal Rearers / Poultry
Associations, Marketing Boards, Local Banks, Self Help Groups
and other Non Governmental Organizations.

5. The State Level Executive Committee (SLEC) shall be
responsible for
(i) Preparing proposals to be placed before the NLM
of Government of India as per the operational guidelines.
(ii) Monitoring the progress of the sanctioned projects/
schemes.
(iii) Reviewing the implementation of the scheme’s
objectives and ensure that the programmes are implemented
in accordance with the guidelines laid  down.
(iv) Ensuring that no duplication of efforts or resources
takes place.
(v) Commissioning / undertaking field studies to monitor
the implementation of the projects.
(vi) Initiating evaluation studies from time to time, as
may be required.
6. The State Level Executive Committee (SLEC) shall meet
as and when required, but shall meet atleast once in a quarter.
The Committee shall be treated as First Class Committee and
the Non Official Members of the Committee are eligible to
Traveling Allowance / Daily Allowance as per rules in force.
7. The Government also orders that the Director of Animal
Husbandry and Veterinary Services shall be the estimating,
controlling and implementing authority under the projects/
scheme covered in National Livestock Mission, Government of
India and he is directed to prepare such proposals to place
the same before the aforesaid Committee  Members, obtain
their approval and to forward the approved proposals along
with the minutes of SLEC to the Government in Animal
Husbandry, Dairying and Fisheries Department for onward
transmission to the National Livestock Mission, Government of
India in co-ordination with Tamil Nadu Veterinary and Animal
Sciences University / Commissioner of Milk Production and
Dairy Development, if need be. In respect of Schemes
implemented by the Tamil Nadu Co-operative Milk Producers’
Federation/ Commissioner of Milk Production and Dairy
Development, the Commissioner of Milk Production and Dairy
Development shall take separate and similar action as stated
above.
8. Further, the Director of Animal Husbandry and Veterinary
Services is also directed to pursue with the Government of
India for nominating their representative who has been included
as a member in the State Level Executive Committee (SLEC).
As per the above operational guidelines of National Livestock
Mission, Government of India, the Director of Animal Husbandry
and Veterinary Services shall service the SLEC and the SLM
and the Panchyat Raj Institutions (PRI) existing in the State
shall be fully involved in implementation of the  Mission, for
forwarding the above proposal to Government of India for
financial assistance.  The Director of Animal Husbandry and
Veterinary Services shall carry out the responsibilities as per
the operational guidelines of NLM, Government of India.
9.
Orders with regard to constituting and  notifying SLM
(State Livestock Mission), DLMC (District Livestock Mission)
SLSMC (State Level Sanctioning and Monitoring Committee)
for operating EDEG (Entrepreneurship Development and
Employment Generation) component through on EDEG Cell,
headed by an Officer not below the rank of Joint Director for
servicing the above SLSMC as per the aforesaid guidelines
shall be issued in due course and the Director of Animal
Husbandry and Veterinary Services is directed to forward such
proposals to the Government at the earliest in conformity with
the said guidelines.
10. This order is issued with the concurrence of Finance
Department vide its U.O. No. 39716/Fin. (AH&F)/2014,
dated 21-7-2014.
