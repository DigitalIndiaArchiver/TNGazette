HOME DEPARTMENT
CONSTITUTION OF ONE SUBORDINATE JUDGE’S COURT TO DEAL WITH MCOP CASES AT COIMBATORE UNDER
TAMIL NADU CIVIL COURTS ACT.
[G.O. Ms. No. 555, Home (Courts II), 19th July 2012.]
NOTIFICATION – I
No. II(2)/HO/431(c-1)/2012.
In exercise of the powers conferred by the first paragraph of Section 4 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act, 1873 (Central
Act III of 1873), the Governor of Tamil Nadu, after consultation with High Court, Madras, hereby fixes the number of
Subordinate Judges to be appointed under the said Act for Coimbatore District as SIX with effect on and from the date on
which the Subordinate Judge assumes charge of the Subordinate Judge’s Court, Coimbatore.
NOTIFICATION – II
[G.O. Ms. No. 555, Home (Courts II), 19th July 2012.]
No. II(2)/HO/431(c-2)/2012.
In exercise of the powers conferred by the second paragraph of Section 4 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act, 1873
(Central Act III of 1873), the Governor of Tamil Nadu, after consultation with High Court, Madras, hereby fixes the number
of Subordinate Judge to be appointed to the Subordinate Judge’s Court at Coimbatore in Coimbatore District as ONE with
effect on and from the date on which the Subordinate Judge assumes charge of that Court.

NOTIFICATION – III
[G.O. Ms. No. 555, Home (Courts II), 19th July 2012.]
No. II(2)/HO/431(c-3)/2012.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 5 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act, 1873 (Central Act III of
1873), the Governor of Tamil Nadu, hereby fixes Coimbatore as the place at which the Subordinate Judge’s Court
shall be held.
NOTIFICATION – IV
[G.O. Ms. No. 555, Home (Courts II), 19th July 2012.]
No. II(2)/HO/431(c-4)/2012.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 10 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act, 1873 (Central Act III of 1873), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu,  hereby  fixes the entire  Coimbatore District as the local limits of the jurisdiction of the Subordinate
Judge’s Court, Coimbatore.
