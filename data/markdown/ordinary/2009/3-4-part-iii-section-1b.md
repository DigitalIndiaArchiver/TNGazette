FINANCE  DEPARTMENT
Amendments to Special Rules  for the Tamil Nadu General Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 549, Finance (OP-I),23rd December 2008,  Margazhi 8, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2039.]
No. SRO B-6/2009.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India, the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu General
Service (Section 16, in Volume - II of the Tamil Nadu Services Manual, 1969) :—
2. The amendments hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on the 15th March 1985.
AMENDMENTS
In the said Special Rules,—
(1) In part-I, after the entries relating to Class XII-C the following Class and entries shall be inserted, namely:—
“Class XII-D Joint Secretary to Government (Non-IAS) in Finance and Planning, Development and Special Initiatives
Departments.”;
(2) In Part-II, after the rules relating to Class XII-C, the following Class and Rules shall be inserted, namely:—
“Class XII-D Joint Secretary to Government (Non-IAS) in Finance and Planning, Development and Special Initiatives
Departments.—
1.
Constitution.—This Class shall consist of the Joint Secretary  to Government (Non-IAS) in Finance and
Planning, Development and Special Initiatives Departments.
2.
Appointment.—Appointment to the post shall be made by promotion from among the holders of the post of
Deputy Secretary to Government (Non-IAS) in the Finance  Department and Planning, Development and Special Initiatives
Departments.
3.
Promotion.—Promotion to the post shall be made on grounds of merit and ability, seniority being considered
only where merit and ability are approximately equal.
4.
Preparation of Approved List.—The crucial date for preparation of approved list for the post is first January
of every year.”
K. GNANADESIKAN,
Principal Secretary to Government.
HOME DEPARTMENT
Amendment to Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Special Police Subordinate Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 1644, Home (Pol.9), 18th December 2008.]
No. SRO B-7/2009.—In exercise of the powers conferred by Sections 8 and 10 of the Tamil Nadu District Police
Act, 1859 (Central Act XXIV of 1859) and Sections 9 and 11 of the Chennai City Police Act, 1888 (Central Act III  of 1888)
read with the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India and all other powers hereunto enabling the Governor of
Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendment to Special Rules for Tamil Nadu Special Police Subordinate Service
(Section 44 in Vol. III of Tamil Nadu Services Manual, 1986).
The amendment hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force with effect from 18th December 2008.

AMENDMENT
In the said Special Rules:
(i)
in Rule 14, in the Table in category (2) in column (2) after the words “the upper age limit shall be 33 years
of age” the following words shall be added namely:—
“Provided further that for the appointment of candidate who is a member of Backward Class/Most Backward
Classes/Denotified Communities the upper age limit shall be 30 years of age”
(ii)
In the same table under category 4 to 6 after the words “age limit shall be 29 years” the following shall be
added namely:—
“Provided further that for appointment of a candidate who is a member of Backward Class/Most Backward Classes/
Denotified Communities age limit shall be 26 years”.
S. MALATHI,
Principal Secretary to Government.
RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND PANCHAYAT RAJ  DEPARTMENT
Amendment to Ad hoc Rules  relating to Tamil Nadu Panchayat Development Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 2, Rural Development and Panchayat Raj (E-3), 2nd January 2009.]
No. SRO B-8/2009.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India, the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendment to the rules published with the Rural Development
Department Notification No.SRO B-63/2000 at pages 73 to 77 of Part—III Section 1(b) of the Tamil Nadu Government
Gazette, dated the 24th May 2000.
2. The amendment hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on the 1st April 2006.
AMENDMENT
In the said Rules, for the expression “Rural Development Department” wherever it occurs, the expression
“Rural Development and Panchayat Raj Department” shall be substituted.
K. ASHOK VARDHAN SHETTY,
Principal Secretary to Government.
