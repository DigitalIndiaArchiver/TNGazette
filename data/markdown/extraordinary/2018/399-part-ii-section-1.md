In the reference second read above, the Additional Chief Secretary/ Commissioner of Revenue Administration has stated as 
follows:-
 
(a) Tamil Nadu is multi hazard prone and faces brunt of the Cyclonic storms during the Northeast Monsoon periods. In 
addition, Tamil Nadu also faces spells of heavy downpours and cloud bursts resulting in damages due to ﬂ oods.

(b) During the current Northeast Monsoon, the Cyclonic Storm “Gaja” hit and crossed the Tamil Nadu coast between 
Nagapattinam and Vedaranyam on the early hours of 16-11-2018. As a result, coastal districts as well as interior districts received 
rainfall coupled with cyclonic winds gusting upto 110 Kmph. On 16-11-2018, the average rainfall recorded in the State was 
22.31 mm with 43 stations recording very heavy rainfall in the State.
 
(c) The winds that accompanied Cyclone Gaja were immensely powerful and as a result trees were completely uprooted 
and strewn across roads. The districts plunged into darkness due to the damage to transmission & distribution lines and 
transformers.  In order to prevent loss of lives, people living in low lying areas were evacuated to relief camps and were provided 
with food, safe drinking water and basic sanitation facilities in the relief shelters. 
 
(d) In spite of the extensive preparedness measures taken by the Government of Tamil Nadu for Cyclone “Gaja”, loss of 
human lives, livestock, poultry and damages to huts, tiled houses, agricultural crops could not be averted and this damage has 
resulted in loss of livelihood of the poor.  Further, due to the magnitude of the Cyclone “Gaja”, extensive damages have also been 
reported to the power infrastructure, aﬀ ecting rural industries and having cascading eﬀ ect on livelihood opportunities in formal and 
informal sectors.  Similarly, agriculture including horticulture and plantation crops, road networks, ﬁ sheries infrastructure, drinking 
water supply, community assets of local bodies etc., have also been extensively damaged, leading to loss of livelihood.
 
(e) The districts of Thanjavur, Nagapattinam, Tiruvarur and Pudukottai faced the brunt of the Gaja cyclone.  The 
cyclone severely aﬀ ected the above districts, caused extensive damages to huts/houses, agriculture, horticulture, infrastructure, 
transport etc., leaving thousands of family in the relief shelters,  inﬂ icted huge blow to power infrastructure, leading to crippling of 
communication and leading to severe impact on livelihood opportunities. 
 
(f) In view of the above reported extensive damages to the infrastructure caused due to Cyclone “Gaja”, normal life of 
the general public has been thrown out of gear in many taluks of Nagapattinam, Tiruvarur, Thanjavur, Pudukkottai districts leading 
to severe loss of livelihood.  Taking into account the largescale damages caused to agricultural/horticultural crops and ﬁ sheries, 
power & infrastructure in many villages, the District Collectors of the above districts have reported that the Districts have been 
severely aﬀ ected due to Cyclone “Gaja”. 
 
(g) In respect of Dindugal district, Kodaikanal division has been severely aﬀ ected and Reddiyarchathiram block has 
been moderately aﬀ ected, besides sporadic damages in Dindigul East, Dindigul west, Nilakottai, Natham, Athoor, Palani, 
Oddanchathram and Vedasandur Taluks of Dindigul.  The District Collectors of other Districts such as Sivagangai, Tiruchirappalli, 
Madurai, Theni, Cuddalore and Karur have stated that their districts are also mildly aﬀ ected due to cyclone storm “Gaja”. 
2. The Additional Chief Secretary/ Commissioner of Revenue Administration has further stated that  considering the fact that 
Cyclone “Gaja” has aﬀ ected all walks of life, ravaged the economy of ﬁ shermen, crippled power, transport infrastructure and dealt 
a heavy blow to agricultural and horticultural crops. The Additional Chief Secretary/Commissioner of Revenue Administration has 
requested the orders of the Government on the following:-
 
(i) To declare the districts of Nagapattinam, Tiruvarur, Thanjavur, Pudukkottai and Kodaikanal division in Dindigul district 
in the State of Tamil Nadu to be declared as “Cyclone Aﬀ ected”. 
 
(ii) To declare the West Taluk of Dindigul District; Manapparai and Marungapuri Taluks of Tiruchirappalli District as 
moderately aﬀ ected; and 
 
(iii) To declare the following Taluks of respective Districts to be declared as mildly aﬀ ected: -
