Secretariat, 12th August 2009.
Rescission of Ad-interim Order attaching of money and
other properties of Thiruvalargal Sri Andal Finance
and its Sister Concerns, Gudiyatham, Vellore District
under Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors
(in Financial Establishments) Act.
No.II(2)/HO/407/2009.
[Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors (in
Financial Establishments) Act, 1997 (Tamil Nadu Act 44 of
1997)—Thiruvalargal Sri Andal Finance and its Sister
Concerns, Gudiyatham, Vellore District— Default in return of
Deposits by the Financial Establishment—Ad-interim order
attaching of the money and other properties of the said
Financial Establishment under Section 3—Order—Issued —
Cancelled.]
The following Government Order is published:
[G.O. Ms. No. 451, (Home (Pol.19), 9th June 2009.]
READ:
(1) G.O. Ms. No. 1066, Home, dated 24th October
2001.
(2) From the Inspector-General of Police, Economic
Offences Wing-II/2001, dated 7th April 2006.
(3) From the Inspector-General of Police, Economic
Offences Wing-II, Chennai, D.O. Lr. No. C1/3827/EOW-II/2001,
dated 26th May 2006.
(4) Government Letter No. 35594/Cts.IIA/2006, dated
13th September 2006 and 23rd October 2008.
(5) From the Inspector-General of Police, Economic
Offences Wing-II, Chennai, even No. and Dated 21st May
2008 and 19th November 2008.
Order.—No. 451, Home (Pol.19), 9th June 2009.
The Government in its order first read above have issued
orders attaching the properties of Thiruvalargal Sri Andal
Finance and its Sister Concerns, Gudiyatham, Vellore District
under Section 3 of the Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests
of Depositors (in Financial Establishements) Act, 1997 for
default in return of deposits made by the depositors after
maturity.
2.
The Inspector-General of Police, Economic Offences
Wing-II, Chennai in his letter second read above has reported
that the case against Sri Andal Finance and its Sister
Concerns, Gudiyatham, Vellore District has been registered
under Non-Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors
(in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997 in the year 1995 i.e.
prior to the enactment of Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests
of Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997. In this
regard the Government have issued orders for attachment
of properties of the said Financial Establishment under
Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors (in Financial
Establishements) Act, 1997 instead of authorization under
Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance 1944 Act.
3.
The Government Pleader, High Court, Chennai in his
opinion has stated that the offence invoived in the present
case is not an offence under the Tamil Nadu Protection of
Interests of Depositors  (in Financial Establishments) Act,
1997. It is an offence only under the provisions of Criminal
Law Amendment Act, 1944. As such, the Government can
also consider to withdraw the G.O.Ms. No. 1066, Home
Department, dated 24th October 2001 and also to issue
separate orders authorizing the Competent Authority viz.,
Deputy Superintendent of Police, Economic Offences Wing
to take appropriate action against Sri Andal Auto Finance
and 3 Sister concerns in Vellore District which will be the
only proper course of law.
4.
The Government after careful examination decided to
accept the proposal of the Inspector-General of Police,
Economic Offences Wing-II, Chennai in para 2 above.
5.
Accordingly in exercise of the powers conferred by
Section 3 of the Tamil Nadu Protection of interests of
Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997
(Tamil nadu Act 44 of 1997), the Governor of Tamil Nadu
hereby rescinds the ad-interim order of attachment issued
in the Government Order first read above.
(By order of the Governor)
S. MALATHI,
Principal Secretary to Government.
