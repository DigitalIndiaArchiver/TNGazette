1. Short Title and Commencement
1.1  These rules shall be called the Tamil Nadu Non-Lapsable Road Safety Fund Rules, 2022. 
1.2  They shall come into force on the date of their publication in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette. 
2. Objectives
2.1 The Tamil Nadu Non-Lapsable Road Safety Fund has been created to  implement Road Safety measures 
such as Road Engineering, Traﬃ  c Enforcement, Trauma Care, etc., and Road Safety awareness creating 
activities through digital/print media, conducting of road safety exhibition, etc. 
2.2 The fund primarily caters to rapid deployment of funds to minimize delays out of conventional funding 
mechanisms to further road safety and to facilitate innovative interventions to aid and augment the vision 
of 'Accident free Tamil Nadu by 2030'. 
3. Definitions
In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires, 
(a)  'Fund' means Tamil Nadu Non-Lapsable Road Safety Fund. 
(b)  'Year' means financial year. 
(c)  'Committee' means 'Inter Departmental Committee on Road Safety Fund'. 
(d)  'Safety' means preventive measures taken for safe road transport. 
(e)  'Traffic Education' means appropriate knowledge of rules and regulations relating to traffic on Road safety.
(f)  'Traffic Enforcement' means measures taken to ensure smooth transport operations, i.e., identifying traffic violations, 
reduction of road accidents, etc. 
(g)  'Emergency Care’  means care taken during emergency situations created during road transportation which includes 
medical and other care. 
4. Constitution
The fund shall be constituted from the amount received through the following: 
(a)  Grant received from State Government. 
(b)  Grants received from the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH), Government of India, International 
Organizations, Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) or any other Non-Profitable Organizations for the purpose 
of Road Safety. 
(c)  By transfer of 50% of the compounding fees collected under Motor Vehicles Rules and spot fines collected by 
Police and Transport Department. 
5. Administration 
5.1  The Transport Commissioner / Road Safety Commissioner shall be the controlling authority of the fund. 
5.2  The fund shall be administered by the Inter Departmental  Committee on Road Safety Fund consisting of the 
following officials.

5.3 The Inter Departmental Committee on Road Safety Fund shall meet once in six months or as and when the necessity 
arises. 
 
5.4 The quorum of the committee shall not be less than four. 
6. Proposals that Qualify for Assistance under the Fund 
6.1  Execution of road infrastructural enhancements. 
6.2  Purchase of barricades, cones and other traffic calming and regulating equipments / measures. 
6.3  Road safety awareness activities such as traffic education parks, road safety exhibition, etc., 
6.4  Creating Road Safety Awareness through digital/print media. 
6.5  Innovative and experimental measures from all Stakeholder Departments to further the cause of Road Safety. 
6.6  Organizing various activities like painting, essay writing, debate, competitions, etc., on awareness of traffic 
rules/regulations to students. 
6.7  Organizing training/workshop at Anna Administrative Staff College. 
6.8 Ensuring collection, quality and analysis of data on Road Safety preferably through iRAD app/RADMS. Accident 
rectification proposals identified through iRAD app shall be accorded priority. 
6.9 Provision of funds to the Education Department towards the activities of Road Safety Club established in schools 
and its subsequent enhancement. 
6.10 Organizing Road Safety week/month every year as directed by Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH), 
Government of India. 
6.11 Preparation of books, audio, visual and other instructional material and organizing training for drivers, traffic police, 
paramedical personnel and different classes of road users. 
6.12 Studies to identify the causes of road accidents, to identify accident prone spots and recommend remedial measures. 
6.13 Preparing slides on traffic rules and their public screening. 
6.14 Put up hoardings and release advertisements in newspapers on traffic rules and road safety. 
6.15 Release of Hon'ble Chief Minister's Road Safety messages during the road safety week / month celebrations every 
year. 
6.16 Proposals on Road Safety enhancements from Village Panchayats shall be actively encouraged and the same shall 
be sent to Transport Commissioner / Road Safety Commissioner after approval by concerned District Road Safety 
Committee.

6.17 Accidents spots as captured by Integrated Road Accident Database (iRAD) and discussed in District Road Safety 
Committee (DRSC) meeting shall be given preference for remedial measures in the Road Safety Fund proposals. 
6.18 Special projects that the Committee considers necessary, a Report on this shall be submitted to the Government

6.19 Purchase and maintenance of computer for traffic data / accident data analysis. 
6.20 Road Safety programmes / any other Special initiatives recommended by the Transport Commissioner/Road Safety

and sanctioned as per the guidelines issued from time to time. 
6.21 State Level Road Safety proposals shall be given top priority. 
7. Procedure for Allocation of Fund 
7.1  Necessary provisions for the Non-Iapsable Road Safety Fund shall be made in the budget by the 
Government every financial year based on the proposal from the Transport Commissioner / Road Safety 
Commissioner. 
7.2  All Proposals from the District Collectors / Stakeholder Departments shall ordinarily be sent to the Transport 
Commissioner / Road Safety Commissioner through the Head of Department concerned after approval by the 
concerned District Road Safety Committee. 
7.3  The State Level Road Safety proposals shall be sent by the Head of Departments directly to the Transport 
Commissioner / Road Safety Commissioner. 
7.4  The Member Secretary shall process the proposal and obtain recommendation of the Transport Commissioner/
Road Safety Commissioner and make necessary arrangement to place before the Inter Departmental Committee 
for approval. 
7.5  The Stakeholder Departments while presenting their proposals before the (Inter Departmental Committee) IDC for 
sanction of Road Safety Fund, not below the rank of second level officer of the department shall explain the details 
of the proposals. 
7.6  On approval of the Committee, the Member Secretary shall issue necessary proceedings. 
7.7  Proposals claiming fund from Road Safety Fund shall have adequate explanation for not claiming the same through 
their own departmental funding mechanisms. 
7.8  No stakeholder department shall claim Road Safety Fund for Project which has already been claimed funding through 
some other scheme. The proposals must be certified that they are not claimed under any other sources. 
7.9 The Head of Department of Stakeholder Departments should quote uniform price for each and every Road Safety 
equipment/work throughout Tamil Nadu while seeking funds through the District Road Safety Committee. 
7.10 Proposals other than Road Safety related works shall not be entertained.
7.11 All the Stakeholder Departments should ensure that there is no repetition of items of works from their respective 
domain. 
7.12 Utmost care shall be exercised by the Stakeholder Departments while sending proposals under Read Safety Fund 
requirements. Any alteration/modification/ratification for the proposals already forwarded shall not be entertained. 
7.13 In case of pending works / purchases, the status of the same shall be communicated to the Transport Commissioner / 
Road Safety Commissioner periodically not exceeding a period of three months. Works sanctioned under 
Road Safety Fund and pending for more than one year shall be taken up by (Inter Departmental Committee) 
IDC for review. 
8. Sanction and Release of Funds   
8.1  The eligibility and the scale of funding and the nature of project shall be determined and decided by the 
(Inter Departmental Committee) IDC only.

9. Uilization of Road Safety Fund   
9.1  The Road Safety Fund sanctioned to the Stakeholder Departments, i.e., Police, Transport, Highways, Health and 
Education Departments shall be utilized only for the purpose for which it is sanctioned. The Road Safety Fund 
should not be utilized for other than the purpose for which the fund was sanctioned and if not, the same should be 
remitted back to the Fund account. 
9.2  All the Stakeholder Departments shall submit the Utilization Certificate (U.C) for the amount sanctioned by the 
(Inter Departmental Committee) IDC before the end of financial year or otherwise after the completion of the project. 
9.3  The Utilization Certificates (U.C) for the sanctioned work shall be sent to the Transport Commissioner/Road Safety 
Commissioner through their respective District Collector (by soft copy) along with supporting documents like 
images/videos/portable document format (pdf) of the executed/purchased works and items. 
9.4  Authentication of Utilization Certificate 
(i)  The Utilization Certificate should be supported by copies of proper vouchers / bills duly signed by the Regional 
Officers/District Level Officers and maintained by their respective departments for audit purpose. 
(ii)  All the Utilization Certificates received shall be authenticated by the Personal Assistant (Accounts) to District 
Collectors concerned.
(iii) Utilization Certificate shall be certified by the respective District Collectors. 
9.5  On completion of scheme / proposal the Stakeholder Departments should have foolproof and transparent accounting 
procedures exclusively for Road Safety Fund. 
9.6  The unspent amount, if any, available with District Collectors / stakeholder department in the savings / current bank 
account should be remitted back to the Fund Account before the end of the financial year. 
9.7  In the beginning of every financial year, i.e., at the time of fresh allotment, previous year Utilization Certificate (U.C) 
shall be submitted. 
9.8  Fresh proposals received from Stakeholder Departments for sanction of Road Safety Fund shall be considered only 
after the receipt of Utilization Certificates (U.C) for the funds sanctioned during the previous years. 
9.9  At the end of each Financial Year, all the Stakeholder Departments shall submit reconciled statement from 
Accountant General (A.G) at Regional Level, for the amount sanctioned under this Road Safety Fund to the Transport 
Commissioner/Road Safety Commissioner.

9.10 The Head of the Departments of respective Stakeholder Departments shall monitor and ensure the timely completion 
of the works sanctioned and any delay / barriers shall be communicated to the Transport Commissioner / Road Safety 
Commissioner at once. 
9.11  Non Lapsable Road Safety Fund has been constituted mainly to utilize the fund without any financial year barriers 
but at the same time project should not be dragged on without any valid reasons. 
10. Audit  
10.1 The fund shall be audited by the Accountant General, at the time intervals prescribed by them. 
10.2 The fund shall be audited once in a year by the Local Fund Audit and a report on this shall be submitted to the 
Government by the Committee. 
11. General 
 
This fund should not be utilized for any compensatory purposes. 
12. Repeal 
 
The Tamil Nadu Road Safety Rules, 2000 is here by repealed. 
13. Interpretation 
 
If there be any questions about the interpretation of these rules, clarification released by the Home Department, Government 
of Tamil Nadu shall be final.
