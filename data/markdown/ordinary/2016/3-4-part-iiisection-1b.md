Amendments to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Agricultural Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 252, Agriculture (AAI), 17th November 2015.]
No. SRO B-5/2016.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India,
the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Agricultural
Service (Section I in Volume II of the Tamil Nadu Services Manual, 1970).
The amendments hereby made shall be deemed to have come into  force on and from the 17th March 1986.
AMENDMENTS
In the said Special Rules,-
(1) in rule 1, under the heading “B ORDINARY CATEGORIES” in “CLASS-IX” after the “Category 9
Lecturer in Agricultural Statistics”, the “Category 10 Horticultural Officer” shall be added;
(2) in rule 2, in the Tabular column, in column (2) and column (3), against the “CLASS-IX” in column (1), the
following entries shall, respectively, be added, namely:-
“Horticultural Officer By direct recruitment”
(3) in rule 4, after item (ii), the following item shall be added, namely:-
“(iii) to the post of Horticultural Officer in Class-IX if he has completed 28 years of age for the holders
of B.Sc. (Horticulture) and 30 years of age for the holders of M.Sc. (Horticulture) or Ph.D in Horticulture:
Provided that on and from 27-02-1989, the age limit shall be 30 years for the holders of B.Sc.
(Horticulture) and 32 years for the holders of M.Sc. (Horticulture) or Ph.D in Horticulture.”;
(4)
In rule 5, in sub-rule (a), - (i) after clause (i) the following clause shall be inserted, namely:- as “(ii) Notwithstanding
anything contained in sub-rule (a) (i) no person shall be eligible for appointment to the post of Horticultural Officer unless he
possesses the degree of B.Sc. (Horticulture).”
(ii) after as so inserted, the clauses (ii) and (iii) shall be renumbered as clauses (iii) and (iv) respectively.
(5) In rule 7, after sub-rule (c), the following sub-rule shall be added, namely:-
“(d) The Director of Horticulture and Plantation Crops shall be the authority competent to declare
the satisfactory completion of Probation for the  holders of the post of Horticultural Officer”.
(6) in rule 9, after the second proviso, the following provisos shall be added, namely:-
“Provided further that the Director of Horticulture and Plantation Crops shall be the authority competent for the posting
and transfer from one region to another region and the Deputy/Joint Director of Horticulture is competent authority for posting
and transfer within the district for the post of Horticultural Officer:
“Provided also that the Director of Horticulture and Plantation Crops shall be the authority competent to
grant all kinds of leave to the Horticultural Officer”.

7. Test.-(a) Every person appointed to the posts of Assistant Director of Horticulture or Horticultural Officer by direct
recruitment shall within a period of two years from the date of his appointment, pass the Account Test for Executive Officer,
Tamil Nadu Government Office Manual Test and the Department Test for officers of the Tamil Nadu
Horticulture Department:
Provided that a pass in the Account Test for Executive Officers shall not be necessary if he has already passed the Account
Test for Subordinate Officers Part I and Part II.
(b) If a person appointed to the post of Assistant Director of Horticulture or Horticultural Officer does not pass the tests
within the period of his probation, he shall not be allowed to draw his second and subsequent increments until he passes the
tests. Such ineligibility to draw increments shall not have the effect of postponing his future increments after he has passed
the tests.
8. Training.- (a) Every candidate appointed to the post of Assistant Director of Horticulture by direct recruitment shall within
the period of probation, undergo the prescribed training for a period of one year. This training shall include Foundational
course-B for three weeks duration conducted at the Anna Institute of Management, Chennai, and a pass in the test conducted
at the end of the said training within the period of his probation. The period of training shall be treated as duty and shall count
for probation and increment. The probation of the candidate shall not be declared until he passes the test conducted by the
Anna Institute of Management, Chennai, at the end of the Foundation course training.
(b) Every candidate appointed to the post of Assistant Director of Horticulture by promotion and who are below fifty years
of age shall also undergo the said training at the Anna Institute of Management, Chennai and pass the test conducted at the
end of the training. A pass in the test will be pre-condition for the sanction of the second and subsequent
increments.
(c) Every candidate on the completion of training shall serve the Government for a period of not less than five years,
failing which he shall be liable to pay a sum of Rs.50000/- (Rupees Fifty thousand only).
9. Probation.- (a) Every person appointed to the post of Horticultural Officer /Assistant Director of Horticulture by direct
recruitment shall, from the date on which he joins duty, be on probation for a total period of two years on duty within a
continuous period of three years.
(b) The persons appointed by recruitment by transfer to the post of Horticultural Officer from among the holders of post
of Deputy Horticultural Officer in Tami! Nadu Horticultural Subordinate Service shall be on probation for a period of one year
on duty within a continuous period of two years.
(c) The Director of Horticulture and plantation crops shall be the authority competent to declare the satisfactory
completion of probation of the holders of the post of Assistant Director of Horticulture.
(d) The Joint Director of Horticulture (or) Deputy Director of Horticulture, in charge of a region, as the case may be, and
the Director of Horticulture and Plantation Crops in the Directorate shall be the authorities competent to declare the satisfactory
completion of probation of the holders of the post of Horticultural Officer under their administrative control.
