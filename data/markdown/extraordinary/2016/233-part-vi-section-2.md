ORDER
An order to provide for specifications, reservation choice and allotment of symbols for the Ordinary Elections, 2016 to
Local Bodies in the State of Tamil Nadu and for matters connected therewith issued by the Tamil Nadu State Election
Commission in the Statutory Order first read above.
WHEREAS, the commission in the Statutory Order second read above notified the list of Recognised Political Parties
and the symbols reserved for them in TABLE I,  the list of Registered – Unrecognised Political Parties who are entitled to
have preference over independents in the allotment of free symbols in TABLE I -A and the list(s) of free symbols in tables
2 to 5 based on the notification issued by the Election Commission of India under the Election Symbols (Reservation &
Allotment) Order, 1968 and as per the provisions made in the election Rules pertaining to Local Bodies Elections, 2016 in
Tamil Nadu;
WHEREAS, the Election Commission of India substituted para 10B in the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment)
Order, 1968,  which  provides concession to candidates set up by a registered (unrecognised)  parties and to unrecognized
parties which were earlier recognized parties more than 6 (six) years back;
In consonance with para 10B of the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968 and in exercise of the
powers conferred under Articles 243 K and 243 ZA of the Constitution of India read with rule 33, 34 and 35 of the

Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules,1995, rule 31, 32 and 33 of the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Third Grade
Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections) Rules, 2006 and as per clause 11 of the Tamil Nadu Local Body

following amendment is hereby made to the Tamil Nadu Local Bodies Election, Symbols (Reservation and Allotment)
Order, 2016.
AMENDMENT
After paragraph 7(1) B, the following paragraph shall be inserted namely:-
“7 (1) C - Concession to candidates set up by registered (unrecognized) parties and to unrecognized parties
which were earlier recognized parties more than 6 years back.
If a political party has been allotted with a Common Symbol in the elections conducted by the Election Commission of
India, it may request to Tamil Nadu State Election Commission to continue to avail the same Common Symbol in the Local
Body Elections, 2016 conducted by the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission also:
Provided that the party sets up candidates at least in 5% (five percent) of the wards for the local bodies scheduled for
party based elections in the State and gives an undertaking to that effect:
Provided further that an application is made to the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission by the said party for the
concession for the allotment of Common Symbol to the candidates set up by it, not later than the third day after the
publication in the Official Gazette of the notification calling the election;
Provided further that the free symbol allotted as a common symbol to the candidates of a party under this paragraph
shall be available for allotment to candidates set up by the other party or independent candidates in those other wards in
which that party has not set up its candidates:
Provided further that the party also gives an undertaking that if the party does not set up candidates in the minimum
number of 5% (five percent) of wards in the State, its candidates shall not be entitled to allotment of a common symbol on
the date of allotment of symbols to them; and, in addition, the party shall be liable for such punitive action as the Commission
may consider appropriate:
Provided also that notwithstanding anything contained in paragraph 7(1) B, a political party which was earlier a
recognized political party and which lost its recognition more than 6 (six) years back will also be eligible under this paragraph
to the concession of allotment of the symbol which was earlier reserved for the party as above”.
