 WHEREAS based on the Order of Government of India to extend the Nation wide lockdown for a period of two weeks 
with effect from 4.5.2020 and taking into account the ground realities and the views expressed during consultation, the 
Government extended the State-wide lockdown for two weeks from 00:00 hrs of 4.5.2020 till 24:00 hrs of 17.5.2020 with 
various relaxations with restrictions in the order tenth read above. Further amendments and relaxations were also ordered in 
the Orders eleventh, twelfth and thirteenth read above.  
NOW THEREFORE the Government of Tamil Nadu based on the review meetings held on various dates, particularly 
views expressed by the District Collectors in the meeting held on 13.5.2020 through Video Conference, views expressed by 
the Medical Experts and Public Health Committee on 14.5.2020 and based on the decision taken in consultation with the 
Senior Ministers to contain the spread of Corona Virus, hereby order to extend the State-wide lockdown from 00:00 hrs of 
17.5.2020 till 24:00 hrs of 31.5.2020 under the Disaster Management Act, 2005 with various relaxations ordered in G.O.Ms.
No.217, Revenue and Disaster Management (DM-II) Department, dated 3.5.2020 and amendments issued thereon with the 
following further guidelines and relaxations: 
I.	 The following activities will continue to remain prohibited until further orders during the above lock down period: 
i.	
All schools, colleges, educational, training and coaching institutions, research institutions, etc.
ii.	
All religious places, places of worship shall be closed for public. Religious congregations are strictly prohibited.
iii.	
All cinema halls, shopping malls, gymnasiums, sports complexes, swimming pools, entertainment parks, theatres, 
bars, auditoriums, assembly halls and similar places.
iv.	
All social, political, sports, entertainment, academic, cultural, religious functions and other gatherings.
v.	
Air, Rail and Public Bus transport for passenger movement. Inter- State Rail services and Trains to and from 
Chennai to other parts of Tamil Nadu (Except Air, Rail and Public Bus transport with special permission from 
Central/State Governments)
vi.	
Taxi, Auto & Cycle Rickshaws. 
vii	
Metro Train/Electric Train services.
viii.	 Inter-state public transport.
ix.	
All lodging houses, hotels and Resorts. (except workers’ hostels)
x.	
Malls, barber shops, salons, spa & beauty parlours.
xi.	
Air-conditioned showrooms selling jewels, cloths and departmental stores will not be permitted.
xii.	
Only 20 persons shall be permitted during funeral.
xiii.	 Marriages shall be permitted with current restrictions.
Existing Restrictions:
Ø In the 12 districts viz Chennai, Kancheepuram, Tiruvallur, Chengalpattu, Villupuram, Cuddalore, Ranipet, Thirupathur, 
Kallakurichi, Tiruvannamalai, Ariyalur and Perambalur, all the present  restrictions will continue without any 
relaxations.
Ø Tourists travel to Nilgiris District, Kodaikanal and Yercaud Tourist Places will be prohibited.
Ø No activity will be permitted in the Containment Zones across the State, during the lockdown period
Ø The activities already allowed in areas falling under the jurisdiction of Greater Chennai Police will continue 
(Except in containment zones).
Ø The activities already allowed in all the areas across the State, except for areas under the jurisdiction of 
Greater Chennai Police will continue (Except in containment zones).

New Relaxations:
Based on the recommendations of the Expert Committee to arrive at the exit strategy on lockdown the following relaxations 
are allowed:
Ø In respect of 25 districts viz., Coimbatore, Salem, Erode, Tiruppur, Namakkal, Karur, Tirunelveli, Thoothukudi, Tenkasi, 
Kanniyakumari, Theni, Madurai, Sivaganga, Virudhunagar, Ramanathapuram, Dindigul, Pudukottai, Tiruchirappalli, 
Thiruvarur, Thanjavur, Nagapattinam, Dharmapuri, Krishnagiri, Vellore and Nilgiris the following relaxations are allowed:
l For Intra-district movement within concerned district without TN e-pass.
l People are requested to use transport facility within concerned district for essential services and for permitted works 
only. 
l For the movement from one district to another district, the existing system of using T.N. e-pass will continue.
l Not more than 20 persons in a Government/Private Bus, which are specially permitted for Government Works and 
Private industries, Not more than 7 persons in a Van, Not more than  3 persons in a SUV like Innova, Not more 
than 2 persons in a small Car (excluding driver) will only be permitted. 
l Rental vehicles and Taxies must be used only for essential services like Agriculture, Business, Medical and Office 
purposes in these 25 districts (Intra district movement only) without TN e-pass. General public should avoid 
unnecessary movement from home.  
Ø The proportion of persons employed in MGNREGS in rural areas will be increased from the present 50% to 100%.
Ø In relaxation to earlier orders, the permission to operate industries are relaxed further and permitted to operate with 
100% of employees in the industries working with less than 100 persons and 50% of employees or a minimum 
of 100 persons in the industries working with more than 100 persons in all Corporations, Municipalities and Town 
Panchayats in all areas across the State, except for areas under the jurisdiction of Greater Chennai Police. 
Ø The private and business organisations which are temporarily closed due to lockdown are allowed to use minimum 
staff for essential maintenance.
Ø Works relating 12th class(+2) examination valuations are exempted. 
Ø Training from independent coaches for the persons who prepare for National and International Sports events are 
exempted. However permission should be obtained from concerned District Collector/ Commissioner, Greater Chennai 
Corporation in Chennai.
Ø Taxi and auto rickshaws are permitted for urgent medical treatment with TN e-pass within the district, in respect of 
12 districts where Intra district movements are otherwise not permitted.
General
All District Collectors and Municipal Commissioners are instructed to ensure that the staff and workers are wearing the 
masks as advised by the Governments, follow social distancing, wash their hands by soaps two times a day and use sufficient 
disinfectants and strictly follow the Standard Operating Procedure issued by Government. As the Lockdown is continuing, 
assembling of more than 5 persons in public places shall not be allowed.
