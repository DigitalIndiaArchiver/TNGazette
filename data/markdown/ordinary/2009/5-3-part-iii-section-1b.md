Amendment to Ad hoc Rules relating to Tamil Nadu Medical Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 419, Health and Family Welfare (AA1), 16th December 2008,
Margazhi  1, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2039.]
No. SRO B-12/2009.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India,
the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendment to the rules published with the Health and
Family Welfare Department Notification No. SRO B-278/80, at pages 315-316 of Part III—Section 1(b) of the
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, dated the 18th June 1980.
2.
The amendment hereby made shall come into force with effect on and from 16th December 2008.
AMENDMENT
 In the said Rules, in rule 4, for sub-rule (b), the following sub-rule shall be substituted, namely:—
“(b) Other
Qualifications.—No person shall be eligible for appointment to the post, unless he possesses the
following qualifications, namely:—
(i)
Must have obtained M.Sc. degree in Speech and Hearing; (or) M.Sc. degree in Audiology; (or) M.Sc. degree
in Speech Language Pathology from the All India Institute of Speech and Hearing, Mysore or from any other University
or Institution recognised by the University Grants Commission for the purpose of its grant or by the Rehabilitation Council
of India; and
(ii) Must have working experience for a period of not less than two years in any Teaching Medical Institution,
affiliated to any University recognized by the University Grants Commission for the purpose of its grant:
Provided that if no suitable person with the above experience is available, persons who are, otherwise qualified, shall
be selected and an in-service training for a period of not less than six months, shall be given after appointment as part
of probation:
Provided further that other things being equal, preference shall be given to released Emergency Commissioned
Officers, released Short Service Commissioned Officers and other Ex-servicemen”.
