W HEREAS on considering the prevailing COVID-19 pandemic scenario a nd as per the directives of Government 
of India, Ministry of Home Aﬀ airs, State-wide complete lockdown was enforced throughout the State with eﬀ ect from 
4.00 A.M. of 10.05.2021 to 6.00 A.M. of 05.07.2021, in order to control the spread of COVID-19. 
2. Now, after reviewing the pandemic situation, the Government hereby order under Disaster Management Act, 2005 
to extend the lock down from 05.07.2021 to 06.00 A.M. on 12.07.2021 with following restrictions and relaxations.
I . The following activities shall continue to remain prohibited during the lockdown period.
 
i. 
Inter-State Public and Private Bus Transport
 
ii. 
All international air travel of passengers except for purposes as permitted by Ministry of Home Aﬀ airs.
 
iii. 
Cinema Theatres / Multiplex 
 
iv. 
All Bars
 
v. 
Swimming Pools
 
vi. 
All social / political gatherings
 
vii. Entertainment / sports / cultural events
 
viii. Schools and Colleges 
 
ix. 
Zoological Parks

Restrictions for Marriages and Funerals:-
 
i. 
Marriage related gathering - Number of guests not to exceed 50. 
 
ii. 
Funeral/ last rites related gathering - Number of persons not to exceed 20. 
All other activities other than those mentioned above shall be permitted.
II. The following permitted activities shall be allowed with certain restrictions as detailed below (Except in 
containment zones):-
 
i. 
All shops which were permitted to operate till 7.00 P.M. shall be permitted till 8.00 P.M.
 
ii. 
Government and Private Industrial Exhibitions shall be permitted for Business to Business interaction only. 
However, in the above exhibitions invitees alone shall be permitted. The Organisers, Proprietors of the Stalls, workers 
shall be permitted after undergoing RTPCR test / Vaccination with two doses.
 
iii. 
Dine-in facility shall be permitted in Restaurants, Hotels, Bakeries from 6.00 A.M. to 8.00 P.M. with
50 percent seating capacity and with adequate ventilation (Parcel facility will continue till 9.00 PM).
 
iv. 
Tea Shops shall be permitted with 50 percent customers at a time by following Standard Operating 
Procedures.
 
v. 
Recreation Clubs shall be permitted to function by allowing Gymnasium, Games and Restaurants alone 
with 50 percent customers at a time and by following the Standard Operating Procedures.
 
vi. 
IT/ITES shall be permitted with 50 percent workforce.
 
vii. Hotels, Lodges and Guest Houses shall be permitted to operate. However, Dormitories and 
dine-in facility in the restaurants attached to Hotels and Lodges shall be permitted with 50 percent capacity 
(with adequate ventilation).
 
viii. Museum, Monuments under Archaeology Department and Archaeological Excavation sites shall be permitted 
from 10.00 A.M. to 5.00 P.M.
 
ix. 
Gymnasiums and Yoga Training Centres shall be permitted with 50 percent customers at a time.
 
x. 
TASMAC shops shall be permitted from 10.00 A.M. to 8.00 P.M.
 
xi. 
All religious places shall be permitted for public worship by following the Standard Operating Procedures. 
“Thiruvizhakkal” and “Kudamuzhukku” shall not be permitted.
 
xii. Textile and Jewellery Shops shall be permitted with 50 percent customers at a time and by ensuring 
adequate ventilation.
 
xiii. Shopping Malls/ Market Complex shall be permitted to operate from 9.00 A.M. to 8.00 P.M. Dine-
in facility in the restaurants and food courts in Shopping Malls / Market Complex shall be permitted with 
50 percent capacity (with adequate ventilation). Cinemas and Play area in the Shopping Malls/ Market Complex shall 
not be permitted.
 
xiv. Intra District and Inter District Public Transport shall be permitted with 50 percent seating capacity, without 
air conditioning facility and by following Standard Operating Procedures.
 
xv. SRF / JRF, M.Phil, Phd. Students shall be permitted to pursue their education related projects in respective 
Colleges and Universities. Colleges and Universities shall function for the above mentioned purpose.
 
xvi. Government Training Institutes like Anna Institute of Management, SIRD etc. shall be permitted to function 
with 50 percent of trainees and by ensuring adequate ventilation. 
 
xvii. Entertainment/Amusement Parks, shall be permitted with 50 percent customers. However, events/rides in 
open spaces alone shall be permitted and water rides shall not be allowed. Wearing of Face Masks, frequent disinfection 
activities shall be ensured by the management.
 
xviii. Metro Rail permitted with 50 percent seating capacity.

III. General
 
(i) 
Shops permitted to function shall follow the following important Standard Operating Procedures.
 
 
 
Shops shall have mandatory hand hygiene (sanitizer dispenser) at the entrance and thermal screening 
shall also be done.
 
 
 
Management shall ensure that the workers and customers wear face masks compulsorily.
 
 
 
All shops and establishments using air conditioning facility shall ensure that the doors and 
windows are kept open for adequate ventilation. In order to ensure social distancing norms, more number of persons 
shall not be allowed at a time inside the shops.
 
 
 
Speciﬁ c markings with suﬃ  cient distance may be made at the entrance of the shops to manage 
the general public standing in the queue.
 
(ii) 
Test-Track-Treat-Vaccination-Covid-19 Appropriate Behaviour principle shall be strictly adhered to.
 
(iii) In order to contain the spread of Covid-19, the Containment Zones shall be demarcated upto Micro Level 
and the containment measures shall be initiated by the District Collectors and the Local Bodies as per the Standard 
Operating Procedures.
 
(iv) Within the demarcated Containment Zones, containment measures shall be scrupulously followed, as under:-
 
 
 
Only essential activities shall be allowed in the Containment Zones.
 
 
 
No activity, except medical emergencies and supply of essential goods and services shall be permitted.
 
 
 
There shall be intensive house-to-house surveillance by surveillance teams formed for the purpose.
 
 
 
The respective Departments shall strictly ensure the enforcement of the Standard Operating 
Procedures issued for various permitted activities.
 
(v) E-pass / E-registration for Inter-District Travel shall not be required.
3. In-order to control the spread of COVID-19, Public are requested to maintain the discipline and Covid appropriate 
behavior by avoiding unnecessarily coming out of the house and gathering in Public places.
4. The public are requested to seek medical advice of the nearest hospital as soon as symptoms of infection appear.
5. Industries which have been permitted to operate shall strictly adhere to the Standard Operating Procedures and 
carry out disinfection activities at regular intervals. Industries shall also initiate immediate action to vaccinate all their 
employees. In case of non-adherence of the Covid-19 appropriate behaviour and the Standard Operating Procedures 
issued, necessary action will be initiated against concerned establishments.
6. The Government also orders that no activities shall be permitted in the Containment Zones. 
7. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/ District Collectors and Police Oﬃ  cers concerned shall take all 
necessary measures for the strict enforcement of National Directives for Covid-19 management viz., wearing of face 
masks, hand hygiene and social distancing, screening & hygiene, frequent sanitization of entire workplace etc., and 
adherence of the Standard Operating Procedures issued for the permitted activities. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai 
Corporation/ District Collectors concerned may initiate appropriate action for imposing ﬁ nes on persons violating the 
National Directives. 
8. Any person violating these measures will be liable to be proceeded against as per the provisions of Section 
51 to 60 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, besides legal action under Section 188 of the IPC, and other legal 
provisions as applicable.
