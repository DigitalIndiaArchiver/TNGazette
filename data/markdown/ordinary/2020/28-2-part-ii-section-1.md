In the letter read above, the Additional Director General of Police / Inspector General of Prisons has stated that Prisons are 
an integral part of Criminal Justice System oﬀ ering punitive and correctional services, who are in conﬂ ict with the laws of the land. 
The punitive facet is to make them realize and repent for the oﬀ ence(s) and the Correctional Services provide an opportunity for 
assimilating them into the main stream of the society, Nowadays, the reformation and rehabilitation of Prison inmates is the core 
object of the Government of India and Tamil Nadu Government. Gradually, the objective of deterrence changed to reformation and 
this led to decrease in Prison punishments and increase in welfare activities and introduction of the system of encouraging good 
work and conduct in the form of remission / review of sentenes / wages / treatment in open conditions (Open Air Prisons / Leave / 
Prison Canteen Facilities / Interviews (in person /telephone) / apart from basic necessities such as good food, clean drinking water, 
clean environment etc.  Prisoners are treated with humane approach and dignity and various correctional measures such as 
education, training in Prison industries, employable training (skill development), Sports activities, libraries, Free Legal Aid, Special 
Interviews are taken to improve the life quality of prisoners in Prisons. Apart from that, Welfare Oﬃ  cers are acting as bridge 
between prisoners and their families and looking after the needs of the prisoners within the ambit of Prison Rules, Psychologists 
and Counsellores are working in Prisons to counsel the newly admitted prisoners and prisoners in distress and depression. 
Probation Wing exclusively for the welfare and rehabilitation of prisoners. After release, the prisoners are accommodated in jobs 
as per the skills and loans are arranged from banks to start petty trades.
2. The Additional Director General of Police / Inspector General of Prisons has further stated that,  the Hon'ble Supreme 
Court of India in W.P.No.406/2013 has directed to adopt uniformity in all the States as per Model Prison Manual 2016. Therefore, 
it would be appropriate to change the nomenclature of the "Tamil Nadu Prison Department" as "Department of Prisons and 
Correctional Services" and to re-designate the post of "Additional Director General of Police / Inspector General of Prisons" 
as "Director General of Prisons and Correctional Services" subject to the condition of without changing the nature of functions 
and pay scale of the Additional Director General of Police / Inspector General of Prisons.  He has, therefore, suggested to the 
Government to change the nomenclature  of the "Tamil Nadu Prison Department" as the "Department of Prisons and Correctional 
Services" and re-designate the post of "Additional Director General of Police / Inspector General of Prisons" as "Director General 
of Prisons and Correctional Services" subject to the condition of without changing the nature of functions and pay scale of the 
Additional Director General of Police/Inspector General of Prisons.
3. The Government have examined the above proposal of the Additional Dirctor General of Police / Inspector General 
of Prisons decided to accept it. Accordingly, Government direct that the nomenclature of the "Tamil Nadu Prison Department" 
be changed as "Department of Prisons and correctional Services" and the post of "Additional Director General of Police/
Inspector General of Prisons" be re-designated as "Director General of Prisons and Correctional Services" subject to the 
condition that there is no change in the nature of functions and pay scale of the Additional Director General of Police / Inspector 
General of Prisons.
4. This order shall come into force with immediate eﬀ ect.
