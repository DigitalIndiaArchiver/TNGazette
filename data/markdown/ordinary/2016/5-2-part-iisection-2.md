[G.O. (D) No. 04, Backward Classes, Most Backward Classes

No. II(2)/BCMBCMW/85/2016.—The Government have
carefully considered the appeal petition dated 19.08.2014
preferred by Thiru S.A.K. Ibrahim and three others under
sub-section (2) of Section 65 of the Waqf Act, 1995 (Central
Act 43 of 1995) to set aside the orders of the Tamil Nadu Waqf
Board in its item No. 92/12, in Rc. No. 13575/02/B8/Madurai,
dated 23.10.2013. It is found that the Tamil Nadu Waqf Board
had issued orders bringing the Kazimar Peria Pallivasal Waqf
(G.S.No. 112/Madurai), Madurai District under its direct
management as Thiru S.A.K. Ibrahim and three others have
been functioning as alleged trustees without the recognition of
the Tamil Nadu Waqf Board. The appellants have formed a
society called “Hazarath Kazi Tajuddin Society” by diverting the
income of the Kazimar Periya Pallivasal, Madurai District which
is also illegal as per the said Waqf Act, 1995 (Central Act 43
of 1995). After Careful consideration of the issues in the appeal
petition, the Government have come to the conclusion that there
is no illegality or incorrectness in the order passed by the
Tamil Nadu Waqf Board vide its proceedings No.92/12, dated
23.10.2013 in R.C.No.13575/02/B8/Madurai, Accordingly, the
appeal petition preferred by Thiru. S.A.K. Ibrahim and three
others is hereby rejected.
T. UDHAYACHANDRAN,
Secretary to Government (Incharge).
