HIGHER EDUCATION DEPARTMENT
Vesting of properties and scheme of administration
relating to “Muthu Natchathram Charitable Endowment
Fund” tenable in the Government College of Education
for Women, Coimbatore under Charitable Endowments
Act.
[G.O.Ms. No. 170, Higher Education (D2),

No.II(2)/HE/732/2010.—In the matter of the Charitable
Endowments Act, 1890, (Central Act VI of 1890) and in the
matter of “Muthu Natchathram Charitable Endowment Fund”
(hereafter referred to as “the said Endowment”) tenable in
the Government College of Education for Women,
Coimbatore-641 100.
I. VESTING OF PROPERTIES
Whereas, an application has been made to the Government
of Tamil Nadu under clause (b) of sub-section (1) of Section 6 of
the Charitable Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI of
1890) by Dr. A. Mary Lily Pushpam, Principal (in-charge),
Government College of Education  for Women, Coimbatore—
641 001, the donor of the said Endowment, for vesting the
property specified in the Schedule below, in the Treasurer of
Charitable Endowments, Chennai, appointed under Section 3
of the said Act for the State of Tamil Nadu, the Governor of
Tamil Nadu, in exercise of the powers conferred by Section 4
of the said Act, hereby orders that the said property be vested
in the Treasurer aforesaid, and be held by him and his
successors-in-office, subject to the provisions of the said Act
and the Rules which from time to time, may be or shall be
made or deemed to have been made thereunder and subject
to the conditions setforth in the Scheme settled under
Section 5 of the said Act, for the administration of the said
Endowment.
THE SCHEDULE
The Property
Rs. 25,000/- (Rupees twenty five thousand only)
II. SCHEME FOR ADMINISTRATION
Whereas, an application has been made to the Government
of Tamil Nadu under clause (b) of sub-section (1) Section 6
of the Charitable Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI of
1890) by Dr. A. Mary Lily Pushpam, Principal (in-charge),
Government College of Education for Women, Coimbatore,
the donor of the said Endowment specified above, for setting
a Scheme for administration of the said Endowment, the
Governor of Tamil Nadu, in exercise of the powers conferred
by sub-section (1) of Section 5 of the said Act, hereby settles
the Scheme setforth in the Schedule below for the
administration of the said Endowment and appoints the
Principal and one senior staff of the said college to administer
the property. The Scheme shall come into operation from
the date of publication of this Notification in the
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette.
THE SCHEDULE
1.
The Fund shall be called “Muthu Natchathram
Charitable Endowment Fund” tenable in the Government
College of Education for Women, Coimbatore-641 001.
2.
The Fund shall be administered by the Principal and
one Senior Staff of Government College of Education for
Women, Coimbatore-641 001.
3.
From and out of the annual interest accruing on the
Fund, the Prize shall be given to the top ranker of Mathematics
Major B.Ed., Student of the Government College of Education
for Women, Coimbatore-641 001.
4.
If more than one student secures the same marks,
the prize shall be equally distributed among them.
5.
The mode by which the interest amount shall be given
as prize to the student shall be specified by the administrators.
6.
The unutilized amount of the interest shall be added
to the corpus of the fund.
7.
The decision of the administrator will be final.
8.
The amount of the Fund shall be reinvested
in Government securities.
K. GANESAN,
Principal Secretary to Government.

No.II(2)/LE/738/2010.—The following draft of a Notification
which it is proposed to issue in exercise of the powers
conferred by clause (b) of sub-section (1) of Section 3 and
sub-section (2) of Section 5 of the Minimum Wages Act, 1948
(Central Act XI of 1948), is hereby published for information
of all persons likely to be affected thereby, as required by
clause (b) of sub-section (1) of Section 5 of the said Act.
2. Notice is hereby given that, the draft Notification will be
taken into consideration on or after the expiry of two months
from the date of publication of this Notification in the
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette and that any objection or
suggestion, which may be received from any person with
respect thereto, before the expiry of the aforesaid period of
two months will be considered by the Government of
Tamil Nadu. Objection or suggestion, if any, should be
addressed to the Principal Secretary to Government, Labour
and Employment Department, Secretariat, Chennai-600 009
through the Commissioner of Labour, Chennai-600 006.

Extra Wages:
Dhoti.— Extra wages shall be paid for the extra work. The rate
of extra wages vary from 5% to 15% or even more on the basic
wages depending upon the size of the pettu border design.
Sarees.— Extra wages shall be paid for the extra work. The
rate of extra wages vary from 5% to 20% or even more on the
basic wages depending upon the Merit/Technique involved in
the extra work (Border, Butta and Mundhi design, etc.)
Explanations.–(1) Dearness Allowance.—In addition to the
minimum wages fixed above, dearness allowance shall be paid
as indicated below:-—
(i) Dearness allowance shall be paid at the rate of 10% of
the basic wages plus extra wages linked with the
productivity from the date of publication of this
Notification in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette.
(ii) Additional increase of 5% on the prevailing basic wages
plus extra wages shall be granted every year on the
1st April till the next revision.
(2) Where the nature of work is the same no distinction in
the payment of wages shall be made between men and women
employees.
(3) Wherever the existing wages are higher than the
minimum wages fixed herein, the same shall be continued to
be paid.
