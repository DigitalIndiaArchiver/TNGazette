The following Government order is published:—
[G.O. Ms. No. 92, Agriculture (OS), 7th June 2011,

1. G.O. Ms. No. 28, Agriculture (OS) Department,

2. From the Commissioner of Agriculture Letter
No. Poc1/5379/10, dated 1st July 2010, 27th December 2010
and 5th April 2011.
Order: Ms. No. 92, Agriculture (OS), 7th June 2011.
In the G.O. first read above, orders were issued for permitting
setting up of Oilpalm Processing Unit at Vellore District and
taking up of Oilpalm activities by M/s. Vaidehi Properties Private
Limited.
2.
The Commissioner of Agriculture in his letters second
read above has stated that M/s. Vaidehi Properties Private
Limited in its representation dated 30th June 2010 has informed
that in terms of scheme of arrangement submitted before the
High Court at  Kolkatta the Honourable High Court has passed
an order in C.P. No. 213 of 2009 and CA No. 319 of 2009 and
has allowed transfer of business of the Agriculture division of
M/s. Vaidehi Properties Private Limited to M/s. Vaidehi Palm
Private Limited as stated in the subject order and he has
therefore requested the Government to give necessary
permission for Title change of the company from the existing
M/s. Vaidehi Properties Private Limited to M/s. Vaidehi Palm
Private Limited and he has also informed that on receipt of the
permission the Memorandum of Understanding already executed
has to be redrafted in the name of Vaidehi Palm Private Limited.
He has also reported that the Special Government Pleader in
his remarks have mentioned that the scheme of amalgamation
of the said two companies have been approved and there is no
impediment for amending the Memorandum of Understanding in
terms of the clause 29 of Memorandum of Understanding.
3. After careful examination, the Government accept the
request of the Commissioner of Agriculture for granting
permission for Title change of the company from the existing
M/s. Vaidehi Properties Private Limited to M/s. Vaidehi Palm
Private Limited and also permit the Commissioner of Agriculture
to execute the memorandum of understanding with the above
firm. The Government also direct the Commissioner of
Agriculture to send a copy of the Memorandum of Understanding
after execution with the firm.
(By Order of the Governor)
K. ARUL MOZHI,

LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT DEPARTMENT
Notifications under Industrial Disputes Act.
All Institutions engaged in procurement, storage
and distribution of Milk and Manufacture of
Milk Products and Registered under the Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 60, Labour and Employment (D2),
5th July 2011.]
No. II(2)/LE/360/2011.—Whereas the Governor of Tamil Nadu
is of the opinion that it is expedient and necessary in the public
interest to add “All Institutions engaged in procurement, storage
and distribution of milk and manufacture of Milk Products and
registered under the Tamil Nadu Co-operative Societies Act,
1983” to the First Schedule to the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
(Central Act XIV of 1947);
Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred by
sub-section (1) of Section 40 of the said Act, the Governor of
Tamil Nadu hereby adds “All Institutions engaged in
procurement, storage and distribution of milk and
manufacture of milk products and registered under the
Tamil Nadu Co-operative Societies Act, 1983” to the First
Schedule to the said Act.
Declaration of All Institutions engaged in procurement,
storage and distribution of Milk and Manufacture
of Milk Products as Public Utility Service under
the Act.
[G.O. (Rt.) No. 366, Labour and Employment (D2),
5th July 2011.]
No. II(2)/LE/361/2011.—Whereas the Governor of Tamil Nadu
is satisfied that public interest requires that “All Institutions
engaged in procurement, storage and distribution of milk and
manufacture of milk products and registered under the
Tamil Nadu Co-operative Societies Act, 1983” should be declared
to be a public utility services for the purpose of the Industrial
Disputes Act, 1947 (Central Act XIV of 1947);
Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred by
sub-clause (vi) of clause (n) of Section 2 of the said Act,
the 
Governor 
of 
Tamil 
Nadu 
hereby 
declares
“All Institutions engaged in procurement, storage and
distribution of milk and manufacture of milk products and
registered under the Tamil Nadu Co-operative Societies
Act, 1983” to be public utility servies for the purposes
of the said Act for a period of six months on and from
the date of publication of this Notification in the
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette.
VISHWANATHA  SHEGAONKAR,
Principal Secretary to Government.

REVENUE DEPARTMENT
Recommendations of the 13th Finance Commission under
Grants-in-aid-District Innovation Fund - Guidelines.
[G.O. Ms. No. 233, Revenue [D.M. III(2)],
26th July 2011.]
No.II(2)/REV/367/2011.
Based on the recommendations of the Thirteenth Finance
Commission, the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby nominates the
District Collector as the Authority for implementation of District
Innovation Fund.
Annexure
GUIDELINES PROPOSED FOR IMPLEMENTING,
THE DISTRICT INNOVATION FUND SCHEME
The following guidelines are proposed for implementing
the Scheme:—
1. The fund will be available only for an activity/scheme for
which there is no regular line of fund allocation from the
Government.
2. The activity/scheme should not involve creation of posts;
nor should there be any recurring expenditure for that activity/
scheme.

[Part II—Sec. 2
3. An application for drawing the fund can be made by any
State Government department or a local body to the District
Collector.
4. The application need not be in any specific format;
however, it should give an outline of the activity/scheme and its
utility and should include a rough cost estimate.
5. The application should be accompanied by the 10%
contribution raised from public or an Non Government
Organizations; this 10% should not have been drawn from
Government or local body funds.
6. The District Collector, if satisfied, will get a proper estimate
prepared ensure that 10% of the cost of the activity/scheme has
been made, and recommend it to the Government through the
Principal Secretary / Commissioner of Revenue Administration.
The Collector should also certify that there is no regular allotment
of funds available from the Government for the proposed line of
activity and that the activity cannot be performed under any of
the existing schemes.
7. The Government in Revenue Department will place such
proposals before the High Level Monitoring Committee headed
by the Chief Secretary incharge of the Finance Commission
grants for its approval. Principal Secretary (Revenue) and
Principal Secretary/ Commissioner of Revenue Administration
will be a special invitee for these meetings.
8. After the High Level Monitoring Committee approves the
activity/scheme, based on a communication from the Principal
Secretary (Revenue) the 90% contribution from the District
Innovation Fund will be released by the Principal Secretary /
Commissioner of Revenue Administration to the District Collector
who will in turn release it to the Department/Local body.
9. For carrying out the activity/scheme, the tender procedure
enumerated in the Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders Act,
1998 and Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders Rules, 2000
should be strictly followed.
10. On completion of the activity/scheme, a Utilisation
Certificate should be sent by the District Collector to the Principal
Secretary / Commissioner of Revenue Administration.
R. RAJAGOPAL,
Principal Secretary to Government.
