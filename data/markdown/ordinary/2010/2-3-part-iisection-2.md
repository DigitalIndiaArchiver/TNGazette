On 24th January 2007 a blast occurred in a Cracker
Factory at Sivakasi Taluk. To enquire into such incidents,
the State Government, direct that a Special Safety Committee
be constituted for providing safety measures to be followed
and to prevent blast accidents in the Fire Crackers Factories
in Virudhunagar District. The Government also appoint under
Section 90(1) of the Factories Act, 1948, a Special Safety
Committee comprising of the following members to enquire
into the causes of the accidents and to prevent the blast
accidents in the Fire Crackers Factories:—
1. Additional Chief Inspector of
.. Chairman
Factories, Office of the Chief
Inspector of Factories,
Chepauk, Chennai-5.
2. Thiru Bhojan, IOFS
.. Member
General Manager (Works),
Tamil Nadu Industrial Explosives
Ltd., Del Post, Vellore.
3. Dr. P. Nagaraj,
.. Member
Lecturer and Department Head,
Mechanical Engineering Division,
MEPCO Schelink Engineering
College, Sivakasi.
2.
The above committee shall be treated as a first class
committee for the purpose of Dearness Allowance and
Travelling Allowance for the non-official members. The
Additional Chief Inspector of Factories is entitled to sanction
the Dearness Allowance and Travelling Allowances to the
above non-official members and the expenditure will be
debited under the following head of account:—
“2230-Labour and Employment-01-Labour-102-Working
conditions and Safety-I-Non Plan-AB. Inspector of Factories-
04-Travel Expenses 01-Tour Travelling Allowances”.
(DPC. 2230-01-102-AB-0410)
3.
The terms and conditions of the Special Safety
Committee are as follows:—
(i)
All the Fire Crackers Factories in Virudhunagar
District should be inspected and the harmful aspects,
Chemical incompatibility and the disastrous effects caused
from that should be observed.
(ii) For this purpose, Risk analysis should be
undertaken.
(iii) According to schedule XXIV under Rule, 95 of the
Tamil Nadu Factories Rules, 1950, it should be analysed
whether all safety measurers are adhered to and if needed,
additional safety guidelines should be recommended.
(iv) According to Explosives Act, it should be analysed
whether the rules are followed in the fire Cracker Factories.
(v)
The workers engaged in the production of
crackers should be provided with sufficient awareness
regarding the accidents. Awareness relating to Safety and
Protection should be offered to them.
(vi) In order to avoid blast accidents, certain alternative
arrangements should be recommended.
4.
The above committee should submit their full-fledged
report to the Government within two months, from the date
of issue of this order.
5.
The Chief Inspector of Factories shall send
necessary proposals to Finance Department for inclusion
of this expenditure in RE/FMA for 2009-10 at the appropriate
stage.
6.
This order issues with the concurrence of the
Finance Department vide its U.O. No. 35635/L&E/09, dated
24th June 2009.
