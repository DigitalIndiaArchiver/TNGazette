ANIMAL HUSBANDRY, DAIRYING AND FISHERIES DEPARTMENT
AMENDMENTS TO THE SPECIAL RULES FOR THE TAMIL NADU ANIMAL HUSBANDRY SUBORDINATE
SERVICE.
[G.O.(Ms).No.23, Animal Husbandry, Dairying & Fisheries (AH7) Department, 27th February 2009.]
No. SRO B-15(a)/2009.
In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India, the Governor of Tamil Nadu
hereby makes the following amendments to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Animal Husbandry Subordinate Service
(Section 2 in Volume-III of Tamil Nadu Services Manual 1970).
The amendment hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on the 28th May of 2008.
AMENDMENT
In the said Special Rules,—
(1)   in rule 1, after Class I, the following class  shall be inserted, namely :—
“Class IA
       Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeon”;
(2) in rule 2, after Class I, in column (1) and the entry relating thereto  the following class and the entry shall
respectively be inserted, namely :—
“Class IA
Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeon
       —Direct Recruitment” ;
(3)  in rule 4, after Class I, in column (1) and the entry relating thereto the following class and the entry shall
respectively be inserted, namely :—
“ Class IA
Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeon – Commissioner / Director of Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Services.”;

(4) in rule 5, after the words “in Madras Veterinary College”, the words, “and Junior  Veterinary Assistant Surgeon
in Animal Husbandry Department.”; shall be added.
(5) in Rule 6, in the Table, after category “Demonstrator” in Column(1) and the corresponding  entries in
Column (2), the following shall respectively be inserted, namely”
“ Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeon
-
30 years of age on the first of July of the year in
which the vacancies are notified.”;
(6) in the rule 7, in the Annexure, in the Table, after Class I in Column (1) and the  corresponding  entries in Column
(2), the following class and the entries shall respectively be inserted, namely :—
“Class IA
Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeon
-
Must have possess a B.V.Sc Degree
and
Must be a Registered Veterinary Practitioner under the Indian
Veterinary Council Act, 1984 (Central Act 52 of 1984)”.
(7) (a) in rule 9, the existing provision shall be numbered as sub-rule (1) ;
(b) after sub-rule (1) as so numbered, following sub-rule shall be added,  namely :—
           (2) The Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeon appointed by direct recruitment shall, pass the following Department
test and Training   within the period of his probation:—
(a) Animal Husbandry Department Test :—
(b)  He shall also undergo the Foundational ‘B’ Course Training for a period of not less than three weeks
at the Anna Institute of Management, Chennai and pass the examination conducted at the end of the
Training within the period of his probation.
(c)  If any person does not pass the test and examination prescribed in (a) and (b) above within the period
of his probation, he shall not be deemed to have completed his probation satisfactorily and he shall
not be eligible to draw increment in the time scale of pay applicable to him, until he passes the said
examination.
(8) in rule 10, following shall be added namely :—
1.  “Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeons shall not be entitled   to  engage  in  private  professional practice as
of right  but may ordinarily do so, subject  to  such conditions as shall be laid down by  the  Government from
time  to time. The Government may at  any time prohibit  any  member of  the service from  engaging in  Private
Professional Practice, if  in  their  opinion, such Private Professional  Practice  interferes  with the efficient
discharge of his official duties.
    2.  “They are entitled to do private practice and may charge fees subject to the conditions that such practice does
not interfere with their legitimate duties and subject to other restrictions as may be imposed from time to time.
(9)  in rule 11, following expressions shall be added, namely :—
    “For Junior Veterinary Assistant Surgeons, all postings and transfers within the Region shall be done by the Joint
Director of Animal Husbandry concerned, subject to guidelines prescribed. The Commissioner/ Director of
Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Services shall have full powers in this regard”.
