Reduction of stamp duty chargeable in respect of
instruments of sale, lease or lease-cum-sale executed
in favour of certain sectors and developed estates
under Micro, Small and Medium Industries Policy, 2008
at the time of allotment under Indian Stamp Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 1, Commercial Taxes and Registration (J1),
6th January 2009.]
No. II(2)/CTR/52/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by clause (a) of sub-section (1) of section 9 of
the Indian Stamp Act, 1899 (Central Act II of 1899), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby reduces the duty chargeable
under the said Act to fifty per cent in respect of the instruments
of sale, lease or lease-cum-sale executed in favour of Micro
and Small Enterprises sectors in the Tamil Nadu Small
Industries Development Corporation or Government developed
estates under Micro, Small and Medium Industries Policy,
2008 at the time of original allotment, based on the transfer
value fixed by the said Corporation or Government in respect
of the said estates.
Reduction of  registration fee payable in respect of the
instruments of sale, lease or lease-com-sale executed
in favour of certain sectors and developed estates
under Micro, Small and Medium Industries Policy, 2008
at the time of allotments under the Registration Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 1, Commercial Taxes and Registration (J1),
6th January 2009.]
No. II(2)/CTR/53/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by section 78-A of the Registration Act, 1908
(Central Act XVI of 1908), as the Governor of Tamil Nadu
is of the opinion that it is necessary so to do in the public
interest, hereby reduces the fee payable under the said Act
to fifty per cent in respect of the instruments of sale, lease
or lease-cum-sale executed in favour of Micro and Small
Enterprises sectors in the Tamil Nadu Small Industries
Development corporation or Government developed estates
under Micro, Small and Medium Industries Policy, 2008, at
the time of original allotments based on the transfer value
fixed by the said Corporation or Government in respect of
the said estates.
Reduction of stamp duty chargeable in respect of the
instruments of sale,  lease or lease-cum-sale executed
in favour of entrepreneurs in privately developed
estates approved under the Micro, Small and Medium
Industries Policy, 2008 at the time of allotment under
Indian Stamp Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 1, Commercial Taxes and Registration (J1),
6th January 2009.]
No. II(2)/CTR/54/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by clause (a) of sub-section (1) of section 9 of
the Indian Stamp Act, 1899 (Central Act II of 1899), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby reduces the duty chargeable
under the said Act to fifty per cent in respect of the instruments
of sale, lease or lease-cum-sale executed in favour of
entrepreneurs in privately developed estates approved under
the Micro, Small and Medium Industries Policy, 2008 at the
time of original allotment, based on the guideline value of
the property.

in favour of entrepreneurs in privately developed
estates approved under the Micro, Small and Medium

In the Government  order first read above orders were
issued to take over the management of Chikkiah Naicker
College, Erode under section 30(1) of the Tamil Nadu Private
Colleges (Regulation) Act, 1976 for a period of two years
with effect from 19th December 2002 and appointed the
Regional Joint Director of Collegiate Education, Coimbatore
Region, as caretaker to run the management of the said
college.
2. In the Government order second read above, the
Government have ordered for extension to take over the
management of the Chikkiah Naikker College, Erode, for a
period of one year with effect from 19th December 2004 and
also to extend the term of the Special Officer appointed as
per the G.O. first read above to run the management of the
said College.
3. In the G.Os. 3rd to 5th read above, the Government
sanctioned the further extension of the take over and
continuance of term of the caretake year by year upto 18th
December 2008. The present sanction expires on 18th
December 2008.
4. In the letter sixth read above, the Director of Collegiate
Education has stated that the term of the period of the
Special Officer expired on 18th December 2008. There is
no proper Educational Agency to run the management of
Chikkiah Naicker College. the W.P.No. 11019/2002 filed by
Thiru R. Aswath, Secretary, Chikkiah Naicker Educational
Board, Erode and W.P.No. 4399/2001 filed by Thiru,
T.A. Shanmugasundaram, Secretary, Mahajana Educational
Board, Erode to hand over the management of the said
college was dismissed by the High Court. Against these
orders the petitioners filed Writ Appeals in W.A. Nos.
1670/2005 and 1671/2005 and they are still pending before
the High Court. The Administration of this College cannot
be transferred to any other agency at present. There is no
Possibility to constitute an Educational Board. The Director
of Collegiate Education has, therefore, requested to extend
the period of take over of the management and term of the
Special Officer for a further period of one year with effect
from 19th December 2008 as per proviso to section 30 (3)
of the Tamil Nadu Private Colleges (Regulation) Act, 1976.
5. The Government, after careful consideration, have
decided that it is expedient in the interests of students’
Education to extend the take over the Management of Chikkiah
Naicker College, Erode. Now, therefore, in exercise of the
powers conferred under the proviso to sub-section (3) of
section 30 of the Tamil Nadu Private Colleges (Regulation)
Act, 1976 (President’s Act 19 of 1976), the Governor of Tamil
Nadu hereby extends the take over the management of the
Chikkiah Naicker College, Erode for a further period of one
year with effect from 19th December 2008 to 18th December
2009 and appoints the Regional Joint Director of Collegiate
Education, Coimbatore as Care Taker to run the management
of the said College.
(By Order of the Governor)
M. KUTRALINGAM,
Principal Secretary to Government (incharge).
