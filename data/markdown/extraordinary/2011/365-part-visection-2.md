Order:
An order to provide for specifications, reservation choice and allotment of symbols on elections to Local Bodies in the State
of Tami Nadu and for matters connected therewith issued by the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission in the Statutory Order
first read above.
Whereas, the commission in the Statutory Order second read above notified the list of Recognised Political Parties and the
symbols reserved for them in TABLE-I, the list of Registered – Unrecognised Political Parties who are entitled to have preference
over independents in the allotment of free symbols in TABLE I -A and the list(s) of free symbols in tables 2 to 5 in accordance
with the rules 33, 34 and 35 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules,1995 and rules 31, 32 and 33 of the Tamil Nadu Town
Panchayats, Third Grade Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections) Rules, 2006.
Whereas, the list of Recognised Political Parties and their symbols were notified based on the notification issued by the
Election Commission of India under the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968 and as per the provisions made
in the said election Rules pertaining to Local Bodies Election in Tamil Nadu.
Whereas, the Election Commission of India vide its notification No.56/99/Judl.III, dated 8th June 1999 substituted a provision
under para 10 in the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968, which provides a concession to candidates
set up by an unrecognised State party which is recognised State Party in some other State (s) or Union Territories at an Election.
Whereas, the said provision made under para 10 of the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968 provides
as follows:—

“10. CONCESSION TO CANDIDATES SET UP A STATE PARTY AT ELECTIONS IN OTHER STATES OR UNION TERRITORIES”.
If a political which is recognized as a State Party in some State or States sets up a candidate at an election in a
constituency in any other State or Union Territory in which it is not a recognised State Party, then such candidate may, to
the exclusion of all other candidates in the constituency be allotted the symbol reserved for that party in the State or States
in which it is a recognised State Party, notwithstanding that such symbol is not specified in the list of free symbols for such
other State or Union Territory, on the fulfilment of each of the following conditions, namely:—
(a) that an application is made to the commission by the said party for exclusive allotment of that symbol to the
candidate set by it, not later than the third day after the publication in the Official Gazette of the Notification calling the election.
(b) that the said candidate has made a declaration in his nomination paper that he has been set up by that party
at the election and that the party has also fulfilled the requirements of clauses (b),(c), (d) and (e) of paragraph 13 read with
paragraph 13A in respect of such candidate; and
(c) that in the opinion of the Commission there is no reasonable ground for refusing the application for such
allotment.
Provided that nothing contained in the paragraph shall apply to a candidate set up by a State Party at an election
in any constituency in a State which that party is not a State Party and where the same symbol is already reserved for some
other State Party in that State.
Whereas, No such provisions were provided in the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment)
Order, 2011, however the Local Bodies elections rules provide for adopting the parties which are Recognised as a National
Party or as a State Party in the State of Tamil Nadu as per the Election Commission of India’s Symbol Order, 1968 as
recognised parties for the purpose of Local Body Elections in Tamil Nadu.
Whereas, Now a political party viz., All India Forward Bloc which is Recognised as a State Party in this State of West
Bengal has represented to this Commission to provide similar concession as under para 10 of the Symbol Order of the
Election Commission of India in the Symbol Orders issued by the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission and allot their party
symbol “LION” to the candidates set up by the said party in the ensuing Local Body Elections.
Whereas, the Commission conducted hearings with the representative of the said Political Party on 24th September 2011 and
perused all the relevant records and documents produced by the party.
Whereas, it is came to know that the State Election Commission, Kerala in its Order No.395/2010/Sec, dated 29th June 2010
reserved the symbol “LION” to All India Forward Bloc Party in the Local Body Elections, in the State of Kerala for Local Body
Elections as provided in para 10 of Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968 of the Election Commission of India,
even though the said Party was not a recognised party in the said State.
Whereas, considering the above, it has become expedient for the Commission to extend such concession to the Candidates
set up at elections by a political party which is a State Party in Other States to avail their Party’s Symbols in the Local Body
Elections also by duly incorporating the similar provisions in the Symbol Order with required changes to suit the requirement for
Local Bodies Elections in the State.
Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred under Articles 243 K and 243 ZA of the Constitution of India read
with relevant sections of the Acts and rules 33, 34 and 35 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules,1995, rules 31, 32 and
33 of the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Third Grade Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections) Rules, 2006 and
as per the clause 11 of the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 2011 the
following amendments are ordered in the Symbol Order issued by the Commission vide S.O.No. 25/2011/TNSEC/EG2,
dated 9th September 2011:-
I. After para 7(1) A, the following may be inserted.
“7 (1) B. CONCESSION TO CANDIDATES SET UP BY A STATE PARTY AT ELECTIONS IN OTHER STATES OR UNION TERRITORIES”
If a political which is recognized as a State Party in some other State or States or Union Territories sets up a candidate at
an election in a ward in any Local Body / Panchayat or for the office of the Chairman/Mayor in any Local Body which were held
on party basis, then such candidate may, to the exclusions of all other candidates in the said election be allotted the symbol
reserved for that party in the State or States in which it is a recognised State Party, notwithstanding that such symbol is not
specified in the list of free for such election, on the fulfillment of each of the following conditions, namely:—
(a) that an application is made to the commission by the said party for exclusive allotment of that symbol to the candidate
set up by it, not later than the third day after the publication in the Official Gazette of the Notification calling the election or within
three days from the date of issue of this order;
