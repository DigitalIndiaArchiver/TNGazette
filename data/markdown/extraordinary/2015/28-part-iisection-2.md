In the letter first read above the District Collector, Cuddalore has stated that the registering body, Neyveli Lignite Corporation
Limited has requested administrative sanction for acquiring 588.08.0 hect. of lands in Arasakuzhi and 7 other villages of
Virudhachalam Taluk, Cuddalore District for formation of fresh drain for Mine вҖ“II in favour of Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited.
2.
The District Collector, Cuddalore has stated that an extent of 170.45.0 hectares of Government Poramboke lands in
Arasakuzhi, Kammapuram, Keenanur, U.Agaraham, U.Kolapakkam, U.Mangalam and Uthangal villages except Gopalapuram
village comes under alienation.  An extent of 8.02.0 hectares in Kammapuram and 27.75.0 hectares in U.Kolapakkam village,
a total extent of 35.77.0 hectares involved under acquisition are wet lands.
3.
The Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited has also stated that 116 families in U. Mangalam and 60 families in Uthangal
Village, a total of 176 families likely to be displaced due to acquisition lands for the formation of Mine II drainage. The Neyveli
Lignite Corporation Limited has also confirmed that Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited will bear the necessary cost and charges
and Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited will provide necessary rehabilitation measures to the eligible displaced person as per
Government approved Resettlement and Rehabiliation Policy.

The District Collector has recommended the administrative sanction proposal through Principal Commissioner and

transfer of lands measuring over an extent of 588.08.0 hectares (35.77.0  hectares of Patta wet, 368.99.5 hectares of patta dry,

Cuddalore District  for  the formation of fresh drain for Mine-II in favour of Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited under the Tamil
Nadu Acquisition of Land for Industrial Purposes Act, 1997 (Tamil Nadu Act 10 of 1999).  The Government also direct that the
District Collector may send separate proposal to Government in Revenue Department in respect of alienation of 170.45.0
hectares of promboke land and 12.86.5 hectares of Tharisu land totally 183.31.5 hectares of land, since orders have to be issued
under RSO 24.  The details of all the lands involved are indicated in the Annexure to this order.

In the letter first read above the District Collector, Cuddalore has stated that the registering body, Neyveli Lignite Corporation
Limited has requested administrative sanction for Acquisition of  242.97.00 hectares of Patta Wet, Patta Dry, Natham/House site,
Tharisu and Government Poromboke lands in Thenkuthu and Vadakuthu   Villages in Kurinjipadi Taluk, Cuddalore District for
expansion  of Mine вҖ“IA of Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited.
2. The Director(Mines), Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited, Neyveli has stated  that a new Thermal Plant of 1000 MW
is proposed for replacement and its planning is in an advanced stage.  In order to continue generation of power from new
1000 MW power plant, lignite has to be supplied from Mine-1A for which the lignite reserves have to be increased.  The land on

4. The Government after careful consideration have proposed to accord administrative sanction  for  acquisition of  242.97.0
hectares (18.71.5 hectares of Patta wet, 180.74.67 hectares of patta dry, 1.85.83 hectares of Natham / house site, 33.30.0 hectares
of poramboke lands and 8.35.0 hectares Tharisu lands) in Thenkuthu and Vadakuthu villages of Kurinjipadi Taluk, Cuddalore District
for expansion of Mine.1A in favour of Neyveli Lignite Corporation Limited subject to the following conditions:
(1) Obtain and send the No Objection Certificate from  the Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Department if the
temple lands comes under the control of Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Department.
(2) Consent of the Public Works Department in respect of water course poramboke lands has to be obtained.
(3) Consent of respective departments in case of other poramboke lands has to be obtained at the time of initiating Land
Acquisition / Alienation process.
(4) To take into consideration the Bill to amend section 105 of the New Land Acquisition Act passed by the Government
of India.
(5) To examine the proposal in respect of alienation/ transfer of lands and to issue separate orders.
5. The Government also direct the District Collector to send the proposal in respect of alienation / transfer of lands to
Government in Revenue Department through Commissioner of Land Administration to issue separate orders.  The details of all
the lands involved are indicated in the Annexure to this order.
