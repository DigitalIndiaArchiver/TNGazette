General Administration Department

Amendment to the Schedule-II of Chennai Port Trust Employees’ (Recruitment, Seniority and Promotion)
Regulations, 2008
Chennai, dated the 25th  May, 2016.
No. SRO C-15/2016.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 23 of the Major Port Trusts, Act, 1963, the Port Trust Board vide its Resolution
No.135, dated 15.03.2008 had seek the approval of the Central Government to change the nomenclature of “Rivetter” as
‘Fabricator’ without any change in the Scale of Pay & Allowances and Duties and Responsibilities of the said category. The
Ministry of Shipping in its letter No. LB-14-A022/2/2015-L, dated 29.04.2016 has conveyed the approval of the competent authority
for changing the nomenclature of ‘Rivetter’ as “Fabricator” in the Schedule of Mechanical and Electrical Engineering Department
of Chennai Port Trust.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Regulation 5 of Chennai Port Trust Employees’ (Recruitment, Seniority and
Promotion) Regulations, 2008, the Port Trust Board vide its Resolution No.31, dated 27.05.2016 has approved the amendment
made to the existing Recruitment Rules for the post of Junior Signalman relating to Qualification and Experience in the Marine
Department.
 In exercise of the powers conferred by Sub-Section (1)(a) of 132 of the Major Port Trusts Act, 1963 (38 of 1963), the
amendments made to the existing Recruitment Rules for the posts of ‘Rivetter’ in the Mechanical & Electrical Engineering Dept
and ‘Junior Signalman’ in the Marine Dept is cause for publication.
AMENDMENT
(i) In the Schedule-II under the said Regulations, the words “Rivetter” appearing in the Recruitment Rules at
Sl. No. 55, 56, 57, 58 & 59 in the Mechanical & Electrical Engineering Department shall be substituted as “Fabricator”.
(ii) In the Schedule-II under the said Regulations, the Recruitment Rules for the post of Junior Signalman at
Sl. No. 9 in the Marine Department, the entries under Column No. 8 shall be substituted by the following:-
“1.
Passed HSC / +2 with Maths, Physics and Chemistry.
2.
Should possess Global Maritime Distress and Safety System - General Operators Certificate
(GMDSS-GOC) issued by Govt. of India vide Notification No. GSR 133, Dated 06.07.1998.
3.
Experience as VTMS operator in Ports or offshore Marine Stations will be preferred.
NOTE:
The qualification regarding experience is relaxable at the discretion of the Chairman in the case of
candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes if at any stage of Selection, the Chairman is of the
opinion that sufficient number of candidates from these communities possessing the requisite experience are not
likely to be available to fill up the vacancies reserved for them.”
Foot Note: The Recruitment Rules in respect of Class II, III and IV posts of all the departments of the Trust included in the
Schedule-II of the Chennai Port Trust Employees’ (Recruitment, Seniority and Promotion) Regulations 2008 was
published in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette vide SRO C-2/2016, dated 22nd May 2015.
C. HARICHANDRAN,
Secretary,
Chennai Port Trust.

JUDICIAL NOTIFICATIONS
Amendments to Insolvency Rules, 1958.
(R.O.C. No. 546/2006/F1)
No. SRO C-16/2016.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 112 of the Presidency Towns Insolvency Act, 1909 (Central Act 3 of 1909),
and of all other powers hereunto enabling and with the previous sanction of the Government of Tamil Nadu, the High Court of
Judicature at Madras hereby makes the following amendments to Insolvency Rules, 1958:-
AMENDMENTS
In the said Rules-
(1) In Order-III, in rule 16, for the expression “a sum of Rs. 150,” the expression “a sum of Rs. 1500/- (Rupees one
thousand and five hundred)” shall be substituted;
(2) In Order-VI, for rule 8, the following rule shall be substituted, namely:-
“8. Procedure for adjudication of the Creditor’s petition/Debtor’s petition-In the event of adjudication on a Creditor’s
petition or Debtor’s petition, a sum of Rs. 2,000/- or any higher amount, which is required to meet out any expenditure for  taking
any proceedings necessary for the administration of the estate, shall be deposited by the petitioning creditor or by the insolvent
in case of debtor’s petition. Any assets realized by such proceedings, shall be applied, in the first place, towards the repayment
of the said advance to the petitioning creditor with such interest thereon, as may be agreed upon, or at such other rate as the
court may allow”.
High Court, Madras,
T. RAVINDRAN,
29th August 2016.
