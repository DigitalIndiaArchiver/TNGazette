Tamil NADU ORDINANCE No. 3 OF 2020.
An Ordinance to provide for the establishment of a Foundation for making 
long-term arrangements for the conversion of ‘Veda Nilayam’, the 
residence of former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, Puratchi Thalaivi 
Dr. J. Jayalalithaa (Late) at Poes Garden, Chennai into a Memorial, 
for upkeep and management thereof, and for the matters connected 
therewith or incidental thereto.
Ex-IV-2—268-204—1
[89]
 WHEREAS, the Hon’ble Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu announced on 
17.08.2017 that ‘Veda Nilayam’, the residence of former Chief Minister of Tamil 
Nadu, Puratchi Thalaivi Dr. J. Jayalalithaa (Late), situated at Poes Garden, 
Chennai, be converted as a Memorial and opened to the public, in remembrance 
of her achievements and sacrifices for the people of Tamil Nadu;
 AND WHEREAS, action to acquire the aforesaid land and building has 
been initiated by the State Government under the Right to Fair Compensation 
and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 
2013 (Central Act 30 of 2013). After completing the Social Impact Assessment, 
preliminary notification under Section 11(1) of that Act was published on 28.06.2019 
and a declaration that the said land and building are needed for conversion as a 
Government Memorial has also been published on 06.05.2020 under Section 19 
(1) of the said Act;
 AND WHEREAS, it will take some more time for the State Government to 
complete the acquisition proceedings under the said Central Act 30 of 2013 and 
to take possession of ‘Veda Nilayam’; 
AND WHEREAS, as on date, there is no declared legal heir to 
Dr.J.Jayalalithaa, former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, and as the aforesaid land 
and building including the movable items therein such as furniture, books, jewels, 
etc., are in a state of disuse for more than three years, it is imminently necessary 
to prevent them from being spoilt and ruined due to lack of proper maintenance;

 AND WHEREAS, the State Government have decided to temporarily

Government for its upkeep and management, till the land acquisition proceedings

 AND WHEREAS, the Legislative Assembly of the State is not in session and 
the Governor of Tamil Nadu is satisfied that circumstances exist which render it 
necessary for him to take immediate action for the purposes hereinafter appearing; 
AND WHEREAS, instructions of the President have been obtained in

1. 	
(1) This Ordinance may be called the Tamil Nadu Puratchi Thalaivi 
Dr. J. Jayalalithaa Memorial Foundation Ordinance, 2020.
	
(2) It shall come into force at once.
Definitions.
 2. In this Ordinance, unless the context otherwise requires,—
	
(a) “Claims Commissioner” means the Claims Commissioner appointed 
under Section 10;
	
(b) “Foundation” means the Puratchi Thalaivi Dr. J. Jayalalithaa 
Memorial Foundation established under section 4; 
	
(c) “Government” means the State Government; 
	
(d) “prescribed” means prescribed by rules made under this Ordinance;
	
(e) “property” means ‘Veda Nilayam’, the residence of former Chief 
Minister of Tamil Nadu Puratchi Thalaivi Dr.J.Jayalalithaa (Late), situated at 
Poes Garden, Chennai, more particularly specified in Part-A of the Schedule 
and includes the movable properties like books, furniture, jewels and other items 
specified in Part-B of the Schedule;
	
(f) “Schedule” means the Schedule to this Ordinance.

Part-A of the Schedule under the Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency

 4. 	 (1) The Government may, by notification, establish, for the purpose of

(2) The Foundation shall be a body corporate by the name aforesaid, 
having perpetual succession and a common seal with power to acquire, hold and

(3) The Foundation shall consist of the following members, namely:—

(4) The tenure of the nominated members shall be for a period of three 
years and they shall be eligible for re-nomination. 
	
(5) (a) The Foundation shall,—
	
	
(i) take necessary steps to maintain the immovable property 
specified in Part-A of the Schedule in good condition, and shall also undertake 
repairs, wherever necessary;
	
	
(ii) take necessary steps for the maintenance, upkeep and safety 
of all the movable properties specified in Part-B of the Schedule;
	
	
(iii) take such precautionary measures as it deems fit to protect the 
property from theft, fire or other damages; 
	
	
(iv) prepare an action plan for conversion of the property into a 
Memorial and send it to the Government for approval;
	
(v) take necessary action to convert the property into a Memorial, on 
transfer of the property to the Foundation under sub-section (3) of Section 3 and 
on approval of the action plan.
	
(b) The Foundation shall be entitled to receive donations and grants 
from any person including the Government, and shall also be entitled to raise 
funds in any manner as it deems fit.

 Explanatory Statement.
On 17.08.2017, the Hon’ble Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu announced that, ‘Veda Nilayam’, the residence of the former 
Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, Puratchi Thalaivi Dr.J.Jayalalithaa (Late) will be converted as a Memorial and will be opened 
to the public, in remembrance of her achievements and sacrifices for the people of Tamil Nadu. To give effect to the 
said announcement, administrative sanction has been given in G.O. (Ms.) No.180, Tamil Development and Information 
Department, dated 05.10.2017 for acquisition of ‘Veda Nilayam’ situated in Poes Garden, Mylapore Taluk, Chennai under 
the Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013 (Central 
Act 30 of 2013).  Action has already been initiated to acquire the aforesaid land and building under the said Act and a 
preliminary notification under section 11(1) of that Act was published on 28.06.2019 and an order over ruling  the objections 
made thereon was issued under section 15 (3) of that Act  on 22.01.2020 .  A declaration that the said land and building 
are needed for conversion as a Government Memorial has also been published on 06.05.2020 under section 19(1) of the 
said Act. 
2. The completion of acquisition proceedings under the said Act will take some more time. As on date, there is no 
declared legal heir to Dr.J.Jayalalithaa, former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. The land and building of the ‘Veda Nilayam’ 
including the movable items therein such as furniture, books, jewels, etc., are in a state of disuse for more than three years 
and hence it is imminently necessary to prevent them from being spoilt and ruined due to lack of proper maintenance. The 
Government have, therefore, decided to temporarily transfer the ‘Veda Nilayam’, including the movable items therein to 
the Government for its upkeep and maintenance, till the land acquisition proceedings are duly completed and possession 
is taken by the Government under the said Central Act 30 of 2013 and to establish a Foundation to make long term 
arrangements for its conversion into a Memorial. 
3.  The Ordinance seeks to give effect to the above decision.
