[G.O. Ms. No. 199, Environment And Forests (FR.5), 13th August 2012.]
No. II(2)/EF/469(c-1)/2012.
WHEREAS  in the Environment and Forests Department Notification No. II(2)/EF/333/2007 published on page 240 of
Part II—Section 2 of the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette dated the 27th June 2007, an extent of 95,859 hectares was
declared as a Tiger Reserve under sub-section (1) of Section 38 V of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 (Central Act 53
of 1972);
AND WHEREAS the Government in the notification issued in  G.O.Ms.No.145, Environment and Forests Department
dated 28-12-2007 declared the area of 958 Sq.km, as critical tiger habitat with the approval of National Tiger Conservation
Authority contained in letter  No. 1501/11/2007 PT dated 03-12-2007  and the said Notification was published as
No. II(2)/EF/822(C)/2007 on page 1 of Part II – Section 2 of the Tamil Nadu Government Gazettee extraordinary
dated the 31st December 2007.
AND WHEREAS  the National Tiger Conservation Authority in letter No.1-8/2007-PT dated 18.01.2011 conveyed the
approval of the competent authority for declaring 521.28 Sq.km. as a buffer area in accordance with the provisions
contained in Section 38 V of  the Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972, as amended by the Wildlife (Protection)  Amendment Act,
2006 (Central Act 39 of 2006);
AND WHEREAS the core or critical tiger habitat areas of National Parks and sanctuaries, have been established, on
the basis of scientific and objective criteria, that such areas are required to be kept as inviolate for the purposes of tiger
conservation, without affecting the rights of the Scheduled Tribes or such other forest dwellers, and notified as such by
the State Government in consultation with an Expert Committee constituted for the purpose;
AND WHEREAS buffer or peripheral area consisting of the area peripheral to critical tiger habitat or core area, identified
and established in accordance with the provisions contained in the said Central Act 53 of 1972, where a lesser degree
of habitat protection is required to ensure the integrity of the critical tiger habitat with adequate dispersal for tiger species,
and which aim at promoting co-existence between wildlife and human activity with due recognition of the livelihood,
developmental, social and cultural rights of the local people, wherein the limits of such areas are determined on the basis
of scientific and objective criteria in consultation with the concerned Gram Sabha and an Expert Committee constituted
for the purpose;
