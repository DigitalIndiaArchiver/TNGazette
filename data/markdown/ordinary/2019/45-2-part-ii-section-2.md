BACKWARD CLASSES, MOST BACKWARD CLASSES  
AND MINORITIES WELFARE DEPARTMENT
Revision Petition Filed by Thiru N. Dastagir, Vellore 
as Hazarath Murthuzawali Masthan Darga, Vellore, 
Assumption of Direct Management of Waqf  by the 
Tamil Nadu Waqf Board under the Waqf Act.
[G.O.(D). No.73, Backward Classes, Most Backward Classes 
and Minorities Welfare (S2), 15th October 2019, Purattasi 28, 
Vikari,Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2050.]
No.II(2)/BCMBCMW/851/2019.—In 
order 
to 
dispose 
of the revision petition ﬁ led by Thiru N.Dastagir under 
sub-section (2) of Section 65 of the Waqf Act 1995, (Central 
Act 43 of 1995), a personal hearing has been proposed on 
09-10-2019. The petitioner i.e., Janab N.Dastagir did not turn 
up for the personal hearing. In order to provide suﬃ  cient 
opportunity to the petitioner to put forth his arguments before 
the appellate authority, he has been informed to appear for 
hearing on 10-10-2019. The personal hearing was conducted 
on 10-10-2019 by the Secretary to Government, Backward 
Classes, Most Backward Classes and Minorities Welfare 
Department. The petitioner did not turn up for hearing 
on 10-10-2019 also inspite of having taken notice of the 
scheduled hearing. The Chief Executive Oﬃ  cer, Tamil Nadu 
Waqf Board along with their counsel were present and put 
forth the case.
On careful examination of the contentions raised in the 
revision petition and the submissions made by the Counsel 
of the Chief Executive Oﬃ  cer, Tamil Nadu Waqf Board, 
it is observed that the direct management of the above 
Waqf ordered vide Board's resolution was set aside by the 
Hon'ble Waqf Tribunal, Vellore. Against the said order, the
Tamil Nadu Waqf Board had preferred Civil Revision Petition 
(PD) No.3098 of 2014 wherein Hon'ble High Court in its 
order dated 04-03-2019 had dismissed the said C.R.P. without 
prejudice to the rights of the petitioner namely Tamil Nadu 
Waqf Board to remove Thiru Maqbul Basha in accordance 
with law. Further, the relief sought for in the present revision 
petition was already granted by the Hon'ble Waqf Tribunal, 
Vellore and also conﬁ rmed by the Hon'ble High Court of 
Madras. 
The term of the Muthawalli expired on 5-6-2006. However, 
due to take over of management by Tamil Nadu Waqf 
Board and also not eﬀ ecting the take over in the waqf, 
the Muthawalli whose term expired continued to hold the 
post for nearly 13 years. Tamil Nadu Waqf Board instead 
should have formed a Management Committee from among 
the hereditary members and ensure proper functioning of the 
Waqf. Tamil Nadu Waqf Board in all cases, in future wherein 
action for Direct Management is passed should take into 
consideration the term remaining of the Muthawalli before 
Direct Management is ordered. The Direct Management 
ordered should not indirectly beneﬁ t the Muthawalli to 
continue beyond his original term. 
The Chief Executive Oﬃ  cer, Tamil Nadu Waqf Board 
is hereby directed to take action to form the management 
committee to Hazarath Murthuzawali Masthan Darga, Vellore 
among the hereditary family members of the Waqf within 
a period of two weeks. A new  management committee 
should submit the detailed accounts in respect of the past 
10 years within a period of one month to the Tamil Nadu 
Waqf Board. The orders of the Tamil Nadu Waqf Board dated 
08-12-2009 assuming the direct management of Hazarath 
Murthuzawali Masthan Darga, Vellore are modiﬁ ed to the 
extent above.
