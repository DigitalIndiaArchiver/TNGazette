3. During review meeting conducted by the Hon’ble Chief
Minister on 30-06-2011, it has been decided to merge the
Chennai Sub-urban Police Commissionerate with Chennai
City Police and form a Greater Chennai Police
Commissionerate.
4. Based on the above decision, the Director General
of Police has sent the proposal and informed to the
Government that the original system of policing followed in
2005 with the Greater Chennai City Police functioning under
the supervision of the Commissioner of Police, Chennai
would be better suited in implementing same process,
including methodology, technology and similar services to
all the citizens of Chennai and adjoining areas and would
ensure:—
(i)
Improved maintenance of Law & Order.
(ii) Better System for Prevention and Detection of Crimes.
(iii) Co-ordinated Traffic Management.
(iv) Effective Man Management and
(v) Fool proof VIP Security.
In view of the above, he has requested the Government
to merge Chennai Sub-urban Police with Chennai Police
and to form a Greater Chennai Police Commissionerate and
to divide the Greater Chennai Police Commissionerate into
four zones viz., North, South, East and West.
5. After careful examination, the Government have decided
to accept the proposal of the Director General of Police for
merger of Chennai Sub-urban Police with Chennai City
Police to form Greater Chennai Police Commissionerate.
Accordingly the Government accord sanction on the
following:—
(i) Merger of Chennai Sub-urban Police with Chennai City
Police and form a Greater Chennai Police Commissionerate.
(ii) Consequent on the merger, the Greater Chennai
Police Commissionerate is divided into four Law and Order
Zones having jurisdiction of 3 districts each as mentioned
in the Annexure-I to this order. The New Organizational set
up of Greater Chennai Police Commissionerate shall be as
shown in the Annexure-II to this order.
(iii) Under Rule 4 (2) of the Indian Police Service (Cadre)
Rules, 1954, the Government sanction four temporary posts
of Joint Commissioners of Police viz., JCOP (CCB), JCOP
Traffic (South), JCOP (West), JCOP (HQ) in the scale of pay
of Rs. 37400-67000 + 8900 (GP) and four temporary posts
of Stenographer in the scale of pay of Rs. 5200-20200 +
2400 (GP) with all admissible allowances under the orders and
rules now in force from time to time for a period of one year
from the date of appointment of the incumbents or the need
therefore ceases whichever is earlier.
(iv) Revive the one post of Deputy Commissioner of Police
(intelligence) which is kept abeyance and also redesignate the
certain posts as mentioned in the Annexure - III to this order.
(v) Sanction a sum of Rs. 101.68 lakhs (Recurring
Expenditure of Rs. 73.12 lakhs + Non-Recurring expenditure
of Rs. 28.56 lakhs). The details of recurring and non-
recurring expenditure are furnished in the Annexure - IV to
this order.
6. The expenditure sanctioned in para 5 (v) above is
debited in the Head of Account of "2055-00-Police".
7. The Chennai City Police (Extension to the Chennai City
Sub-urban Area) Act, 2008 has to be repealed to achieve
merger of Chennai Sub-urban Police with the Chennai City
Police and to form a Greater Chennai Police Commissionerate.
The Director General of Police is directed to send necessary
proposal to repeal the relevant provision of the above said Act.
8. The Director General of Police is also directed to sent
the proposals to Government for inclusion of expenditure
such as Communication equipments, Arms & Ammunition
under Modernisation of Police Force Scheme.
9. The expenditure is an item of "New Instrument of
Service". The approval of the Legislature will be obtained
in due course. Pending approval of the Legislature, the
expenditure will be initially met by sanction of an advance
from the Contingency Fund, orders regarding which the
Government in Finance (BG.I) Department will issue. The
Director General of Police is directed to send necessary
proposals to Government in Finance (BG.I) Department, in
the prescribed format along with a copy of this order for
sanction of an advance required for the current year from
Contingency Fund.
10. He is also directed to send a draft explanatory note,
indicating the total cost of the scheme, the cost that would
be required for the implementation during the financial year
etc., to Government in Finance (BG.I) Department for inclusion
of the expenditure in the Supplementary Estimates for bringing
it to the notice of the Legislature in due course.

PUBLIC DEPARTMENT
(S.C.)
Order of detention of certain person and asking
appearance before certain official under Conservation
of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling
Activities Act.
[G.O. No. SR.I/271-17/2011, Public (SC),
2nd December 2011.]
No. II(2)/PUSC/500/2011.—Whereas, an order has been
made by the Government of Tamil Nadu in G.O.No.
SR.I/271-10-2011, Public (SC) Department, dated
22nd September 2011, under Section 3(1)(i) of the Conservation
of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities
Act, 1974 (Central Act 52 of 1974), directing that Thiru Shaik
Nasir Ahmed. son of Thiru Shaik, resident of No. 18/270, Mandy
Syed Mia Street, Cuddapah, Andhra Pradesh, be detained;
And Whereas, the said Thiru Shaik Nasir Ahmed, son of
Thiru Shaik, resident of No.18/270, Mandy Syed Mia Street,
Cuddapah, Andhra Pradesh, cannot be found;
And Whereas, the Government of Tamil Nadu have reasons
to believe that the said Thiru Shaik Nasir Ahmed, son of Thiru
Shaik, resident of No.18/270, Mandy Syed Mia Street, Cuddapah,
Andhra Pradesh in respect of whom the detention order has
been made, has absconded and is concealing himself so that
the said detention order cannot be executed.
Now therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred by
Section 7 (1) (b) of the Conservation of Foreign Exchange
and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974
(Central Act 52 of 1974), the Government of Tamil Nadu
hereby direct the said Thiru Shaik Nasir Ahmed, son of Thiru
Shaik, resident of No. 18/270, Mandy Syed Mia Street,
Cuddapah, Andhra Pradesh, to appear before the
Superintendent of Police, Thiruvallur, at his office within a
period of 15 days (fifteen days) from the date of publication of
this order in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette.
KUMAR JAYANT,
Secretary to Government.
