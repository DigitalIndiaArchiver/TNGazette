WHEREAS on considering the prevailing Covid-19 pandemic scenario and as per the directives of Government of 
India, Ministry of Home Aﬀ airs, State-wide lockdown/restrictions was enforced throughout the State with eﬀ ect from
4.00 A.M. of 10.05.2021 to 6.00 A.M. of 15.09.2021, in order to control the spread of COVID-19. 
 2. The Hon’ble Chief Minister has conducted review meeting on the following issues:-
 
a. 
To evaluate Corona prevention activities, importance of vaccination coverage and actions to be taken. 
 
b. 
The Expert Committee of National Institute of Disaster Management (NIDM) of Union Government has 
reported the possibility of third wave during the month of September and October and also suggested preventive measures 
to be adopted.
 
c. 
Spread of corona virus and Nipah Virus in neighbouring state of Kerala and actions need to be taken in 
this regard.
3. Now, based on the above review meeting, the Government hereby order, under Disaster Management Act, 2005 
to extend the restrictions upto 31.10.2021 with the following instructions:-
Restrictions
 
Public gatherings in religious festivals/consecrations, political, socio-cultural and religion related congregation, 
which were already prohibited, will continue to be prohibited upto 31.10.2021 in order to avoid super spreader events. 
 
The Garlanding of statues for leaders who contributed for the freedom movement and development of
Tamil language shall be performed after obtaining prior permissions from the District Collectors. The family members of 
the leaders (not more than 5 persons) and oﬃ  ce-bearers of recognised political parties (not more than 5 persons) are 
allowed to participate and pay their ﬂ oral tributes by following the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) and maintaining 
Social distance.

Permitted activities 
All permitted activities in existence shall continue to be permitted with existing restrictions.
General
(i) 
All shops and public gathering places should adhere to the following important Standard Operating Procedures.
• 
Shops shall have mandatory hand hygiene (hand sanitizer with dispenser) at the entrance. 
• 
Management shall ensure that the workers and customers wear face masks compulsorily.
• 
All shops and establishments shall ensure that the doors and windows are kept open for cross ventilation. 
In order to ensure social distancing norms, more number of persons shall not be allowed at a time 
inside the shops.
• 
Speciﬁ c markings with suﬃ  cient distance may be made at the entrance of the shops to manage the 
general public standing in the queue.
• 
Action will be taken against the commercial/other organizations which violate the above guidelines and 
prescribed limit of customers.
(ii) 
‘Test-Track-Treat-Vaccination-Covid-19 appropriate behaviour’ principle shall be strictly adhered to.
(iii) In order to contain the spread of Covid-19, the Containment Zones shall be demarcated upto Micro Level and 
the containment measures including vaccination to the people in this area shall be initiated by the District Collectors 
and the Local Bodies as per the Standard Operating Procedures.
(iv) Within the demarcated Containment Zones, containment measures shall be scrupulously followed, as under:
• 
Only essential activities shall be allowed in the Containment Zones. No activity, except medical emergencies 
and supply of essential goods and services shall be permitted.
• 
There shall be intensive house-to-house surveillance by surveillance teams formed for the purpose.
4. In order to control the spread of Corona third wave, vaccination is important, Therefore, all eligible persons are 
requested to get vaccinated.
 5. In order to control the spread of corona, the public are requested to perform their religious festivals in 
their houses, avoid moving to crowded places and to make use of the public transport only if necessity arises.
 6. Considering the interest of students’ education and psychological well-being, the classes for 9th, 10th, 
11th and 12th and colleges are being conducted with the cooperation of teachers and parents. The local body 
authorities, Health Department and School Education Department oﬃ  cials are requested to continue the control 
measures against the spread of corona virus in School and College premises.
7. The public are also requested to seek medical advice of the nearest hospital as soon as symptoms of infection 
appear.
8. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/District Collectors and Commissioners of Police/ Superintendents 
of Police concerned shall take all necessary measures for the strict enforcement of National Directives for Covid-19 
management viz., wearing of face masks, hand hygiene and social distancing, screening & hygiene, frequent 
sanitization of entire workplace etc., and adherence of the Standard Operating Procedures issued for the permitted 
activities. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/District Collectors concerned may initiate penal action by 
imposing ﬁ nes on persons violating the National Directives. 
9. Any person violating these measures will be liable to be proceeded against as per the provisions of
Section 51 to 60 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, besides legal action under Section 188 of the IPC, and other 
legal provisions as applicable.
