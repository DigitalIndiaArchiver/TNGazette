ANIMAL HUSBANDRY DAIRYING AND FISHERIES
DEPARTMENT.
Specification of “Minor Veterinary Services” and persons
to be permitted to do such Veterinary Services under
the Indian Veterinary Council Act.
[G.O. (D) No. 231, Animal Husbandry Dairying and
Fisheries (AH3), 28th December 2012.]
No.II(2)/AHDF/108/2013.—In exercise of the powers
conferred under the Explanation to the proviso to clause (b)
of Section 30 of the Indian Veterinary Council Act, 1984
(Central Act 52 of 1984), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby
specifies the following as minor veterinary services to be
rendered by the Livestock Inspectors, who have successfully
completed the training programme conducted  by the Department
of Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Services. Tamil Nadu. The
Minor Veterinary services are to be rendered under the
supervision and direction of any Graduate Veterinarian having
a valid registration with any State Veterinary Council/Indian
Veterinary Council for the purpose of the said Act:—
1.
Application or administration of the drugs as
specified in Schedule H of the Drugs and Cosmetic Rules,
1945 under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 (Central Act
XXIII of 1940), strictly as prescribed by a registered veterinary
practitioner.
2.
Compounding and dispensing of drugs or
therapeutic preparations.
3.
Rendering preliminary aid through oral administration
of analgesics and antipyretics in cases of pain and fever.
4.
Performing castration by closed method,
de-horning, dis-budding and de-beaking.
5.
Assisting registered veterinary practitioner in
surgical or gynaecological interventions.
6.
Prophylactic vaccinations of animals.
7.
Handling of superficial ailments like wounds,
abscesses, external or superficial haemorrhages, burns etc.,
8.
Washing of mouth, hooves, feet, udder etc., with
antiseptic of medicated preparations in conditions like foot
and mouth disease, mastitis, stomatitis, etc.
9.
Collection and dispatch of samples of blood,
serum, urine, faeces, semen, milk and other specimens for
laboratory examination.
10. Assisting in the surveillance of infetious diseases,
laboratory investigations, and other related technical works
including outbreak control measures.
11. Collect, compile, maintain and report disease
related data.
12. Provide first-aid in cases of emergencies.
Artificial insemination may be performed by any person,
including the Livestock Inspectors, trained specifically for the
purpose, by the Department of Animal Husbandry or the Tamil
Nadu Livestock Development Agency or any Governmental
Agencies approved for this purpose.
GAGANDEEP SINGH BEDI,
Secretary to Government.
