JUDICIAL NOTIFICATIONS
REVISION OF SCALES OF PAY RULE FOR MADRAS HIGH COURT SERVICE
(Including Madurai Bench of Madras High Court, Madurai, ofﬁ ce of the Ofﬁ cial Assignee, High Court, Madras,
Tamil Nadu State Judicial Academy and its Regional Centres, Coimbatore and Madurai, Tamil Nadu Mediation 
and Conciliation Centre, High Court Madras and its Madurai Bench and Arbitration Centre, High Court, Madras).
(Roc. No. 16686/2017/G2)
No. SRO C-4/2018.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Article 229 of the Constitution of India and all other powers, the Honourable 
the Chief Justice, High Court, Madras is hereby pleased to issue the following Rule for the ﬁ xation of Pay in respect of the 
members of the High Court Service (including Madurai Bench of Madras High Court, Madurai, ofﬁ ce of the Ofﬁ cial Assignee, 
High Court, Madras, Tamil Nadu State Judicial Academy and its Regional Centres, Coimbatore and Madurai, Tamil Nadu 
Mediation and Conciliation Centre, High Court Madras and its Madurai Bench and Arbitration Centre, High Court, Madras). 
RULE
Consequent on the recommendation of the Ofﬁ cial Committee and the orders contained in G.O.Ms.No.303, Finance 
[Pay Cell] Department, dated 11.10.2017, revising the scale of pay to the State Government employees (including the 
employees of the High Court, Service) The Hon'ble Chief Justice, with the approval of the Governor of Tamil Nadu, is 
pleased to revise and ﬁ x the scales of pay as speciﬁ ed in the Schedule hereunder to the various posts in the Madras 
High Court Service (including Madurai Bench of Madras High Court, Madurai, ofﬁ ce of the Ofﬁ cial Assignee, High Court, 
Madras,Tamil Nadu State Judicial Academy and its Regional Centres, Coimbatore and Madurai, Tamil Nadu Mediation 
and Conciliation Centre, High Court Madras and its Madurai Bench and Arbitration Centre, High Court, Madras). 
The scale of pay speciﬁ ed in the Schedule shall take notional effect from 1st January, 2016 and monetay beneﬁ t 
with effect from 1st October, 2017. The Tamil Nadu Revised Pay Rules, 2017, notiﬁ ed in G.O.Ms.No.303, Finance 
[Pay Cell] Department, dated 11.10.2017 and the amendment issued by the Government in this regard shall be applicable 
to the members of the High Court Service (including Madurai Bench of Madras High Court, Madurai, ofﬁ ce of the 
Ofﬁ cial Assignee, High Court, Madras,Tamil Nadu State Judicial Academy and its Regional Centres, Coimbatore and 
Madurai, Tamil Nadu Mediation and Conciliation Centre, High Court Madras and its Madurai Bench and Arbitration Centre, 
High Court, Madras). 
The provision regarding the matters like Selection Grade/Special Grade, Special Pay and Special allowances for 
Certain posts, Dearness Allowances, House Rent Allowance, City Compensatory Allowances and other Allowances shall 
be as prescribed in G.O.Ms.No.303, Finance [Pay Cell] Department, dated 11.10.2017, G.O. Ms. No.304, Finance 
[Pay Cell] Department, dated 13.10.2017, G.O.Ms.No.305, Finance [Pay Cell] Department, dated 13.10.2017, 
G.O. Ms. No. 306, Finance [Pay Cell] Department, dated 13.10.2017, G.O. Ms. No.307, Finance [PayCell] Department, 
dated 13.10.2017 and G.O.Ms.No.311, Finance [Pay Cell] Department, dated 23.10.2017, and these Rules shall also 
apply to the Ofﬁ cers and Staff of the High Court Service (including Madurai Bench of Madras High Court, Madurai, ofﬁ ce 
of the Ofﬁ cial Assignee, High Court, Madras,Tamil Nadu State Judicial Academy and its Regional Centres, Coimbatore 
and Madurai, Tamil Nadu Mediation and Conciliation Centre, High Court Madras and its Madurai Bench and Arbitration 
Centre, High Court, Madras). 
The option, as indicated in Rule 6 of the Tamil Nadu Revised Scales of Pay Rules 2017, shall be exercised within 
three months from the date of the coming into the force of these Rules.

The Madras High Court (Appointment and Conditions of Service of Division - III Assistants on Co-Terminus 
Basis) Rules, 2018.
(Roc. No. 23/2016/CON.ESTT.I)
No. SRO C-5/2018.
The Hon’ble Supreme Court in Renu and others vs. District and Sessions Judge, Tis Hazari and another 
(C.A.No.979 of 2014) [(2014) 14 SCC 50] has laid down guidelines for direct Recruitment, including constitution 
of a Centralized Recruitment Cell. 
Pursuant thereto, in view of large number of vacancies in Division-III of the Madras High Court Service 
and since the High Court does not have the required manpower, expertise and infrastructure facilities to

deal with the process of recruitment to ﬁ ll up such large Number of vacancies, the Tamil Nadu Public 
Service Commission was requested by the High Court to take up the recruitment process for the said 
posts. However, the Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission has expressed their inability to accede to the 
request of the High Court since the prescribed qualiﬁ cation for Division-III posts in the High Court Service 
is 8th Standard pass, more number of candidates, who had highly qualiﬁ ed, would also apply and that the 
expected applications would be ranging from 15 to 20 lakhs, in which event, the recruitment work proposed 
by the High Court would involve handling of a large number of applications, incurring huge expenditure on 
conducting examination and diversion of manpower, which may cause disturbance to various recruitment 
programmes scheduled already by the said Commission. 
In view of the above fact and further in view of the fact that the  constitution of the Centralized Recruitment Cell may 
take some considerable time, the High Court of Judicature at Madras, represented by its Registrar General, has ﬁ led M.A.No.798 
of 2017 in the above said Renu’s case before the Hon’ble Supreme Court, praying to permit the High Court of Madras, to appoint 
personnel to vacancies in Division-III of the Madras High Court Service, on co-terminus basis of the services of the Hon’ble Judges 
in the Madras High Court, pending constitution and full-ﬂ edged functioning of the Centralized Recruitment Cell and the Hon’ble 
Supreme Court has allowed the said Application on 18-9-2017. 
Thereafter, the High Court has collected the Rules framed by the Punjab and Haryana High Court, with regard to the 
services of the co-terminus employees in their establishment for reference. 
Thus, now, in view of the order of the Hon’ble Supreme Court, dated 18-9-2017 made in M.A.No.798 of 2017 in 
C.A.No.979 of 2014 and in exercise of the powers conferred by Articles 229 (1) and (2) of the Constitution and of all other powers 
hereunto enabling, the Chief Justice, hereby makes the following Rules, namely:-
1. SHORT TITLE AND COMMENCEMENT.-(1) These Rules may be called the Madras High Court (Appointment and 
Conditions of Service of Division-III Assistants on Co-Terminus Basis) Rules, 2018 
(2) These Rules shall come into force from 31-01-2018. 
2. DEFINITIONS.-In these Rules, unless the context otherwise requires,- 
 
(a) "Chief Justice" means the Chief Justice of the Madras High Court; 
 
(b) "High Court" means the High Court of Judicature at Madras; 
 
(c) "Hon'ble Judge concerned" means the Hon'ble Judge of the Madras High Court, on whose nomination the 
co-terminus employee has been appointed under these Rules; 
 
(d) "Registrar General" means the Registrar General of the Madras High Court; 
 
(e) "Registry" means the Ofﬁ ce of the Registrar General of High Court of Madras;
 
(f) "Service on co-terminus basis" means the service of an employee, coinciding with the period of service of 
the Hon'ble Judge concerned in the Madras High Court, from the date of his appointment on co-terminus basis, till the 
end of tenure of the Hon'ble Judge concerned. 
3. APPOINTMENT.- (1) Every Hon'ble Judge of the High Court of Madras is eligible to temporarily employ two persons 
as Division-III Assistants during his tenure of ofﬁ ce in the High Court of Madras under these Rules. 
 
(2) Every Hon'ble Judge of the High Court of Madras may nominate up to two medically ﬁ t persons as his 
Division-III Assistants to be appointed to the service on co-terminus basis, as per his prerogative, purely .on temporary 
basis, for the period coinciding with the tenure of his, ofﬁ ce in the Madras High Court. Such nomination shall be 
accompanied with his Medical Fitness Certiﬁ cate issued by any Assistant Civil Surgeon of the Government Hospital. 
 
(3) On such nomination by the Hon'ble Judge concerned, the Registrar-General shall issue necessary appointment 
orders to the said persons in the service on co-terminus basis. 
 
(4) In case of termination of the services of any of such person, the Hon'ble Judge concerned may nominate 
another person as his Division-III Assistants on co-terminus basis, in the same manner as provided in sub-rule (2). 
4. CONDITIONS OF SERVICE.-(1) On appointment, the co-terminus employee shall be attached to the Hon'ble Judge 
concerned only and shall not be intra-transferred among other Hon'ble Judges or to the Registry. He shall be posted 
to work in the ofﬁ ce of the Hon'ble Judge concerned at the High Court of Madras or Madurai Bench of High Court of 
Madras and also in the residence of the Hon'ble Judge concerned.
 
(2) The Service of the co-terminus employee shall come to an end immediately and simultaneously with the end 
of tenure of Ofﬁ ce of the Hon'ble Judge concerned in the High Court of Madras.

(3) The service of a co-terminus employee can be terminated at any point of time, without any prior notice and 
without assigning any reason, irrespective of the length of his co-terminus employment. 
(4) The Registrar General shall maintain a list of co-terminus employees, whose services came to an end with the 
retirement or transfer or elevation of the Hon'ble Judge concerned and whenever a new Judge is appointed in the 
High Court of Madras, either on eIevation or by transfer, such list shall be placed before such Hon'ble Judge for 
consideration to nominate any of them in the list as his nominee as co-terminus employee. If the Hon'ble Judge is 
not willing to engage the services of any of such person, he may nominate fresh persons in the same manner as 
provided in sub-rule (2) of rule 3. The persons in the said list shall have no claim or vested right to be nominated as 
co-terminus employee. 
(5) If the Hon'ble Judge transferred from other High Court has already been permitted to have the services of 
the Division-III Assistants or any other Class-IV cadre employee (in whatever name the said category is called in that 
High Court) from the permanent cadre of the High Court from where the Hon'ble Judge is transferred to the Madras 
High Court on foreign service, such equal number shall be deducted from the entitlement of two co-terminus employees 
of the Hon'ble Judge for nomination. The Pay, allowances, leave and other beneﬁ ts of such person shall be as per the 
terms and conditions of his service in the High Court concerned or as provided in rule 5 whichever is more beneﬁ cial 
to him.
(6) A co-terminus employee is not entitled to claim regularization of service or absorption in any cadre in the Registry 
on regular basis, or any other beneﬁ ts, except the beneﬁ ts provided for in these Rules, irrespective of length of period 
of his co-terminus employment in the Madras High Court. 
(7) A co-terminus employee attached with the Hon'ble Judge concerned, may be allowed to continue in co-terminus 
employment of the Hon'ble Judge concerned till the end of next English calendar month followed by the English calendar 
month of retirement or transfer or elevation of the Hon'ble Judge concerned, on a speciﬁ c request made by the Hon'ble 
Judge concerned to the Hon'ble Chief Justice, so as to enable him to make necessary transit arrangements. 
(8) A co-terminus employee shall submit his property statement at the time of joining or re-joining duty and shall not 
acquire any movable or immovable assets without prior intimation or permission of the Registrar-General. 
(9) The co-terminus employee shall not indulge in any act prejudicial to the interest of either the Hon'ble Judge 
concerned or the High Court and he shall, at all points of time, maintain utmost integrity and discipline. 
5. PAY, ALLOWANCES, LEAVE AND OTHER BENEFITS.- 
 
(1)  Notwithstanding anything contained in any other rules, a co-terminus employee will get as remuneration the 
starting basic of  an employee under Category-2 of Section-II of Division-III of the Madras High Court Service Rules, 
2015 plus Dearness Allowance, House Rent Allowance, Medical Allowance admissible, from time to time, during the 
period of his employment on co-terminus basis. The co-terminus employee shall not be entitled to any annual increment, 
bonus, selection grade, super time scale, etc.
(2) A co-terminus employee shall not be entitled to avail,- 
 
(a) more than one casual leave for every completed one month's service; and 
 
(b) 10 days of Medical Leave on production of medical certiﬁ cate for every completed one year of service: 
 
 
Provided that no co-terminus employee shall avail any kind of leave without the prior permission of the 
Hon'ble Judge concerned. 
(3) If any selection/recruitment is made in future to any post in the Registry, the person appointed and continuing in 
service as co-terminus employee as on the date of Notiﬁ cation issued by the Registrar-General, can apply to the said 
selection / recruitment, subject to fulﬁ llment of requisite qualiﬁ cation, as 'in service candidate': 
Provided that such co-terminus employee shall not cross the age limit prescribed for 'in service candidates' in the 
Madras High Court Service Rules: 
Provided further that such co-terminus employee cannot have any preference / claim in such selection.   
6. POWERS OF THE HON'BLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE: If any question or doubt arise regarding interpretation of 
these rules or an issue not covered under these Rules, the decision of the Hon'ble Chief Justice shall be ﬁ nal.
