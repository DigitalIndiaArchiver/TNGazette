The Government of India has approved the “Pradhan 
Mantri’ Matsya Sampada Yojana–A scheme to bring about 
Blue Revolution through sustainable and responsible 
development of ﬁ sheries sector in India” at a total investment 
of Rs.20,050 crore for its implementation for a period of 
5 (ﬁ ve) years from FY 2020-21 to FY 2024-25 in all the 
States and Union Territories (UTs) for smooth implementation 
of Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana.
2. The Government of India has also requested to 
constitute a State Level Approval and Monitoring Committee 
in each State and District Level Committee at the District 
Level for smooth implementation of the Pradhan Mantri 
Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) including its supervision 
and monitoring.  
3. The Director of Fisheries has sent the proposal 
to constitute State Level Approval and Monitoring 
Committee & District Level Committee and requested the 
Government to issue necessary notiﬁ cation for constitution 
of State Level Approval and Monitoring Committee and 
District Level Committee with Terms of References for the 
smooth implementation, supervision and monitoring of projects 
sanctioned under Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana 
(PMMSY) in the State.
4. The Government after careful examination of the 
proposal of the Director of Fisheries and as per the 
Guidelines issued by the Government of India have decided 
to accept it. Accordingly, the Government constitute the 
State Level Approval and Monitoring Committee at State Level 
and District Level Committee at District Level comprising of 
the following members and roles / responsibilities & Terms 
of References:

3. On 03-08-2019 at 12.55 hours Fireman Sh. S.Mohan 
Sekar of Thiruchengode Fire and Rescue Services station 
was performing standby duty around the Cauvery River. 
He saw two persons swept away due to high current in 
Cauvery River and struggling in the river to save their 
live near Patlur Parisalthurai, Eraiyamangalam, Namakkal 
District, Tamil Nadu. He immediately informed the Station 
Oﬃ  cer Sh. P.Sivakumar and without caring to risk to his 
life, jumped into the river which had 150 metre depth 
with water current around 1500 cubic feet per second 
He swam 200 metres in the river in order to save the 
boy and his father. Fireman C.Durairaj, Leading Fireman 
Sh. A.Govindasamy and Fireman Driver Sh. Satishkumar under 
the command and control of Station Oﬃ  cer Sh.Sivakumar 
Pandiyarajan reached at the spot and they also jumped 
into the river without caring to risk to their lives to help 
Fireman Sh.S.Mohan in the rescue operation. After a long 
struggle in the high current they rescued the boy and father 
in unconscious condition. Immediately the crew performed 
Cardiopulmonary Resuscitiation and Seved their lives.

devotion to duty of a very high order.
