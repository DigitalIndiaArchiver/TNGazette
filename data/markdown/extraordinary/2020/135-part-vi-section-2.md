TAMIL NADU STATE ELECTION COMMISSION, CHENNAI-600 106.
[local bodies elections - tamil nadu state election commission - concession to 
political parties - certain amendments to the tamil nadu local bodies election 
symbols (reservation and allotment) order, 2019 - issued.]
(S.O.No.9/2020/TNSEC/EG(B2), (Amendment to Notification Dated the 19th March 2020)
No. VI(2)/12(a)/2020.
An order to provide for specifications, reservation choice and allotment of symbols for 
the Ordinary Elections, 2019 to Local Bodies in the State of Tamil Nadu and for matters 
connected therewith issued by the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission in the Statutory 
Order first read above.
WHEREAS, the Commission in the Statutory Order second to  fourth read above notified 
the  list of Recognised Political Parties and the symbols reserved for them in TABLE I, the 
list of Registered - Unrecognised Political Parties who are entitled to have preference over 
independents in  the allotment of free symbols in TABLE I-A and the list(s) of free symbols 
in Tables 2 to 5 based on the notification issued by the Election Commission of India under 
the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968 and as per the provisions made 
in the election Rules pertaining to Local Bodies Elections, 2019 in Tamil Nadu;
WHEREAS, the Election Commission of India substituted para 10B in the Election Symbols 
(Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968, which provides concession to·candidates set up by a 
registered (unrecognised) parties and to unrecognized parties which were earlier recognized 
parties more than 6 (six) years back;
In consonance with para 10B of the Election Symbols  (Reservation & Allotment) 
Order, 1968 and in exercise of the powers conferred under Articles 243 K and 243 ZA 
of the Constitution of India read with rule 33, 34 and 35 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats 
(Elections) Rules,1995, rule 31, 32 and 33 of the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Municipalities 
and Corporations (Elections) Rules, 2006 and as per clause 11 of the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election 
Symbols  (Reservation   & Allotment)  Order,  2019,  certain concessions are hereby extended 
Accordingly, in supersession of the State Election Commission’s S.O.No.23/2019/TNSEC/
EG1 dated 25.10.2019 published at pages 1 to 2 of Part VI-Section 2 Notification 


2	
Tamil  nadu  government  gazette  extraordinary
No.VI(2)/98(c)/2019 of the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette Extraordinary No.411, dated 30.10.2019, the 
following amendment is hereby made to the Tamil Nadu Local Bodies Election Symbols (Reservation and 
Allotment) Order, 2019.
Amendment
After paragraph 7( 1) B, the following paragraph shall be inserted namely:-
“7 (1) C - Concession to candidates set up by registered (unrecognized} political parties and to 
unrecognized parties which were earlier recognized parties more than 6 years back.
If a registered unrecognized political party has been allotted with a Common Symbol in the elections 
conducted by the Election Commission of India and not cancelled subsequently, the said registered 
unrecognized political party may request the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission to continue to allot 
the same Common Symbol in the Local Body Elections conducted by the Tamil Nadu State Election 
Commission also subject to the following conditions:
Provided that if a Common Symbol allotted by ECI to one or more registered political parties in 
different elections prior to Local Body Elections, that Common Symbol shall be considered for allotment 
to the party to whom the Common Symbol were allotted by the ECI in the recently held Election:
Provided further that  an application is made to the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission by 
the said party for the concession for the allotment of Common Symbol to the candidates set up by 
it, not later than the third day after  the  publication  in  the  Official  Gazette  of  the  notification 
calling  the election along with the details of No. of wards / offices in which the said party wishes to 
get concession for the allotment of Common Symbol;
Provided further that the free symbol allotted as a Common  Symbol shall not be entitled for the 
candidates set up by the party other than those wards / offices for which the details has been furnished 
as per para 3 to the Commission under this provision:
Provided further that if the party does not set up candidates as furnished, the party shall be liable 
for such action as the Commission may consider appropriate as per existing laws:
Provided further that the free symbol allotted as a common symbol to the candidates of a party 
under this paragraph shall be available for allotment to candidates  set up by the other party or 
independent candidates in those other wards in which that party has not set up its candidates; and
Provided also that  notwithstanding  anything  contained  in  paragraph 7( 1) B, a political party which 
was earlier a recognized political party  and which lost its recognition more than 6 (six) years back will 
also be eligible under this paragraph to the concession of allotment of the symbol which was earlier 
reserved for the party as above.”
(By Order of the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission)
