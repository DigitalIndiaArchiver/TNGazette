BILL INTRODUCED IN THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF THE
STATE OF TAMIL NADU
Under Rule 130 of the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly Rules, the following Bill
which was introduced in the Legislative Assembly of the State of Tamil Nadu on
24th October 2013 is published together with Statement of Objects and Reasons for
general information:—
L.A. Bill No. 20 of 2013
A Bill to amend the Tamil Nadu Advocates’ Clerks
Welfare Fund Act, 1999.
BE it enacted by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Tamil Nadu in the
Sixty-fourth  Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
1.
(1) This Act may be called the Tamil Nadu Advocates’ Clerks Welfare
Fund (Amendment) Act, 2013.
   (2) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government
may, by notification, appoint.
2.
In section 15 of the Tamil Nadu Advocates’ Clerks Welfare Fund Act,
1999 (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act),-
    (1) in sub-section (3), for the expression “rupees fifty”, the expression
“rupees one hundred” shall be substituted;
    (2) in sub-section (4), for the expression “rupees fifty”, the expression
“rupees one hundred” shall be substituted;
   (3) in sub-section (5), for the expression “rupees one thousand”, the
expression “rupees two thousand” shall be substituted.
3. In section 16 of the principal Act, in sub-section (2), for the expression
“rupees fifty thousand”, the expression “rupees two lakh” shall be substituted.

STATEMENT OF OBJECTS AND REASONS
The Tamil Nadu Advocates’ Clerks Welfare Fund Act, 1999 (Tamil Nadu
Act 25 of 1999) was enacted for the constitution of a Welfare Fund for the
benefit of Advocates’ Clerks and for payment of lumpsum amount from out
of the Fund on cessation of  employment or on the death of the member
of the Fund.
2.  The Bar Council of Tamil Nadu has resolved to enhance the financial
assistance paid to the nominee or where there is no nominee to his dependants,
on the death of the member of the Tamil Nadu Advocates’ Clerks Welfare Fund,
from rupees fifty thousand to two lakh rupees and in order to meet the additional
expenditure, the Bar Council has also recommended to increase the application
fee, admission fee and membership fee. The Government have decided to accept
the recommendations of the Bar Council of Tamil Nadu and decided to amend
the said Tamil Nadu Act 25 of 1999 suitably for the purpose.
3.  The Bill seeks to give effect to the above decision.
