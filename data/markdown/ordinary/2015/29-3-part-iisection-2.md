READ:
1. From the Principal Secretary to Governor, Raj Bhavan

ORDER: No. 836, Public (Pro.III), 6th July 2015.
The Government of Tamil Nadu is pleased to appoint Lt
Akhilendra Singh Somvanshi (52657-Z), Indian Navy as Aide-
De-Camp (Navy) to His Excellency the Governor of Tamil Nadu
for a period of two years with effect from 19th June 2015 FN.
2. The Government further direct that the appointment of
Lt Akhilendra Singh Somvanshi (52657-Z), Indian Navy as
Aide-De-Camp (Navy) to His Excellency the Governor of
Tamil Nadu will be on the following terms and conditions of
the services intimated by Indian Navy, New Delhi vide their
letter second read above:
1. Tenure.
The period of deputation will be two years with effect
from the date the officer is struck of strength from his
last appointment in the Navy. During the period of
deputation, the officer will neither be recalled nor allowed
to revert to the Navy without notice of at least six months.

The officer, will however, be liable to be recalled at any
time if exigencies of service so necessitate.
2. Pay and Allowances.
The officer will have the option to draw either Defence
Service rates of pay or the Civilian rates of pay for the post.
Option once exercised shall be final. In either case, the cost
will be borne by the Government of Tamil Nadu.
3. Deputation Allowance.
While on deputation, the officer will be entitled to deputation
allowance admissible vide Ministry of Defence letter
No.1(26)/97/VIII/D(Pay)/Service, dated 29-02-2000, as amended
from time to time.
4. Leave.
The officer will be entitled to leave as per Navy Leave Rules
1970, as amended from time to time.
5. Leave Travel Concession.
The officer will be entitled to Leave Travel Concession as
applicable to Defence Service Officers, from time to time, the
cost of which will be borne by Government of Tamil Nadu,
Warrants or concessional voucher, where normally issued
under naval rules, will be obtained from nearest Naval / Defence
station, in accordance with relevant rules.
6. Medical Attendance.
The officer will be entitiled to Medical Attendance as
admissible to Defence Service Officers of his rank/status, in
Defence or Civil Hospital, cost of which would be borne by
Government of Tamil Nadu.
7. Permanent Transfer Allowance.
The officer will be entitled  to the allowances for permanent
transfer at the scale applicable to Defence Service officers of
his rank, in accordance with FR 1983 and TR 1991, as amended
from time to time, both on transfer and on revision there from
to the Navy, the expenditure for which will be borne by
Government of Tamil Nadu.
8. Ration / Cash Allowance.
The officer will also be entitled to draw free ration or cash
allowance in lieu, in accordance with instructions contained in
this Ministry's letter 3(1)/83D(QS), dated 30th July 1984.
9. Naval Group Insurance Fund. IN Amenities fund and
In Benevolent Association Fund.
The officer will continue to contribute to the above funds at
the normal rates as applicable and recoveries on account
thereof will be remitted direct to the Secretaries of the Funds
at Integrated Headquarters of Ministry of Defence (Navy)
New Delhi.
10. Subscription of DSOP Fund.
The Subscription towards DSOP Fund will continue to be
recovered from the officer and will be remitted to the logistics
Officer-In-Charge, Naval Pay Office, Mumbai, for credit to the
officer's Provident Fund Account.
11. The other terms and conditions will be as laid down in
the MoD letter No.4(15)/58/99/D(MS), dated 25.02.1970, as
amended from time to time.
3. This order shall be deemed to have come into force
from 19th June 2015 F.N.
(By Order of the Governor)
JATINDRA NATH SWAIN,
Princial Secretary to Government.
