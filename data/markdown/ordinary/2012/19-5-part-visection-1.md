(Roc. No. 22687/2011/DP2)
No. VI(1)/150/2012.
In exercise of the powers conferred under Sub-Section 1 of Section 33 of  Town and Country Planning
Act 1971 (Act No. 35 of 1972). The Commissioner of Town and Country Planning, Chennai in his Proceedings
Roc. No. 22687/2011  DP2, dated: 24th April 2012, Proposes to make the following Deletion of 30'.0" widening portion
pt. in proposed “AA” 60'.0" Scheme Road in (old S.No. 24/2pt) T.S.No. 13/1pt of Ward-A, Block-22 Sattur Detailed
Development Plan No. 5 approved by the Director of town and Country Planning in his Proceedings Roc. No. 891/1992
DP2, dated: 8th December 1993 of Sattur Local Planning Authority/Sattur Municipality and the said notification was
Published in Tamil Nadu Government Gazette Part-VI, Section-1, Page No. 305 dated 30th August 1994.
2.
Any person affected or interested in this draft individual variation may within sixty days from the date of publication
of this notification in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette can represent in person or submit in writing to the
Member Secretary/Commissioner, Sattur Local Planning Authority/Municipality any objection and suggestions relating
thereto.
3.
The variation with plan may be inspected at free of cost any time during office hours at the above said Local
Planning Authority Office.
VARIATION
1.
Wherever the expression Map No. 4 DDP/MR/DTCP No. 20/1993 occurs the expression Map No. 5 DDP(V)/DTCP
No. 8/2012 shall be added at the end to be read with.
2.
In Schedule III, Part-I 30'.0" widening portion part in proposed 60'.0" “AA” Scheme road shall be deleted.
3.
In Schedule-IV (form No. 7) in Serial No. 1 entry 30'.0" widening portion pt in proposed “AA” 60.’0" Scheme Road
shall be deleted and in column No. 4 “0.9630 Acres” shall be substituted.
4.
In Schedule-V (Form No. 6) all the entries in the 30'.0" widening portion pt in proposed “AA” 60'.0" Scheme Road,
shall be deleted.

JUDICIAL NOTIFICATIONS
Adjournment of Court of Small Causes, Chennai for Summer Vacation, 2012
(Dis. No. 1366/2012.)
No. VI(1)/153/2012.
Except as hereunder mentioned the Presidency Court of Small causes, Chennai will remain closed for the Summer
Vacation, 2012 from 01-05-2012 to 03-06-2012 (both days inclusive).
Tmt. N. Gunavathi, IV Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be  the Vacation Judge for First turn from
01-05-2012 to 17-05-2012.
Thiru R. Bharanidharan, VIII Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be the Vacation Judge for Second turn from
18-05-2012 to 03-06-2012.
Thiru E. Bakthavatchalu, XIII Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be the Additional Vacation Judge for the
First turn from 01-05-2012 to 17-05-2012.
Thiru A. Phlip Nicholas Aalex,  XIV Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be the Additional Vacation Judge for
the Second turn from 18-05-2012 to 03-06-2012.  The Additional Vacation Judges will deal with Rent Control Original
Petitions.
Thiru E. Bakthavatchalu, XIII Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be the Vacation Officer for first turn
from 01-05-2012 to 17-05-2012 and Thiru A. Phlip Nicholas Alex, XIV Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be
the vacation officer for second turn from 18-05-2012 to 03-06-2012.
The vacation Judges unless otherwise notified will sit on all Thursdays, thereafter during the vacation, to dispose the
emergent petitions, No Plaint, No Rent Control Appeals or others matters will be received during the period of vacation,
unless it is urgent and it is accompanied by an application duly supported by an Affidavit showing the urgency, no fresh
execution petition in Rent Control matters will be received during the vacation.
The office of the Copyists will be kept open from Monday to Friday each week between 10.30 A.M. to 4.00 P.M. for
delivering the certified copies which are already made ready.  The Office of the Central Nazarath Section will be kept
open from Monday to Friday each week between 10.30 A.M. to 4.00 P.M. and the Process Services  Applications will
be received on Wednesday & Thursdays only.
The Office of the Registrar will be kept open from Monday to Friday each week but the urgent matters will be
received on Wednesday during the vacation and such matters will be posted before the Judges for hearing on the
succeeding Thursday in open court.
If any case requires immediate attention, party concerned or his Advocate should give 24 hours notice of the
same to the office in charge and the necessary papers will be sent to  the residence of the vacation Judge for orders.
No application shall be presented at the residence of the vacation Judge under any circumstances.
Money will be received in the treasury on Wednesday and Thursday every week and money will be paid out only on
Thursday during when the vacation Judges are holding courts and on orders made by the Judges for such payments.
With a view to avoid difficulty to the parties, petitioners and the members of the staff, Suit, Rent control Original petition,
Rent Control Appeals and the petitions of other proceedings which are to be filed on the re-opening days will also be
received and returned.  The matters will be made available for taking returns in the regular courts between 11.00 A.M.
and 3.00 P.M. on Thursday only.
