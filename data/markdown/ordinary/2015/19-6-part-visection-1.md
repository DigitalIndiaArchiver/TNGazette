JUDICIAL NOTIFICATIONS
Adjournment of Court of Small Causes, Chennai, for Summer Vacation, 2015.
(Dis. No. 848/2015.)
No. VI(1)/122/2015.
Except as hereunder mentioned the Presidency Court of Small Causes, Chennai will remain closed for the
Summer Vacation 2015 from 01-05-2015 to 31-05-2015.
The Summar vacation (for the year 2015) of this Court will be from 01-05-2015 to 31-05-2015. The First turn will be
from 01-05-2015 to 16-05-2015 and the Second turn of the vacation will be from 17-05-2015 to 31-05-2015.
The Office of the Court of Small Causes, Chennai will function from Monday to Wednesday and Friday each week
from 10 a.m. to 4.30 p.m., on Thursday (i.e.) court sitting day, from 10 a.m. to 5.45 p.m. during the summer vacation 2015.
Tmt. I. Jeyanthi, VIII Judge, Court of Small Cause, Chennai will be the Vacation Judge for First turn from 01-05-2015
to 16-05-2015.
Tmt. G. Vijayarani, IX Judge, Court of Small Cause, Chennai will be the Vacation Judge for Second turn from
17-05-2015 to 31-05-2015.
Thiru C. Sasikumar, XIII Judge, Court of Small Cause, Chennai will be the Additional Vacation Judge for First turn from
01-05-2015 to 16-05-2015. The Additional Vacation Judge will deal with Rent Control Original Petition.
Thiru R. Karl Marx, XVI Judge Court of Small Cause, Chennai will be the Additional Vacation Judge for second turn
from 17-05-2015 to 31-05-2015, the Additional Vacation Judge will deal with Rent Control Original Petitions.
Thiru N. Sachidhanandham, XV Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be the vacation officer for First turn from
01-05-2015 to 16-05-2015.
Thiru R. Karl Marx, XVI Judge, Court of Small Causes, Chennai will be the Vacation Officer for Second turn from
17-05-2015 to 31-05-2015.
The filing Section will be functioning on every Wednesdays and the sitting of the Judges will be on every Thursdays
of the respective week during the Summer Vacation 2015.
The Vacation Judges unless otherwise notified will sit on all Thursday of every week during the vacation, to dispose
the emergent petitions. No fresh plaint petition, no Rent Control appeals or other matters will be received during the period
of vacation, unless it is urgent and it is accompanied by an application duly supported by an Affidavit showing the urgency.
The Copyist Section of the Court of Small Causes will be kept open from Monday to Friday each week and between
10.30 a.m. to 3.00 p.m. for receiving copy application and for delivering the certified copies, which were already made ready
and no fresh certified copy will not be made ready during summer vacation.
The Nazir Section will function from Monday to Friday each week and Process Application will be received between
10.30 a.m. to 3.00 p.m. on Wednesdays. Warrants will be issued only on Wednesdays and Thursdays of the respective
weeks, which were already filed and made ready. Warrants which are being filed on Wednesday will be made ready and
will be issued only on the next day (i.e.) Thursday. If the Warrants returned for reasons, (i.e. since Police protection not
available), the same will be re-issued only on next Wednesday or Thursday, if the Petitioner/Decree Holder wants to
execute the Warrant and makes such endorsement.
The Office of the Registrar will be kept open from Monday to Friday each week, but the urgent matters will be received
on Wednesdays during the vacation and such matters will be posted before the Vacation Judges and the Additional Vacation
Judges for the hearing on the succeeding Thursday in the open court.
Money will be received in the Treasury Section on Wednesdays and Thursdays of every week and money will be paid
out only on Thursdays during the Vacation, when the Vacation Judges are holding courts and on orders made by the
vacation Judges for such payments.
With a view to avoid difficulty to the parties/petitioners and the members of the staff, Suit, Rent Control Original
Petition, Rent Control Appeals and the petitions of other proceedings which are to be filed on the re-opening day, will also
be received and returned. The case bundles will be made available for taking returns in the regular courts between
11.00 a.m. and 3.00 p.m. on Thursday only.
B. GOKULDAS,
Chief Judge.
Court of Small Causes,
A. PHILIP NICHOLAS ALEX,
Chennai-104,
Registrar.
28th April 2015.

Notification Regarding the Arrangements made by Sessions Judge, Chennai Division, Chennai for Attending
Urgent Criminal Matters during Summer Recess, 2015.
(Dis. No. 5101/2015/E1)
(Roc. No. 138/2015/E1)
No. VI(1)123/2015.
1.In exercise of the powers conferred Under Section 10(3) of the Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, the Sessions Judge,
Chennai Division makes the following arrangements for attending Urgent Criminal Work during Summer Recess, 2015
(01-05-2015 to 31-05-2015).
2.Tmt. A. Kayalvizhi, B.Sc.,B.Ed.,B.L., III Additional Judge, City Civil Court, Chennai will be incharge of Criminal Work
during Summer Recess from 01-05-2015 to 31-05-2015.
3.The Vacation Sessions Judge will hold Courts on Wednesdays in every week during the vacation for the disposal
of urgent Criminal Matters.
4.The Chief Administrative Officer, City Civil and Sessions Court, Chennai will be the Vacation Officer during Summer
Recess, 2015. In her absence, Sherishtadar will be the Vacation Officer.
Procedure for the Receipt and Disposal of Urgent Criminal Matters during Summer Recess, 2015.
5.All urgent applications, Criminal Appeals, etc., shall be received only on Tuesdays upto 2.00 p.m. The matters will
be placed for orders before the Vacation Sessions Judge only on Wednesdays during vacation at 10.30 a.m. Applications
and other matters shall not be presented at the residence of the Vacation Sessions Judge.
6.In all Bail Applications, including the applications for Anticipatory Bail, Notice shall be given to the City Public
Prosecutor before filing the papers in Court and necessary endorsements shall be obtained from the City Public Prosecutor.
7.Clear and authenticated typed copies of the Judgements should be filed whenever certified copies are not easily
decipherable.
8.In all cases wherein notices are to be issued sufficient number of copies of memorandum of grounds, petitions and
affidavits shall be filed for service on the City Public Prosecutor as well as the Respondents concerned.
City Civil Court,
N. AUTHINATHAN,

Principal Judge.
