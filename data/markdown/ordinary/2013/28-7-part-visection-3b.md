Election to the Authorities of the University
(No. ME2/AC/Election/2013.)
No. VI-3(b)/16/2013.
It is hereby notified that the following elections will be held under section mentioned hereunder as per the Madurai
Kamaraj University Act, 1965 to elect:
(i) THREE MEMBERS to the SYNDICATE by the ACADEMIC COUNCIL from among the members of whom
two shall be Teachers of affiliated colleges and the remaining shall be a person other than a teacher of an affiliated
college under Section 19(a) Class II - Other members (2) of the Madurai Kamaraj University Act, 1965.
(ii) FIVE MEMBERS to the SENATE by the members of the ACADEMIC COUNCIL from among themselves
of whom not less than three shall be Teachers of affiliated colleges under Section 15(a) Class III - Others members (2)
of the Madurai Kamaraj University Act, 1965.
(iii) SIX MEMBERS to the STANDING COMMITTEE of the Academic Council by the members of the
ACADEMIC COUNCIL of whom three shall be members of the Academic Council who are Principals or Teachers of
affiliated colleges and the remaining three shall be persons other than members of the Syndicate under Chapter XVI
Statutes 2 of Madurai Kamaraj University Volume I Calendar.
The last date and hour for receipt of nominations is Friday, the 26th July, 2013 at 04.00 p.m. by the
Registrar in-charge, Madurai Kamaraj University, Palkalai Nagar, Madurai-625 021 and the date and time regarding the
further election process can be had by the candidates from the Registrar in-charge.

PERIYAR UNIVERSITY, SALEM.
Notification for Constitution of Selection Committee for the Appointment of the Vice-Chancellor,
Periyar University.
[PU/Estt/D2/010998/2013]
No. VI-3(b)/17/2013.
It is hereby notified that a  selection committee for the appointment of Vice-Chancellor, Periyar University has to be constituted
under section 12 sub section (1) and (2) of Periyar University Act, 1997.
Each member of the Syndicate as well as Senate shall have a right to nominate not more than one person of high standing
who is not a member of any of the authorities of the University, to the committee referred to in section 12 sub (1) and (2) of the
Act.
Nomination forms are available in the office of the Registrar, Periyar University, Salem.  Every nomination must be presented
before the Registrar, Periyar University, Salem either by the proposed or the seconder or the person proposed before 3.00 p.m.
on 1st August 2013.
Every nomination shall be in writing by the proposer who shall be a member of the Senate and seconder in writing by another
member of the Senate called the seconder in the form prescribed.  No nomination shall be valid unless it contains the signatures
of both the proposer and the seconder.
The person proposed expressing his consent to be nominated shall sign the nomination form.
The Scrutiny of nominations will take place on the same day in the office of the Registrar, Periyar University, Salem by 3.15
p.m.  The Proposer or the seconder or the proposed nominees are entitled to be present at the time of the scrutiny.  The list of
persons whose nominations have been declared valid by the Registrar shall be published on the notice board of the office of the
University on the same date.  A copy of the list shall be sent to the persons nominated for election, if he/she or they were not
present at the time of scrutiny.
Any persons whose nominations has been declared valid may withdraw his/her nomination by a letter in writing and attested
by another member of the Senate and sent by a registered post to the Registrar so as to reach him not later than seven clear
days such withdrawal, once made shall be final.

If the number of persons validly nominated and who have not withdrawn is only that person shall be deemed to have been duly
elected and nominated by the Senate to the Committee referred to in Statue 9 and shall be so declared by the Registrar.
If the number of persons validly nominated and who have not withdrawn is more than one, each member of the Senate
shall be informed of the date and hour fixed by the Vice-Chancellor for the meeting and a list of such persons shall be sent
to each member of the Senate not less than ten clear days fixed for the meeting.
The election shall be held at the next ordinary meeting of the Senate immediately proceeding the date when the office
of the Vice-Chancellor shall fall vacant, provided that the Vice-Chancellor shall convene special meeting of the Senate for
election of the nominee if he thinks it necessary.
The election shall be conducted by secret ballot at the place specially set apart for the purpose and the ballot box properly
locked and sealed shall be provided to receive the votes of the nominees.
All the members present at the meeting shall be entitled to vote.  No. vote can be given by proxy or by post.  Members
present shall sign in the nominal electoral roll kept for the purpose as a record of voting at the election.
When a member has received a ballot paper, he shall proceed to the place arranged for making the voted and shall mark
thereon by “*” against the name for whom he intends to vote.  The number of nominees for whom each elector may vote shall be
shall be only one.  The member shall then fold the ballot paper and drop it in the ballot box placed in front of the Returning Officer.
If a member inadvertently spoils a ballot paper, he may return it to the Registrar, who shall, if satisfied of such
inadvertence, give him another ballot paper, and retain the spoiled paper, and this spoiled paper shall be immediately
cancelled and the fact of such cancellation shall be noted on the counterfoil.
Two scrutinizers shall be selected by the  Chairperson of the meeting of the Senate to count the votes polled.  The counting
shall be done in the presence of the members of the Senate present and the Chairperson.  The person securing the highest
number of votes shall be declared by the Chair duly elected and nominated by the Senate to Committee referred to in the Act.
The Chairperson of the meeting held for the purpose of the election under Act shall conduct others items of business,
if any, but the business of electing a member to the committee shall proceed all other business and shall be disposed of before
the meeting is adjourned or before any other item, if any on the agenda is considered.
The Statutes in this Chapter relating to the procedure for nomination and election of person by the Senate to the
Committee referred to in Act shall as far as may be necessary apply to the nomination and election of a person by the
Syndicate to the committee mentioned in the Act the word ‘Syndicate’ being  read in substitution of the word ‘Senate’ wherever
necessary.
Not withstanding anything contained herein shall be competent for the Syndicate to elect such nominee to the Committee
by a resolution assented to by the majority of the members of the Syndicate after obtaining the consent of the concerned
nominee to serve on the committee.
Nomination papers that are not enclosed in an envelope super scribed and sent by the Registered post or not delivered
in person or by messenger before the stipulated time shall be declared invalid.

THIRUVALLUVAR UNIVERSITY, SERKKADU, VELLORE.
Election to the Syndicate and Academic Council of the Thiruvalluvar University,
Vellore from among themselves.
(No. TU/R/Election Notification-Publication/2013/6592))
No. VI-3(b)/19/2013.
1. Under Chapter IV, Section 23 Sub-Section (b) (4) of the Thiruvalluvar University Act, 2002 (Tamil Nadu Act 32 of 2002),
TWO MEMBERS to the SYNDICATE are to be elected by the Principal of Affiliated Colleges of Thiruvalluvar University from
among themselves in accordance with the system of proportional representation by means of the single transferable vote.
2. Under Chapter III, Section 20 Sub-Section (1), (i)  of the said Act, TEN PRINCIPALS of Affiliated Colleges are to be
elected from among themselves and under Sub-Section (1) (j) of the said Act, TEN TEACHERS OF AFFILIATED AND APPROVED
COLLEGES Other then Principals of Affiliated Colleges are to be elected to the Academic Council from among themselves in
accordance with the system of proportional representation by means of the single transferable vote.
3. Under Chapter III, Section 20 Sub-Section (1) (O) of the said Act, TWO SECRETARIES of Private Colleges are to be
elected from among themselves to the Academic Council in accordance with the system of proportional representation by
means of the single transferable vote.
4. The persons getting elected will hold office as a Member of the Syndicate / Academic Council for a period of
THREE YEARS from the date of declaration of results.
5. Nomination Papers shall be enclosed in an envelope addressed to the Registrar and superscribed “ NOMINATION  TO THE
SYNDICATE BY THE PRINCIPALS” – “NOMINATION TO THE ACADEMIC COUNCIL BY THE PRINCIPALS” and “NOMINATION
TO THE ACADEMIC COUNCIL BY THE TEACHERS [other than Principals]” and “NOMINATION TO THE ACADEMIC COUNCIL
BY THE SECRETARIES” as indicated below and sent by Registered Post with Ack. Due so as to be received by the Registrar or
delivered to the Registrar during Office Hours either in Person or by messenger within the date and hour fixed.
