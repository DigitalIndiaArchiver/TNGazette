with existing guidelines and certain relaxations upto 06.00 A.M. of 31.07.2021- under the 
Disaster Management Act, 2005.

WHEREAS on considering the prevailing Covid-19 pandemic scenario and as per the directives of 
Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs, State-wide complete lockdown was enforced throughout the 
State with effect from 4.00 A.M. of 10.05.2021 to 6.00 A.M. of 19.07.2021, in order to control the spread of COVID-19. 
2. Now, after reviewing the pandemic situation, the Government hereby order under Disaster Management Act, 2005 
to extend lock down from 19.07.2021 to 06.00 A.M. on 31.07.2021 with following restrictions and relaxations.
I. The following activities shall continue to remain prohibited during the lockdown period. 
	
(i)	
Inter-State Public and Private Bus Transport  (Except Puducherry)
	
(ii)	
All international air travel of passengers, except for purposes as permitted by Ministry of Home Affairs
	
(iii)	 Cinema Theatres / Multiplex  
	
(iv)	 All Bars
	
(v)	 Swimming Pools
	
(vi)	 All social / political gatherings
	
(vi)	 Entertainment / sports / cultural events

(vii)	 Schools and Colleges
	
(viii)	Zoological Parks
All other activities other than those mentioned above shall be permitted.
Restrictions for Marriages and Funerals:-
	
(i)	
Marriage related gathering - Number of guests not to exceed 50. 
	
(ii)	
Funeral/ last rites related gathering - Number of persons not to exceed 20. 
II. The following activities shall be allowed in addition to the existing permitted activities throughout the 
State (Except in containment zones):-
	
(i)	
Considering the employment opportunities for the Vocational Training Students, All Vocational Training 
Centres (ITIs and Industrial Schools) and Typewriting/ Short hand Technical Institutes are allowed with 50 percentage of 
Students at a time on rotation basis by following SOP. 
	
(ii)	
Teachers are allowed to work at the schools to ensure that all administrative tasks including student 
admissions, Book distribution, Syllabus preparation etc., are carried out smoothly. 
III. General
	
(i)	
Shops permitted to function shall follow the following important Standard Operating Procedures.
 Shops shall have mandatory hand hygiene (sanitizer dispenser) at the entrance and thermal screening shall 
also be done,
 Management shall ensure that the workers and customers wear face masks compulsorily.
 All shops and establishments using air conditioning facility shall ensure that the doors and windows 
are kept open for adequate ventilation.  In order to ensure social distancing norms, more number of persons 
shall not be allowed at a time inside the shops.
 Specific markings with sufficient distance may be made at the entrance of the shops to manage the 
general public standing in the queue.
	
(ii)	
Test-Track-Treat-Vaccination-Covid-19 Appropriate Behaviour principle shall be strictly adhered to.
	
(iii)	 In order to contain the spread of Covid-19, the Containment Zones shall be demarcated upto Micro Level 
and the containment measures shall be initiated by the District Collectors and the Local Bodies as per the Standard 
Operating Procedures.
	
(iv)	 Within the demarcated Containment Zones, containment measures shall be scrupulously followed, as under:
 Only essential activities shall be allowed in the Containment Zones.
 No activity, except medical emergencies and supply of essential goods and services shall be permitted.
 There shall be intensive house-to-house surveillance by surveillance teams formed for the purpose.
 The respective Departments shall strictly ensure the enforcement of the Standard Operating  
Procedures issued for various permitted activities.
3.  In-order to control the spread of COVID-19, Public are requested to maintain discipline and Covid appropriate 
behaviour by avoiding unnecessarily coming out of the house and gathering in Public places.
4. The public are requested to seek medical advice of the nearest hospital as soon as symptoms of infection appear.
5. Industries which have been permitted to operate shall strictly adhere to the Standard Operating Procedures and 
to carry out disinfection activities at regular intervals. Industries shall also initiate immediate action to vaccinate all their 
employees. In case of non-adherence of the Covid-19 appropriate behaviour and the Standard Operating Procedures 
issued, penal action will be initiated against concerned establishments.
6. The Government also orders that no activities shall be permitted in the Containment Zones.

7. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/ District Collectors and Commissioners of Police/ Superintendents 
of Police concerned shall take all necessary measures for the strict enforcement of National Directives for Covid-19 
management viz., wearing of face masks, hand hygiene and social distancing, screening & hygiene, frequent 
sanitization of entire workplace etc., and adherence of the Standard Operating Procedures issued for the permitted 
activities. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/ District Collectors concerned may initiate penal action for 
imposing fines on persons violating the National Directives. 
8. Any person violating these measures will be liable to be proceeded against as per the provisions of 
Section 51 to 60 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, besides legal action under Section 188 of the IPC, and other 
legal provisions as applicable.
