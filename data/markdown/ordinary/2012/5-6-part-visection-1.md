Elections to the Tamil Nadu Medical Council, Chennai during the year 2012.
(Ref:TNMC/Elec.5(1)(f)/2012.)
PRECEPT
No. VI(1)/63/2012.
In accordance with rule 2, in regard to the Elections to the Tamil Nadu Medical Council, Chennai, under clause (f) of
sub-section (1) of section 5 of the Madras Medical Registration Act, 1914 (Act IV of 1914) as amended by the Madras Medical
Registration (Amendment) Act XVI of 1938, notice is hereby given that an election of Seven Members of the Council to be
elected from amongst themselves by the registered Medical Practitioners whose names appear in the printed Medical
Registers (Electoral Roll-Compact Disc) available in the Medical Council Office upto the year 2011 will be held.
Nomination paper in Form II under the rule will be supplied by the Returning Officer to any elector who may apply for
the same. These must be filed in accordance with the instructions thereon and returned to the Returning Officer not later
than 3.P.M on Monday, the 27th February, 2012. The scrutiny of nomination papers will take place at 11.A.M. on Tuesday
the 28th February, 2012 at the office of the Returning Officer. A list of valid nominations will be published in the Tamil Nadu
Government Gazette on Wednesday the 14th March, 2012. Last date for withdrawal of nomination is 3 P.M. on Wednesday
the 28th March, 2012. Publication of final list of valid nominations is on Wednesday the 11th April, 2012. Voting papers and
other forms connected therewith will be sent by Speed Post by the Returning Officer on or before Friday, the 25th May, 2012
to all electors with instructions printed therein and the voting papers are returnable to the Returning Officer not later than
5 P.M. on Monday, the 18th June, 2012. The scrutiny and counting of votes will take place at the office of the
Returning Officer from 19th June, 2012 (Tuesday) 11.A.M. onwards.
If for any reason of the dates fixed for the various proceedings in the conduct of the election happens to fall on a holiday
the proceedings fixed for that day will take place in the next day and at the time fixed already.
All Communications regarding the elections should be addressed as hereunder.
The Returning Officer, Tamil Nadu Medical Council, Chennai Elections, New No. 914, Old No. 569, Poonamallee
High Road, Arumbakkam, Chennai-600 106.

ELECTION OF SEVEN MEMBERS TO THE TAMIL NADU MEDICAL COUNCIL, CHENNAI.
(Ref:TNMC/Elec.5(1)(f)/2012.)
No. VI(1)/65/2012.
Notice is hereby given pursuant to the provisions of rule 5 in regard to the elections to the Tamil Nadu Medical Council,
Chennai under clause (f) sub-section (1) of Section 5 of the Madras Medical Registration Act, 1914 as amended by the
Madras Medical Registration (Amendment Act XVI of 1938) that the election of seven members to the Tamil Nadu Medical
Council, Chennai,  to serve for a term of five years is to be held and the electorate is called upon to elect the members.
Nominations of eligible persons to fill the vacancies are invited.
Each candidate must be nominated by separate nomination paper but any person entitled to vote at the election may sign
the nomination paper of any number of candidates not exceeding the number to be elected and for which he is entitled to
vote.  Every nomination paper must be in Form II giving all the details as required therein.
The nomination paper must reach the undersigned not later than 3 P.M. on Monday the 27th February, 2012 from
whom nomination papers may be obtained on application. Nomination papers which are not received by the Returning Officer
by the aforesaid date and time will be invalid.  Each candidate standing for election shall deposit or cause to be deposited
with the Returning Officer the sum of Rupees fifty in Cash on or before 27th February, 2012 (3 P.M.) and no candidate
shall be deemed to be duly nominated unless such deposit has been made.

Having total extent of 4.88.0 hectares are declared as Multistoried Building Area for construction of Educational Building
up to maximum height of 23.62 m as per Tamil Nadu multistoried and Public Building Rules, 1973 and instructions thereof.
In addition to that it is also requested to ensure the compliance of the following conditions at the time of clearing the
building plans.
CONDITIONS:
1. The Multistoried building for Educational Use should be constructed with the conditions that the building should satisfy
the parameters of Floor Space Index, Plot Coverage etc. The applicant should obtain necessary clearance and No Objection
Certificate (NOC) for the plans from the Authorities viz. Commissioner of Town and Country Planning, Highways Department,

Authority, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board and the applicant should obtain EIA clearance from the Ministry of Environment
and Forest Department, Government of India as per Central Government’s latest notification and submit it to the authority
concerned.
2. Multistoried Building should be earthquake resistive structure. The building shall be designed by the Registered
Professional Structural Engineer who shall also sign in all the plans.
3. The Multistoried Building should be designed based on NBC 2005 and an undertaking to this effect should be given

4. Ramp must be provided to lift room for the use of physically challenged persons.
5. Ramp ratio should be mentioned in the plan as 1:10.

8. Sufficient lifts must be provided as per Tamil Nadu Lifts Act and Rules 1997 and the building should satisfy all the
Multistoried and Public Building Rules 1973.
9. Fire Extinguishers should be provided wherever necessary and also no objection certificate must be obtained from
the Fire Service Authority.
10. Rain Water Harvesting must be provided as per the G.O.Ms.No. 138, MA & WS Department, dated 11th October
2002 and the arrangements should follow the contour at the site. Detailed Map should be produced.
11. Suitable arrangements should be made for tapping solar energy in the buildings, as per G.O.Ms.No. 112, MA & WS
Department, dated 16th August 2002.
12. FSI and plot coverage should be adopted as per rules and regulations and instructions thereof applicable for Multistoried
Building.
13. Height between each floor shall not be less than 3 m.
14. Open stair case for emergency escape should be provided in the building.
15. Expansion joints of 75 mm gap have to be provided in the building at 45 metres intervals.
16. No objection certificate from Highways and Rural Works Department about road widening, if any should be provided.
17. Necessary Lightning arresters should be provided.
18. The adequacy of STP has also to be certified by an authority for recycling of waste water.
19. As the site abuts National Highway, a green strip of 10' width and a service road of 30' width has to be provided
and shown in the drawing while applying for building plan approval.
20. The applicant should furnish the full detailed report about the water supply (both drinking and other purposes) and
necessary undertakings to obtain water from the Government desalination plants to provide sufficient water supply to the
consumers in the building with excessive extraction of more ground water in that area.
21. Affidavit regarding stability of the building which has to be duly signed by the Architect, owner and structural design
engineer in a Rs.100/- stamp paper on the following norms.
Structural designs are made after conducting due soil test of the site as per National Building Code 2005 and amendments

and we all are held responsible for the structural safety/stability.
