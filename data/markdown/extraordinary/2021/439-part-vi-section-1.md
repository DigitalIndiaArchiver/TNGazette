NOTICE UNDER SECTION 15(1) OF TAMIL NADU HIGHWAYS ACT, 2001 (TN ACT 34 OF 2002) READ WITH 
THE TAMIL NADU LAND ACQUISITION LAWS (REVIVAL OF OPERATION, AMENDMENT AND VALIDATION) 
ACT, 2019.
No. VI(1)/253(a-1)/2021.
The Government of Tamil Nadu having been satisﬁ ed that the lands speciﬁ ed in the schedule below are 
acquired under Chennai–Kanniyakumari Industrial Corridor Project for Highways purpose to wit for widening of SH-86, 
Omalur-Sankari-Tiruchengode-Paramathi Road from KM 5.78 to KM 9.00 at Sikkampatti (Part-1) Village, Omalur Taluk, 
Salem District, and it having already been decided that the entire amount of compensation to be awarded for the lands 
to be paid out of the funds controlled and managed by the Divisional Engineer(H), Salem (CKICP), Tamil Nadu after 
having considering the cause shown by the owner or other persons having interest on such lands as the case may 
be do hereby publish the following notice is issued under sub-section (1) of Section 15 of the Tamil Nadu Highways 
Act, 2001 (TN Act 34 of 2002).
NOTICE
Under sub-section (1) of Section 15 of the Tamil Nadu Highways Act, 2001 (Tamil Nadu Act 34 of 2002) read 
with the Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition Laws (Revival of Operation, Amendment and Validation) Act, 2019, the Governor 
of Tamil Nadu hereby notiﬁ ed that the Dry lands speciﬁ ed in the schedule below and measuring to an extent of 464 
sq.mtr. are acquired in Sikkampatti (Part-1) Village, Omalur Taluk, Salem District, to the same, a little more or less is 
needed for Chennai-Kanniyakumari Industrial Corridor Project for State Highways purpose, to wit for widening of SH-86, 
Omalur-Sankari-Tiruchengode-Paramathi Road from KM 5.78 to KM 9.00.
The plan of the lands under acquisition are kept in the Oﬃ  ce of the Special District Revenue Oﬃ  cer (Land 
Acquisition), Chennai-Kanniyakumari Industrial Corridor Project, Salem and oﬃ  ce of the Special Tahsildar (Land Acquisition), 
Chennai–Kanniyakumari Industrial Corridor Project, Sankari may be inspected at any time during the oﬃ  ce hours.
