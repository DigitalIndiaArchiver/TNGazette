Order : No. 1237, Home (Police XIX),
26th September 2008.
In the Government Order first above, Government have
issued orders for the Ad-interim attachment of properties
belonging to one of the partners of M/s. Thirumal Finance
Consultants, Madurai.
2. In the reference second read above, the Speicial Court
under Tamil Nadu Portection of Interests of Depositors (in
Financial Estabalishments) Act, 1997, in its Orders, dated
13th June 2007 in O.A.No. 84/2004 Filling by the Competent
Authority to make Ad-interim Order of Attachment effected
in G.O.Ms.No. 443, Home (Cts.IIA) Department, dated
27th May 2002 as absolute has dismissed the Petition as
not pressed, in view of the endorsement made by the
petitioner’s Counsel.
3. Consequently, the accused R.Pattabiraman, S/o
A.Ramachandran, Partner of M/s. Thirumal Finance
Consultants, Madurai has filed Writ Petition in W.P .No.,
17145 of 2008, in the High Court of Madras, The High Court
in its Order, dated 21st July 2008 in the said Writ Petition
has observed that the case of the Petitioner is that the entire
amounts payable to the depositors were already paid and
that necessary endorsement was made in O.A. No.84 of
2004 which was also brought to the Notice of that Court
and that the only claim made by the Petitioner is that inspite
of the fact that the entire amounts payable to the depositors
were already paid, the Order of Attachment passed by the
Government was not raised till date. It has further observed
that representation to that effect was sent to the respondents
1 and 2 on 25th June 2002 and prayed that Court to direct
the first respondent to pass Orders on the same.
Considering the said fact, the High Court  of Madras passed
Orders in the said Writ Petition No.17145 of 2008, dated
21st July 2008 that the Court is of the view that without going
into the merits of the Case, it is suffice to direct the first
respondent, that is Government, to dispose of the
representation), dated 25th June 2002 sent by the Petitioner
and accordingly the first Respondent is directed to dispose
of the same, on merits and in accordance with law.
4. The Competent Authority and District Revenue Officer,
Madurai in his letters fourth read above, has requested to
pass necessary Orders rasing the Order of Attachment. The
Inspector General of Police, Economic Offences Wing -II in
his letter fifth read above has also endorsed the proposal
of the Competent Authority and District Revenue Officer,
Madurai.
5. In compliance of the said Orders of the Special Court
in O.A.No. 84/2004, dated 13th June 2007, and the Orders
of the High Court of Madras in W.P.No.17145 of 2008, dated
21st July 2008 and in exercise of the powers conferred by
Section 3 of the Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of
Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997
(Tamil Nadu Act 44 of 1997), the Governor of Tamil Nadu
hereby raise the Order of Attachment issued in
G.O. Ms. No. 443, Home (Courts II-A) Department,
dated 27th May 2002.

COMMERCIAL TAXES AND REGISTRATION
DEPARTMENT
Notification under Indian Stamp Act
Reduction of Stamp Duty Chargeable in respect of
instruments of lease or sale of land executed in favour
of all manufacturing Industries or expansion units under
the Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 87, Commercial Taxes and Registration (J1),
30th September 2008.]
No. II(2)/CTR/497/2008.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by clause (a) of sub-section (1) of Section 9 of
the Indian Stamp Act, 1899 (Central Act II of 1899), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby reduces fifty per cent of the
duty chargeable under the said Act in respect of the
instruments of lease or sale of land, executed in favour of
all manufacturing industries setting up new or expansion
units with an investment in eligible fixed assets of more than
Rs. 5 crores in private industrial parks conforming to the
specifications mentioned in Annexure-A to the New Industrial
Policy 2007 and approved by the Government:
Provided that the value for the purpose of computation
of duty in respect of instruments of sale is the value set
forth in the instruments, and in respect of instruments of
lease, the consideration mentioned therein.
Reduction of Stamp Duty Chargeable in respect of
instruments of lease or sale of land and New ready
built factory buildings executed in favour of all
manufacturing units setup in State Industrial Promotion
Corporation of Tamil Nadu Industrial Parks under
the Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 87, Commercial Taxes and Registration (J1),
30th September 2008.]
No.II(2)/CTR/498/2008.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by clause (a) of sub-section (1) of Section 9 of
the Indian Stamp Act, 1899 (Central Act II of 1899), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby reduces fifty per cent of the
duty chargeable under the said Act in respect of the
following:—
(i) instrument of lease or sale of land irrespective
of location meant for industrial use executed in favour of
manufacturing units setup in State Industries Promotion
Corporation of Tamil Nadu Industrial Parks; and
(ii) Instruments of lease of new ready built factory
buildings irrespective of location executed in favour of
manufacturing units setup in State Industries Promotion
Corporation of Tamil Nadu Industrial Parks:
Provided that the value for the purpose of computation
of duty in respect of instruments of sale is the value set
forth in the instruments and in respect of instruments of
lease, the consideration mentioned therein.
RAJEEV RANJAN,
Secretary to Government.

No. II(2)/FIN/499/2008.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by under clause (1)  of Article 293 of the Constitution
of India, the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby guarantees
unconditionally and irrevocably the repayment of principal  to

principal is redeemed fully from the date of issue of bounds.

HOUSING AND URBAN DEVELOPMENT DEPARTMENT
Variations to the Master Plan for the Tirupur Local
Planning Area under Tamil Nadu Town and Country
Planning Act.
[G.O. (2D) No. 1246, Housing and Urban Development
(UD4-1), 10th October 2008.]
No. II(2)/HOU/500/2008.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by sub-section (4) of Section 32 of the Tamil Nadu
Town and Country Planning Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act 35
of 1972), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the
following Variations to the Master Plan for the Tirupur Local

Notification No. II(2)/HOU/573/2006, at page 375 of Part II—

under the heading “Pudhu Palayam Village”, in the tabular
