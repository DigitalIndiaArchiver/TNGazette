TAMIL NADU ORDINANCE No. 2 OF 2012.
An Ordinance further to amend the Tamil Nadu Transparency in
Tenders Act, 1998.
Whereas, the Legislative Assembly of the State is not in session and the
Governor of Tamil Nadu is satisfied that circumstances exist which render
it necessary for him to take immediate action for the purpose hereinafter
appearing;
Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred by clause (1) of
Article 213 of the Constitution, the Governor hereby promulgates the following
Ordinance:—
1.
(1) This Ordinance may be called the Tamil Nadu Transparency in
Tenders (Amendment) Ordinance, 2012.
(2) It shall be deemed to have come into force on the 1st day of
October 2011.
2.
In section 16 of the Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders Act, 1998
(hereinafter referred to as the principal Act), in clause (c), for the words
“eleven years”, the words “sixteen years” shall be substituted.
3. Notwithstanding anything contained in the principal Act, all things done
or actions taken by any officer or authority, on or after the 1st day of
October 2011 and before the 22nd November 2011,  which are in conformity

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT
According to clause (c) of section 16 of the Tamil Nadu Transparency in
Tenders Act 1998 (Tamil Nadu Act 43 of 1998), as amended by Tamil Nadu
Act 28 of 2006, the provisions of sections 9 and 10 of the said Act, relating
to invitation and evaluation and acceptance of tender by the Tender Inviting
Authority, shall not apply to procurement from certain departments of
Government, public sector undertakings, statutory boards and such other
institutions in respect of goods manufactured or services provided by them
for a period not exceeding eleven years from the date of commencement of
the said Tamil Nadu Act 43 of 1998, namely, the 1st October 2000. With
a view to encourage the said departments, public sector undertakings, statutory
boards and such other institutions to become viable and competitive in the
purchase process, the Government have decided to extend the exemption
provided to such Government Departments, public sector undertakings, etc.,
under clause (c) of section 16 of the said Tamil Nadu Act 43 of 1998 for
a further period of five years on and from the 1st October 2011 and to amend
the said clause (c) of section 16 of the said Act for the purpose. Accordingly,
the Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders (Amendment) Ordinance, 2011
(Tamil Nadu Ordinance 5 of 2011) was promulgated by the Governor on the
20th November 2011 and the same was published in the Tamil Nadu Government
Gazette Extraordinary, dated the 22nd November 2011.
2.
After the promulgation of the said Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders
(Amendment) Ordinance, 2011 (Tamil Nadu Ordinance 5 of 2011), the Legislative
Assembly of the State re-assembled on the 15th December 2011. On that
day, no other business, except passing a resolution on Mullai Periyar Dam
was transacted in the Legislative Assembly. Therefore, the Bill to replace the
Ordinance was not introduced in the Legislative Assembly on the
15th December 2011. The six weeks time limit specified in clause (2) of Article
213 of the Constitution starts from the 15th December 2011. As a result, the
Ordinance would cease to operate on the 25th January 2012, on which date
the six weeks period expires. Hence, it has been decided to promulgate again
an Ordinance, to extend the exemption provided to such Government Departments,
public sector undertakings, etc., under clause (c) of section 16 of the said
Tamil Nadu Act 43 of 1998 for a further period of five years on and from
the 1st October 2011 and to amend the said clause (c) of section 16 of the
said Act for the purpose.
3.
The Ordinance seeks to give effect to the above decision.
