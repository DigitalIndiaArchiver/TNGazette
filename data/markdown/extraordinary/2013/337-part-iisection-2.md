In order to strengthen the fire services in  the Municipal Corporations with a populations of more than one million the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby notifies the following “Fire Hazard Response and Mitigation Plans” in respect of Chennai,
Coimbatore and Madurai City Municipal Corporations.
“Fire Hazard Response and Mitigation Plans”
Under 13th Central Finance Commission General Performance Grant, for the year 2011-12 and 2012-13 only a sum
of Rs.35.30 crores has been released by Government of India to all Corporations and Municipalities except Town Panchayats
and out of which a sum of Rs.13.11 crores is ear-marked for Chennai, Coimbatore and Madurai Corporations. Further, the
tentative release of 13th Central Finance Commission General Performance Grant to the above Municipal Corporations for
the year 2013-14 and 2014-15 would be Rs.10.07 crores and Rs.11.87 crores respectively.

   The Government of Tamil Nadu have decided to allocate 10% of the performance grant tentatively to Fire and Rescue
Services Department towards purchase of vehicles/equipments for revamping the Fire and Rescue Services in Chennai, Coimbatore
and Madurai Municipal Corporation  areas for the years 2011-2012 , 2012-2013 , 2013-14 and 2014-15 under 13th Central Finance
Commission General Performance Grant, which works out to Rs.350.50  lakhs as mentioned below:-
Funds transfer:
The Urban Local Bodies shall transfer the amounts as mentioned above from the grant already released under 13th Central
Finance Commission for the year 2011-12 and 2012-13 to the Director, Fire and Rescue Services Department. Further,  on receipt
of General  performance Grants under 13th Central Finance Commission for the year 2013-14 and 2014-15, the above said Urban
Local Bodies shall transfer its proportionate share  of the grant received to the Director, Fire and Rescue Services Department
for revamping the Fire and Rescue Services in the above said Municipal Corporation areas.
Revamping the Fire and Rescue Services in Chennai, Coimbatore and Madurai Municipal Corporation areas:
The list of fire fighting/rescue vehicles and equipments available with Fire and Rescue Services  Department in Chennai City,
Coimbatore and Madurai Municipal Corporation areas and the flow chart in responding the calls to render service to the public in
these areas are shown in the appendix.   The following items of vehicles and equipment will be purchased by the Fire and Rescue
Services Department by availing 10% of the General Performance Grants for revamping the Fire and Rescue Services in Chennai
Metropolitan, Coimbatore and Madurai  Municipal Corporation areas in the State. The Director, Fire and Rescue Services shall
initiate action well in advance to follow the procedure for procurement of the items so as to procure the items on receipt of the
funds through Local Bodies and to furnish the utilization certificate immediately.
The ADGP/Director ,Fire and Rescue Services Department has sent  the proposal towards purchase of vehicles/equipments
for revamping the Fire and Rescue Services in above cities a tune of Rs. 328.40. lakhs.
The Government have decided to earmark Rs.350.50 lakhs to Fire and Rescue Department from the General Performance
grant under 13th  Finance Commission  recommendation to Urban Local Bodies for purchase of vehicles/equipments to revamping
Fire and Rescue Services in Chennai, Coimbatore and Madurai Corporations for the years 2011-12, 2012-13, 2013-14 and
2014-15.  The Government also accept the proposal of  ADGP/Director ,Fire and Rescue Services Department towards purchase
of vehicles/equipments for revamping the Fire and Rescue Services in above cities to a tune of Rs. 328.40. lakhs. The  balance
amount of Rs. 22.10 lakhs  may be utilised for purchase other vehicles/equipments needed for that department for Chennai   -
Rs.15.90 lakhs, Coimbatore - Rs.3.20 lakhs and Madurai - Rs. 3.00 lakhs.
