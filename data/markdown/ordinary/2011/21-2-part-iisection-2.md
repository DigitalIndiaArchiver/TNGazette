HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE DEPARTMENT
Appointment of Drugs Inspector under Drugs and
Cosmetics Act.
[G.O. (D) No. 495, Health and Family Welfare (M1),
13th May 2011, Chithirai 30, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2042.]
No. II(2)/HF/246/2011.—In exercise of the powers conferred
by sub-sections (1) and (4) of Section 21 of the Drugs and
Cosmetics Act, 1940 (Central Act XXIII of 1940), the Governor
of Tamil Nadu hereby appoints Selvi G. Sobana as Inspector
for the purpose of the said Act for the entire State of
Tamil Nadu and specifies the Director of Drugs Control,
Tamil Nadu, as the authority to whom the said Inspector shall
be officially subordinate.
V.K. SUBBURAJ,
Principal Secretary to Government.

No. II(2)/PLAR/250/2011.—Under the Rules governing the
Governor’s Vigilance Medal issued in G.O.Ms.No. 249,
Personnel and Administrative Reforms (N) Department, dated
25th February 1977, as amended  subsequently, the Governor
of Tamil Nadu is pleased to award the Governor’s Vigilance,
Medal  for the years 2008 and 2009 to the following six
Investigating Officers of the Directorate of Vigilance and Anti-
Corruption for their meritorious service and the names of
those officers are hereby published in the Tamil Nadu
Government Gazette as required under rule 15 of the
said Rules:—
1. For the year 2008:
(i) Thiru R. Pandiarajan, Inspector of Police , Vigilance
and Anti-Corruption, Sivagangai.
(ii) Thiru A. Ulaganathan, Inspector of Police, Vigilance
and Anti-Corruption, Thanjavur (now transferred from Director
of Vigilance and Anti-Corruption) and
(iii) Thiru R. Krishnasamy, Inspector of Police, Vigilance
and Anti-Corruption, CSU-3, Chennai.
2. For the year 2009:
(i) Thiru A. Periyasamy, Deputy Superintendent of
Police Vigilance and Anti-Corruption, Namakkal.
(ii) Thiru V. Sathish, Inspector of Police, Vigilance and
Anti-Corruption, Krishnagiri and
(iii) Thiru R. Murali, Deputy Superintendent of Police,
Special Investigation Cell, Vigilance and Anti-Corruption,
Chennai.

RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND PANCHAYAT RAJ
DEPARTMENT
Notifications under Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act
Disqualification of certain Ward Member of Moonchirai
Village Panchayat, Moonchirai Panchayat Union,
Kanniyakumari District under the Act.
[G.O (D) No. 198, Rural Development and Panchayat Raj
(P.R.IV), 18th May 2011.]
No. II(2)/RDPR/251/2011.—Whereas, a question has arisen
as to whether Thiru K.S. Sivakumar, 4th Ward Member,
Moonchirai Village Panchayat, Moonchirai Panchayat Union,
Kanniyakumari District has become disqualified under
clause (i) of sub-section (3) of Section 38 of the Tamil Nadu
Panchayats Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 21 of 1994), as he
has absented himself from three consecutive meetings of the
said Village Panchayat;
And, whereas, the opinion of the Tamil Nadu State Election
Commission has been sought for regarding the disqualification
of the said member of the said Panchayat, as required under
sub-section (2) of Section 41 of the said Act;
And, whereas, the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission,
after conducting an enquiry, has stated that on verification
of the relevant records it has been confirmed that, Thiru
K.S. Sivakumar, 4th Ward Member of the said Village
Panchayat, has not attended more than three consecutive
meetings of the said Village Panchayat held on and after
26th September 2008 and has also opined that the said
Thiru K.S. Sivakumar, 4th Ward Member, Moonchirai Village
Panchayat, Moonchirai Panchayat Union, Kanniyakumari
District is disqualified to continue as member of the
said Moonchirai Village Panchayat, under clause (i) of
sub-section (3) of Section 38 of the said Act;
Now, therefore, under sub-section (1) of Section 41 of
the Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 21
of 1994), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby decides that
Thiru K.S. Sivakumar, 4th Ward Member, Moonchirai Village
Panchayat, Moonchirai Panchayat Union, Kanniyakumari
District, has become disqualified from holding the office of
Member of the said Moonchirai Village Panchayat, under

Disqualification of certain Ward Member of Aathivilai
Village Panchayat, Thakkalai Panchayat Union,
Kanniyakumari District under the Act.
[G.O. (D) No. 199, Rural Development and Panchayat Raj
(P.R.IV), 18th May 2011.]
No. II(2)/RDPR/252/2011.—Whereas, a question has arisen
as to whether Thiru T. Nagarajan, 3rd Ward Member, Aathivilai
Village Panchayat, Thakkalai Panchayat  Union, Kanniyakumari
District has become disqualified under clause (i) of
sub-section (3) of Section 38 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats
Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 21 of 1994), as he has absented
himself from three consecutive meetings of the said Village
Panchayat;
And, whereas, the opinion of the Tamil Nadu State Election
Commission has been sought for regarding the disqualification
of the said member of the said Panchayat, as required under
sub-section (2) of Section 41 of the said Act;
And, whereas, the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission,
after conducting an enquiry, has stated that on verification
of the relevant records it has been confirmed that, Thiru
T. Nagarajan, 3rd Ward Member of the said Village Panchayat,
has not attended more than three consecutive meetings of
the said Village Panchayat held on and after 20th November
2009 since he has gone to foreign country for employment
and has also opined that the said Thiru T. Nagarajan,
3rd Ward Member, Aathivilai Village Panchayat, Thakkalai
Panchayat Union, Kanniyakumari District is disqualified to
continue as member of the said Aathivilai Village Panchayat,
under clause (i) of sub-section (3) of Section 38
of the said Act.
Now, therefore, under sub-section (1) of Section 41 of
the Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 21
of 1994), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby decides that
Thiru T. Nagarajan, 3rd Ward Member, Aathivilai Village
Panchayat, Thakkalai Panchayat Union, Kanniyakumari District,
has become disqualified from holding the office of Member
of the said Aathivilai Village Panchayat, under clause (i) of
sub-section (3) of Section 38 of the said Act, as he has
absented himself from three consecutive meetings of the said
Village Panchayat held on and after 20th November 2009.
Disqualification of certain Ward Member of Kottaipatti
Village Panchayat, Vempakottai Panchayat Union,
Virudhunagar District under the Act.
[G.O (D) No. 200, Rural Development and Panchayat Raj
(P.R.IV), 18th May 2011.]
No. II(2)/RDPR/253/2011.—Whereas, a question has arisen
as to whether Thirumathi R. Kaleeswari, 1st Ward Member,
Kottaipatti Village Panchayat, Vempakottai  Panchayat  Union,
Virudhunagar District has become disqualified under
clause (i) of sub-section (3) of Section 38 of the Tamil Nadu
Panchayats Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 21 of 1994), as he
has absented herself from three consecutive meetings of the
said Village Panchayat;
And, whereas, the opinion of the Tamil Nadu State Election
Commission has been sought for regarding the disqualification
of the said Member of the said Panchayat, as required under
the sub-section (2) of Section 41 of the said Act;
And, whereas, the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission,
after conducting an enquiry, has stated that on verification
of the relevant records, it has been confirmed that, Thirumathi
R. Kaleeswari, 1st Ward Member of the said Village Panchayat,
has not attended more than three consecutive meetings of
the said Village Panchayat held on and after 6th August,
2009 and has also opined that the said Thirumathi
R. Kaleeswari, 1st Ward Member, Kottaipatti Village Panchayat,
Vempakottai Panchayat Union, Virudhunagar District is
disqualified to continue as member of the said Kottapatti
Village Panchayat, under cluse (i) of the sub-section (3) of
Section 38 of the said Act.
Now, therefore, under sub-section (1) of Section 41 of
the Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 21
of 1994), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby decides that
Thirumathi R. Kaleeswari, 1st Ward Member, Kottipatti Village
Panchayat, Vempakottai Panchayat Union, Virudhunagar
District, has become disqualified from holding the office of
Member of the said Kottapatti Village Panchayat, under
clause (i) of sub-section (3) of Section 38 of the said Act,
as she has absented herself from three consecutive meetings
of the said Village Panchayat held on and after
6th August, 2009.
K. ALLAUDIN,
Principal Secretary to Government.
