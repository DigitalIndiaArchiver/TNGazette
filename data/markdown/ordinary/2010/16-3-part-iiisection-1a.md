NOTIFICATIONS  BY  GOVERNMENT
HOME DEPARTMENT
Draft Amendments to the Tamil Nadu Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989
[G.O. Ms. No. 364, Home (Tr. VII), 9th April 2010.]
No. SRO A-14/2010.—The following draft of amendments to the Tamil Nadu Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, which it is
proposed to make in exercise of the powers conferred by Section 96 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988
(Central Act 59 of 1988) is hereby published for the information of all persons likely to be affected thereby, as required
by sub-section (1) of Section 212 of the said Act.
2. Notice is hereby given that the draft amendment will be taken into consideration on or after the expiry of thirty days
from the date of publication of this Notification in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette and that any objection or
suggestion, which may be received from any person with respect thereto before the expiry of the aforesaid period will
be considered by the Government of Tamil Nadu.
3. Objection or suggestion, if any, should be addressed in duplicate to the Principal Secretary to Government, Home
Department, Chennai-600 009.
DRAFT AMENDMENTS
In the said Rules:—
1. in rule 172, after sub-rule (10), the following sub-rule shall be added, namely:—
“(11) It shall be a condition of permit of every transport vehicle that the vehicle shall not carry or display
advertisements in contravention of rule 343”; and
2. In rule 343, in sub-rule (a), for the third proviso, the following provisos shall be substituted, namely:—
“Provided also that in the case of stage carriage, no advertisement shall be carried or displayed on the front
wind screen or rear window or side windows of the vehicle and if there are glass panels above the
windows on the sides of the vehicle, advertisements may be displayed on such panels”.
“Provided also that the advertisements shall not be offensive, obscene or cause for major distraction to the
road users”.
EXPLANATORY NOTE
As per the third proviso to rule 343 (a) of the Tamil Nadu Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, in the case of stage carriages,
no advertisement shall be carried except inside the vehicle and rear portion of the exterior body. As the rule stands
today, advertisements on the side portion of the body are not permitted. In W.P. No. 6818/09, dated 23rd November 2009
seeking direction against violation of the above rule, the High Court of Madras has directed the State Government to
consider amending rule 343 of the said rules, as suggested by the State in their counter affidavit before the High Court.
Considering the road safety and security of the passengers, it has been proposed not to allow advertisements in the front
wind screen or rear window or side window of the Stage carriages. However, it has also been proposed to permit display
of advertisements on the glass panels above the windows on the sides of the vehicle. It has also been proposed to
prescribe a condition on the permit of a transport vehicle that it should not carry or display advertisements in contravention
of rule 343 of the said Rules. The draft amendment provides for this.
