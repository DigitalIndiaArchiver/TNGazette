Draft Amendment to the Tamil Nadu Mediation Rules, 2010
(Roc. No. 4711-A/2013/F1.)
No. SRO C-3/2016.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 122 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (Central Act V of 1908)
and clause (d) of sub-section (2) of Section 89 of the said Code, the High Court of Madras hereby proposes the
following amendment to the Tamil Nadu Mediation Rules, 2010, as amended by Amendment to the said Rules made
vide High Court's Roc. No. 4711-A/2013/F1 and the same is hereby published as required by Section 122 of the Code for
public information.
The existing sub-rule (1)(b) of Rule 5 of the Tamil Nadu Mediation Rules, 2010 shall be substituted as follows:—
“(b) Legal Practitioners with atleast 10 years' standing at the Bar at the level of the Supreme Court or the High Court
or the District Court of equivalent status."
It is further notified that any suggestion or objection to the abovesaid draft amendment may be forwarded to the
undersigned within 30 days of the initial publication of this amendment.
High Court, Madras,
P. KALAIYARASAN,
3rd February 2016.
Registrar-General.

INTRODUCTION OF ac 25 KV TRACTION
(Pro forma 10-02)
“WARNING TO ROAD USERS”
No. SRO C-5/2016.
It is notified for information of the public that in connection with introduction of 25 KV ac electric traction over the
newly laid double line track between Vriddhachalam and Ariyalur stations, from OHE Mast location No.
218/33
(km/ch:218/807.85) to OHE Mast location No. 270/7 (km/ch:270/140.80) on Villupuram-Tiruchchirappalli section in
Tiruchchirappalli division of Southern Railway, height gauges have been erected at all the level crossings with clear height
of 4.67 m above road level with a view to prevent loads of excessive height from coming into contact or dangerous proximity
to live traction wires.
Public are hereby notified to observe the height specified above for the purpose of loading vehicles and to see that the
loads carried in road vehicles do not infringe the height gauges under any circumstances.
The dangers of a load of excessive height are as follows:-
(i)   Danger to the height gauge and consequent obstruction to the road as well as the railway line.
(ii)  Danger to the materials or equipment carried or for the vehicle itself.
(iii)  Danger of fire and risk of life due to contact with or dangerous proximity to the conductors.

NOTIFICATION
(u/s 373 of the C.C.M.C. Act 1919)
(R.D.C.No. TL/603/2015)
Considering the representation of the Madras Pharmaceutical Wholesaler Association, Chennai-3 towards their
participation in health services and the growth in modernized surgical equipments, it was felt to cover the trade of Selling
of Surgical Equipments in the licencing schedule of the Chennai Corporation u/s 287 of the Chennai City
Municipal Corporation Act, 1919.
Accordingly, the Chennai Corporation Council in Resolution No.555/20l5, dated.30.09.2015 has resolved to include it in
its licencing schedule and levy licence fee for the  trade of Selling of Surgical Equipment from wholesale and retail vendors.
Henceforth, the trade Selling of Surgical Equipments wholesale and retail is included in  the licencing Schedule - I,
Sl.No.83 with the exisiting annual fee structure as for other trades
i.e. (a) Rs.500/- for 500 sq.ft., (b) Rs.750/- for 501 to
1000 sq.ft., (c) Rs.5000/- for above 1000 sq.ft. and the issue of trade licence will commence with effect from 15-11-2015
(other fee / charges wherever applicable).
The above notice issued u/s 98A(1)(2) of the Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919.
