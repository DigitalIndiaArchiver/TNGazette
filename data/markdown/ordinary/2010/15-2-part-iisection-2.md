LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT DEPARTMENT
Exemption to the Madras Craft Foundation, Chennai for
certain period under Employees’ State Insurance Act.
[G.O. (D) No. 171, Labour and Employment (L1), 26th
March 2010, Panguni 12, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2041.]
No. II(2)/LE/216/2010.—In exercise of the powers conferred
by Section 88 read with Section 91-A of the Employees’ State
Insurance Act, 1948 (Central Act XXXIV of 1948), the Governor
of Tamil Nadu hereby exempts the employees working in
Dakshina Chitra, Muttukadu of Madras Craft Foundation,
Chennai from the provisions of the said Act for the period
from 1st January 2005 to 31st December 2006.
2. The above exemption is subject to the following
conditions namely:—
(a) The aforesaid Institution wherein the employees
are employed shall maintain a Register showing the names
and designations of the exempted employees;
(b) Notwithstanding this exemption, the employees
shall continue to receive such benefits under the said Act to
which they might have become entitled to on the basis of the
contributions paid prior to the date from which exemption
granted by this Notification operates.
3. The employer of the said institution shall submit in
respect of the period during which that Institution was subject

Census of India 2011-Circular No. 14
Subject.—Responsibilities and duties of the public
under the Census Act, 1948 and Issue of Notification by
the State Government/UT Administrations directing the
public to co-operate in smooth conduct of Census of
India 2011.
The Census of India is the largest administrative exercise
carried out in the country that provides data on various socio-
economic parameters and demographic profile of the entire
country’s population. It is undertaken as per the Census Act,
1948 (37 of 1948) and Census Rules, 1990 and amendments
made thereunder. The preparations for conduct of 2011
Census are in full swing. The 1st Phase of Census 2011
i.e. Houselisting & Housing Census is scheduled from 1st
April 2010 to September 2011 in all State/Uts depending
upon their local conditions. The details required for the
preparation of the National Population Register will also be
canvassed during this phase. The second phase of Census
Operations would be conducted from 9th February to
5th March 2011.
2.
Further under the Census Act, 1948 provisions have
been made in Section 11 of Census Act, 1948 imposing
penalities on the erring public. The important sections of the
Act are reproduced below for ready reference and for due
notification by the State Government.
(i)
Section 8(1) A census officer may ask all such
questions of all presons within the limits of the local area
for which he is appointed as, by instructions issued in this
behalf by the Central Government and published in the
Official Gazette, he may be directed to ask.
(2) Every person of whom any question is asked
under sub-section (1) shall be legally bound to answer such
question to the best of his knowledge or belief:
Provided that no person shall be bound to state the name
of any female member of his household and no woman shall
be bound to state the name of her husband or deceased
husband or of any other person whose name she is forbidden
by custom to mention.
(ii) Section 9 Every person occupying any house,
enclosure, vessel or other place shall allow census officer
such access thereto as they may require for the purposes
of the census and as, having regard to the customs of the
country, may be reasonable, and shall allow them to paint
on, or affix to, the place such letters, mark or numbers as
may be necessary for the purposes of the census.
(iii) Section 10(1) Subject to such orders as the
Census Commissioner may issue in this behalf, a census-
officer may, within the local area for which he is appointed,
leave or cause to be left a schedule at any dwelling house
or with the manager or any officer of any commercial or
industrial establishment, for the purpose of its being filled
up by the occupier of such house or of any specified part
thereof or by such manager or officer with such particulars
as the Census Commissioner may direct regarding the inmates
of such house or part thereof, or the persons employed under
such manager or officer, as the case may be, at the time
of the taking of the census.
(2) When such schedule has been so left, the
said occupier, manager or officer, as the case may be, shall

Order:—No. 79, School Education (U1), 24th March 2010.
In the G.O. first read above, guidelines were issued by the
Government pertaining to grant of affiliation, staff list approval
and admission of students in Private Teacher Training
Institutes, recognized by the Southern Regional Committee,
National Council for Teacher Education, from the academic
year 2008-2009. The Clause (VI) - (VISITS TO INSTITUTES)
of the G.O. stipulated that the Principals of District Institutes
of Education and Training shall be competent, besides the
Director and any other authority authorized by him in that
behalf to make visit to the self financing Private Teacher
Training Institutes, to ensure the staff position and conduct of
classes.
2. The Hon'ble Supreme Court of India in its judgement
dated 13th April 2009 in the Writ Petition (Civil) No. 483/2004
has directed as follows:—
(i) Before granting recognition or affiliation, the
concerned State Governments and Union Territories are
directed to ensure that the buildings are safe and secured
from every angle and they are constructed according to the
safety norms incorporated in the National Building Code of
India.
(ii) All existing Government and private schools shall
install fire extinguishing equipments within a period of six
months.
(iii) The School buildings be kept free from inflammable
and toxic material. If storage is inevitable, they should be
stored safely.
(iv) Evaluation of structural aspect of the school may
be carried out periodically. We direct that the concerned
engineers and officials must strictly follow the National Building
Code. The safety certificate be issued only after proper
inspection. Dereliction in duty must attract immediate
disciplinary action against the concerned officials.

(v) Necessary training be imparted to the staff and
other officials of the school to use the fire extinguishing
equipments.
3. In the G.O. 2nd read above, the directions of the
Supreme Court of India has been communicated to the Director
of School Education I Director of Elementary Education /
Director of Matriculation Schools I Director of Teacher
Education Research and Training to take action to adhere the
directions of the Supreme Court.
4. Based on the orders issued in the G.O 2nd read above,
the Director of Teacher Education Research and Training has
submitted proposals to incorporate the directions of the
Supreme Court of India in the G.O. 1st read above.
5. After careful examination of the proposal of the Director
of Teacher Education Research and Training, the Government
incorporate the following conditions in clause (VI) -VISITS TO
INSTITUTES after sub-clause (b) in the G.O. 1st read above:-
(c) that it shall be ensured that the buildings are safe and
secure from every angle and they are constructed according
to the safety norms incorporated in the National Building
Code of India.
(d) that all existing private Teacher Training Institutes
shall install fire extinguishing equipments within a period of
six months.
(e) that the institute building be kept free from inflammable
and toxic material. If storage is inevitable, they should be
stored safely.
