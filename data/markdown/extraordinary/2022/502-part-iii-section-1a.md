The following draft of the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Rules, 2022, which the State Government 
proposes to make, in exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of section 22 of the Transgender 
Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019 (Central Act 40 of 2019), is hereby published for information of all 
persons likely to be aﬀ ected thereby, as required by sub-section (1) of the said section 22 of the said Act.
2. Notice is hereby given that the draft Rules will be taken into consideration on 
or after the expiry of thirty days from the date of publication of this Notification in the 
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette and that any objection or suggestion, which may be received from any 
person with respect thereto, before the expiry of the aforesaid period of thirty days will be considered by the 
Government of Tamil Nadu. Objection or suggestion, if any, should be addressed to the Additional Chief Secretary 
to Government, Social Welfare and Women Empowerment Department, Secretariat, Chennai–600 009 through 
the Director of Social Welfare, Chennai–600 015.

1. Short title.- These rules may be called the Tamil Nadu Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) 
Rules, 2022.
2. Deﬁ nition.- In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires,-
(a) “Act” means the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019 (Central Act 40 of 2019);
(b) “applicant” means a transgender person who submits an application under rule 3;

(c) “application” means the application form as provided in Form–I;
(d) “Government” means the State Government;
(e) oﬃ  cial documents” shall include the documents listed in Annexure II;
(f) certiﬁ cate of identity” means a certiﬁ cate issued by the District Collector under section 6 or section 7 of the Act as 
in Form–III or Form–IV respectively;
(g) “Form” means a Form annexed to these rules in Annexure I;
(h) "identity card” means a photo identity card issued in Form – V to a transgender person under section 6 or issued 
in Form – VI to a transgender person on change of gender under section 7 on the basis of “certiﬁ cate of identity” issued by 
the District Collector;
 (i) "medical institution” means any medical institution whether hospital or clinic, private or public, in rural areas or urban 
or overseas;
(j) "medical intervention” includes any gender aﬃ  rming medical intervention under taken by an individual to facilitate the 
transition to their self-identiﬁ ed gender, including but not limited to counseling, hormonal therapy, and surgical intervention, if any;
(k) section” means a section of the Act;
(l) Welfare Board” means the Tamil Nadu Transgender Welfare Board, a society registered under the Tamil Nadu Societies 
Registration Act, 1975 [Tamil Nadu Act 27 of 1975];
(m) words and expressions used herein but not deﬁ ned and deﬁ ned in the Act shall have the same meaning assigned 
to them in the Act.
 
3. Application for issue of certiﬁ cate of identity under section 6 or section 7.- (1) Any transgender person desirous 
of obtaining a certiﬁ cate of identity shall make an application in Form–I.
(2) The application shall be submitted to the District Collector through “Thirunangai Mobile Application”:
Provided that the Government may undertake measures, as it deems appropriate, to facilitate the submission of applications 
for transgender persons living in remote areas or disadvantaged conditions:
Provided further that in case of a minor child, such application shall be made by a parent or guardian of such minor 
child and in the case of a child in need of care and protection, by the competent authority under the Juvenile Justice (Care 
and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 (Central Act 2 of 2016).
(3) Notwithstanding anything contained in these rules Transgender persons who have oﬃ  cially recorded their change in 
gender, whether as male, female or transgender, prior to the coming into force of the Act shall not be required to submit an 
application for certiﬁ cate of identity under these rules and all such persons shall enjoy all rights and entitlements conferred 
on transgender persons under the Act:
Provided that any such person may submit application for issue of Certiﬁ cate of identity.
4. Procedure for issue of certiﬁ cate of identity.-The District Collector shall, subject to the correctness of the applicant’s 
particulars, get the application processed based on the aﬃ  davit in Non-Judicial stamp paper of Rs.10/- attested by Notary 
with seal, submitted declaring the gender identity of the person in Form II, with out any medical or physical examination and 
there after issue an identiﬁ cation number to the applicant, which may be quoted as proof of application.
5. Issue of certiﬁ cate of identity for a transgender person under section 6.- (1) The District Collector shall issue a certiﬁ cate 
of identity in Form-III to the applicant by following the procedure provided in rule 4 indicating the gender of such person.
(2) The certiﬁ cate of identity shall be issued within thirty days from the date of receipt of duly ﬁ lled in application along 
with the aﬃ  davit.
(3) The certiﬁ cate of identity issued under sub-rule (1), shall be the basis to change the gender as well as the name 
and the photograph, if so necessitated, of the transgender person in all such oﬃ  cial documents listed in Annexure II, in 
accordance with the gender speciﬁ ed in the said certiﬁ cate of identity.
(4) The District Collector shall, at the time of issuance of the certiﬁ cate of identity under sub-rule (1), issue a transgender 
identity card in Form–V to the applicant.
(5) The Government shall maintain a register for the issuance of certiﬁ cate of identity and the transgender identity card.
(6) The authority who issued the oﬃ  cial document, on an application made by an applicant under rule 3, shall change 
the name or gender or photograph or any of this information of the applicant in the oﬃ  cial documents within ﬁ fteen days of 
making of such application.

(7) Any oﬃ  cial document wherein gender, name or the photograph of transgender is revised based on the said certiﬁ cate 
of identity, shall bear the same serial or reference number as in the original oﬃ  cial document.
6. Procedure for issue of a certiﬁ cate of identity for change of gender.- (1) If a transgender person undergoes medical 
intervention towards a gender aﬃ  rming procedure, either as a male or female, such person may apply in Form – I, along with 
a certiﬁ cate issued to that eﬀ ect by the Medical Superintendent or Chief Medical Oﬃ  cer of the medical institution in which that 
person has undergone the said medical intervention, to the District Collector for the issue of a revised certiﬁ cate of identity.
(2) The District Collector shall, on receipt of an application referred to in sub-rule (1), shall verify the genuineness of 
the said medical certiﬁ cate, which shall not include any physical examination.
(3) The applicant shall be currently residing in the area under the jurisdiction of the District Collector as on the date of 
application and an aﬃ  davit to this eﬀ ect shall be submitted along with the application in Form-I and no additional evidence 
shall be called for.
7. Issue of certiﬁ cate of identity under section 7.- (1) The District Collector shall issue a certiﬁ cate of identity in Form – IV 
to the applicant seeking change in gender, indicating the gender of such a person as male or female, as the case may be.
(2) The District Collector shall issue the certiﬁ cate under sub-rule (1) within ﬁ fteen days from the date of its receipt of 
the application.
(3) The certiﬁ cate of identity issued under sub-rule (1), shall entitle the applicant to record or change the gender, as well 
as the photograph and name, if so necessitated, of transgender person in all such oﬃ  cial documents listed in the Annexure-II, 
in accordance with the gender speciﬁ ed in the said certiﬁ cate of identity as male or female, as the case may be.
(4) The District Collector while issuing the certiﬁ cate of identity for change of gender shall simultaneously issue an 
identity card in Form-VI to the applicant.
(5) The authority who issued the oﬃ  cial document, on an application made by an applicant under sub-rule (3), shall 
change the name or gender or photograph or any of this information of the applicant in the oﬃ  cial documents within ﬁ fteen 
days of making of such application.
(6) Any oﬃ  cial document wherein gender, name or photograph of transgender person is revised based on the said 
certiﬁ cate of identity, shall bear the same serial or reference number as in the original oﬃ  cial document.
8. Communication of rejection of application.- (1) In case of rejection of application made under rule 3, the District 
Collector shall inform the applicant the reason for such rejection within thirty days from the date of receipt of such application.
(2) The District Collector may within sixty days from the date of such rejection, review the decision of rejection 
of the application based on the reply submitted by the applicant regarding the reason for rejection communicated in 
sub-rule (1).
9. Right to appeal.- (1) The Director of Social Welfare is designated as the Appellate Authority. The applicant shall have 
a right to appeal, within ninety days from the date of intimation of the rejection of the application, to the Appellate Authority.
(2) The Appellate Authority may entertain an appeal after the expiry of the period speciﬁ ed above, if it is satisﬁ ed that 
the applicant was prevented by suﬃ  cient cause from preferring an appeal in time.
10. Welfare measures for transgender persons.- (1) The Government shall formulate welfare schemes to protect the rights 
and interests of transgender persons and facilitate their access to the welfare schemes through the Transgender Welfare Board.
(2) The Government shall create institutional and infrastructure facilities, rehabilitation centre temporary shelters, short 
stay homes and accommodation, separate wards in hospitals and wash rooms in establishments, within two years from the 
date of coming into force of these rules, to protect the rights of transgender persons.
(3) Every establishment shall provide training and education to stakeholders in developing appropriate change in 
behaviour towards transgender persons.
(4) All educational institutions shall have a committee which shall be accessible for transgender persons in case of any 
harassment or discrimination, with powers to ensure that transgender students do not have to be aﬀ ected by the presence 
of the persons bullying them, including teachers.
11. Provisions for non-discrimination.- The Government shall take adequate steps to prohibit discrimination in any 
Government or private organisation including in the areas of education, recruitment, employment, promotion of  health care, 
public transportation, sports, leisure, recreation and opportunity to hold public or private oﬃ  ce.
12. Equal opportunities in employment.- (1) Every establishment shall implement all measures for providing a safe working 
environment and to ensure that no transgender person is discriminated in any matter relating to equal employment opportunities 
including, but not limited to, infrastructure adjustments, recruitment, employment  beneﬁ ts, promotion and other related issues.

(2) Every establishment shall provide an equal opportunity policy for transgender persons.
(3) The establishment shall display the equal opportunity policy, including the details of the Complaints Oﬃ  cer, preferably 
on their website, failing which, at conspicuous places in their premises.
(4) The equal opportunity policy of an establishment shall, inter-alia, contain the following details namely:-
    (a) Infrastructural facilities (such as unisex toilets), measures put in for safety and security (transportation and guards) 
and amenities (such as hygiene products) to be provided to the transgender persons so as to enable them to eﬀ ectively 
discharge of their duties in the establishment;
    (b) Applicability of all rules and regulations of the establishment regarding service conditions of employees;
 
(c) Conﬁ dentiality of the gender identity of the employees;
 
(d) Complaints of the oﬃ  cers.
13. Grievance redressal.-  (1) Every establishment shall designate a complaint oﬃ  cer in accordance with section 11 of 
the Act.
(2) The complaint oﬃ  cer shall enquire into a complaint received within ﬁ fteen days from the date of its receipt.
(3) The head of the establishment shall take action on the enquiry report submitted by the complaint oﬃ  cer within ﬁ fteen 
days from the date of submission of the report.
(4) The Joint Director (Women Welfare), the Directorate of Social Welfare at State Level and District 
Social Welfare Officer in the District are designated as Grievance Redressal officers for ensuring proper 
implementation of the provisions of Chapter V of the Act with special reference to sub-sections (1) and (2) of 
section 12.
(5) Every Grievance Redressal oﬃ  cer shall ensure resolution of grievances within thirty days from the date on which the 
grievance is brought to his/her notice.
(6) The Government shall put in place a monitoring system for tracking the number of complaints ﬁ led, enquired and 
action taken of all the establishments in the State.
