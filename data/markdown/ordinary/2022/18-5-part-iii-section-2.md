JUDICIAL NOTIFICATION
Amendment to the Madras High Court Designation of Senior Advocates Rules, 2020.
(R.O.C. No. 59095/2020/SCS)
No. SRO C-14/2022. 
	
In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 34 (1) read with Section 16 (2) of the Advocates Act, 1961, the 
High Court of Judicature at Madras, hereby makes the following amendment to Rule 4 (1) of the Madras High Court 
Designation of Senior Advocates Rules, 2020, as published in Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, Part III—Section 2, 
Issue No. 29, dated 15-07-2020.
Amendment
	
	
For Rule 4 (1) of the Madras High Court Designation of Senior Advocates Rules, 2020, the following Rule shall 
be substituted namely:-
	
	
"4. Qualification Criteria:-
	
	
(1) An advocate will be qualified to be designated as 'Senior Advocate', if he
(a) has 10 years' standing as an Advocate in the Madras High Court or its Bench at Madurai and 
Courts subordinate to it, including Tribunals, or a combined standing of 10 years' as an advocate 
and as a District & Sessions Judge, or as a Judicial Member of any Tribunal in India whose 
qualification for eligibility for such appointment, is not less than that prescribed for appointment 
as a District Judge; 
(b) exhibits distinction and eminence for legal acumen and special knowledge and maintains integrity, 
reputation and high ethical standards expected of a Senior Advocate both inside and outside 
the Court;
(c) has 15 judgements to his credit, in the preceding five years, where he has contributed to the 
growth of law;"
High Court, Madras,	
P. DHANABAL,
