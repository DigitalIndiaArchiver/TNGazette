notification under the Corona INFECTION PREVENTION AND CONTROL CORONA VIRUS DISEASE 
(COVID-19) UNDER THE EPIDEMIC DISEASES ACT, 1897.
[G.O.Ms. No. 150, Health and Family Welfare (P1), 22nd March 2020,  
Panguni 9, Vikari, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2051.]
No. II(2)/HFW/216(d)/2020.
WHEREAS the Hon’ble Prime Minister has required the Nation to implement self imposed quarantine at home to prevent 
person to person transmission of COVID-19 from 7.00 A.M. to 9.00 P.M. on 22nd March, 2020;
WHEREAS it is feared that after 9.00 P.M. people may come out in large numbers thereby defeating the purpose of social 
distancing;
AND WHEREAS it has been decided to continue the self imposed quarantine in the State upto 5.00 A.M. on 23rd March, 
2020; 
NOW THEREFORE in exercise of the powers conferred under Section 2 of the Epidemic Diseases Act, 1897 
(Central Act 3 of 1897), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes temporary regulations for the period from 
9.00 P.M. on 22nd March, 2020 to 5.00 A.M. on 23rd March, 2020 as follows:
1.
Everyone shall stay at home and shall venture out of home only for basic essential needs.
2.
Not more than 5 persons shall congregate at any public place.
3.
All shops, commercial establishments, offices and factories, workshops, godowns etc., shall remain closed.
4.
All foreign returnees are directed to remain under strict home quarantine.
5.
No public transport services including operation of taxis,  auto-rickshaws etc., are permitted.

o.	
Manufacturing units engaged in production of essential commodities.
p.	
Transport of all kinds to and from hospitals, airports, railway stations, bus terminals / bus stands.
q.	
Private establishments that support the above services or are linked to the efforts for containment of 
COVID-19 shall remain open.
3. The District Collectors are hereby authorized to take all necessary actions for enforcement and implementation of the 
aforesaid measures. If there is any doubt as to whether a service establishment is essential or not, the Collector shall be the 
competent authority to decide.  Local police shall render necessary assistance as and when requisitions are made by the 
aforementioned officers.
4. Any person found violating the containment measures shall be deemed to have committed an offence punishable under 
Section 188 of Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860).
