FINANCE DEPARTMENT
Unconditional and irrevocable guarantee on the repayment
of principal with interest on the Bonds to be issued
for certain period by the Tamil Nadu Electricity Board,
Chennai.
[G.O. Ms. No. 161, Finance (Loans and Advances Cell),
23rd April 2009, Chithirai 10, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2040.]
No. II(2)/FIN/204/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred under clause (1) of Article 293 of the Constitution
of India and in supersession of the Notification issued in
G.O. Ms. No. 27, Finance (L & A Cell) Department, dated
28th January 2009 and published on page 67 in Part II—
Section 2 (Issue No. 5) of the Tamil Nadu Government
Gazette, dated the 11th February 2009, the Governor of Tamil
Nadu hereby guarantees unconditionally and irrevocably the
repayment of principal to the aggregate value of Rs. 600
Crores (Rupees Six hundred Crores only) together with interest
and other charges thereon on the bonds to be issued during
the year 2008-2009 and from 1-4-2009 to 30-6-2009 by the
Tamil Nadu Electricity Board, Chennai-600 002, till the principal
is redeemed fully from the date of issue of bonds.
P. UMANATH,
Deputy Secretary to Government.
HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE DEPARTMENT
Notifications under Charitable Endowments Act.
Vesting of Property and Scheme for Administration relating
to Endowment Fund for Dr. S.V.R. Sanjeevi Rajah,
Professor of Surgery (Retd.), Government Theni Medical
College, Theni, Contribution of Funds towards Gold
Medal Examination for MBBS Students under the Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 422, Health and Family Welfare (E1),
17th December 2008, Margazhi 2, Thiruvalluvar
Aandu-2039.]
No. II(2)/HF/205/2009.—In the matter of the Charitable
Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI of 1890) and in the
matter of Endowment Fund for Dr. S.V.R. Sanjeevi Rajah,
Professor of Surgery (Retd.), Government Theni Medical
College, Theni Contribution of funds towards Gold Medal
Examination for MBBS Students.
I. VESTING OF PROPERTY.
Whereas an application has been made to the Government
of Tamil Nadu under clause (b) of sub-section (i) of Section 6
of the Charitable Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI of
1890) by Dr. S.V.R. Sanjeevi Rajah, the donor of the
Endowment Fund specified above for vesting Rs. 60,000/-
(Rupees Sixty Thousand only) in the Treasurer of Charitable
Endowments appointed under the said Act for the State of
Tamil Nadu. The Governor of Tamil Nadu in exercise of the
powers conferred by Section 4 of the said Act, hereby directs
that the said amount be vested in the Treasurer aforesaid
and be held by him and his successors in office, subject
to the provisions of the said Act, and to any rules, which,
from time to time, may be or shall deemed to have been
made thereunder, upon trust for purpose and subject to the
conditions setforth in the Schemes settled under Section 5
of the said Act for the administration of the said Fund.
II. SCHEME FOR ADMINISTRATION
Whereas an application has been made to the Government
of Tamil Nadu under clause (b) of sub-section (i) of Section
6 of the Charitable Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI
of 1890), by Dr. S.V.R. Sanjeevi Rajah (Retired), the donor
of the Endowment Fund specified above, for settling the
Scheme for the administration of the said Fund. The Governor
of Tamil Nadu in exercise of the powers conferred by Section
5 of the said Act, hereby settles the Scheme setforth in the
Schedule below for the administration of the said Fund and
appoint from the date of publication of this Notification in the
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette as the date on which the
Scheme as settled shall come into operation.
SCHEDULE
The Endowment Fund shall be called as “Dr. S.V.R.
Anatomy Gold Medal Examination” The Gold Medal
Examination has to be conducted by the Department of
Anatomy for the very first batch of MBBS students and later
this Gold Medal Examination has to be conducted by the
Department of General Surgery when this very first batch of
MBBS students appear for their final year examination in
Surgery and thereafter the name of the endowment to be
named as “Dr. S.V.R. Surgery Gold Medal Examination” to
be conducted every year from the Department of Surgery
only.
2. The Government Theni Medical College, Theni INSIGNIA
shall be embossed on the obverse or reverse side of the
Gold Medal.
3. The Dean, Government Theni Medical College, Theni
shall be the administrator of the Fund and shall deposit the
amount furnished by the donor Dr. S.V.R. Sanjeevi Rajah
with the Treasurer of Charitable Endowments. Government
of Tamil Nadu, Chennai-2 who will invest or reinvest the
amount and proceeds in Government securities to the best
interest of the Funds.
4. The interest accrued annually shall be utilized for
purchase of Gold Medal every year and it shall be given
to the best student annually on the College Day.
5. As and when there is need for more funds to conduct
this examination, funds will be contributed by the donor or
any one of his family members to conduct this examination
periodically every year.
6. The unutilized amount accrued in the past shall be
added to the Corpus of the Fund.

Vesting of Property and Scheme for Administration relating
to Endowment Fund for “Dr. K.P. Ananthan Memorial
Cash Award” for the MBBS Students who stand first
in Physiology in the University Examination (Both
Theory and Practical) at Thanjavur Medical College,
Thanjavur under the Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 423, Health and Family Welfare (E1),
17th December 2008, Margazhi 2, Thiruvalluvar
Aandu-2039.]
No. II(2)/HF/206/2009.—In the matter of the Charitable
Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI of 1890) and in the
matter of Endowment Fund for “Dr. K.P. Ananthan Memorial
Cash Award” for the MBBS students who stand first in
Physiology in the University Examination (Both Theory and
Practical) at Thanjavur Medical College, Thanjavur.
I. VESTING OF PROPERTY
Whereas an application has been made to the Government
of Tamil Nadu under clause (b) of sub-section (i) of Section 6
of the Charitable Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI
1890) by Dr. A. Ganesan, the donor of the Endowment Fund
specified above for vesting Rs. 50,000/-(Rupees Fifty Thousand
only) in the Treasurer of Charitable Endowments appointed
under the said Act for the State of Tamil Nadu. The Governor
of Tamil Nadu in exercise of the powers conferred by Section
4 of the said Act, hereby directs that the said amount be
vested in the Treasurer aforesaid and be held by him and
his successors in office, subject to the provisions of the said
Act, and to any rules, which, from time to time, may be or
shall deemed to have been made thereunder, upon trust for
the purpose and the subject to the conditions setforth in the
Scheme settled under Section 5 of the said Act for the
administration of the said Fund.
II. SCHEME FOR ADMINISTRATION
Whereas an application has been made to the Government
of Tamil Nadu under clause (b) of sub-section (i) of Section
6 of the Charitable Endowments Act, 1890 (Central Act VI
of 1890), by Dr. A. Ganesan, the donor of the Endowment
Fund specified above, for settling the Scheme for the
administration of the said Fund. The Governor of Tamil Nadu
in exercise of the powers conferred by Section 5 of the said
Act, hereby settles the Scheme setforth in the Schedule
below for the administration of the said Fund and appoint
from the date of publication of this Notification in the
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette as the date on which the
Scheme as settled shall come into operation.
SCHEDULE
1. The Endowment Fund shall be called as
“Dr. K.P. Ananthan Memorial Cash Award” for the MBBS
Students who stand first in the University Examination in
Physiology both in Theory and Practical at Thanjavur Medical
College, Thanjavur.
2. The cash award be given to the MBBS student who
stand first in the University Examination in the Physiology
both in Theory and Practical.
3. The Dean, Thanjavur Medical College, Thanjavur shall
be the administrator of the Fund and shall deposit the amount
furnished by Dr. Ganesan with the Treasurer of Charitable
Endowments. Government of Tamil Nadu, Chennai who will
invest or reinvest the amount and proceeds in Government
securities to the best interest of the Funds.
4. The interest accrued annually shall be utilized for
making the cash award and it shall be given to the best
student annually on College Day.
5. If more than one student stand first in the subject of
Physiology in the University Examination, the cash award
shall be equally distributed among the students.
6. The student should be regular student and he should
earn more than 85% attendance in all the subjects in the
first year.
7. The best outgoing student shall be selected by the
Board of Examiners and its Chairman and recommended by
the Dean, Thanjavur Medical College, Thanjavur. The
recommendation shall be duly signed by the Chairman and
Examiners and endorsed by the Dean, Thanjavur Medical
College, Thanjavur.
8. Dr. Navaneethan, MSO (Ortho) is one of the
administrative, members for the Endowment.
V.K. SUBBURAJ,
Principal Secretary to Government.

Census of the Population of India during the year 2011.
[Letter No. 11594/2009-1, Public (Law Officers),
20th April 2009.]
No. II(2)/PULO/226/2009.—The following notification of
the Ministry of Home Affairs (office of the Registrar, General,
India), New Delhi, the 20th February, 2009 is republished:—
S.O.562(E).—In exercise of the powers conferred by
Section 3 of the Census Act, 1948 (37 of 1948), the Central
Government hereby declares that a census of the population
of India shall be taken during the year 2011. The reference
date for the census shall, except for the State of Jammu
and Kashmir and snow bound non-synchronous areas of
Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand, be 00.00 hours of the
first day of March 2011:
Provided that for the State of Jammu and Kashmir and
snow bound non-synchronous areas of Himachal Pradesh
and Uttarakhand, the reference date shall be 00.00 hours
of the first day of October, 2010.
