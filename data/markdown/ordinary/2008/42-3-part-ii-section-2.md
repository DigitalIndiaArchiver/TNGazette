LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT  DEPARTMENT
Direction regarding certain provision not apply to employees
who engaged workers for running Motor Transport
Vehicles in plantations under Motor Transport Workers
Act, 1961 and Tamil Nadu Motor Transport Workers
Rules, 1965.
[G.O.Ms.No. 112, Labour and Employment (H1),
22nd September 2008.]
No. II(2)/LE/515/2008.—In  exercise of powers conferred
by clause (iii) of sub-section (2) of Section 38 of the Motor
Transport Workers Act, 1961 (Central Act 27 of 1961), the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby directs that the provisions
of the said Act and the Tamil Nadu Motor Transport Workers’
Rules, 1965 shall not apply to the class of employers who
engage workers for running motor transport vehicles in any
plantations, for a period of three years on and from the date
of publication of this notification in the Tamil Nadu Government
Gazette, subject to the following conditions, namely:—
(1) the motor transport workers employed in the plantation
industry should enjoy all the benefits under the Plantations
Labour Act, 1951 (Central Act LXIX of 1951);
(2) they should be paid overtime wages for the work
done in excess of forty-eight hours a week; and
(3) they should be supplied with one pair of semi closed
chappal for every twelve months.
RAMESH KUMAR KHANNA,
Principal Secretary to Government.
