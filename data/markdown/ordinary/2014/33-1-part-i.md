(c). The Commissioner of Revenue Administration, while
offering his remarks on his representation, has admitted that
he and two others (viz. Thiru K.A. Annamalai, Thiru
P. Senthilkumar and Thiru S. Sivashanmugaraja) were appointed
as per orders of the Supreme Court and Tamil Nadu
Administrative Tribunal from the Reserve List of 1991 Special
Direct Recruitment for SC/ST.
Therefore, the vacancies relate to the year 1991-1992 as
was notified by the Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission
based on the vacancies reported by the Government to the
Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission. Had the vacancies
been correctly reported by the Government to the Tamil Nadu
Public Service Commission, their names also would have been
found in the main list (instead of in the Reserve list) of the
Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission and they would have
also joined as Deputy Collectors along with 11 other
Deputy Collectors in September 1992 itself.
(d). In the orders of the Tribunal in O.A.No.3400/93 dated
29-09-1995, it was observed by the Tribunal that had all the
vacancies that existed were notified the persons kept in the
Reserve list would have come up in the main list. The Supreme
Court also dismissed the SLP preferred by the Government/
Tamil Nadu Public Service Commisssion and observed that
they do not want to interfere with the order of Tamil Nadu
Administrative Tribunal.
(e). Their seniority should be fixed along with
11 candidates Viz. V. Shobana and others and their name have
to be fixed below Thiru T. Abraham as per Rule 35(a) of Tamil
Nadu State and Subordinate Service Rule which says the
seniority of a person in a service, class or category or grade
shall unless he has been reduced to a lower rank as punishment
be determined by the rank obtained by him in the list of approved
candidates drawn up by the Tamil Nadu Public Service
Commission or other Appointing Authority, as the case may be,
subject to the rule of reservation where in applies.
(i) Fixation of seniority with reference to Rule 35(aa) of
Tamil Nadu State and Subordinate Service Rule done in ordinary
circumstances will not hold good in their case in view of the
orders of Supreme Court and Tamil Nadu Administrative Tribunal
direction.
(ii) Even if it is decided to fix the seniority as per 35(aa)
of Tamil Nadu State and Subordinate Service Rules, he has
pointed out that the rule has to be read along with the proviso
to the said rule which is extracted below.—
 “Provided that where the junior appointed by a particular
method or recruitment happens to be appointed to a service,
class, category or grade, earlier than the senior appointed by
the same method of recruitment, the senior shall be deemed
to have been appointed to the service, class, category or grade
on the same day on which the junior was  so appointed”.
(f). In view of the above and as the delay in joining as
Deputy Collector was not their fault, they should not be
penalized and justice rendered. Therefore, their seniority
may kindly be fixed along with the 11 candidates i.e.
V. Shobana and other i.e. below Thiru T. Abraham, based on
the orders of Supreme Court and direction of Tamil Nadu
Administrative Tribunal.
(g). Regarding the remarks of Commissioner of Revenue
Administration that, his representation is time barred and
he should have represented/preferred an appeal either
before 22-11-1999 or 16-10-2000, he has pointed out that
the inter-se-seniority problem is for all Three of them (Viz.
Thiru K.A. Annamalai, Thiru P Senthilkumar and Thiru
S. Sivashanmugaraja). Thiru P. Senthilkumar has already
preferred an appeal on behalf of all three persons on
08-01-1999 itself for fixation of inter-se-seniority at the
appropriate place and as on date no reply has been given
by the Government in Revenue Department to him or to any
of them and therefore the issue is still pending with the
Government in Revenue Department and his representation
has to be construed as one in continuation of the earlier
representation made by Thiru P. Senthilkumar pending with the
Government and therefore the rule of limitation will not apply to
their case”.

(ii). The situation narrated above will explain that the

reading of the seniority list approved in the G.O.(Ms)No.796,
Revenue Department, Dated 18-08-1995 reveal that the
provisions under Rule 35(a) read with rule 2A(ii) has been
followed in their case. If the criteria of date of appointment as
said under Rule 35(aa) had been followed, the eleven direct
recruits who have been appointed to service as Deputy
Collectors in the year 1992 would have been placed on top of
the inter-se-seniority list approved in the said Government
order whereas it has been done otherwise that the direct
recruits of the year 1991 have been placed at the bottom of
the inter-se-seniority list of the year 1991 below the last
person of the transferee Deputy Collectors (whose panel
for appointment as Deputy Collectors has been approved in
the year 1995) by following Rule 2A(ii) of the special rules of
Deputy Collectors. From the above it is understood that by
keeping the seniority of the eleven directly recruited Deputy
Collectors for the year 1991 intact as approved by Tamil Nadu
Public Service Commission without any modification or split,
the Govemment have followed the provisions under
rule 35(a) of Tamil Nadu State and Subordinate Service Rules
without any violation. By placing the above eleven person
below the last transferee Deputy Collector approved for the
year 1991 during 1995, the Government have followed the
provisions available under Rule 2A(ii) of special rule for Deputy
Collectors. The direct recruits of the year 1991
have joined/appointed to the service as Deputy Collectors in
September 1992 whereas the transferee Deputy Collectors
have been appointed to service in the year 1995 only.
