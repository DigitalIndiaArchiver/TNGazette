SARS COV-2 virus causes COVID-19. Corona virus infection spreads from person to person directly through 
respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs and sneezes (<20% of transmission). People also become 
infected by touching surfaces frequently touched by hands (>80 %).
It is important to ensure we identify pockets of critical interventions for a focused management of Covid 19 at field 
level. A district will be considered under Green Zone, if there are no confirmed cases so far or there is no reported case 
since last 21 days in the district.  The list will be revised on a weekly basis or earlier and communicated to States for further 
follow up action in consonance with the directions issued by Ministry of Home Affairs under the Disaster Management 
Act’2005.  The List of the districts as per above classification is attached herewith in Annexure.  It is further highlighted that 
based on field feedback and additional analysis at state level, States may designate additional red or orange zones as 
appropriate. However, States may not relax the zonal classification of districts classified as red/orange as communicated 
by the Ministry.  For the districts having one or more municipal corporations, the corporations and other areas of districts 
may be treated as separate units. If one or more of these units have reported no cases for last 21 days, they can be 
considered as one level lower in zonal classification, in case the district is in Red/Orange Zone. District authorities should, 
however, exercise due caution in such areas so that these areas remain free from COVID19 cases. It is critical to ensure 
that necessary action for containment so as to break the chain of transmission of virus is initiated in both red and orange 
zone districts reporting confirmed.

The containment zones in these districts have to be delineated based on:
l	
Mapping of cases and Contacts
l	
Geographical dispersion of cases and contacts
l	
Area with well demarcated perimeter
l	
Enforceability
Depending on above factors, for urban areas - residential  colony / Mohallas / municipal wards or police-station area / 
municipal zones / town etc., as appropriate can be designated as containment zones. Similarly, for rural areas – villages / clusters 
of villages or group of police stations/ gram panchayats/blocks etc., as appropriate can be designates as containment zones.  The 
area should be appropriately defined by the district administration/local urban body with technical inputs from local level.  In the 
sprit of effective containment, it is advisable to err on the side of caution.  Further, a buffer zone around containment zone has to 
be demarcated. 
Necessary action should then be initiated in these areas as part of the containment Action Plans already communicated 
including-
l	
Stringent Perimeter control 
c	
Establishing clear entry and exit points 
c	
No movement except for medical emergencies and essential goods & services
c	
 No unchecked influx of population

l	
Testing of all cases as per sampling guidelines 
l	
Contact tracing 
l	
Clinical management of all confirmed cases 
Similarly, in buffer zones, extensive surveillance for cases through monitoring of ILI/SARI cases in health facilities has 
to be taken up. 
Strategies for Corona Virus Infection Prevention and Control
1.	
Strategies to prevent the entry of the virus into a country or state or district or in a local area include thermal screening, 
entry and exit hand wash, self quarantine, hospital isolation of persons with symptoms.
2.	
Respiratory Hygiene including remaining inside the home when anyone has cold, cough, fever, sneeze after getting 
medical advice and test for COVID-19, cough etiquette, hand washing and surface cleaning and disinfection practices 
including hospitals, public transport and similar locations.
3.	
Avoiding all kinds of mass gatherings 
4.	
Effective seamless lab-based surveillance by testing all severe Acute Respiratory Illness (SARI) cases, Influenza Like 
Illnesses (ILI), contact testing, testing people with low immunity.
5.	
If tested positive, isolation and admission in COVID hospital as per doctor advise.
6.	
Isolation and treatment of positive cases in hospitals and based on the experience and need, steady strengthening of 
hospital capacity both in Govt sector and private sector, including the separation of fever OP and IP from other hospital 
services. 
7.	
Special care for pregnant mothers and children and special arrangements for the hospitals catering to special patient 
groups like cancer, geriatrics, transplants and other similar categories. 
8.	
Home quarantine/ facility quarantine of high-risk contacts.

Strategies aimed at Delaying the spread
l	
Essentially for any kind of Influenza Like Illness (ILI) the strategy is delaying the spread of the infection in the community 
as much as possible, since it will enable the society, government and health care system to get prepared to handle the 
situation. 
l	
Broader flattened epidemic curve is the goal since it will enable the younger society to develop herd immunity and in 
the process the lightning ability of the virus will be severely crippled.
Containment Strategies
1. General Principles
l	
Prevention of further spread in the locality by restricting the movements and implementing all known scientific 
interventions is the key objective of containment strategy
l	
Although administrative units are preferred, case patient movement and local epidemiology are the factors to be 
considered for decidingthe  locations/areas to be brought under containment zone.
l	
If administrative units overlap with each other, all concerned local bodies should be involved in containment 
operations.
1. Single or less than 5 Positive Cases
1.1.Village Panchayat
In the village panchayat, village (habitation/hamlet) containing the residence of positive case is demarcated as 
containment zone. 
l	
If the individual stayed in a village (habitation / hamlet), such village (habitation / hamlet), may be brought under 
containment zone. If the positive cases spread across village panchayat, the concerned village panchayat  will be 
brought under containment zone.
l	
If the residence happens to be at the border of the concerned village  panchayat, then neighbouring village also be 
included in containment zone.
l	
The Block Medical Officer, Block Development Officer, Block Health Supervisor after a rapid appraisal of the case 
and geographical situation in consultation with the DDHS can decide the number of villages to be brought under 
containment zone.
1.2. Urban Area(Corporation/Municipality)
l	
The street containing the residence of the positive case is demarcated as the containment zone.
l	
If the street is very long a reasonable stretch to be brought under containment to restrict movement.
l	
If the residence happens to be the corner of the street, then the adjoining street also be included in 
containment zone.
l	
Quick aerial view from the roof top a nearby high rise building and rapid appraisal of the movement of the positive 
case and local people, City Health Officer and Commissioner.
1.3.Urban Area (Town Panchayath)
l	
The street containing the residence of the positive case is demarcated as the containment zone. 
l	
If the residence happens to be the corner of the street, then the adjoining street also be included in 
containment zone. 
l	
The Block Medical Officer, Executive Officer of the Town Panchayath and Block Health Supervisor after a rapid 
appraisal of the case and geographical situation in consultation with the DDHS can decide the area to be brought 
under containment zone.

2.Cluster of Cases: If there are five and more positive cases in a locality then it will be considered as clusters.
2.1.Village Panchayat
l	
If a number of cluster falls in a village or group of villages then group of such villages can be brought under 
containment.
l	
The Block Medical Officer, Block Development Officer, Block Health Supervisor after a rapid appraisal of the cases 
and geographical situation in consultation with the DDHS can decide the number of Village Panchayats to be 
brought under containment zone. 
l	
In a block with larger geographical area, sparse population and distance between the case village / hamlet / 
habitation and other surrounding village / hamlet  / habitation is more than two kms and there is no movement 
between the affected villages, containment areas can be restricted to a group of affected villages.
l	
In case of thickly populated blocks with smaller geographical area like the suburban blocks the whole block may be 
brought under containment. 
l	
If the cluster happens to be at the border area of a block, then the adjoining block also be included in the containment 
zone.
2.2.Urban Areas (Corporation/Municipality/Town panchayat)
2.2.1.Corporation: 
l	
If the cluster falls within the Ward, then the Ward in which the residences of the positive cases are located should 
be demarcated as the containment zone. 
l	
If the residence happens to be the border of the ward, then the adjoining wards may also be included in containment 
zone. 
l	
Quick aerial view from the roof top a nearby high rise building and rapid appraisal of the movement of the positive 
cases and local people, City Health Officer and Commissioner.
2.2.2.Municipality: 
l	
If the cluster falls within the Ward, then the Ward in which the residences of the positive cases are located should 
be demarcated as the containment zone. 
l	
If the residence happens to be on the border of the ward, then the adjoining streets may also be included in 
containment zone. 
l	
Quick aerial view from the roof top a nearby high rise building and rapid appraisal of the movement of the positive 
cases and local people, City Health Officer and Commissioner.
2.2.3.Town Panchayat: 	
l	
The whole Town Panchayat where the clustering of cases happens is demarcated as containment zone.
3.Gated Community/ Hostels/Apartments
l	
Epidemiological factors should be taken into consideration before deciding on the strategies
l	
Housing design, number of people living/ working/ visiting, floors, transport and other epidemiological factors should 
be considered
l	
DDHS and District Collector/ City Commissioner should directly take charge and decide the strategies
4. Surveillance Zone (Buffer Zone)
l	
In rural area 0.5 kms radius can be demarcated as buffer zone and in urban area buffer zone to be earmarked based 
on the geo spatial spread & ground realities.
l	
Extensive mike announcement should be done around five kms area for reporting of ILI and SARI cases.
l	
Surveillance should be intensified in all local clinics/hospitals/PHCs/major hospitals for ILI and SARI based on the 
health care service utilisation pattern in that particular area.
l	
Cross notification and enquiry even in neighbouring districts will be of use.

5
Scaling down
l	
The containment operations shall be deemed to be over 28 days, if no active cases in the zone and from the 
confirmation of lastpositive case in the containment zone.
l	
In Cluster case reported containment zones, areas can be released partially also depending on the local geographical 
situation and population movement.
l	
At least one dedicated team of a medical officer and Health Inspector should monitor the area for 28 days from the 
date of negative test of last positive case.
l	
Surveillance and other routine activities should be continued as per guidelines with additional care routinely.
l	
In rural area with 0.5 kms as radius & in urban area the buffer zone can be created as per the Geospatial spread as 
per administrative convenience.
