TAMIL  NADU  GOVERNMENT  GAZETTE  EXTRAORDINARY
WHEREAS, the Election Commission of India  substituted a provision under para 10 in the Elections Symbols
(Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968,  which  provides a concession to candidates set up by an unrecognised  State
party which is recognised State Party in some other State (s) or Union Territories at an Election.
WHEREAS, the said provision made under para 10 of the Elections Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968
provides as follows:-
“10.  CONCESSION TO CANDIDATES  SET UP A STATE PARTY AT ELECTIONS IN OTHER STATES OR
UNION TERRITORIES:
If a political party which is recognized as a State Party in some State or States sets up a candidate  at an
election in a constituency in any other State or Union Territory in which it is not a recognised State Party, then such
candidate may, to the exclusion of all other candidates in the constituency be allotted the symbol reserved for that
party in the State or States in which it is a recognised State Party, notwithstanding that such symbol is not speci-
fied in the list of free symbols for such other State or Union Territory,  on the fulfilment of each of the following
conditions, namely:-
(a) that an application is made to the commission by the said party for exclusive   allotment  of that symbol
to the candidate set by it,  not  later than the third day after the publication in the Official Gazette of the
Notification calling the election.
(b)  that the  said candidate has made a declaration in his nomination paper  that he has been set up by
 that party at the election and that the party has also fulfilled the requirements of clauses  (b),(c), (d)
and  (e) of paragraph 13 read with paragraph 13A in respect of such candidate;  and
(c)  that in the opinion of the Commission there is no reasonable ground for refusing the application for such
 allotment:
    Provided that nothing contained in the paragraph shall apply to a candidate set up by a  State Party at an
election in any constituency in a State which that party is not a State Party and where the same symbol is already reserved
for some other State Party in that State.
WHEREAS, no such provisions were provided in the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election  Symbols (Reservation &
Allotment) Order, 2016, however the Local Bodies elections rules provide for adopting the parties which are Recognised
as a National Party or as a State Party  in the State of Tamil Nadu as per the Election Commission of India’s Symbol
Order, 1968 as recognised  parties for the purpose  of Local Body Elections in Tamil Nadu.
WHEREAS, now certain political parties which are recognised as a State Party in other State(s) have represented
this Commission to provide similar concession as under para 10 of the Symbol Order of the Election Commission of India
in the Symbol orders issued by the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission and to allot their party symbol to the
candidates set up by their party in the ensuing Local Body Elections.
WHEREAS, the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission during the Ordinary Elections, 2011 issued orders to the effect
that the symbol reserved for the party which is recognised as a State Party in some other State or State(s) or Union
Territories be allotted, the symbol reserved for that party in the State or State(s) in which it is a recognised State Party,
as provided in para 10 of Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968 of the Election Commission of India.
WHEREAS, considering the above, it has become expedient for the Commission to extend such concession to the
Candidates set up at elections by a political party which is a State Party in Other States to avail their Party’s symbols
in the Local Body Elections also  by duly incorporating the similar provisions in the Symbol Order with required changes
to suit the requirement for Local Bodies Elections in the State.
Now, THEREFORE, in exercise of the powers conferred under Articles 243 K and 243 ZA of the Constitution of India
read with relevant sections of the Acts and rule 33, 34 & 35 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules,1995, rule
31, 32 & 33 of the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Third Grade Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections)
Rules, 2006 and as per the clause 11 of the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election Symbols (Reservation  & Allotment) Order,
2016, the following amendments are ordered in the Symbol Order issued by the Commission vide S.O.No. 17/2016/
TNSEC/EG1, dated 9-9-2016:-

WHEREAS, the Election Commission of India  inserted a provision under para 10A in the Elections Symbols
(Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968,  which  provides a concession to candidates set up by an unrecognised  party
which was earlier recognised as a National or State Party.
WHEREAS, the said provision made under para 10A of the Elections Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968
provides as follows:-
“10-A.
CONCESSION TO CANDIDATES SET UP BY AN UNRECOGNISED PARTY   WHICH WAS EARLIER
RECOGNISED AS A NATIONAL OR STATE PARTY”
            If a Political Party, which is unrecognised at present but was a Recognised National or State Party in
any State or Union Territory not earlier than six years from the date of notification of the election sets
up a candidate of an election in a constituency in any State or Union Territory or not, then such candidate
may, to the exclusion of all other candidates in the Constituency, be allotted the symbol reserved earlier
for that party when it was a Recognised National or State party, notwithstanding that such symbol is not
specified in the list of free symbols for such state or Union territory, on the fulfilment of each of the following
conditions, namely:-
(a) that an application is made to the Commission by the said party for the exclusive allotment of that
symbol to the candidate set up by it, not later than the third day after the publication in the official
Gazette of the notification calling the election;
(b) that the said candidate has made a declaration in his nomination paper that he has been set up by
that party of the election and that the party has also fulfilled the requirements of clauses (b), (c), (d)
and (e) of paragraph 13 read with paragraph 13A in respect of such candidate; and
(c) that in the opinion of the Commission there is no reasonable ground for refusing the application for
such allotment.
    Provided that nothing contained in the paragraph shall apply to a candidate set up by the said party at an
election in any constituency in a State or Union territory where the same symbol is already reserved for some other
National or State Party in that State or Union Territory”.
WHEREAS, no such provisions were provided in the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election  Symbols (Reservation &
Allotment) Order, 2016, however the Local Bodies Elections Rules provide for adopting the Parties which are Recognised
as a National Party or as a State party  in the State of Tamil Nadu as per the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment)
Order, 1968 as recognised  parties for the purpose  of Local Body Elections in Tamil Nadu.
WHEREAS, while bringing the said provision under para 10-A for extending a concession to the political parties, the
Election Commission of India considered that a party that loses its recognition shall not lose its symbol immediately but
shall be given that facility to use that symbol for sometime to try or retrieve its status.
WHEREAS,  it is also pertinent to mention that in the judgment dated 23-9-2008 of the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India
in Civil Appeal No. 5803/2008 filed against the Election Commission of India challenging the sanctity of para 10-A of
Symbol Order of the Election Commission of India, the Hon’ble Court dismissed the Appeal and upheld the orders of the
Commission and thereby allowed to extend the provision 10-A enabling the Political Parties which lose their status of
recognition to avail their exclusive symbols for a period of six years from the date of such de-recognizisation.
Whereas the Commission during the Ordinary Elections 2011 issued orders amending its symbol order
No. 25/2011/TNSEC/EG2 dated 09-09-2011 enabling the unrecognised political parties which lose their status of
recognition as a National or State party to avail the exclusive symbols for a period of six years from the date of such
de-recognizisation.

WHEREAS, considering the above, it has become expedient for the Commission to extend such concession to the
Political Parties which were earlier recognised as a National Party or State Party in the State of Tamil Nadu to avail their
Party's symbols in the Local Body Elections also for a period of six years from the date of de-recognition ordered by the
Election Commission of India duly incorporating the similar provisions in the Symbol Order with required changes to suit
the requirement for Local Bodies Elections in the State.
Now, THEREFORE, in exercise of the powers conferred under Articles 243 K and 243 ZA of the Constitution of India
read with relevant sections of the Acts and rule 33, 34 & 35 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules,1995, rule
31, 32 & 33 of the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Third Grade Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections)
Rules, 2006 and as per the clause 11 of the Tamil Nadu Local Body Election Symbols (Reservation  & Allotment) Order,
2016, the following amendment is ordered in the Symbol Order issued by the Commission vide
S.O.No. 17/2016/TNSEC/EG1, dated  09-09-2016:-
I. After para 7(1)A, the following may be inserted.
“7 (1)B. CONCESSION TO CANDIDATES SET UP BY AN UNRECOGNISED PARTY   WHICH WAS EARLIER
     RECOGNISED AS A NATIONAL OR STATE PARTY”
If a Political Party, which is unrecognised at present but was a Recognised National or State Party in the State
of Tamil Nadu not earlier than six years from the date of notification of the election sets up a candidate at an
election in a ward in any Local body / District Panchayat which are held on party basis then such candidate may,
to the exclusion of all other candidates in the said election, be allotted the symbol reserved earlier for that party
when it was a Recognised National or State party in Tamil Nadu notwithstanding that such symbol is not specified
in the list of free symbols for such election, on the fulfilment of each of the following conditions, namely:-
(a)  that an application is made to the Commission by the said party for the exclusive allotment of
      that symbol to the candidate set up by it, not later than the third day after the publication in the
      official Gazette of the notification calling the election;
(b)  that the said candidate has made a declaration in his nomination paper that he has been set up by
that party of the election and that the party has also fulfilled the requirements of clauses (b), (c),
(d) and (e) of paragraph 8  and
(c)  that in the opinion of the Commission there is no reasonable ground for refusing the application
for such allotment.
    Provided that nothing contained in the paragraph shall apply to a candidate set up by the said party at an
election in any Ward/Local Body where the same symbol is already reserved for some other National or State Party.
