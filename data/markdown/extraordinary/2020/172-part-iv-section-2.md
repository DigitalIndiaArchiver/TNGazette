The following Ordinance which was promulgated by the Governor on the 
25th April 2020 is hereby published for general information:—
TAMIL NADU ORDINANCE No.  1 OF 2020.
Ex-IV-2—268-172—1
An Ordinance further to amend the Tamil Nadu Public Health  
Act, 1939.
WHEREAS the Legislative Assembly of the State is not 
in session and the Governor of Tamil Nadu is satisfied that 
circumstances exist which render it necessary for him to take 
immediate action for the purposes hereinafter appearing;
NOW, THEREFORE, in exercise of the  powers conferred 
by clause (1) of Article 213 of the Constitution, the Governor 
hereby promulgates the following Ordinance:-
1. (1) This Ordinance may be called the Tamil Nadu Public 
Health (Amendment) Ordinance, 2020. 
Short title and 
commence-
ment.
   (2) It shall come into force at once.
Tamil Nadu Act III 
of 1939. 
2.  In Section 74 of the Tamil Nadu Public Health Act,1939, 
the following sub-section shall be added, namely:-
Amendment of 
section 74. 
 “(6) Any person who, either by himself or as a member of 
a group, obstructs or prevents or attempts to obstruct or prevent 
the cremation or burial of the body of any person who has died 
while suffering from a notified disease shall be punished with 
imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than one year 
but which may extend to three years and with fine.”.
	
BANWARILAL PUROHIT,
25th  April 2020.	
Governor of Tamil Nadu.

 EXPLANATORY STATEMENT
Certain incidents of public obstructing the burial or cremation of deceased persons who had died due to 
COVID-19 have been brought to the notice of the Government. Such acts cause indignity to the human corpse, 
which is highly condemnable. The Government have therefore decided to make such acts a punishable offence 
and accordingly decided to amend the Tamil Nadu Public Health Act, 1939 (Tamil Nadu Act III of 1939) for the 
purpose.
	
2. The Ordinance seeks to give effect to the above decision.
