Order : No. 310, Public (Special-A), 29th March 2012.
The Government in consultation with the Hon’ble High Court,
Madras, appointed 17 District Judges (Entry Level) among others
Thiru S. Pandiarajan, Advocate, Tirunelveli at Sl. No. 4 in the
Government Order first read above. Now it has been brought
to the notice of the Government that on the date of application
i.e., on 6th July 2010, Criminal Case No.2/2007 on the file of
Pavoorchathiram Police Station, Tirunelveli District, was pending
against Thiru S. Pandiarajan as per the notification calling for
application to the post of District Judge (Entry Level), Thiru
S. Pandiarajan submitted his application dated 6th July 2010,
declaring ‘NIL’ against column No.10 which requires a candidate
to declare ‘whether the applicant is involved in any litigation
Civil/Criminal’.
2.The Hon’ble High Court called for a report from the
Principal Judge, Tirunelveli. The Government informed the
Hon’ble High Court. Madras vide D.O. Letter dated
18th February 2011 that Cr.No.2/2007 has been treated as
“Mistake of fact” and the same was approved by the Judicial
Magistrate, Tenkasi on 11th February 2011 by assigning
Rc. No.9/2011. In the light of the report  of the Principal District
Judge, Tirunelveli, and the remarks of the Government
D.O. Letter dated  18th February 2011, the Hon’ble High Court
considered the representation of Thiru S. Pandiarajan and
found that Thiru S. Pandiarajan has not only suppressed the
pendency of the Criminal Case against him, but also made a
false declaration  in his application, thereby he rendered
NOTIFICATIONS BY GOVERNMENT
himself ineligible/disqualified to apply for the post of District
Judges (Entry Level) and also for the consequential selection.
The Hon’ble High Court, Madras has withdrawn the
recommendation made to Government to appoint Thiru
S. Pandiarajan as District Judge (Entry Level).
3.The Government forwarded the further representation of
Thiru S. Pandiarajan, dated 25th March 2011 to the Hon’ble
High Court, Madras. The Hon'ble High Court, Madras considered
his representation and rejected the same, finding no merit in it.
4.Therefore the Government direct that name of Thiru
S. Pandiarajan, Advocate, Tirunelveli alone appearing against
Sl.No.4 in the appointment order made in para 1 of the
Government Order first read above shall be deleted  with
immediate effect and his appointment as District Judge
(Entry Level) are hereby cancelled.
(By order of the Governor)

[Appointment—Tamil Nadu State Judicial Service—
Appointment of Thiru P. Sivakumar, Appointment as District
Judge (Entry Level)—Notified.]
The following Government Order is published:—
[G.O. Ms. No. 311, Public (Special-A),  29th March 2012,
Panguni 16, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2043.]
READ:
1.G.O.Ms.No.16, Public (Special-A) Department, dated
5th January 2011.
2.G.O.Ms.No.310, Public (Special-A) Department, dated
29th March 2012.
Order : No. 311, Public (Special-A), 29th March 2012.
In Contunation of G.O. second read above, under rule 5 of
the Tamil Nadu State Judicial Service (Cadre and Recruitment)
Rules, 2007, the Government in consultation with the  Hon’ble
High Court, Madras, Chennai hereby appoint Thiru
P. Sivakumar, son of Thiru M. Prakasam, Salem as District
Judges (Entry Level).
2.The above said District Judge (Entry Level), will draw a
pay of Rs.51,550/- in the scale of pay of Rs.51,550-1230-
58930-1380-63070 and other usual allowances and he will be
on probation for a total period of 2 years from the date of
joining duty within a continuous period of three years. The
above said candidate is requested to furnish Physical fitness
certificate direct to the Registrar General, High Court of Madras
under intimation to Govenment.

3.During the period of probation, the above District Judge
