DIRECTORATE OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND PREVENTIVE MEDICINE.
Notiﬁ cation for use of Aadhaar under Rule 3 of Aadhaar Authentication for Good Governance 
(Social Welfare, Innovation, Knowledge) Rules, 2020
(R.No. 1541509/SBHI-I/S1/2021)
No. SRO C-24/2022.
Rule 3 of Aadhaar authentication for Good Governance (Social Welfare, innovation, Knowledge) Rules, 2020, (1) allow 
the Aadhaar authentication by requesting entities in the interest of Good Governance, Preventing leakage of public funds, 
promoting ease of living of residence and enabling better access to services for them for the following purposes, namely, 
 
(a) Usage of digital platforms to ensure good governance 
 
(b) Prevention of dissipation of social welfare beneﬁ ts; and 
 
(c) enablement of innovation and the spread of knowledge. 
(2) Aadhaar authentication under sub-rule (1) shall be on a voluntary basis.  
                               and 
Section 4(4)(b)(ii) of Aadhaar Act permit an entity to perform authentication, if the Authority is satisﬁ ed that the 
requesting entity is, 
 
(a) complaint with such standards of privacy and security as may be speciﬁ ed by regulations; and 
 
(b) (i) permitted to oﬀ er authentication services under the provisions of any other law made by parliament; or 
 
 
(ii) seeking authentication for such purpose, as the Central Government in consultation with the Authority, 
and in the interest of State may prescribe. 
Aadhaar requirement for the beneﬁ ciaries under Civil Registration System 
(G.O.Ms.No.351 Health and Family Welfare (AB2) department dt. 09.10.2017, G.O.Ms.No.17 Information Technology 
(E1) Department, dated 23.09.2021, Govt.Lr.No.136/AB2/2022-2, Health and Family Welfare Department, dt.08.04.2022, 
D.O.lr.No.1477703/IT/(E1)/2021-2, dt.23.05.2022 of Information Technology Department.
R.No.1541509/SBHI-I/S1/2021, dt.08.06.2022) 
The Department of Public Health and Preventive Medicine (herein after referred to as the Department) is implementing 
the Civil Registration System program to register the occurrence of Births and Deaths and to issue Birth/Death certiﬁ cate 
by the jurisdictional Birth and Death Registrar as per the provisions of the Registration of the Birth and Death Act, 1969 
and Tamil Nadu Registration of Births and Deaths Rules, 2000. 
The online CRS common software is implemented across the State for reporting and registering the occurrence of 
the Birth, Death and Still Birth under Civil Registration System. 
The Department has decided to collect the Aadhaar number of the beneﬁ ciaries on voluntary basis under 
Civil Registration System implemented by Government of Tamil Nadu. The use of Aadhaar number collected under 
Civil Registration System will enable to get the accuracy of the beneﬁ ciary details and for seamless sharing of data 
through data purity towards data driven decision support system as envisaged by the Government of Tamil Nadu. 
Now, therefore in pursuance of Rule 3 of Aadhaar authentication for Good Governance (Social welfare, Innovation, 
Knowlepge) Rules, 2020 and as per Section 4(4)(b)(ii) of Aadhaar Act, 2016 the Government of Tamil Nadu hereby 
notiﬁ es the following namely, 
1. Any individual who requires to register for getting Birth /Death certiﬁ cate under Civil Registration System shall 
hereby requires to furnish the proof of Aadhaar number if available for establishing of the identity of the individual for 
the purpose of registration of the birth and death on voluntary basis and undergo Aadhaar authentication. 
2. Incase of registration of birth of a child, Aadhaar number of the mother and father if available shall be furnished 
on voluntary basis. 
3. Incase of registration of death of an individual, Aadhaar number of the deceased, father, mother and spouse if 
available shall be furnished on voluntary basis. 
4. The above provision shall be applicable in all Districts of Tamil Nadu. 
5. This notiﬁ cation shall come in to eﬀ ect from the date of its publication in the Oﬃ  cial Gazette.
