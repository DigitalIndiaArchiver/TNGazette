NOTIFICATIONS  BY  HEADS  OF  DEPARTMENTS,  ETC.
TAMIL NADU STATE ELECTION COMMISSION, CHENNAI.
[ELECTIONS —ORDINARY ELECTIONS—RURAL AND URBAN  LOCAL BODIES — SAFEGUARDS AGAINST
PERSONATION — MARKING INDELIBLE INK — FURTHER DIRECTION — AMENDMENT—ORDERS—ISSUED.]
(S.O.No. 97/TNSEC/2011/EG-2.)
No. VI(2)/511(b)/2011.
In pursuance of sub-rule (I)(a) of rule 47 of the Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules, 1995 and  rule 45 and 67
of the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Third Grade Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections) Rules, 2006,
the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission had directed in the S.O. No. 31/2011/TNSEC/EG2, dated the
14th September 2011, that before delivering the ballot paper or ballot papers to the elector, or before allowing to record
his/her vote in Electronic Voting Machine, an indelible ink should be applied as a line from the top end of the nail to the bottom
of the first joint of the left forefinger.
Whereas, in view of the bye election to Tiruchirappalli West Assembly Constituency has been scheduled on
13th October 2011, the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission had directed in the S.O.No. 95/2011/TNSEC/EG2, dated
the 1st October 2011 that in the Corporation area within the Tiruchirappalli West Assembly Constituency, where the poll
for the election of Mayor/Ward Councillors of the said Corporation is Scheduled on 17th October 2011 before delivering the
ballot paper or ballot papers to the elector, or before allowing to record his / her vote in Electronic Voting Machine indelible
ink should be applied as a line from the top end of the nail to the bottom of the first joint of the left middle finger.
Now, in supersession of the S.O.No. 95/2011/TNSEC/EG2, dated the 1st October 2011 the Tamil Nadu State Election
Commission directed that in the Tiruchirappalli City Municipal Corporation where the poll for the election of Mayor/Ward
Councillors is Scheduled on 17th October 2011 before  allowing to record his / her vote in Electronic Voting Machine
indelible ink should be applied as a line from the top end of the nail to the bottom of the first joint of the left middle finger.
(By Order of the Tamil Nadu State Election Commission)
