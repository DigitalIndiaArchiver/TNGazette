National Highways Authority of India has taken up Six laning/Four laning of National Highways under National Highways
Development programme. In the urban areas, National Highways Authority of India has taken up construction of bye-
passes in order to avoid entry of traffic inside the towns. However due to delay in entrustment of works to Concessionaire
and slow progress of these projects, the existing National Highways stretches in urban areas have become very damaged.
The abandoned National Highways stretches are not maintained properly by National Highways Authority of India. The
poor status of the National Highways abandoned stretches brought severe criticism from the public.
2. The Chief Engineer (National Highways) Highways Department in the letter first read above has sent a proposal
in consultation with Project Directors concerned and recommended the 11 stretches (81.27 Kms) can be taken over
immediately at an approximate cost of Rs. 8233.00 lakhs for permanent restoration by the Construction and Maintenance
wing of the Highways Department for putting an end to the further deterioration of these roads.
3. The Chief Engineer (Construction and Maintenance) Highways Department in the letter second read above has
therefore, requested the Government, to declare the 11 National Highways stretches as State Highways urban stretches
as a special case to take up permanent restoration works so as to avoid public criticism.
