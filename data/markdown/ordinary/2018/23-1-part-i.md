PUBLIC DEPARTMENT
(Protocal-III)
[G.O. Ms. No. 370, Public (Protocal-III), 24th May 2018.]
No.I/PUPRO/21/2018.—Major APS Rathore (IC-75630) from 
Indian Army has been appointed as Aide-De-Camp (Army) to 
Hon'ble Governor of Tamil Nadu on deputation basis for a period 
of two years with effect from 09-02-2018 FN on the terms and 
conditions of the sevices intimated by Indian Army, New Delhi, 
Military Secretary, Integrated HQ of MOD (Army), Military 
Secretary's Branch, MS 3C, South Block, New Delhi 110 011,
Lr. No. A/13013/ADC/Policy/MS 3C, dated 09-02-2018.
Terms and Conditions
1. General. (Min of Def letter no. 4(15)58/99/S/D(MS) 
dated 25th February 1970) The ofﬁ cer belongs to Indian 
Army holding the rank of Major. The ofﬁ cer has the option to 
get his pay drawn in the parent cadre plus Deputation Duty 
Allowance at the rates prescribed by Government of India 
letter No.1(26)/97/VIII/D (Pay/Services) dated 29th February 
2000 as amended from time to time. 
2.Tenure. The period of deputation will be for two years with 
effect from 09th February 2018 to 08th February 2020 (Refer 
Integrated Headquarters of Ministry of Defence (Army), Military 
Secretary's Branch (MS 3C) letter No A/ 13013/ADC/Policy/ 
MS 3C dated 09th February 18 (Copy attached). Under no 
circumstances, this period will be extended. Undertaking given 
by the ofﬁ cer will be held against the ofﬁ cer if any request 
for extension is received from any quarter whatsoever, and 
ofﬁ cer himself will be responsible for any adverse effects on 
his career consequently. The ofﬁ cer, is however, liable to be 
recalled at any time if the exigencies of service so necessitate, 
without relief. 
3. Turnover.The borrowing organization will forward 
requirement of fresh ofﬁ cer's panel directly to MoD / D(MS) 
four to six months in advance from the date of the deputation 
of the present incumbent is to expire. Accordingly, the Governor 
will interview fresh ofﬁ cers and ofﬁ cer selected will be posted 
as relief four days before the present incumbent moves out. If 
less than four months notice is given, the present incumbent 
will move out on due date on expiry of his deputation period. 
Relief will report at the earliest on issue of posting with 30 
days notice. 
4. Basic Pay.The Basic Pay will be ﬁ xed on the basis of 
option given by the ofﬁ cer. Military Service Pay and Grade 
Pay will also be included as part of basic 'Pay' for all purpose. 
5. Dearness Allowance. Officer will be entitled for 
Dearness Allowance on 'Pay' at the prescribed rates laid down 
by the Government of India from time to time. 
6. Ration Allowance. Ofﬁ cer will be entitled to draw ration 
allowance as prescribed by the Government of India. 
7. Accommodation. He will be entitled to rent free 
accommodation. He will also be entitled to free supply of water 
and electricity up to a limit of 6.25% of his basic pay in both 
and up to 5% of pay for electricity only, as per rates amended 
by Government of India from time to time. The ofﬁ cer will be 
required to surrender the defence pool accommodation, he 
will be treated as unauthorized occupant and recovery would 
be regulated under Para 14(b) of Q & R i.e. assessed rent 
or market rent whichever is more.
8. House Rent Allowance. The ofﬁ cer will be entitled for 
HRA at prescribed rates laid down from time to time where 
government accommodation cannot be provided to him. He 
is also entitled for reimbursement where accommodation is 
hired by ofﬁ cer in the civil area due to non-availability of 
Government accommodation. 
9. Transport Allowance. The ofﬁ cer will be admissible 
for TA, as per prescribed rates based on classiﬁ cation of the 
city in which he is posted, from time to time. 
10. Counter Insurgency/Field/Risk/Uncongenial Area 
Allowance. The Ofﬁ cer will be entitled to Counter Insurgency 
/ Field / Risk / Uncongenial Area Allowance as admissible 
to equivalent Army Ofﬁ cer, if applicable, in the area where 
ofﬁ cer is posted. 
11. Kit Maintenance Allowance. The ofﬁ cer belongs 
to Indian Army and he should be ready for Army duties in 
exigencies of circumstances. Therefore, he should maintain 
his kit. The Indian Army Ofﬁ cers are provided Kit Maintenance 
allowance that would be admissible to the ofﬁ cer during 
deputation as per GOI, MoD letter No 1 (22)/97/D. (Pay / 
Services) dated 08th January 1998 as amended from time 
to time. 
12. Traveling Allowance (TA) and Joining Time. The 
beneﬁ ts of TA (including Composite Transfer Grant)• on transfer 
from Army on deputation and on reversion to his parent cadre 
(both ways) will be admissible to the ofﬁ cer and his family at 
the same rate as applicable to Army Ofﬁ cers while serving in 
Army. The expenditure on this account will be borne by the 
Borrowing Agency. The amount of advance of TA granted to the 
ofﬁ cer transferred on deputation basis should be reimbursed 
to the PCDA (O), Pune, by boroowing agency in lump sum by 
sending a Cheque or Demand Draft. Concerning advance of 
TA sanctioned to the ofﬁ cer on his reversion from deputation 
to his parent cadre, the ofﬁ cer should forward the TA claim 
directly to borrowing agency for ﬁ nal settlement within a period 
of one month. The Ofﬁ cer will be entitled for joining time from 
Army to Borrowing Agency and on reversion, on completion 
of his deputation, to Army under the Rules of Government of 
India. The expenditure for joining time pay on this account 
will be borne by the Borrowing Agency and reimbursed to 
PCDA (O), Pune. 
13. Beneﬁ ts of Leave Rules. The ofﬁ cer will be governed 
by the Defence Service Rules for the beneﬁ ts of Leave Rules 
applicable to the Army Ofﬁ cers during his deputation period, if 
he opts for beneﬁ ts of his parent pay. The Borrowing Agency 
will be liable to pay leave salary in respect of disability incurred 
in or during the deputation even if such a disability manifests 
itself at the end of deputation at the termination of deputation. 
14. Medical Beneﬁ ts. Borrowing Agency will reimburse 
medical expenses incurred by the ofﬁ cer for self and his entitled 
family members, as per Rules of the Borrowing Agency for 
medical beneﬁ ts, during the period of deputation on actual 
basis. Ofﬁ cer will be entitled for free medical aid, in case a 
military hospital exists in the station.

15. Leave and Leave Allowances. Leave, leave 
allowances and leave travel concession will be regulated by 
Army Rules. Where railway warrants, or concession vouchers 
are normally issued under such rules, the ofﬁ cer will be 
allowed cash value in lieu or railway warrants or concession 
voucher can be obtained from nearest SSO / OC station, in 
accordance with the relevant rules.  
16. Ofﬁ cer Contribution to Army Group Insurance Fund 
(AGIF), Defence Service Ofﬁ cers Provident Fund Scheme 
(DSOPF) and Army Ofﬁ cers Benevolent Fund (AOBF). The 
Ofﬁ cer will continue to subscribe to AGIF, DSOPF and AOBF 
during deputation on prescribed rates from time to time. The 
amount will be sent to AGI, DSOPF and AOBF through CDA 
(0 monthly through cheques. 
17. Leave Salary and Pension Contribution during 
Deputation. The Borrowing Agency shall pay Salary and 
Pension contribution, at prescribed rates, during the deputation 
i.e. following the date of relieving from Iridian Army till he 
joins the parent cadre on completion of deputation with 
the Borrowing Agency as per existing rules and regulations 
amended from time to time. 
18. Children Education Allowance. During the period of 
deputation, the ofﬁ cer will be eligible to claim the beneﬁ ts under 
Children Education Allowance from the Borrowing Agency at 
prescribed rates in respect of his children, from time to time, 
as admissible. 
19. Beneﬁ ts under Pension.The ofﬁ cer will be regulated 
by Defence Service Rules for pensionary beneﬁ ts including 
Pension, Gratuity, Commutation of Pension, Leave Encashment 
etc., as per Army rates of pay as drawn by the ofﬁ cer from 
time to time. 
20. General Rules. In all matters relating to conditions of 
service not speciﬁ cally covered by the above items, the ofﬁ cer 
shall be governed by the Rules and Regulations applicable to 
the ofﬁ cers in the Indian Army, from time to time. 
21. Income Tax. Salary is subject to normal income tax 
rules. 
22. Speciﬁ c Terms and Conditions of Service Ofﬁ cers 
Who Draw Service Rates of Pay While on Deputation 
(Annexure II to Min of Def letter No 4 (15)/ /58/99/S/D(MS) 
dated 25th Feb 1970). 
(a) Rank.Ofﬁ cers posted as AsDC with six years of 
reckonable service will hold the rank of Major and those with 
less than six years of service will hold the rank of acting Major.
(b) Service. The entire period of deputation will count in 
full as effective commissioned service for purpose of seniority, 
promotion, increment of pay, pensionary awards and leave 
etc in the Army. 
(c) Pay & Allowances. At service rates admissible under 
P&A regulations (Ofﬁ cers) Army. 
(d) Outﬁ t Allowance. The period of deputation of the 
ofﬁ cer, who draw service rates of pay and allowances and who 
is required to wear and continues to wear various ceremonial 
and other uniform while in civil employment, reckons in full 
towards the qualifying service for the drawl of renewal Outﬁ t 
Allowance. If the allowance becomes due, during the period 
of deputation, it will be paid to the ofﬁ cer and the expenditure, 
as a whole, will be borne by the borrowing authorities. Any 
special accoutrement and accessories required by the ofﬁ cer 
for ofﬁ cial purpose in the capacity of appointment held in the 
borrowing agency, he will be entitled for the special allowance 
by the borrowing organization to be borne by them. 
(e) Leave. As per Leave Rules for the service, Vol I Army, 
the liability for leave salary would be regulated on allocation 
basis in accordance with photographs 1 and 2 of Appendix 
3 'B' (Il) of account code Vol I. 
23. Interpretation/Relaxation of Rules. In case of any 
hardship and any doubt, the power of interpreting the rules/
regulations and their relaxation, if any, is reserved with 
Integrated Headquarters of Ministry of Defence (Army).
