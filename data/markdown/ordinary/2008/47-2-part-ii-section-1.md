No.II(1) BCMBCMW/58/2008.—The following office memorandum of the Government of India, Ministry of Human
Resource Development Department of Higher Education, Shastri Bhavan, New Delhi, dated 13th October 2008, is
republished:—
OFFICE  MEMORANDUM
Subject:—Revision of income criteria to exclude socially advanced persons/sections (Creamy Layer) from Other
Backward Classes (OBCs).
Following the decision of the Supreme Court delivered on 16th November 1992 in the case of Indra Sawhney and
others Vs. Union of India and others, the Government of India had constituted an expert committee to make
recommendations on the socio-economic criteria to be adopted for excluding the ‘Creamy Layer’ from amongst OBCs.
The report of the Expert Committee was considered and accepted by the Government and the categories of the
persons/sections to be excluded from the purview of the reservation orders for OBCs were notified in the schedule to the
Office Memorandum No.36012/22/93-Estt.(SCT), dated 8th September 1993 issued by the Department of Personnel and
Training (DOPT).
2. The Committee while specifying the criteria for application of the rule of exclusion of socially advanced persons
amongst OBCs, inter-alia, recommended review of income criteria after every three years.  The Committee observed
“Since the rupee value is bound to undergo change, the income criteria in terms of rupee as stated above, will
accordingly stand modified with the change in value of rupee.  The modification exercise may, normally speaking,
be undertaken every three years but if the situation so demands the interregnum  may be less.”
3. Keeping in view the above recommendations of the Expert Committee, the Government of India decided to constitute
a review Committee consider the issue of modification of the income criteria laid down by the Expert Committee for
exclusion of OBCs as per Creamy Layer concept.
4. Subsequently, it was decided that the work relating to review of income criteria to exclude creamy layer from OBCs
may be entrusted to the National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC).  On the basis of the recommendation of
the NCBC, the income limit for determining the Creamy Layer among the backward classes was raised from Rs. 1.00
lakh to Rs. 2.5 lakhs and DOPT issued an OM No. 36033/3/2004-Estt. (Res.), dated 9th March 2004 to this effect.
5. As the Creamy Layer criterion was to be revised after expiry of three years from the last revision, the NCBC was
again requested on 27th December 2007 to review the existing ceiling of income/wealth to determine the Creamy Layer
amongst the OBCs.
6. The NCBC considered the socio-ecomomic development of the Other Backward Classes appearing in various
surveys and reports published by the Government of India and also took into consideration the increasing  trend in the
GDP, net per capita income, all round economic growth, acceleration in inflation in primary commodities and continued
high escalation in price of the commodities in fuel group.  There has been a steady increase in the costs for medical,
transport and education.  The recommendation of the Sixth Central Pay Commission recommending the existing rates
of most of the allowances to be doubled and has also been taken note of.  The Commission taking into consideration
the above factors and the time by which the report will be accepted and implemented, arrived at a figure of
Rs. 4.5 lakhs.
7. The Commission submitted its reports on the 1st July 2008 to the Government of India and recommended that “The
Income limit for determining the creamy layer among Other Backward Classes (OBC) be raised from Rs. 2.5 lakhs to
Rs. 4.5 lakhs”.

8. With the approval of the competent authority, the recommendation of the NCBC was accepted to raise the income
limit for determining the creamy layer among Other Backward Classes (OBCs) from Rs. 2.5 lakhs to Rs. 4.50 lakhs.
Accordingly, the existing entry against category VI in the Schedule to the OM No. 36033/3/2004-Estt.(Res.) dated
9th March 2004 issued by the DOPT of gross annual income of Rs. 2.5 lakhs, shall be read as Rs. 4.5 lakhs for the
purpose of determination of creamy layer for admission of OBC candidates into the Central Educational Institutions.
9. The provision of this Office Memorandum takes effect immediately in respect of admissions into Central Educational
Institutions.

II(1)/BCMBCMW/59/2008.— The following Office Memorandum of the Government of India, Ministry of Personnel, Public
Grievances and Pensions, Department of Personnel and Training, New Delhi, dated 14th October, 2008, is republished:—
OFFICE  MEMORANDUM
Subject.—Revision of income criteria to exclude socially advanced persons/sections (Creamy Layer) from the
purview of reservation for Other Backward Classes (OBCs).
The undersigned is directed to invite attention to this Department’s O.M. No.36012/22/93-Estt.(SCT), dated 8th September,
1993 which inter alia provided that sons and daughters of persons having gross annual income of Rs.1 lakh or above
for a period of three consecutive years would fall within the creamy layer and would not be entitled to get the benefit of
reservation available to the Other Backward Classes. The limit of income for determining the creamy layer status was
raised to Rs. 2.5 lakhs vide this Department’s OM of even number dated 9th March 2004.  It has now been decided to
raise the income limit from Rs. 2.5 lakhs to Rs. 4.5 lakhs per annum for determining the creamy layer amongst the OBCs.
