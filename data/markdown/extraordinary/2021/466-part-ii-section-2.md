WHEREAS on considering the prevailing Covid-19 pandemic scenario and as per the directives of 
Government of India, Ministry of Home Aﬀ airs, State-wide lockdown/restrictions was enforced throughout the 
State with eﬀ ect from 4.00 A.M. of 10.05.2021 to 31.10.2021, in order to control the spread of COVID-19. 
 2. The Hon’ble Chief Minister has conducted review meeting with oﬃ  cers to evaluate the Corona 
prevention activities, Spread of corona virus in neighbouring states and measures to be taken to control the 
spread of Corona infection due to possible large gathering of public during the upcoming festival season. 
3. Now, based on the above review meeting, the Government hereby order, under Disaster Management 
Act, 2005 to continue the existing restrictions upto 31.10.2021 as in the Government Order G.O.(Ms.) 
No. 552, Revenue and Disaster Management (DM-IV) Department, 11th September 2021  with the following 
relaxations:-
Relaxations:
To facilitate public to redress their grievances locally, conduct of Public Grievance Day 
meetings by the Collectors are permitted in all the District collectorates on all Mondays 
by following Standard Operating Procedures (SOP).

Conduct of Monthly Farmers' Grievance Day meeting is permitted in all the District collectorates by 
following Standard Operating Procedures (SOP).
Based on the suggestions made by the medical experts, Educationist and parents, classes for 9th, 
10th, 11th and 12th standards in Schools and Colleges are already permitted to function. Similarly, 
taking into account the views expressed by the Medical Experts, Educationist and Parents and 
considering the psychological well-being of the students and to avoid huge learning gap in the society 
and in the interest of students’ education, the direct classes for 1st to 8th standards shall 
be permitted in all the schools with eﬀ ect from 01.11.2021 by duly following the appropriate 
standard operating procedures. The School Education Department shall take necessary preparatory 
action and prescribe appropriate standard operating procedures for conducting classes regularly.
All allowed activities in existence shall continue to be permitted with existing restrictions.
Public worship in the religious places on Friday, Saturday and Sunday will not be permitted.
Public gatherings in religious festivals/consecrations, political, socio-cultural and religion related 
congregation, which were already prohibited, will continue to be prohibited.
 General
i) 
Public are requested to take vaccine in the special vaccination camp conducted by the District Collectors/ the 
Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation and Local Bodies.
ii) 
The following important Standard Operating Procedures shall be strictly adhered to all the shops and places 
of Public gathering:-


Shops shall have mandatory hand hygiene (hand sanitizer with dispenser) at the entrance. 


Management shall ensure that the workers and customers wear face masks compulsorily.


All shops and establishments shall ensure that the doors and windows are kept open for 
cross ventilation. In order to ensure social distancing norms, more number of persons shall not be 
allowed at a time inside the shops.


Speciﬁ c markings with suﬃ  cient distance may be made at the entrance of the shops to 
manage the general public standing in the queue.
 

Action will be taken against the commercial/other organizations which violate the above 
guidelines and prescribed limit of customers.
iii) 
‘Test- Track- Treat- Vaccination- Covid-19 appropriate behaviour’ principle shall be strictly adhered to.
iv) 
In order to contain the spread of Covid-19, the Containment Zones shall be demarcated upto Micro Level and 
the containment measures including vaccination to the people in this area shall be initiated by the District Collectors 
and the Local Bodies as per the Standard Operating Procedures.
v) 
Within the demarcated Containment Zones, containment measures shall be scrupulously followed, as under:


Only essential activities shall be allowed in the Containment Zones. No activity, except 
medical emergencies and supply of essential goods and services shall be permitted.


There shall be intensive house-to-house surveillance by surveillance teams formed for the 
purpose.
4. The public are requested to be careful and avoid moving to crowded places during the upcoming festival 
season as the spread of third wave of corona could be avoided only by following such preventive measures. 
All commercial establishment are also requested to follow the preventive measure without any lapses.
5. The public are also requested to seek medical advice of the nearest hospital as soon as symptoms of infection 
appear.
