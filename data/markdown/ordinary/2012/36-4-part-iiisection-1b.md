ENVIRONMENT AND FORESTS DEPARTMENT.
Amendments to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Forest Subordinate Service.
[G. O. Ms. No.206, Environment and Forests (FR-2), 13th August 2012.]
No. SRO B-43/2012.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution
of India, the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the Special Rules for the
Tamil Nadu Forest Subordinate Service (Section 12 of Part III-B in Volume III of the Tamil Nadu Services
Manual, 1970).
2. The amendments hereby made shall come into force on 13th August 2012.
AMENDMENTS.
In the said Special Rules,—
(1) for rule 2-A, the following rule shall be substituted, namely:—
“ 2-A, Appointment as Forest Watcher.— Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-rule (a) of rule 2, the
posts of Forest Watcher shall be filled up from among the Social Forestry Workers, Plot Watchers or the
Scheduled Tribes engaged as Anti-Poaching Watchers, who have rendered service as such for a period of not less
than ten years in total as on the 1st January of the year in which recruitment to the post of Forest Watcher is made,
or the Scheduled Tribe Youths living in forest settlements of Eastern or Western Ghats:
Provided that thirty percent of the total vacancies in the post of Forest Watcher shall be filled up equally from
among the Scheduled Tribes engaged as Anti-Poaching watchers and the Scheduled Tribe Youths living in the
forest settlements of Eastern or Western Ghats.”:
(2) in rule 4, in sub-rule (a),—
(i) in the last proviso thereunder for the expression “posts of Forest Watcher and Mali”, the expression
“post of Mali shall be substituted;
(ii) after the last proviso, as so amended, the following proviso shall be added, namely:-
“Provided also that in the case of direct recruitment to the post of Forest Watcher, the age limit specified above
shall not apply to the persons in the State Level Seniority list of Social Forestry Workers and Plot Watchers and
to the Scheduled Tribes engaged as Anti-Poaching Watchers.”.;
(3) in rule 5, in the Table,—
(i) for the proviso to item (ii) in column(3), against the method “Promotion of Forest Watchers” in column (2)
and against the category “Forest Guards” in column(1) thereof the following proviso shall be substituted, namely:—
“Provided that this qualification shall not be applicable to the Malis, who were appointed from among the Social
Forestry Workers and Plot Watchers and to the Forest Watcher, who were appointed from among the Social
Forestry Workers, Plot Watchers and Scheduled Tribes engaged as Anti-Poaching Watchers.”;
(ii) in the proviso to item (a) in column (3), against the method “Direct recruitment” in column (2), and
against the category “Forest Watchers” in column (1) thereof , after the words “Social Forestry Workers and Plot
Watchers”, the words “and the Scheduled Tribes engaged as Anti Poaching Watchers” shall be inserted.”;
C.V. SANKAR,
Principal Secretary to Government.

Amendment to Ad hoc Rules relating to the Post of Personal Assistant to Directorate of Prosecution.
[G. O. Ms. No. 632, Home (Courts-VIA), 24th August 2012.]
No. SRO B-45/2012.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendment to the rules issued with the Home Department Notification
No. SRO B-33-2003 published at pages 46 to 48 of Part III—Section 1(b) of the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, dated the
9th April 2003.
2. The amendment shall be deemed to have come into force on and from the date of issue of orders.
AMENDMENT.
In the said Rules, in rule 3 in sub- rule (a) in the TABLE for the entries in column (3), against the serial number 5 in
column (1), the following entries shall be substituted, namely:—
(i) “By recruitment by transfer from among the holders of the post of Superintendent, in the Directorate of
Prosecution in the Tamil Nadu Ministerial Service;
(ii) By transfer from among the holders of the post of Section Officer in Secretariat, if no suitable person is available
by the above method of appointment; or
(iii) By transfer from among the holders of the post of Personal Assistant (Administration) in the Police Department,
if no suitable person is available by the above two methods of appointment.”
R. RAJAGOPAL,
Principal Secretary to Government.
