In the Government Order first read above, the Government have bifurcated the Department of Inspectorate of Factories
from the Labour Department and functioning as a separate department with effect from August 1987 with the Chief
Inspector of Factories as the Head of the Department.
2. The Hon’ble Minister (Labour) while moving this Department’s demand on the floor of the Assembly on 11-5-2012
has made an announcement that the renaming of the Department of ‘Inspectorate of Factories’ as ‘Directorate of Industrial
Safety and Health’ and re-designation of ‘Chief Inspector of Factories’ as ‘Director of Industrial Safety and Health’.
3. In the letter second read above, the Chief Inspector of Factories has stated that the Department of Inspectorate of
Factories is bifurcated from the Labour Department and functioning as a separate department with effect from August 1987
with the Chief Inspector of Factories as the Head of the Department and it is enforcing the Factories Act 1948 and other
14 allied Labour Legislations in the registered factories and the department was named as Inspectorate of Factories and

5.  The Chief Inspector of Factories has requested that with a view to express the functional importance, the existing
name of the Inspectorate of Factories department may be renamed  as “Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health” and
also nomenclature of the Officers may be re-designated and has sent necessary proposal for amendment to the Tamil
Nadu Factory Service.
6. After careful examination, the Government have decided to accept the proposal of the Chief Inspector of Factories
and accordingly direct that the name of the office of the ‘Inspectorate of Factories Department’ be changed as ‘Directorate
of Industrial Safety and Health’ and the following posts be re-designated as under, without any up-gradation, without any
change in the scale of pay and without any additional financial commitment to Government.
