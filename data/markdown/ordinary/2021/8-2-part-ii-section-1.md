--------
Order: No. 54, Backward Classes, Most Backward Classes and Minorities Welfare (BCC), 15th July 2020.
Various representations were received for issuance of Backward Classes certiﬁ cate to Ezhuva and Thiyya Communities. 
The views of the Tamil Nadu Backward Classes Commission were obtained on the requests of Ezhuva and Thiya Communities. 
In continuation, a Committee was constituted to examine the requests of Ezhuva and Thiyya Communities for issuance of 
Backward Classes Community Certiﬁ cate in the State, vide Government Order fourth read above.
 2. In the Government Order ﬁ rst read above, Ezhuva Community in Kanniyakumari District and Shencottah Taluk of 
Tirunelveli District (Serial No.23 of the List of Backward Classes) were notiﬁ ed as Backward Classes in Tamil Nadu.
 3. In the Government Order Second read above, it has been ordered to issue community certiﬁ cates to all the communities, 
declared or notiﬁ ed as Backward Class or Most Backward Class or Denotiﬁ ed Community in the restricted area or territory, 
as and when they migrate to other area or territory, declaring the same status of the respective community in the restricted 
area or territory by the authorities, competent to issue community certiﬁ cates in the migrated area or territory. 
4. The Committee constituted to examine the requests of Ezhuva and Thiyya Communities for issuance of Backward 
Classes Certiﬁ cate throughout the State, has recommended in its report ﬁ fth read above, as follows:-
“The members of Ezhuva community, entered in Serial No.23 of the List of Backward Classes notiﬁ ed in G.O.(Ms) No.85, 
Backward Classes, Most Backward Classes & Minorities Welfare Department, dated 29-07-2008, may be granted Backward 
Classes community certiﬁ cate throughout the State, as they are distributed in all the districts of the Tamil Nadu. The Committee 
also recommends that the orders issued in para 4 of G.O.(Ms) No.58, Social Welfare Department, dated 23-01-1976, may 
be followed in these cases.”
5. The Government after careful consideration, accept the recommendation of the Committee and clarify that the Backward 
Classes Community Certiﬁ cate may be issued to Ezhuva Community throughout the State of Tamil Nadu considering their 
presence in all the districts of the State, as recorded in the survey conducted during the year 1983, by the Tamil Nadu Second 
Backward Classes Commission (Ambasankar Commission). The District Collectors and the Certiﬁ cate Issuing Authorities are also 
instructed to adhere the following orders issued in para 4 of G.O.(Ms) No.58, Social Welfare Department, dated 23.01.1976, 
while issuing the community certiﬁ cate to the members of Ezhuva Community in the State of Tamil Nadu:-
“Only persons who belong to Tamil Nadu and to one of the communities included in the list of Backward Classes shall 
be treated as Backward Classes. Persons belonging to other States shall not be treated as Backward Classes in this State 
even though they may belong to one of the communities included in the list of Backward Classes.”

[BACKWARD CLASSES, MOST BACKWARD CLASSES AND MINORITIES WELFARE – ISSUANCE OF COMMUNITY 
CERTIFICATE OF THE CHILDREN BORN TO THE PARENTS BELONGING TO TWO DIFFERENT CASTES – 
CLARIFICATIONS – ISSUED.]
The following Government Order is Published.—
[G.O. Ms. No. 8, Backward Classes, Most Backward Classes and Minorities Welfare (BCC), 9th Feburay 2021,
Thai 27,  Saarvari ,Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2052.]
Rඍඉඌ :
G.O.Ms. No. 477, Social Welfare Department, dated 27-06-1975
--------
Order: No. 8, Backward Classes, Most Backward Classes and Minorities Welfare (BCC), 9th February 2021.
Vide G.O read above, Government have been extending certain concessions to the members of Scheduled Tribes, Scheduled 
Castes and Backward classes from time to time and orders were issued stating that children born out of inter-caste marriages, 
shall be considered to belong to either the community of the father or the community of the mother according to the declaration 
of the parents.
 2) Various requests have now been received by the Government to issue community certiﬁ cates to children born out of inter 
caste marriages, based on the caste of either the father or the mother.
 3) The Government after careful examination hereby clariﬁ es that the children born out of marriage between parents of two 
diﬀ erent castes shall be considered to belong to either the caste of the father or the caste of the mother based on the declaration 
of the parent/s.
 4) Based on this declaration of the parent/s, Revenue authorities are hereby instructed to issue appropriate ‘Community 
Certiﬁ cates’ (Backward Classes/Most Backward Classes/ Denotiﬁ ed Communities/ Other Backward Classes/ Scheduled Caste 
/ Scheduled Tribe for Government of Tamil Nadu/ Government of India) to such children according to their eligibility, by virtue of 
belonging to such a caste. 
 5) The Commissioner of Revenue Administration is requested to give suitable instructions to the Community Certiﬁ cate 
issuing Authorities as per the clariﬁ cation issued in para 4 above.
(By Order of the Governor)
 
 
 
CHANDRA MOHAN. B,
