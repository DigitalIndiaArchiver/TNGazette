2. The Office of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Tirunelveli, Official Receiver, Tirunelveli and Judicial Magistrate, Shencottah, 
Nanguneri, Cheranmahadevi, Sivagiri and Radhapuram will remain open throughout the vacation and holidays declared above.
3. No Plaint or petition except application for grant of copies and Batta Memos for service and execution of Process will be 
received by the Civil Courts during the Vacation.
4. The Office of the Copyist, Process Service and Execution Department shall be kept open throughout the vacation and that 
no arrest warrant shall be executed during the vacation. The Office of the other departments shall remain closed. 
	
Arrangements shall, however, be made.
	
(i). for furnishing the Copies and sending Process.
	
(ii). for transmitting the records to the High Court required in pending Proceedings.
	
(iii). for attending all administrative correspondence.
5. In view of the instructions contained in the Criminal Rules of Practice and Circular Orders and as ordered in High Court's 
Circular R.o.c. No.2/64-C-3, dated: 29th April, 1964 applications in urgent Criminal matters should be made to the High Court. 
However, urgent applications can also be made to the vacation Sessions Judge, Tirunelveli if appointed to hear urgent Criminal 
applications relating to Tirunelveli District.
6. Under Section 30 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act as amended by Section 4 of the Tamil Nadu Act, 17 of 1959 if a 
vacation Civil Judge is appointed for Tirunelveli District. Suits, Appeals and other proceedings of urgent nature will be received and 
disposed of by him in terms of the Rules that may be framed in this regard.
7. In terms of High Court's Circular P.Dis. No. 573/60, dated 22nd August 1960 the Office of the Estate Abolition Tribunal, 
Tirunelveli will function in full for the purpose of receiving papers etc., during the vacation.
