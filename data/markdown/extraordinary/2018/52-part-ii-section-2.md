WHEREAS minimum rates of wages were ﬁ xed for the Hosiery Manufactory in G.O.(Ms.) No.4844, 
Industries, Labour and Co-operation Department, dated 21-10-1960 and no revision of minimum rates of 
wages have been made for the employment in Hosiery manufactory afterwards;
AND WHEREAS no minimum rates of wages were ﬁ xed for the Hosiery manufactory including Knitwear 
which is included in the said manufactory, wage settlement were entered into between the employers and 
employees of the said manufactory under section 12(3) of the Industrial Disputes Act,1947 (Central Act XIV 
of 1947) and last settlement was entered into before the Deputy Commissioner of Labour, Coimbatore in 
Na.Ka.No.2/1083/2012, dated 16-2-2012 which was in force from 30-1-2012 to 29-1-2016;
AND WHEREAS, the Government decided to revise the minimum rates of wages in the Hosiery 
manufactory which includes Knitwear Industry in G.O.Ms.No.111, Labour and Employment (J1) Department, 
dated 12-7-2012 and accordingly constituted a  Committee under Section 5(1) (a) of the Minimum Wages 
Act,1948 (Central Act XI of 1948) by including the employers and employees in Hosiery and Knitwear 
manufactory;
AND WHEREAS minimum rates of wages were revised for Tailoring Industry in G.O. (2D) No.59, 
Labour and Employment (J1) Department, dated 10-10-2014, wherein a separate wages for Export garments 
manufactory under Tailoring Industry were ﬁ xed;
AND WHEREAS, the President of Tiruppur Exporters’ Association in their Letters, dated 16-1-2014 
and 30-1-2015 represented  the Commissioner of Labour for clariﬁ cation and the Commissioner of Labour 
in Letter No.Z3/57821/2014, dated 4-2-2015 clariﬁ ed that a separate committee as stated in para 3 above

to ﬁ x the revised minimum wages for the employment in Hosiery manufactory was formed and it is under 
process for treating the Knitwear manufactory as a sub-entity;
AND WHEREAS, the Committee constituted under Section 5(1) (a) of the Minimum Wages Act,1948 
(Central Act XI of 1948), after taking into consideration the working conditions of the hosiery and knitwear 
industry, submitted its report to the Government vide Letter No. e.f.v©. M3/6075/2012, dated 10-07-2014 
by the Chairman, Minimum Wages Advisory Committee/Deputy Commissioner of Labour, Coimbatore–18; 
AND WHEREAS based on the said report and also in consultation with Minimum Wages (State) 
Advisory Board, the Government issued G.O. (2D) No. 5, Labour and Employment (J1) Department, dated 
27-01-2016;
AND WHEREAS, there is confusion as to whether the export of the Hosiery  Manufactory including 
knitwear  come under G.O. (2D) No. 59, Labour and Employment (J1) Department, dated 10-10-2014, 
which is applicable to Tailoring industry, since there is a separate heading in the said Government Order 
for export garment manufactory;
AND WHEREAS the High Court of Madras in its Order, dated 27-04-2017 in Review Petition No. 27 
to 51 of 2017 directed to approach the Government for seeking clariﬁ cation as to the applicability of the 
relevant Government Orders; 
AND WHEREAS  in order to avoid confusion to implement G.O. (2D) No.5, Labour and Employment 
(J1) Department, dated 27-01-2016 for the Hosiery manufactory including Knitwear manufactory, the 
Government have decided to amend the Notiﬁ cation issued in the said Government Order in consultation 
with the Minimum Wages (State) Advisory Board; 
NOW, THEREFORE,  in exercise of the powers conferred under sub-section (1) of Section 10 of 
the Minimum Wages Act, 1948 (Central Act XI of 1948), the Governor of Tamil Nadu, in consultation 
with Minimum Wages (State) Advisory Board  hereby makes the following amendment to Labour and 
Employment Department Notiﬁ cation No.II(2)/LE/120/2016, published at page 127 of Part II – Section 2 of 
the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, dated the 17th February 2016, namely:-
AMENDMENT
In the said Notiﬁ cation, in the Schedule, the following Explanation shall be added at the end, namely:- 
“Explanation.-For the purpose of this Schedule, the Hosiery manufactory includes knitwear manufactory, 
whether for export or domestic purpose”.
MANGAT RAM SHARMA,
Principal Secretary to Government.
