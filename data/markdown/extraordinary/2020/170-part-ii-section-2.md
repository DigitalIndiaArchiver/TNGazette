TAMIL  NADU  GOVERNMENT  GAZETTE  EXTRAORDINARY
Based on the recommendations given by the Expert Committee formed by the Government and decision taken by the 
State Disaster Management Authority meeting held on 20-4-2020, orders were issued in the order fourth read above to extend 
the lockdown without any change till 3rd May 2020 and not avail of the entire relaxations which was provided for in the 
Rural areas and non-containment zones. Further certain relaxations were provided in the orders fifth and sixth read above.
The Principal Secretary/Commissioner of Revenue Administration has stated that Hon’ble Chief Minister reviewed the 
situation due to the outbreak of COVID-19 in the State and based on the discussion with the Medical and Public Health 
Experts a decision was taken on them by Hon’ble Chief Minister and issued as Press Release dated 24-4-2020. Though the 
spread of the Corona virus (Covid-19) has come under control in rural areas, there are possible chances for more spread 
of Corona Virus in the urban pockets particularly in the areas where people gather in large number and hence there is an 
increase in the number of Covid-19 cases in Corporations like Chennai, Coimbatore and Madurai.
During the discussion with the Medical and Public Health Experts, they have observed that the spread of Covid-19 could 
be brought under control only if the lock down is further intensified in Urban areas. Considering the current situation and 
the observations of the Medical and Public Health Experts, the Government have decided to further intensify the lock down 
measures in the Urban areas and ordered the following measures under the Disaster Management Act, 2005 :
•	
To intensify the present lock down measure, a complete lock down will be implemented in the Corporation limits 
of Chennai, Coimbatore and Madurai from 26-4-2020 Sunday 6.00 A.M. to 29-4-2020 Wednesday 9.00 P.M.
•	
Complete lock down will also be implemented in Corporation limits of Salem and Tiruppur from 26-4-2020 Sunday 
6.00 A.M. to 28-4-2020 Tuesday 9.00 P.M.
During this lock down period the following essential services alone would be permitted
(1)	
Hospitals, Medical Labs, pharmaceutical shops, Milk supply, Ambulance and Hearse Vehicle Services and allied 
Medical related activities.
(2)	
Departments involved in essential services viz., Secretariat, Health and Family Welfare , Police, Revenue and 
Disaster Management, Electricity, Aavin, Local Bodies, and Drinking Water Supply Departments with required 
staff only.
(3)	
Central Government Offices and Banks for the maintenance of essential services with attendance not exceeding 
the 33 per cent of total staff. 
(4)	
 Petrol and Diesel Bunks (8 AM to 12 Noon only), LPG, Gas agencies, Public Distribution System shops, connected 
Food Corporation of India and Tamil Nadu Civil Supplies Corporation Godowns and its transportation.
(5)	
Amma Canteens, ATMs will function normally.
(6)	
Only Home delivery of Parcel Foods ordered through phones from hotels / restaurants is permitted.
(7)	
Homes for senior citizens / physically challenged/ orphans and caregivers of senior citizens residing in their 
homes are permitted.
(8)	
The Community kitchens run by District Administration, Social Welfare and other local bodies for providing food 
to the destitutes will continue to function.  
(9)	
NGOs and Organisations helping the poor and needy would be permitted after obtaining prior permission from 
the concerned  authorities.
(10)	 Vegetable markets like Koyambedu will be permitted to operate with certain restrictions. Similarly, mobile units 
for sale of vegetables and fruits is permitted. The shops which were permitted hitherto are not allowed to open 
in these days.
(11)	 Print and Electronic Media.
Apart from the essential services permitted above, all other activities are not allowed in the above period.  Other Government 
offices (including Registration Offices) shall not function. IT sector Staff may work from home and all private establishments 
will remain closed. 
The existing restrictions/permissions imposed in the remaining areas other than the above Corporation Limits will continue.
During the lock down period, the restrictions in the containment zones would be further intensified. In these areas 
disinfectants would be sprayed twice a day. The movement of general public would be restricted in other areas of the 
Corporations completely.  Severe action would also be initiated against the persons who violate the lock down conditions 
besides the seizure of the vehicles.
