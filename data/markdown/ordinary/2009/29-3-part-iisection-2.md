Drought Prone Areas Programme (DPAP) is being
implemented in Tamil Nadu since 1992-93. Under DPAP,
watershed development projects are sanctioned by
Government of India. Presently this scheme is being
implemented in 80 notified blocks of 17 districts. So far totally
1622 watershed projects have been sanctioned to
Tamil Nadu.
2.
Intergrated Wasteland Development Programme
(IWDP) is being implemented in Tamil Nadu since
1993-94 in non DPAP blocks. At present this programme is
being implemented in 96 blocks of 24 districts. So far totally
945 watershed projects have been sanctioned to
Tamil Nadu.
3.
At State level, Commissioner DPAP is monitoring the
implementation of the above mentioned schemes. At district
level, Project Officer, District Rural Development Agency is
implementing the programmes with 100 supporting staff
(Techncial/Ministerial Staff). In 2007-08 the Government of
India have stopped to sanction new projects and introduced
the New common guidelines for watershed development
projects. The ongoing DPAP & IWDP programmes will be
ended on 2011. The Government of India insisted to implement
common guidelines for watershed development programme
from 2008-09 onwards.
 4. For implementation of Government of India’s New
Common Guidelines to the DPAP & IWDP schemes as
requested by NRAA, Government of India, a State Level
Nodal Agency will be constituted and the Chairperson and
Chief Executive Officer of State Level Nodal Agency will be
nominated.
5.
Accordingly the Executive Director, Tamil Nadu
Watershed Development Agency (TAWDEVA), has sent a
proposal to Government to constitute a State Level Nodal
Agency. He has stated that the implementation of common
guidelines will bring necessary convergence, co-ordination,
networking and harmonization with the emerging policies,
programmes and funding portfolio of different ministries.
It is also informed that NWDPRA scheme in the States will
be implemented on the basis of these common guidelines
with effect from 01-04-2008 and also requested to appoint
the Chairperson and the Chief Executive Officer for the SLNA
as per Para 24 of the guidelines. He has also stated that
the appointment of Chairperson and Chief Executive Officer
and other members shall be notified shortly.
6.
The Executive Director, TAWDEVA has stated that
the State Level Nodal Agency for Watershed Development
Projects in the state may be constituted and necessary
Government notification also be made at the earliest to avoid
delay in getting Government of India funds for new watershed
development projects from various ministries of Government
of India to the State. The Technical Expert (Watershed
Development), Ministry of Agriculture, NRAA, Government of
India, New Delhi have informed that with reference to
para 25 of Common Guidelines it was decided that the
representative of State Forest Department should also be a
member in the said State Level Nodal Agency. Therefore as
requested by NRAA, Government of India, the State Level
Nodal Agency having an independent Bank account, the
appointment of Chairperson, Chief Executive Officer of State
Level Nodal Agency and other Representatives as per para
22 to 28 of Common guidelines. He has also requested the
Government to constitute State Level Nodal Agency to monitor
the implementation of DPAP & IWDP with composition of
following members:—

DECLARATION
Under Section 6 of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 (Central
Act I of 1894) the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby declare
that the lands specified in the schedule below and necessary
measuring by the same, a little more or less, are needed
for a public purpose to wit for the purpose of construction
of New Government Medical College in No. 4
Shanmugasundarapuram Village, Andipatti Taluk, Theni
District. Under sub-section (2) of Section 17 of the said Act
the Government of Tamil Nadu further directs that the
possession of the said land may be taken in the expiry of
15 days from the date of publication of the notice mentioned
in Section 9 (1) of the Act. A plan of the lands is kept in
the office of the Revenue Divisional Officer, Periyakulam and
may be inspected at any time during office hours.
