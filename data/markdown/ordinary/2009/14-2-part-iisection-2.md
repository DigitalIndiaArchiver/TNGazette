Constitution of District Munsif’s Court at Katpadi
in Vellore District under Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act.
NOTIFICATION-I
[G.O. Ms. No. 234, Home (Courts-III), 18th March 2009.]
No. II(2)/HO/161/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by Section 4 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts
Act, 1873 (Central Act III of 1873), the Governor of Tamil
Nadu, after consultation with the High Court, Madras, hereby
fixes the number of District Munsif to be appointed to the
District Munsif’s Court, Katpadi in Vellore District as one with
effect on and from the date on which the District Munsif,
Katpadi, assumes charge of that Court.
NOTIFICATION-II
[G.O. Ms. No. 234, Home (Courts-III), 18th March 2009.]
No. II(2)/HO/162/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by Section 4 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts
Act, 1873 (Central Act III of 1873), the Governor of Tamil
Nadu, herby alters  the number of District Munsif’s to be
appointed  for Vellore District as fourteen with effect on and
from the date on which the District Munsif,  Katpadi, assumes
charge of that Court.
NOTIFICATION-III
[G.O. Ms. No. 234, Home (Courts-III), 18th March 2009.]
No. II(2)/HO/163/2009.—In exercise of the powers
conferred by Section 10 of the Tamil Nadu Civil Courts
Act, 1873 (Central Act III of 1873), the Governor of Tamil
Nadu hereby varies the local limits of the jurisdiction of the
Subordinate Judge’s Court in Vellore District to the effect
that the said Subordinate Judge’s Court, Vellore, shall have
and exercise original jurisdiction over the District Munsif’s
Court, Katpadi in Vellore District in addition to its existing
jurisdiction with effect on and from the date on which the
District Munisif, Katpadi assumes charge of that Court.

of Depositors (in Financial Establishment) Act.
Ad-interim Order attaching the Properties due to default in
return of Deposits by the Mohan Enterprises,
Arupukottai, Virudhunagar District under the Act.
[G.O.Ms. No. 213, Home (Police-XIX), 6th March 2009.]
No. II(2)/HO/168/2009.—Whereas, complaints have been
received from a number of depositors that Mohan Enterprises,
Arupukottai, Virudhunagar District a Financial Establishment,
which has defaulted the return of deposits made by the
depositors after maturity;
2. And, Whereas, the Government are satisfied that the
said Financial Establishment is not likely to return the
deposits to the depositors and hence, the Government have
to protect the interests of such depositors;
3. And, Whereas, the immovable properties specified in
the Schedule to this order are alleged to have been procured
by the said Financial Establishment and the Partners of the
said Financial Establishment from and out of the deposits
collected from the depositors.
4. Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred
by Section 3 of the Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of
Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997
(Tamil Nadu Act 44 of 1997), the Governor of Tamil Nadu
hereby makes an ad-interim order attaching the Immovable
Properties of said Mohan Enterprises, Arupukottai,
Virudhunagar District and of its partners, as specified in the
Schedule to this Order and Transfers the contral over the
said Immovable Properties to the Competent Authority,
namely, the Competent  Authority and District Revenue
Officer, Virudhunagar District appointed under the provisions
of the said Act, for the purpose.
5. The Competent Authority is requested to pursue further
action in accordance with the procedure laid down in
sub-sections (3) and (4) of Section 4 of the said Act and
also the formalities prescribed in the Tamil Nadu Protection
of Interests of Depositors (in Financial Establishments)
Rules, 1997.
6. The Special Public Prosecutor, Special Court for the
Tamil Nadu Protection of Interest of Depositors (in Financial
Establishments) Act, 1997 is requested to render necessary
assistance to the Competent Authority, in filing the application,
before the said Special Court.

Ad-Interim order attaching the Properties due to default in
return of deposits by the RMS Corporation, W.B. Road,
Tiruchirappalli District under the Act.
[G.O. Ms. No. 215, Home (Police XIX), 6th March 2009.]
No. II(2)/HO/169/2009.—Whereas, complaints have been
received from a number of depositors that RMS.
Corporation, W.B. Road, Tiruchirappalli, Tiruchirappalli
District, a Financial Establishment, which has defaulted
the return of deposits made by the depositors after
maturity;
2. And, whereas, the Government are satisfied that the
said Financial Establishment is not likely to return the
deposits to the depositors and hence, the Government
have to protect the interests of such depositors;
3. And, whereas, the property specified in the Schedule
to this order are alleged to have been procured by one
Thiru Ramanathan (deceased), father of Tmt. Amutha and
Gokulakrishnan and husband of Tmt. Jayalakshmi,
Partners of the said Financial establishment and have
been developed as the shopping complex from and out
of the deposits collected from the depositors;
4. Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers conferred
by Section 3 of the Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests
of Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1997
(Tamil Nadu Act 44 of 1997), the Governor of Tamil Nadu
hereby makes an ad-interim order attaching the said
property  of (Late) Thiru Ramanathan, Father of
Tmt. Amutha and Gokulakrishnan, and husband of
Tmt. Jayalakshmi, legal heirs and partners of the said
RMS Corporation, W.B. Road, Tiruchirappalli, Tiruchirappalli
District, as specified in the Schedule to this Order and
transfers the control over the said property to the
Competent Authority, namely, the District Revenue Officer,
Tiruchirappalli appointed under the provisions of the said
Act, for the purpose.
5. The Competent Authority is requested to pursue
further action in accordance with the procedure laid down
in sub-sections (3) and (4) of Section 4 of the said Act
and also the formalities prescribed in the Tamil Nadu
Protection 
of 
Interests 
Depositors 
(in 
Financial
Establishments) Rules, 1997.
6. The Special Public Prosecutor, Special Court for the
Tamil Nadu Protection of Interests of Depositors (in
Financial Establishments) Act, 1997 is requested to render
necessary assistance to the Competent Authority, in filing
the application, before the said Special Court.
