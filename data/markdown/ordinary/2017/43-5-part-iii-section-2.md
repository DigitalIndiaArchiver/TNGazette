Under Section 57 of the Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of Financial and other Subsidies, Benefits and Services)
Act, 2016 (Central Act 18 of 2016) for the State Resident Data Hub (SRDH)
(Rc.No.K-38/1/2017/TNeGA)
Portal (http://makkal.tn.gov.in)
No.SRO C-27/2017.
WHEREAS, the National Population Register (NPR) is a Register of usual residents of the country, which is being
prepared at the local  (Village/sub-Town), sub-District, District, State and National level under  provisions of the
Citizenship Act, 1955 (Central Act No.57) and the  Citizenship (Registration of Citizens and issue of National Identity
Cards)  Rules, 2003 and hence it is an authenticated database. It is mandatory for  every usual resident of India to
register in the NPR. A usual resident is  defined for the purposes of NPR as a person who has resided in a local area
for the past six months or more or a person who intends to reside in that  area for the next six months or more;
AND WHEREAS, ‘State Resident Data Hub’ has been created by the  Government of Tamil Nadu as a secure,
unified, digitized data repository  derived from the National Population Register to service all departments and  it is
envisaged as the single source of data for Tamil Nadu State’s residents  including address and has provision for
Aadhaar authentication and it will  help the Government in better targeting,. effective service delivery, greater
accountability and more efficient monitoring of schemes;
AND WHEREAS, the provisions of the Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of  Financial and other Subsidies, Benefits and
Services) Act, 2016 and  Regulations under the above said Act have come into effect from 12th  September, 2016 and
notifications to this effect have been published in the  Official Gazette of Government of India. Section 57 of the Aadhaar
(Targeted Delivery of Financial and other Subsidies, Benefits and Services)  Act, 2016 permits the use of Aadhaar
number for establishing the identity of  an individual for any purpose pursuant to any law or any contract to this  effect.
AND WHEREAS, the use of Aadhaar as identity document for delivery  of services or benefits or subsidies simplifies
the Government delivery  processes, brings in transparency and efficiency, and enables beneficiaries to  get their
entitlements directly in a convenient and seamless manner.
AND WHEREAS State Resident Data Hub (SRDH) obviates the need of  the residents to approach different departments
for changing or correcting  demographic data individually through synchronization of core elements of  the databases like
Name, Address, etc.
NOW, THEREFORE, in pursuance of the provisions of Section 57 of  the Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of Financial and
other Subsidies, Benefits  and Services) Act, 2016 (Central Act 18 of 2016), the Commissioner of  e-Governance and Chief
Executive Officer, Tamil Nadu e-Governance Agency  hereby authorize the use of Aadhaar number for establishing the
identity of  an individual for purpose specified hereunder:-
(1) All residents who wish to avail the services of the ‘State Resident  Data Hub (SRDH)’ portal
(‘http://makkal.tn.gov.in) are required to  furnish proof of possession of Aadhaar number or undergo  Aadhaar authentication.
(2) Such residents of Tamil Nadu desirous of correcting or changing  their demographic details like name, age,
Gender, address etc to  avail the services offered by various departments of the  Government of Tamil Nadu are hereby
required to provide their  Aadhaar number or undergo Aadhaar authentication and then correct their demographic details
in the ‘State Resident Data Hub  (SRDH)’ portal (‘http://makkal.tn.gov.in) in any of the e-Sevai  Centres by furnishing any
documents listed in the Annexure I as  proof of identity and proof of address.
(3) Any change or correction in the demographic details made in the  State Resident Data Hub (SRDH), shall
be shared with departments mentioned hereunder with the resident consent so  that the saved changes can also be
carried out in the databases of  the respective departments with their concurrence:-
(i)
Civil Supplies Department - Public distribution scheme card
(ii) Election - Electoral Photo Identity card
(iii) Transport - Driving license
(iv) Treasuries and Accounts - Pension
(v) Welfare of Differently-Abled Persons Department
(vi) Other Departments requiring such data for implementing  their schemes
