WHEREAS, the Reserve forest areas of Anthiyur taluk of Erode district, as speciﬁ ed in the Schedule, are located 
between the Sathyamangalam Tiger Reserve in Tamil Nadu, the Male Mahadeshwara Wildlife Sanctuary and the Cauvery 
Wildlife Sanctuary in the State of Karnataka. The area has adequate ecological, faunal and ﬂ oral signiﬁ cance and should 
be managed as a wildlife sanctuary for protecting and conserving wildlife habitat and to mitigate human wildlife conﬂ ict;
AND WHEREAS, the reserved forest areas of the Anthiyur taluk of Erode district are a complex hilly tract and form an 
important part of the Eastern Ghats before they meet the Western Ghats at the Nilgiris;
AND WHEREAS, the area speciﬁ ed in the Schedule will form a large, contiguous network of protected areas, enabling 
uniform legal status and protection in this landscape. This landscape maintains continuity from Cauvery Wildlife Sanctuary 
in the State of Karnataka to the Nilgiri Biosphere through Sathyamangalam Tiger Reserve and Biligiri Rangaswamy Temple 
Tiger Reserve.  This provides suﬃ  cient area for the conservation of the varied and rich biodiversity of the region;
AND WHEREAS, the area speciﬁ ed in the Schedule is a vast elephant landscape comprised of diverse ﬂ ora and 
fauna. The landscape is connected to the Nilgiris and the Kollegal forests of the State of Karnataka, which further connect 
to the Wyanaad and Cauvery forests scapes, respectively, making it one of the most diverse habitats for various life-forms;
AND WHEREAS, the area speciﬁ ed in the Schedule is the heart of this contiguous and diverse landscape. It forms 
an important corridor connecting Sathyamangalam Tiger Reserve to Male Mahadeshwara Wildlife Sanctuary and Cauvery 
Wildlife Sanctuary, respectively, making it a landscape crucial for healthy tiger populations;
AND WHEREAS, the area speciﬁ ed in the Schedule facilitates unhindered genetic exchange among many species 
and forms the nuclear axis of the conservation of species in the entire wildlife habitat. It is an important corridor for the 
dynamic tiger populations migrating to the Male Mahadeshwara Wildlife Sanctuary and Cauvery Wildlife Sanctuary, and 
this forest area is one of the tiger corridors approved by the National Tiger Conservation Authority (NTCA) and it holds 
a signiﬁ cant tiger population;

AND WHEREAS, the area speciﬁ ed in the Schedule has a good diversity of ﬂ ora and fauna, such as ﬁ sh, amphibians, 
reptiles, birds, and mammals. There are about 70 species of butterﬂ ies, 35 species of ﬁ sh, 10 species of amphibians, 
25 species of reptiles, 5 terrestrial invertebrates, 233 species of birds and 48 species of mammals reported;
AND WHEREAS, signiﬁ cant populations of large herbivores have been recorded in this region, and the areas are part 
of the Nilgiris-Eastern Ghats Elephant Reserve (Nilgiri Elephant Reserve);
AND WHEREAS, the area speciﬁ ed in the Schedule is an important catchment of the Palar River that drains into 
the Cauvery River at Stanley Reservoir and it is an important source of water for agriculture. It is an important natural 
sandal-bearing belt and an actively sandal-regenerating forest;
NOW, THEREFORE, in exercise of the powers conferred by clause (b) of sub-section (1) of section 26-A of the 
Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 (Central Act 53 of 1972), the Governor of Tamil Nadu, having considered that the area, the 
boundaries of which are speciﬁ ed in the Schedule below, is of adequate ecological, faunal, ﬂ oral, natural and zoological 
signiﬁ cance, for the purpose of protecting, propagating and developing Wildlife and its environment, hereby declares that 
the areas speciﬁ ed in the Schedule below, shall be a Sanctuary, namely Thanthai Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary with eﬀ ect on 
and from 30.01.2024.
