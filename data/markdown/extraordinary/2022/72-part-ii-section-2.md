ALONG WITH RELAXATIONS UNDER THE DISASTER MANAGEMENT ACT, 2005.

WHEREAS on considering the prevailing Covid-19 pandemic scenario and as per the directives of Government of India, 
Ministry of Home Affairs, State-wide lockdown/ restrictions was enforced throughout the State with effect from 4.00 A.M. of 
10-05-2021 to 31-01-2022 in order to control the spread of COVID-19. 
2. Based on the recommendations of the expert team of Doctors and Public Health Specialists and based on the directives 
of Government of India, Ministry of Home Affairs, State Government have been directed to impose necessary restrictions to 
control corona infection and if necessary, to take action under Section 144 of the Criminal Procedure Code, 1973.
3. The Hon’ble Chief Minister has conducted review meeting with Minister and officers to evaluate the Corona prevention 
activities. Due to the steps taken by the Government and adequate medical infrastructure, the spread of Covid-19 has been 
reduced. Moreover, considering the employment, economic activities, welfare of the students and restoring the normalcy in 
public livelihood, relaxations are provided. 
4. Now, the proposal of the Principal Secretary/Commissioner of Revenue Administration is accepted and the Government 
hereby orders to implement the following restrictions from 01-02-2022 to 15-02-2022 under Disaster Management Act 2005, 
in order to contain the spread of Covid-19: - 
	
i. Existing restrictions on Public gathering in Social, Cultural and Political Congregation will continue. 
	
ii. Standard Operating Procedure issued by the State Election Commission should be followed during the urban 
local body election. 
	
iii. Play Schools and Nursery Schools (LKG, UKG) will not be permitted to function.
	
iv. Exhibitions will not be permitted.

v. All cultural events to be conducted by Government and Private will not be permitted.
	
vi. Only 50 per cent capacity of dine-in facility in Hotels, Lodges, Bakeries, Guest Houses and Dormitories 
shall be permitted to operate.  
	
vii. Marriage and Marriage related gathering shall be permitted with guests not exceeding 100.
	
viii. Funeral/Last rites gathering shall be permitted by not exceeding 50 persons.
	
ix. The Textile and Jewellery Shops owner should ensure that the customers not exceeding 50 per cent at a time 
shall be allowed. 
	
x. Recreation Clubs shall be permitted to function by allowing Gymnasium, Games and Restaurants with 
50 per cent customers at a time.
	
xi. Gymnasiums and Yoga Training Centres shall be permitted with 50 per cent customers at a time.
	
xii. Multiplex/Cinemas/Theatres shall be permitted to function with maximum of 50 per cent seating capacity.
	
xiii. Indoor Stadia shall be permitted to function for conducting sports competitions with 50 per cent spectators by 
following Standard Operating Procedure. No restriction for usual trainings.
	
xiv. All indoor auditorium shall be permitted to conduct functions like Seminars, Music, Drama with 
50 per cent spectators by following Standard Operating Procedure.
	
xv. Beauty Parlour, Salons and Spas shall be permitted with 50 per cent customers at a time.
	
xvi. Entertainment Park  /Amusement Park except water sports shall be permitted to function with 50 per cent capacity.
	
All other restrictions imposed earlier for controlling the Covid-19 activities shall be withdrawn, except the 
above-mentioned restrictions.
Educational Institutions:-
	
i. Physical Classes from 1st to 12th Standards in all schools will function with effect from 01-02-2022 by 
following Standard Operating Procedures.
	
ii. Government and Private Universities/ Colleges/ Industrial Training and other Training Institutes will be 
permitted for physical classes with effect from 01-02-2022 except the institutions designated as Covid Care Centre.
	
The concerned educational institutions are requested to take all preparatory works before starting the classes.
Removal of lockdown:-
	
i. Night curfew from 10.00 PM to 05.00 AM shall be withdrawn with effect from 28.01.2022.
	
ii. No complete lockdown on 30-01-2022 (Sunday).
	
iii. All religious places/ place of worship will be allowed on Friday, Saturday and Sunday with effect from 28-01-2022 
by following Standard Operating Procedures.
General Instructions:-
z	 Those who have not taken vaccination and those who have taken first dose and not completed the second 
dose of vaccination are requested to be vaccinated immediately.
z	 Shops shall have mandatory hand hygiene (hand sanitizer with dispenser), Thermal screening at the entrance. 
z	 Management shall ensure that the workers and customers wear face masks compulsorily failing which action 
will be taken as per the law in force.
z	 The employees and owners of the commercial establishments should be vaccinated compulsorily and should 
hold the vaccination certificate.
h	 ‘Test- Track- Treat- Vaccination- Covid-19 appropriate behaviour’ principle shall be strictly adhered to.
h	 In order to contain the spread of Covid-19, the Containment Zones shall be demarcated upto Micro Level and 
the containment measures including vaccination to the people in this area shall be initiated by the District 
Collectors and the Local Bodies as per the Standard Operating Procedures.
h	 Within the demarcated Containment Zones, containment measures shall be scrupulously followed, as under:

z	 Only essential activities shall be allowed in the Containment Zones. No activity, except medical emergencies 
and supply of essential goods and services shall be permitted.
z	 The implementation of the directives for Covid–19 management shall be monitored continuously and in case 
of violation, action may be initiated and fine may be imposed against individuals and organizations. 
5. The public may be requested to wear face mask and maintain social distance without fail by following preventive 
measures.
6. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation /District Collectors and Commissioners of Police/Superintendents of 
Police concerned shall take all necessary measures for the strict enforcement of National Directives for Covid-19 management 
viz., wearing of face masks, hand hygiene and social distancing, screening & hygiene, frequent sanitization of entire 
Workplace etc., and adherence of the Standard Operating Procedures issued for the permitted activities. The Commissioner, 
Greater Chennai Corporation/District Collectors concerned may initiate penal action for imposing fines on persons violating 
the National Directives. 
7. Any person violating these measures will be liable to be proceeded against as per the provisions of Section 
51 to 60 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, besides legal action under Section 188 of the IPC, and other legal provisions 
as applicable.
