I. PRELIMINARY
1. Short title and commencement.-(a) These rules may be called the Tamil Nadu Police (Reforms) Rules, 2022. 
  
 
(b) They shall come into force on the 3rd November 2022. 
2. Deﬁ nitions.- (1) In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires.- 
 
(i)  
"Act" means the Tamil Nadu Police (Reforms) Act, 2013 (Tamil Nadu Act 22 of 2013); 
 
(ii)

(iii)

sub-section (5) of Section 8 of the Act; 
 
(iv)     "City" means the jurisdiction under the control of Commissioner of Police;

(vii)    "District Committee" means the District Police Establishment Committee in the Districts constituted under 
 
 
sub-section (5) of Section 8 of the Act;

II. THE STATE SECURITY COMMISSION 
3. Meetings.-(1) 
The Commission shall meet at least once in a year. 
 
 
(2) 
The meeting of the Commission shall be held at any place as desired by the Chairperson 
 
 
 
of the Commission. 
 
 
(3) 
The Quorum for a meeting of the Commission shall be one third of its total strength. 
 
 
(4) 
Notice of the meeting shall be given to the members at least seven days before the day appointed 
 
 
 
for such meeting. 
 
 
(5) 
The notice shall be sent to the members by any one or more of the following modes, namely:- 
 
 
 
 
(a) by local delivery; 
 
 
 
 
(b) by post; 
 
 
 
 
(c) by electronic means. 
 
 
(6) 
The Member-Secretary shall record the proceedings of the meeting and prepare the minutes of the 
 
 
 
meeting. 
4. Constitution of Expert Committee- (1) The Commission, if necessary, to achieve the object of clause (b) of section 6
of the Act, may constitute a panel of experts familiar with the functioning of the police or sociological or criminological or 
public administrative studies. 
 
 
(2) The Commission may decide,- 
 
 
    (i) the number of experts to be appointed; 
 
 
    (ii) ﬁ elds from which the experts to be chosen; 
 
 
   (iii) terms of reference of the experts; and 
 
 
   (iv) such other matters relating to Expert Committee. 
III. THE POLICE ESTABLISHMENT BOARD 
5. Meetings of the Board.-(1) The Board shall meet at least once in every three months. 
6. Procedures.-The Board shall formulate the procedures to carry out all or any of the functions speciﬁ ed in sub-section (3)
of Section 8 of the Act.

(a) Additional Director General of Police (Administration); 
 
 
 
(b) Additional Director General of Police (Law and Order); and 
 
 
 
(c) Additional Director General of Police (Intelligence).

shall send proposal to the Government based on such recommendations. 
(6) The State Committee shall make recommendations relating to transfers and postings of oﬃ  cers in the rank of the 
Additional Superintendent of Police, Deputy Commandant, Assistant Commissioner of Police, Deputy Superintendent of Police 
and Assistant Commandant to the Director: General of Police. The Director General of Police shall issue orders on such 
recommendations. 
(7) The State Committee shall also make recommendations relating to promotion of oﬃ  cers in the rank of Inspector of 
Police to the Director General of Police. The Director General of Police shall issue orders on such recommendations. 
(8) The State Committee shall consider the matters relating to the inter-jurisdictional transfers of the Inspectors of Police.

V. ZONAL POLICE ESTABLISHMENT COMMITTEES 
8. Zonal Committee.-(1) There shall be a Zonal Committee for each zone. 
 
(2) The Zonal Committee shall consist of the following members, namely:- 
 
 
(a) Zonal Inspector General of Police; 
 
 
(b) Range Deputy Inspectors General of Police in the Zone concerned; and 
 
 
(c) Commissioners of Police, if reporting to Zonal Inspector General of Police concerned. 
 
(3) The Zonal Inspector General of Police shall be the Chairperson and also the Convener of the Zonal Committee. 
 
(4) The Zonal Committee shall meet at least once in every three months.
 
(5) The Zonal Committee shall consider the matters relating to intra- zonal transfer of Inspectors of Police,
Sub-Inspectors of Police and other ranks. 
 
(6) The Zonal Committee shall make recommendations to the Board for award of medals. 
9. Zonal Committee for Armed Police.- (1) There shall be a Zonal Committee for Armed Police, which shall consist 
of the following members, namely:- 
 
(a) Additional Director General of Police, Armed Police; and 
 
(b) Inspector General of Police / Deputy Inspector General of Police, Armed Police.

VI. RANGE POLICE ESTABLISHMENT COMMITTEES 
 
11. Range Committee.- (1) There shall be a Range Committee for each range. 
 
(2) The Range Committee shall consist of the following members, namely:- 
 
 
(a) Range Deputy Inspector General of Police; and 
 
 
(b) District Superintendents of Police of the Range concerned. 
 
(3) The Range Deputy Inspector General of Police shall be the Chairperson and also Convener of the Range 
Committee. 
 
(4) The Range Committee shall meet at least once in every three months.  
 
(5) The Range Committee shall consider the matters relating to intra-range transfer and postings of Inspectors of 
Police, Sub-Inspectors of Police and other ranks. 
 
(6) The Range Committee shall consider the matter relating to the promotions and upgradation of Head Constables 
as Sub-Inspectors of Police or Special Sub-Inspectors of Police within the Range. 
 
(7) The Range Committee shall make recommendations to the Zonal Committee for award of medals. 
 
12. Range Committee for Armed Police.-(1) There shall be a Range Committee for Armed Police, which shall 
consist of the following members, namely:- 
 
 
(a) Inspector General of Police / Deputy Inspector General of 
 
            Police, Armed Police; and 
 
 
(b) Two senior most Commandants. 
(2) The Inspector General of Police / Deputy Inspector General of Police shall be the Chairperson and Convener of the 
Range Committee for Armed Police. 
(3) The Range Committee for Armed Police shall meet at least once in every three months. 
(4) The Range Committee for Armed Police shall consider the matters relating to transfer and postings of police personnel 
coming under the Tamil Nadu Special Police Subordinate Service within its jurisdiction and the Chairperson shall issue orders 
in this regard.

VII. CHENNAI CITY POLICE ESTABLISHMENT COMMITTEE. 
13. Chennai City Committee.- (1) There shall be a Chennai City Committee in respect of Chennai City. 
 
(2) The Chennai City Committee shall consist of the following members, namely:- 
 
 
(a) All Additional Commissioners of Police in Chennai; and 
 
 
(b) Joint Commissioner of Police, Intelligence Section. 
 
(3) The senior most Additional Commissioner of Police shall be the Chairperson of the Chennai City Committee 
and the Additional Commissioner of Police (Head Quarters) shall be the Convener. 
 
(4) The Chennai City Committee shall meet at least once in every three months. 
(5) The Chennai City Committee shall make recommendations relating to inter-jurisdictional transfer and postings 
of Inspectors of Police and Sub-Inspectors of Police within the Chennai City to the Commissioner of Police, Chennai. 
The Commissioner of Police, Chennai shall issue orders on such recommendations. 
(6) The Chennai City Committee shall make recommendations relating to promotion or upgradation of Police personnel 
up to the rank of Sub-Inspector of Police and Special Sub-Inspector of Police to the Commissioner of Police, Chennai. 
The Commissioner of Police, Chennai shall issue orders on such recommendations. 
(7) The Chennai City Committee shall have the following Sub-Committees namely.- 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(a)  Sub-Committee to recommend transfers within Armed Reserve:- There shall be a Sub-Committee 
consisting of Additional Commissioner of Police, Headquarters as the Chairperson and Deputy Commissioner 
of Police, Administration, Deputy Commissioner of Police, Armed Reserve-I, Deputy Commissioner of 
Police, Armed Reserve-II and Deputy Commissioner of Police, Motor Transport as the 
members. This Sub-Committee shall make recommendations relating to transfers of Sub-Inspectors of 
Police and other ranks within Armed Reserve and the Chairperson shall issue orders. 
(b) Sub-Committee to recommend transfers within Control Room:- There  shall be a  Sub Committee consisting of 
Additional Commissioner of Police, Headquarters as the Chairperson and Deputy Commissioner of Police, Modern 
Control Room, Commissioner of Police, Armed Reserve-I and Deputy Commissioner of Police, Motor Transport 
as the members. This Sub-Committee shall make recommendations relating to transfers of Sub-Inspectors of 
Police and other ranks within Control Room and the Chairperson shall issue orders. 
(c)  Sub-Committee to recommend transfers within Intelligence Section:- There shall be a Sub-Committee consisting 
of Additional Commissioner of Police / Joint Commissioner of Police, Intelligence Section as the Chairperson 
and Deputy Commissioner of Police, Security Chennai Police, Assistant Commissioner of Police, Administration 
(Intelligence Section) and Assistant Commissioner of Police, Administration (Security Chennai Police) as the 
members. This Sub-Committee shall make recommendations relating to transfers of Sub-Inspectors of Police 
and other ranks within Intelligence Section and the Chairperson shall issue orders. 
(d)  Sub-Committee to recommend transfers within City Crime Branch:- There shall be a Sub-Committee 
consisting of Additional Commissioner of Police, City Crime Branch as the Chairperson and Deputy Commissioner 
of Police, City Crime Branch-I and Deputy Commissioner of Police, City Crime Branch-II as the members. 
This Sub-Committee shall make recommendations relating to transfers of Sub-Inspectors of Police and other 
ranks within City Crime Branch and the Chairperson shall issue orders.

XII. POLICE COMPLAINTS DIVISION 
19. Police Complaints Division.-(1) The Crime Branch CID Wing in the Police Department shall function as the Police 
Complaints Division under the administrative control of the Additional Director General of Police, Crime Branch CID, under 
the overall control of the Director General of Police. 
 
(2) The Director General of Police or the Additional Director General of Police, Crime Branch CID, shall entrust the 
complaints referred to the Police Complaints Division for enquiry under Sections 13 and 16 of the Act by the State Police 
Complaints Authority and the District Police Complaints Authority to the ﬁ eld units of the Crime Branch CID to carry out 
investigations. 
 (3) The Police Complaints Division shall complete its enquiry within six months from the date of receipt of a complaint 
and send the report to the State Police Complaints Authority or the District Police Complaints Authority, as the case may be. 
(4) The Director General of Police or the Additional Director General of Police, Crime Branch CID may also engage 
retired Police Oﬃ  cers or retired personnel from other department, if required, for the purpose of investigation' under the Act 
with the prior approval of the Government. The Additional Director General of Police, Crime Branch CID shall send proposals 
to the Director General of Police in this regard along with the terms and conditions of service of the retired personnel. 
The Director General of Police shall send such proposal with his recommendations to the Government for approval.
