Tamil Nadu Government Gazette Extraordinary, dated the 14th June 2024 and after considering the objections 
and representations thereto made through petitions and in person, the State Government hereby approve the New 
Comprehensive Scheme under sub-section (2) of Section 100 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (Central Act 59  of 
1988) as detailed in the Schedule annexed herewith.
NOW, THEREFORE, the New Comprehensive Scheme, 2024 as so approved is hereby published, as required 
under sub-section (3) of Section 100 of the said Act.
This notiﬁ cation will come into eﬀ ect from its date of Publication and the services under the New Comprehensive 
Scheme, 2024 to the public will be rolled out starting from 01.05.2025.

Salient Features of the Scheme
(I) 
Objective:
The objective of the scheme is to improve the accessibility 
of bus services in urban and rural areas and to ensure last mile 
connectivity.
(II) Starting/ Terminal Point:
The starting point / the terminal point shall be any location on 
the un-served habitation/ village. One of the points can be a bus 
stop or a bus stand.
(III) Maximum route length:         
The maximum permitted route length shall be 25 km.
(IV) Minimum un-served route length: 
The minimum un-served route length shall not be less than 
65% of the total route length.
(V) Fixing of minimum route length:
While issuing permit, the Regional Transport Authority shall 
determine and ﬁ nalize the route length. The route length so ﬁ xed by 
the Regional Transport Authority shall be such that the un-served 
route shall not be less than 65% of the total route length.

(IX) Deﬁ nition for Un-Served Route:
(Based on bus operations) 
A route or part of the route where there is no service by a stage 
carriage or a mini bus, or, a route or part of the route wherein the 
trips provided by the existing operator(s) are insuﬃ  cient to cater to 
the needs of the public and the number of trips being performed is

(X) Maximum mini bus permits in a route: 
The Regional Transport Authority / District Collector shall

route. If the number of applications for that route exceeds the 
number of permits notiﬁ ed, the operators will be selected by drawal 
of lots.

(a) 
If a route notiﬁ ed under this scheme has more passenger 
density or potential and the existing operator is unable or 
unwilling to cater thereto, the Regional Transport Authority/ 
District Collector may allow additional operator(s); 
(b) 
If more than one operator apply for the same route, lot
