AMENDMENTS TO THE TAMIL NADU TOWN PANCHAYATS, THIRD GRADE MUNICIPALITIES, MUNICIPALITIES AND

[G.O.Ms. No. 127, Municipal Administration and Water Supply (Election), 16th September 2016.]
No. SRO A-16(e-4)/2016.
In exercise of the powers conferred by section 303 of the Tamil Nadu District Municipalities Act, 1920 (Tamil Nadu Act V of
1920), section 347 of the Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919 (Tamil Nadu Act IV of 1919), section 431 of the Madurai
City Municipal Corporation Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act 15 of 1971), section 430 of the Coimbatore City Municipal Corporation Act,
1981 (Tamil Nadu Act 25 of 1981), read with section 8 of the Tiruchirappalli City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act
27 of 1994) and section 8 of the Tirunelveli City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 28 of 1994) and section 8 of the
Salem City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 29 of 1994), the Governor of Tamil Nadu, after consultation with the
Tamil Nadu State Election Commission, hereby makes the following amendments to the Tamil Nadu Town Panchayats, Third
Grade Municipalities, Municipalities and Corporations (Elections) Rules, 2006:—
AMENDMENTS
In the said Rules,-
 (1) in rule 22,-
(a) in sub-rule (1), for the expression “Councillors or Chairmen or Mayors”, the expression “Councillors” shall be
substituted;
(b) in sub-rule (2),-
(i) in clause (ii), for the expression “11.00 a.m. and 3.00 p.m”, the expression “10.00 a.m. and 5.00 p.m” shall
be substituted;
(ii) in clause (iii), for the expression “11.00 a.m”, the expression “10.00 a.m” shall be substituted;
(2) for rule 23, the following rule shall be substituted, namely :-

“23. Notice of Election.- (1) In pursuance of the notification of the State Election Commission under rule 22, the Returning
Officer shall cause to be published on the appointed date, a notice of election in Tamil in Form 2 and also in any other
language or languages in which the electoral roll is published for the election of councillors in the Town Panchyat or Third
Grade Municipality or Municipality or Corporation and also affix a copy of the notice in two conspicuous places in the
concerned wards.
(2) The notice of election shall contain the following particulars, namely:-
(a) the ward or wards for which elections are to be held;
(b) whether the ward is reserved or not; if reserved, whether it is reserved for the Scheduled Caste or the Scheduled
Tribe and/or for woman;
(c) the dates on which, the place or places at which and the hours between which nomination papers shall be
presented;
(d) the person or persons by whom the nomination papers will be received;
(e) the dates on which and the hours at which and the place at which the nomination papers will be taken up
for scrutiny;
(f) the last date on which and the hours upto which the nomination papers can be withdrawn;
(g) the date on which and the place or places at which and the hours between which the votes of the electors
will be taken at the poll, should there be a poll;
(h) the date on which and the place or places and the hour at which the Returning Officer will commence the
counting of votes;
(i) the date before which the election shall be completed; and
(j) the date on which the first meeting of the newly elected Councillors shall be held, in the case of ordinary
elections.”;

in the case of election of councillors and in any ward of the respective Town Panchayat or Municipality or Corporation in

Transitional Area or Municipality or Corporation in the election of councillors”, shall be substituted;

“94. Election of Chairman, Vice-Chairman, Mayor and Deputy Mayor.- The Chairman and Vice-Chairman of a Town
Panchayat or Third Grade Municipality or Municipal Council or the Mayor and Deputy Mayor of a Corporation Council shall
be elected by and from among the councillors of the wards of the Town Panchayat or Third Grade Municipality or Municipality
or Corporation, as the case may be, in the manner prescribed in these rules, at a meeting specially convened by the
Returning Officer:
Provided that where the office of the Mayor or Chairman of a council is reserved for persons belonging to Scheduled
Castes or Scheduled Tribes and / or for women under the provisions of the Act(s), only persons belonging to that category
shall be eligible to contest the election for such office:
Provided further that where no such reservation has been made, nothing contained in the above proviso shall be deemed
to prevent any member belonging to Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes or women of a Town Panchayat or Third Grade
Municipality or Municipality or Corporation, as the case may be, from contesting the election as Chairman or Mayor.”;
