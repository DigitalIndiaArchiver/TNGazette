are awared the Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Police Medal for 
Gallantry in recognition of the courageous eﬀ ort taken by them 
as detalied below:-
 
 
(i) Thiru S. Boominathan, Special Sub-Inspector of 
Police was working at Navalpat Police station. On 20.11.2021, 
while conducting vehicle check at Poolangudi colony of 
Navalpat PS limit on 21.11.2021 early morning, two unknown 
persons came in a two wheeler along with a stolen goat, 
did not stop their vehicle and ﬂ ed away. Immediately, SSI
Thiru S. Boominathan has chased them for more than 
10 kilometers by crossing the district boundary and secured 
the criminals in Keeranur PS limit of Pudukottai District. While 
conduction enquiry with the two criminals, he was brutally 
assaulted on his head and inﬂ icted cut  injuries. He has died 
and criminals escaped from there. The bravery attempt and 
chase made by the deceased SSI Thiru S. Boominathan of 
Navalpattu PS is an act of gallantry and lost, his valuable life 
in the operation.
 
(ii)   On 17.9.2021, one S. Julius Santhakumar of 
East Saveriyarpuram has set ﬁ re on his motor cycle in the

and Thiru Senthilkumar, Grade II PC 1736 of Somarasampet 
Police Station, Tiruchirappalli District and tried to secure the 
troublesome person, the infuriated person had abused the 
team of police with grimy words and shown a knief kept hidden 
in his lap. Though the police team warned him to surrender 
the knife, he stabbed the SI and the constables. P. Jeyaraj, 
Grade II PC 1271 had sustained injuries on his right toes and
Thiru Senthilkumar, Grade II PC 1736 had sustained injuries 
on his head. The team had chased and secured the accused.
        (iii) On receipt wireless information from Thiruvannamalai 
District about the robbery of a car, cash of Rs.15000/- and 
Cell Phones, on 15.09.2021 Thiru R. Prasath, Grade II PC 
515, Pattukottai Town Police Station, Thanjavur District had a 
glimpse of he robbed vehicle and stopped the same. The driver 
drove the car faster, even in wrong routes and one ways. Thiru 
R. Prasath, Grade II PC 515, had also chased the vehicle by 
his "scooty". While chasing the accused by foot, the Constable 
had fell down at one point and sustained injuries without 
considering his pain had secured the accused.

ii. In this regard it has been requested to segregate Palanjur

from 330 to 428 of Mevalur Kuppam 'B' Revenue village of 
Kancheepuram District and to transfer the administrative 
control of Revenue and other Department by annexing

Poonamallee Taluk in Thiruvallur District.

Gazette No.1 Dated:19.01.1999 the list of Villages of

Poonamallee Taluk in serial number 31. Therefore, it is 
confirmed that the Palanjur village was already notified

control of the Village is with the Kancheepuram District.

Thiruvallur District was taken up for hearing on 27.08.2019.

proposal to the Government in this regard.
