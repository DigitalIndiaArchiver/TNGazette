In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of section 17 of the   Tamil Nadu State Commission 
for the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Act, 2021 (Tamil Nadu Act 17 of 2021), the Governor of 
Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the Tamil Nadu State Commission for the Scheduled 
Castes and Scheduled Tribes Rules, 2022.  
2. The amendments hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on and from the 
13th October 2021.  
Amendments
In the said Rules,- 
      1.  for rule 1, the following rule shall be substituted, namely :- 
               “1. “Short title and Commencement. – (1) These rules may be called the Tamil Nadu State 
Commission for the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Rules, 2022. 
     		
(2) These rules shall be deemed to have come into force on and from the  13th October 2021.”; 
      2. in rule 3,- 
       (1) in the marginal heading, for the expression “Honorarium”, the expression “Salary” 
shall be substituted;
   	 	
 (2) for sub-rule (1), the following sub-rule shall be substituted, namely:-

  “(1) The Chairperson of the Commission shall be paid salary and allowances as are admissible to a serving Judge 
of the High Court:
Provided that if a person appointed as Chairperson, is in receipt of any pension, his salary shall be reduced by the 
gross amount of pension drawn by him.”; 
 3. in rule 4,- 
    (1) in the marginal heading, for the expression “Honorarium”, the expression “Salary” shall be substituted;
    (2) for sub-rule (1), the following sub-rule shall be substituted, namely:-
  	
  “(1) The Vice-Chairperson shall be paid salary and allowances as are admissible to the rank of Secretary to the 
State Government.”; 
4. in rule 5,-
    (1) in the marginal heading, for the expression “Honorarium”, the expression “Salary” shall be substituted;
    (2) for sub-rule (1), the following sub-rule shall be substituted, namely:-
 	
  “(1) The other Members shall be paid salary and allowances as are admissible to the rank of Additional Secretary 
to the State Government.”.
