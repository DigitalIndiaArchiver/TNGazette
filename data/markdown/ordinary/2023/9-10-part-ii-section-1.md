No. II(1)/AGFW/3/2023.—Whereas, the Governor of the State is of the opinion that it is expedient and necessary for 
the reason that,
 
(a) the insecticides specified in the Schedule are either extremely or highly toxic or moderately toxic but have 
highly toxic effect due to use for blending in an unauthorized manner;
 
(b) use of the insecticides specified in the Schedule appended hereto, or any batches thereof or any  combinations 
of such insecticides is likely to involve risk to human beings or animals;
	
(c)	 the research report submitted by the Tamil Nadu Agricultural University, Coimbatore and the High Level Committee 
constituted to examine the possibility of banning of hazardous insecticides have recommended to prohibit the insecticides 
specified in the Schedule appended hereto for sixty days as per the provision contained in section 27 of the Insecticides Act, 
1968 (Central Act 46 of 1968).
Now, therefore in exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of Section 27 of the Insecticides Act, 1968 (Central 
Act 46 of 1968), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby prohibits the sale, distribution or use of insecticides specified in the 
Schedule below or any combination thereof for a period of sixty days from the date of publication of this notification in the 
Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, pending investigation into the matter.

No. II(1)/AGFW/4/2023.—Whereas, the Governor of the State is of the opinion that it is expedient and necessary for 
the reason that,
	
(a)	 3% yellow phosphorus which is being used as Insecticide (Rodenticide) is not a registered insecticide.
	
(b)	 Government of India have stated that as per the provisions of the Insecticides Act, 1968 (Central Act 46 of 
1968) and the Insecticides Rules, 1971, if an insecticide is not registered for Insecticidal / Agricultural use, and is available 
and being used for the said purpose, then it contravenes the provisions of the said Act and Rules and therefore the State 
Government may initiate appropriate action in this regard.

(c)	 this unregistered pesticide is being sold in the open market across the State viz., Petty shops, Super markets 
etc., which is highly poisonous and likely to involve high risk to Human beings and Animals.
	
(d)	 National Health Mission, Chennai, in its study report also indicated that 3% Yellow Phosphorus which is being 
used as Rodenticide is the major cause of suicidal death across the State.
	
(e)	 The High Level Committee also recommended imposing immediate ban on sale of 3% Yellow Phosphorus for 
Agricultural use;
AND WHEREAS the 3% Yellow Phosphorus  (Phosphorus paste) is an insecticide, which has not been registered  under 
the Insecticides Act, 1968 (Central Act 46 of 1968);
NOW, THEREFORE,	 in exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of Section 18 of the Insecticides Act, 1968, 
(Central Act 46 of 1968), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby prohibits the sale, stocking, exhibition for sale, distribution, 
transportation or use of phosphorus paste or any preparation containing any such substance in the whole of the State.
