HOME DEPARTMENT
Draft Amendment to Tamil Nadu Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989.
[G.O. Ms. No. 982, Home (Transport.VII), 23th November 2009.]
No. SRO A-39(a)/2009.
The following draft of an amendment to the Tamil Nadu Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, which it is proposed
to make in exercise of the powers conferred by Section 96 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (Central Act 59 of
1988) is hereby published for the information of all persons likely to be affected thereby, as required by
sub-section (1) of Section 212 of the said Act.
2. Notice is hereby given that the draft amendment will be taken into consideration on or after the expiry
of fifteen days from the date of publication of this Notification in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette and that
any objection or suggestion, which may be received from any person with respect thereto before the expiry
of the aforesaid period will be considered by the Government of Tamil Nadu.
3. Objection or suggestion, if any, should be addressed in duplicate to the Principal Secretary to Government,
Home Department, Chennai-600 009.
DRAFT AMENDMENT
In the said Rules, in rule 149, for the expression “delegate to the Secretary to Transport Commissioner,
Secretaries to State Transport Authority Assistant Secretaries” the expression “delegate to the Regional Transport
Authority, Secretary to Transport Commissioner, Secretaries to State Transport authority and Assistant Secretaries”
shall be substituted.
Explanatory Note
In order to enable the Regional Transport Authority of the concerned district to formulate routes for plying
mini buses in the districts considering the interests of the travelling public in the district concerned it is
considered necessary to empower the State Transport Authority to delegate its powers under clause (ca) of
sub-section (3) of Section 68 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (Central Act 59 of 1988) to the Regional Transport
Authority. The proposed draft amendment will enable the Regional Transport Authority to formulate routes for
plying mini buses in the district concerned to satisfy the need of the travelling public.
