 V.O. Chidambaranar Port Trust, tuticorin 
The V.O. Chidambaranar Port Trust (Issue of Entry Permit for Vehicles/Equipments/Persons) (Amendment) 
Regulation, 2017.
(No. Tra-Opnop-Veh-Dwell-V1-14/41761.)
No. SRO C-2/2018.
In pursuance of sub-section (2) of Section 124 read with sub-section (1) of Section 132 of the Major Port Trust 
Act,1963 (38 of 1963), the V.O. Chidambaranar Port Trust hereby publishes the following regulations as required under 
said sections, namely by superseding the earlier notification published in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette vide 
SRO C-20/2017 in Part III–Section 2 dt 30-08-2017 and dt 06-09-2017.
1. Short Title and Commencement: 
	
(i) These regulations may be called “The V.O. Chidambaranar Port Trust (Issue of Entry Permit for 
Vehicles/Equipments/Persons) (Amendment) Regulations, 2017”.
	
(ii) These amended regulations shall come into force from the date of final publication in the Official Gazette 
after approval by the Central Government.
2. In the Tuticorin Port Trust (issue of Entry Permit for Vehicles/Equipments/Persons) Regulations, 2005 (herein after 
referred to as the principal regulations), in regulation (1),-
	
(i) in sub-regulation (1), for the words and brackets “Tuticorin Port Trust (issue of Entry Permit for Vehicles/
Equipments/Persons)” the words and brackets “V.O. Chidambaranar Port Trust (Issue of Entry Permit for Vehicles/
Equipments/Persons)” shall be substituted;
	
(ii) in sub-regulation (2), for the words “These will not apply” the words “These regulations shall not apply to 
visitors” shall be substituted.
3. In the principal regulation, in regulation 2,-
	
(i) in clause (b), for the words “the Board of Trustees of the Port of Tuticorin”, the words “ The V.O. Chidambaranar 
Port Trust” shall be substituted;
	
(ii) in clause (d), for the words “Tuticorin Port Trust”, the words, “V.O. Chidambaranar Port Trust” shall be substituted.
4. In the principal regulations, in regulation 3,-
	
(i) in clause (ii) for the word “Chairman”, the word “Traffic Manager” shall be substituted;
	
(ii) in clause (iii) after the word “designation”, the words “Aadhaar Number”, shall be inserted;
	
(iii) in clause (iv), after the words “The entry permit shall be”, the words “issued at the pass section and shall 
be”, shall be inserted.
5. In the principal regulations, in regulation 4, for sub-regulation (c), the following sub-regulation shall be substituted, 
namely:-
“(c) In the case of equipment, in addition to the documents, mentioned at sub-regulation (2), certificates obtained 
from the competent authority under the Dock Safety Regulations,1990 i.e., Form-II, Form-IV and Form-VI shall also be 
produced”.
6. In the principal regulations, in regulation 5,-
	
(i) in sub-regulation (a), for the words starting with “In the case of entry” and ending with the words “under the 
Regulations”, the following shall be substituted, namely:-
“In the case of entry of persons into the port, the following shall be construed as violations under these regulations:”
	
(ii) in sub-regulation (a), for clause (i), the following clause shall be substituted, namely:-
	
“(i) furnishing of false information or misrepresentation of facts by the persons or agents for obtaining entry 
permit”;

“(xv) Carrying projected articles without red lamp or red flag at the front and rear end.”;
	
	
(d) after clause (xvi) the following clause shall be inserted, namely:-
	
“(xvii) driving the Vehicle without wearing the uniform stipulated by the Regional Transport Officer.”
	
7. In the principal regulations, for regulation 6, the following regulation shall be substituted, namely:-
6. Penalties - (a) For violation by persons in obtaining or using the entry permit, the permit of the person shall be 
suspended or cancelled by the Traffic Manager.
	
(b) For violation in respect of Vehicle entry, listed in sub-regulation (b) of regulation 5, penalty of two hundred 
rupees and or banning of entry into the Port for a period up to one week on each occasion would be imposed by the 
Traffic Manager and if violation of any kind is noticed on a third occasion within six months, the Vehicle entry shall be 
banned into the Port area for a period of one month, in addition to levy of fine by the Traffic Manager.
	
(c) Dwell time and penalty for Commercial Vehicles inside the Port area shall -
	
	
(i) for the purpose of levy of penalty for overstay of commercial vehicles inside the Port area, the time shall 
count from Check Post I or II on entry and back to Check Post II or I on exit and for excess stay time within the 
Port area beyond the specified duration shall involve penalty and any relaxation due to reasons beyond the control of 
either parties is required, then, the relaxation of penalty is left to the discretion of the Traffic Manager.
	
	
(ii) The Dwell time shall be fixed by the Port in consultation with the Port Users and notified to the users 
in the form of a “Trade Notice”.
