ORDER:—G.O. Ms. No. 259, Home (SC), 29th May 2019.
The following amendments are made to the Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Police Medal Rules issued in the Government 
Order first read above:- 
Aඕඍඖඌඕඍඖග-I
In the said Rules, in Rule 13 for the expression, "Medal for Gallantry will carry a monthly monetary allowance" the 
expression "Medal for Gallantry will carry a cash prize" shall be substituted.
In the said Rules, in Rule 13 clause (a) under the title "For Gallantry" for the expression "All the recipients of Gallantry 
award shall be entitled to Monetary Allowance of Rs.300/-p.m. irrespective of their ranks, without any restriction. Where an 
officer who has already been awarded the Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Police Medal for Gallantry is subsequently awarded 
another medal for a further act of gallantry, he shall be paid the full allowance, in addition to the original allowance", the 
expression "All the recipients of Gallantry award prior to 5-2-2014 shall be entitled to Monetary Allowance of Rs.300/- p.m. 
irrespective of their ranks, without any restriction and where an officer who has already been awarded the Tamil Nadu Chief 
Minister's Police Medal for Gallantry is subsequently awarded another medal for a further act of gallantry after 5-2-2014, he 
shall be paid the full cash prize, in addition to the original allowance/ cash prize". 
In the said Rules, in Rule 13 clause (b) under the title "For Gallantry" the expression "the Medal alone would be forfeited 
in case of misconduct for the recipient of Medal after 05-02-2014" shall be included. 
In the said Rules, in Rule 13 clause (c) and (d), the expression "Monetary allowance" and "allowance", shall be read 
as "cash prize" for the recipient of the Medal after 5-2-2014. 
In the said Rules, Rule 14 shall be read as " The award may be made posthumously and in such cases, the lumpsum 
grant/cash prize attached to the award of outstanding devotion to duty/Gallantry shall be paid to the family".
2.  The following amendments are made to the Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Prison Service Medal Rules issued in the 
Government Order second read above:-  
 
.  
Aඕඍඖඌඕඍඖග-II
In the said Rules, in Rule 12 under the title "For Gallantry" the following sub-clauses (a) to (d) shall be included:-

(a)  
All the recipients of Gallantry award prior to 5-2-2014 shall be entitled to Monetary Allowance of Rs.300/- 
p.m. irrespective of their ranks, without any restriction. Where an officer who has already been awarded the 
Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Prison Service Medal for Gallantry is subsequently awarded another medal for 
a further act of gallantry, he shall be paid the full allowance, in addition to the original allowance. Where an 
officer who has already been awarded the Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Prison Service Medal for Gallantry is 
subsequently awarded another medal for a further act of gallantry after 5-2-2014, he shall be paid the full 
cash prize, in addition to the original allowance/cash prize; 
(b)  
The allowance shall be granted from the date of the act for which the award is given and unless it is forfeited 
for misconduct, shall continue until death. The medal alone would be forfeited in case of misconduct for the 
recipient of Medal after 5-2-2014; 
(c)  
Where a recipient of the Medal before 5-2-2014 is in receipt of the Monetary allowance at the time of his 
death, it shall be continued for life or till remarriage of his widow (the first married wife having the preference). 
In the case of posthumous award of the Medal or bar prior to 5-2-2014, the allowance shall be paid to the 
widow (the first married wife having preference) from the date of Gallantry act for which the award is made, 
for her life or till re-marriage. In case of Posthumous award of the Medal or bar after 5-2-2014, the cash prize 
shall be paid to the widow (the first married wife having preference); and 
(d)  
The act of Gallantry, which is worthy of recognition by the award of the Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Prison 
Service Medal  for Gallantry, when performed by one upon whom the  Decoration had been conferred already, 
may be recorded by a  bar attached to the riband by which the Medal is suspended.  For every such additional 
act, an additional bar may be added.  
In the said Rules, Rule 13 shall be read as "The award may  be made posthumously' and in such cases, the lumpsum 
grant/cash prize attached to the award of outstanding devotion to  duty/Gallantry shall be paid to the family".

Service Medal for Gallantry, when performed by one upon whom the Decoration  had been conferred already, 
may be recorded by a bar  attached to the riband by which the Medal is suspended. For  every such additional

Amendments to the Tamil Nadu District Mineral Foundation Rules, 2017.

In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (4) of Section 15 of  the Mines and Minerals (Development and 
Regulation) Act, 1957 (Central Act  67 of 1957), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following  amendment to 
the Tamil Nadu District Mineral Foundation Rules, 2017,  namely:-  
Aඕඍඖඌඕඍඖගඛ
In the said Rules, in rule 13, after sub-rule (4), the following sub rule shall be  inserted, namely:-  
"(4A). Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-rules(1), (2), (3) and (4), an amount not exceeding one percentage 
(1%) of the total monthly receipts of the Fund shall be transferred, by the 10th day of every succeeding month, to 
and deposited in the bank account  maintained and operated, in this behalf, by the Director of Geology and  Mining 
for meeting the expenses for the State/Regional level  monitoring of the activities of District Mineral Foundations 
of the State including the implementation of the projects sanctioned by them viz. modernisation of the Department 
by adopting state of the art  technologies/systems in the Information Technology and e-governance  practices/
systems, Development of Infrastructure, conducting  Meetings, Trainings, etc.; 
Provided that the Director of Geology and Mining shall constitute a  committee, which consists of the Director of Geology 
and Mining, a  senior official from the Directorate of Geology and Mining and a  concerned officer from the Secretariat in 
Industries Department not  lower than Deputy Secretary to Government and the Committee shall  be headed by the Director 
of Geology and Mining, for monitoring the  implementation of projects and the regulation of the expenditure from  the Fund 
for e-governance and District Mineral Foundation schemes  monitoring expenditure. Any expenditure from the Fund shall be 
made  after proper scrutiny and recommendations by the Committee.  
Provided further that this 1% of total monthly receipts of District  Mineral Foundation Fund shall be from and within the 
maximum 6% of  the annual receipts of District Mineral Foundation Fund earmarked for  the administrative, supervisory and 
overhead costs or establishment  expenses of the Foundation.
