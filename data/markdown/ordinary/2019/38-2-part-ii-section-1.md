2. The use of Aadhaar for the members of the Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT) will result in 
accuracy of the member details. This will also help in ascertaining identities of the members at the time of providing beneﬁ ts 
in a convenient and hassle free manner. Aadhaar obviates the need for producing multiple documents to prove one’s identity.
3. Members who are in possession of Aadhaar, shall register the Aadhaar Number towards their enrolment to the existing 
Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT) in the Enumeration Register maintained by the Village Administrative 
Oﬃ  cers for the proof of his/her identity and authentication thereof.

4. Members who are not yet enrolled for Aadhaar, are hereby required to make application for Aadhaar enrolment 
in case he/she is entitled to obtain Aadhaar as per section 3 of the Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of Financial and other 
Subsidies, Beneﬁ ts and Services) Act, 2016 (Central Act No.18 of 2016).  Such member may visit any Aadhaar enrolment 
centre /Permanent Enrolment Centre (list available at www.uidai.gov.in) to get enrolled for Aadhaar.
5. The Special Tahsildars (Social Security Scheme) / Village Administrative Oﬃ  cers will guide / facilitate the enrolment 
of Aadhaar for such members who are not having Aadhaar.  Those members who have enrolled for Aadhaar and have 
not received Aadhaar should register the 28 digit Aadhaar enrolment ID number printed on the Aadhaar enrolment slip (or) 
Government approved Photo Identity card like voter ID, Pan Card, Passport, Driving License etc.
6. Commissionerate of Land Reforms has inorganically seeded the Aadhaar Number of few of its Chief Minister’s 
Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT) members through the State Family Data Base (SFDB) set up by the Tamil Nadu 
e-Governance Agency (TNeGA).  The inorganically seeded Aadhaar number of the Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu 
Thittam (CMUPT) members shall be obtained by the Commissionerate of Land Reforms from the Tamil Nadu e-Governance 
Agency (TNeGA).  For the members whose Aadhaar number are not seeded inorganically, the Commissionerate of Land 
Reforms has proposed to seed the Aadhaar numbers organically through the Special Tahsildars (Social Security Scheme) 
in the Districts. 
7. The Commissioner for Revenue Administration has seeded the Aadhaar Numbers for beneﬁ ciaries of the Old Age 
Pension Schemes implemented by Revenue and Disaster Management Department. Some of the Old Age Pension beneﬁ ciaries 
are also members of the Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT).  The seeded Old Age Pension data would 
be used to seed the Aadhaar Number for the Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT) member so as to 
obviate the need for the same member to again seed the data in the Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT).
8. The Chief Minister’s Uzhavar Pathukappu Thittam (CMUPT) members data seeded with Aadhaar Numbers may also 
be used by the Special Tahsildars (SSS) in the Districts for the purpose of identifying the members/beneﬁ ciaries.
 
ATULYA MISRA,
 
 
 
 
Additional Chief Secretary to Government.
