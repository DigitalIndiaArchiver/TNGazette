JUDICIAL NOTIFICATIONS.
Amendment to the Madras High Court Service Rules.
(Roc. No. 20/2014/Con.B2./RAC.)
No. SRO C-13/2014.
In exercise of the powers conferred by Article 229 (1) and (2) of the Constitution of India and of all other
powers connected thereto, the Hon’ble the Acting Chief Justice is pleased to make the following amendment to the
Madras High Court Service Rules:—
AMENDMENT
In Rule 6 (b) of the Madras High Court Service Rules, after the entries beginning with “Notwithstanding anything contained
in the aforesaid provision” and ending with “such law degree.”,
the following shall be added:—
“Provided that members of the service holding a law degree granted by a recognized Indian University
but not recognized by Bar Council of India for admission as Advocate or Attorney of an Indian Court,
may also be considered for appoinment by promotion to the Category 2 of Division-I excluding the post of Master,
if otherwise eligible on merit-cum-seniority basis.”
The above amendment shall come into force with effect from 21st July 2014.
High Court, Madras,
P. KALAIYARASAN,
21st July  2014.
