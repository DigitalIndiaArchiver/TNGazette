Ad hoc Rules relating to the Post of Therapeutic Assistant in Yoga and Naturopathy Clinics of the
Indian Medicine in the Tamil Nadu Medical Subordinate Service
[G.O. Ms. No. 264, Health and Family Welfare (IM-2) (2), 19th August 2008, Aavani 3, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2039.]
No. SRO B-45/2008.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India,
the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following rules:—
2.
The rules hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on the 6th September 2002.
RULES
The General and the Special Rules applicable to the holders of the permanent posts in Branch II-Indigenous Medicine
of the Tamil Nadu Medical Subordinate Service, shall apply to the holders of the temporary post of Therapeutic Assistant
sanctioned  from  time to time, for the Yoga and Naturopathy Clinics of the Indian System of Medicine, subject to the
modifications specified in the following rules:—
2. Constitution.—The post shall constitute a separate category in the said Branch of the said service.
3. Appointment.—Appointment to the post shall be made as follows:—
(i)
by recruitment by transfer from the category of Hospital Worker, in the Tamil Nadu Basic Service, working in
the Yoga and Naturopathy Clinic; or
(ii) by recruitment by transfer from any other service; or
(iii) by direct recruitment.
4. Appointing authority.—The Appointing authority for the post shall be the Head of Institution concerned.
5. Qualifications.—(a) Age-No person shall be eligible for appointment to the post, by direct recruitment, if he has
completed or will complete the age of thirty  years on the first day of July of the year in which the selection for appointment
is made.
(b) Other qualifications.— No person shall be eligible for appointment to the post, unless he possesses the
Minimum General Educational Qualification.
6. Training.—The Candidates selected for appointment shall undergo training in the Yoga and Naturopathy Techniques
for a period of six months and the said period shall count for probation.
7. Probation.—Every person appointed to the post shall from the date on which he joins duty, be on probation for
a total period of two years on duty within a continuous period of three years.
8. Pay.—There shall be paid to the holder of the post, a monthly pay calculated in the scale of
Rs. 2,610—60—3,150—65—3,540.
9. Transfer.—The holders of the post shall be liable for transfer from one Institution to another, wherever the post
is sanctioned. The authority competent to transfer from one institution to another institution shall be the Head of
Department (i.e. the Special Commissioner/Commissioner of Indian Medicine and Homoeopathy).
10. Reservation of appointment.—The rule of reservation of appointments (General Rule 22) shall apply for appointments
to the post by direct recruitment.

RULES
   The General and Special Rules applicable to the holders of the permanent posts in Branch II-Nursing of the
Tamil Nadu Medical Service shall apply to the holder of the temporary post of Principal, College of Nursing sanctioned
from time to time, subject to the modifications specified in the following rules:—
2. Constitution.—The post shall constitute a separate category in the said branch of the said service.
3. Appointment.—Appointment to the post shall be made by promotion  from the category of Deputy Director of
Medical Education (Nursing) or Reader in Nursing.
4. Qualifications.—No person shall be eligible for appointment to the post, unless he possesses the following
qualifications, namely:—
(i)
Must have passed a Master's degree in Nursing in any speciality concerned or its equivalent, recognized by
the Indian Nursing Council;
(ii) Must possess teaching experience in Nursing either in School of Nursing or in College of Nursing, or in both,
for a period of not less than 10 years in the capacity of Nursing Tutor Grade-II or Nursing Tutor Grade-I
or Lecturer in Nursing or Reader in Nursing;
(iii) Must have passed the following Departmental Tests:—
(a) Account Test for Executive Officers.
(b) Tamil Nadu Medical Code.
5. Pay.—There shall be paid to the holder of the post, a monthly pay calculated in the scale of
Rs. 12,750—375—16,500.
Ad hoc Rules relating to Temporary Post of Reader (Now Associate Professor) in Spine
Surgery in Government Medical Colleges in the Tamil Nadu Medical Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 310, Health and Family Welfare (A1), 23rd September 2008,
Purattasi 7, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2039.]
No. SRO B-47/2008.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of Constitution of India, the
Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following rules:—
2. The rules hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on the 12th May 2006.
RULES
The General and Special rules applicable to the holders of the permanent post of Clinical Reader in category 9,
in Class I in Branch-I Medical of the Tamil Nadu Medical Service shall apply to the holders of the temporary post of
Reader in Spine Surgery in Government Medical Colleges, subject to the Modifications specified in the following rules:—
2. Constitution.—The post shall constitute a distinct category in the said class of the said Branch of the said service.
3. Appointment.—Appointment to the post shall be made as follows:—
(i)  by transfer from Clinical Lecturer or from any other category included in the cadre of Senior Civil Surgeon
in Class I Branch I Medical of the said service; or
(ii) by promotion from the category of Assistant Professor or Clinical Tutor in the Tamil Nadu Medical Service.
4. Qualifications.—No person  shall be eligible for appointment to the post, unless he possesses the following
qualifications, namely:—
(i) Must have obtained the degree in M.S. (Orthopaedics) or a Post Graduate degree in Spine Surgery of any
University or Institution;
(ii) Must have teaching experience for a period not less than four years in the subject in a recognized Medical
College or teaching institution;
(iii) Must have working experience for a period not less than four years in the Orthopaedic Surgery Unit under
the Department of Orthopaedics of a recognized Medical College Hospital, of which two years must be in
Spine Surgery; and
(iv) Must have done atleast 200 numbers of spine surgeries as certified by the Head of the Department and
authenticated by the Head of the Institution concerned;

 HOME DEPARTMENT
Amendment to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Fire and Rescue Services
[G.O. Ms. No. 1224, Home (Police-XVII), 24th September 2008, Purattasi 8, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2039.]
No. SRO B-48/2008.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India,
the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendment to the Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Fire and
Rescue Services (Section 13 of Part-III A of the Service Rules in Volume II of the Tamil Nadu Services Manual, 1969).
2. The amendment hereby made shall come into force with effect on and from the 24th September, 2008.
AMENDMENT
In the said Rules, in rule 2, under the "TABLE", in column (2), for the entries against the entry "Class-I—Director
of Fire and Rescue Service" in column (1), the following entry shall be substituted, namely:—
"Filling from the Cadre of Indian Police Service Officers in the rank of Additional Director General of Police."
S. MALATHI,
Principal Secretary to Government.
PERSONNEL AND ADMINISTRATIVE REFORMS DEPARTMENT
Amendment to Fundamental Rules
[G.O.Ms. No. 179, Personnel and Administrative Reforms (FR-IV), 29th September 2008,
Purattasi 13, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2039.]
No. SRO B-49/2008.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 read with Article 313 of the
Constitution of India and of all other powers hereunto enabling, the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following
amendment to the Fundamental Rules.
2. The amendment hereby made shall be deemed to have come into force on the 3rd November, 2006.
AMENDMENT
In the said Fundamental Rules, in rule 56, in sub-rule (3), to clause (f), after the proviso, the following proviso shall
be added, namely :—
“Provided further that the appointing authority may also withhold the permission for voluntary retirement sought
for by a Government servant, if the post held by him has been declared as “Scarce Category” by the administrative
department concerned in Secretariat and whose continuation in Government service is absolutely essential in public
interest.”
A. ELANGOVAN,
Principal Secretary to Government.
