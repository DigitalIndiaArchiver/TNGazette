In the order first read above, the Hon’ble First Bench of
Madras has ordered that;
‘the petitioner has affirmed an affidavit on 28.01.2016
tendering an unqualified apology for not disclosing the
material facts which have been set out in the affidavit filed
in support of the writ petition and seeks leave to withdraw
from the proceedings.   Further, the Hon’ble First Bench of
Madras High Court wanted to satisfy themselves over the
matter in issue and thus, direct the present writ petition to
be converted into a suo-motu proceedings and to implead
the Companies viz., M/s. Southern Enterprises, M/s. Indian
Garnet Sand Company Private Limited and M/s. Grace
Minerals and further directed those companies to file Counter
Affidavit.
7.  The Hon’ble First Bench of Madras High Court
has appointed Dr. V. Suresh, Advocate as Amicus to assist
them in this matter since the petitioner has now withdrawn
from the proceedings.  A complete set of paper book be
supplied to the Learned Amicus by the Registry.”
During the hearing on 11.1.2017, the Amicus Curiae
has submitted a memo for the consideration of the Hon’ble
Court.   In his memo in para 2 and 3, he has requested that;
“ 2. direct the Government of Tamil Nadu and the
Government of India to form special squads comprising of
Police, Revenue, Mining and other departments for patrolling
of sea shore areas to detect and stop mining of beach sand
across the coastal districts of Tirunelveli, Kanniyakumari
and Tuticorin.

3.  To constitute a special team headed by a
Secretary level officer of the State Government and officials

2.  In the order second read above, the Hon’ble
High Court in its order dated 11.1.2017 has ordered  in para
5 as follows’
“In so far as the second and third requests are
concerned, we call upon the State Government and the
Central Government to look into the issues jointly and suggest
a strategy.”
3. In accordance with the orders of Hon’ble High Court
of Madras, the Government hereby constitute a Special Team
headed by a Secretary Level Officer and  nominates
Thiru Mahesan Kasirajan, I.A.S., Commissioner of Sugar
(Batch: 2000) for assessment of stocks of Beach Sand
Minerals in various places of Thoothukudi, Tirunelveli and
Kanyakumari Districts under section 23 (B), 24 of Mines and
Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957.
(a) This Special Team will also consist of officials
from;
1.
Customs and Excise Department, Government
of India
2.
(i)  Shri Ramesh Kumar, Head, Beach Sand
and Off-shore Investigation, Atomic Mineral  Division,
Hyderabad.
(ii)  Shri Soumen Sinha, Scientific Officer-F,
Atomic Energy Regulatory Board, Mumbai.
3.
 Department of Geology and Mining, Revenue
and Survey Department for the estimation of the stocks of
Beach Sand Mineral kept in various places of Thoothukudi,
Tirunelveli and Kanyakumari Districts.
(b) This Special Team shall exercise all the powers
enumerated in the sections 23(B) and 24 of Mines and
Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957.
(c) The District Collectors of Thoothukudi, Tirunelveli
and Kanyakumari will produce the list of places where stock
of Beach Sand Minerals being kept in the respective Districts
for the assessment by the team
(d) The Special Team is hereby directed to complete
the inspections expeditiously and submit it’s report to the
Government within three months.
