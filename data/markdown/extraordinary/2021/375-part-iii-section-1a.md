AMENDMENTS TO THE TAMIL NADU PREVENTION OF ILLEGAL MINING, TRANSPORTATION AND STORAGE OF 
MINERALS AND MINERAL DEALERS RULES, 2011.

No. SRO A-14(g)/2021.
In exercise of the powers conferred under Section 23(c) of the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 
1957 (Central Act 67 of 1957), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the Tamil Nadu 
Prevention of Illegal Mining, Transportation and Storage of Minerals and Mineral Dealers Rules, 2011.
2. It shall come into force on the date of publication in the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette.
Aඕඍඖඌඕඍඖගඛ
In the said rules, 
 (1) in rule 2, 
 
 
(i) after clause (iii), the following clause, shall be inserted, namely:-
 
 
 
“(iii-a) “CCTV" means a Closed Circuit Television system involving one or more cameras connected to 
one or more monitors for surveillance and monitoring of illegal transportation of minerals”;
 
 
(ii) after clause (iv), the following clause shall be inserted namely:-
 
 
 
“(iv-a) “Control room" means a protected room in the premises of District Collectorate wherein the 
CCTV cameras are monitored twenty four hours by establishing suﬃ  cient monitors and manpower by the District Collector”;
(2) after rule 9, the following rule shall be inserted, namely:-
“9A Installation of CCTV Cameras.-   (1) If the District Collector considers it necessary with a view to prevent the illegal 
transportation of minerals across the borders of the District, he may, install CCTV camera units at any place, in consultation 
with Superintendent of Police of the district concerned by an order in writing.

(2) All such CCTV units established shall be monitored by establishing a centralised control room at the premises of the 
District Collectorate by the District Collector or any other oﬃ  cer authorized by him in this behalf for live streaming of videos 
from the CCTV.
(3) The oﬃ  cials in-charge of the control room, shall monitor the videos and identify transportation of minerals, if any. In 
all such cases of transportation of minerals identiﬁ ed or suspected as illegal, the oﬃ  cials in-charge of the control room shall 
inform such incident to the enforcing oﬃ  cials authorized under sub-section (4) of Sections 21 and 24 of the Act for immediate 
action and record that for future purpose.  All the data received through live streaming shall be stored in the server for future 
reference.
(4) The Enforcing Oﬃ  cials shall take immediate action and furnish the action taken report to the District Collector within 
twenty four hours from the time of receipt of the information from the centralised control room.
(5) The District Collector shall establish a robust customer care centre which is functional twenty four hours at the 
centralized control room”.
 
N. MURUGANANDAM,
