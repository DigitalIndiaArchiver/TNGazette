Based on the Government of India’s letter first’ and second read above, the Government in their orders 3rd read above
issued orders for Constituting the State Level Executive Committee (SLEC) under the Chairmanship of
Chief Secretary to Government.
2. Further, the Government of India in its letter fourth read above, has informed that besides constituting State Level Executive
Committee (SLEC), the following Committees have to be Constituted under National Livestock Mission by the
State Government:-
1. District Livestock Mission Committee (DLMC) at the District level.
2. State Level Sanctioning and Monitoring Committee (SLSMC).
and requested to take necessary steps to initiate the above set-up and submit the proposals under National Livestock Mission
for financing duly recommended by State Level Executive Committee.
3. Further, as per the National Livestock Mission (NLM) operational guidelines of Government of India, the State Government
shall also constitute and notify a State Livestock Mission at operation level in the State which would be an empowered body for
implementing the Mission Programmes (NLM Programmes of Government of India) at State and District level with Principal
Secretary/Secretary, Animal Husbandry Department as the State Mission Director and the Director of Animal Husbandry to
service the State Livestock Mission and State Level Executive Committee (SLEC) also.
4. Consequent to the above direction of Government of India in their NLM operational guidelines, the Government after careful
examination issue orders that District Livestock Mission Committee (DLMC), State Level Sanctioning and Monitoring Committee
(SLSMC) and State Livestock Mission (SLM) be constituted for implementing the Mission Programmes of NLM with the following
members as indicated below and the functions of the above committees are also detailed below:-

Functions of SLM :-
•
Prepare Strategic, Perspective and Annual Action Plan in consonance with Mission’s goals and objectives and in close
co-ordination with Technical Support Groups, SAUs and ICAR Institutes; and oversee its implementation.
•
Organize base-line survey and feasibility studies in different parts (District, Sub-District, or a cluster of Districts) to
determine status of fodder-/livestock production, potential and demand and tailor assistance accordingly.
•
 Receive funds from National Mission Authority, State Government and other sources, pool financial resources for
carrying on mission’s activities, maintain proper accounts thereof and submit utilization certificate to National Mission
Authority.
•
Release funds to Implementing organizations and oversee, monitor and review implementation of the programmes.
•
Assist and oversee Mission’s programmes being implemented through Farmers’ Societies, NGOs, growers associations,
co-operatives, self help groups, Farmers Producer Organisations (FPOs), and other similar entities;
•
Organize Workshops, Seminars and training Programmes for sensitization of all interest groups /associations at State/
District / Sub-District level to provisions of NLM, as also for technology transfer and skill development, with the help of
SAUs, ICAR Institutes, ATMAs / KVKs, Civil Society Organisations and other institutions having technical expertise.
•
Furnish monthly progress reports to DADF and also upload the same by 5th of each month, on the website of the
Department/Mission.
•
Operationalise Information Communication Technology (ICT) enabled Management Information System (MIS) up to
grass  root level and, if need be, develop and host its own website.
•
Supervision and monitoring of the implementing agencies.
