and Co-operation of Krishi Bhavan New Delhi, dated 03-03-2010, the Government of Tamil Nadu vide. Lr. No 11255/AP1/2011,
dated 08-06-2011, The Commissioner of Agriculture Letter No. G1/20665/2011, Dated 14-06-2011 for implementation of the
Pilot Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme (WBCIS) trough Agriculture Insurance Company of India Limited (AIC),
IFFCO-TOKIO, ICICI- LOMBARD, CHOLAMANDALAM-MS,HDFC-ERGO General Insurance company during Kharif 2011 his
appended as Annexure I for Paddy I (Kar/Kuruvai/Sornavari), Paddy II (Samba/Thalady/Pishanam), Pulses (Blackgram), Millets,
Redgram, Maize, Cumbu (Bajra),Groundnut, Gingelly (Sesame), Sunflower, Cotton Irrigated and Rainfed, Vegetables Crops
(Onion,Tomato, Chillies), Tapioca, Turmeric, Oil Palm and Banana  in 11 Districts, viz. Dharmapuri, Perambalur, Ariyalur, Salem,
Virudhunagar. Villupuram, Dindigul, Coimbatore, Thiruppur, Theni and Thirunelveli. Oil Palm coverage is only for the districts
of Nagapattinam, Thiruvarur, Thanjavur, Cuddalore, Trichy and Perambalur.
Guidelines for participating in WBCIS – Kharif - 2011 are as follows:
 The National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (NAIS) stands withdrawn for Kharif 2011 for Paddy I (Kar/Kuruvai/Sornavari),
Paddy II (Samba/Thalady/Pishanam), Pulses (Blackgram), Millets, Redgram, Maize, Cumbu (Bajra), Groundnut, Gingelly (Sesame),
Sunflower, Cotton Irrigated and Rainfed, Vegetables Crops (Onion,Tomato, Chillies), Tapioca, Turmeric and Banana in
11 Districts, viz. Dharmapuri, Perambalur, Ariyalur, Salem, Virudhunagar, Villupuram, Dindigul, Coimbatore, Thiruppur, Theni
and Thirunelveli viz., w.r.t. Loanee Farmers. The Loanee farmers would be covered under the Pilot WBCIS on  Compulsory
basis which is mandatory as per Govt. Of India Guide lines.
 Non-Loanee Farmers have the choice of NAIS and the pilot WBCIS to choose from. The Non Loanee farmers interested
in the pilot WBCIS in these selected districts/blocks can insure their respective  crops through the banking network or insurance
intermediaries or the authorized representatives of AIC and Other private Insurance company viz., IFFCO-TOKIO, ICICI-
LOMBARD, CHOLAMANDALAM-MS,HDFC-ERGO General Insurance company.

 The sum insured is pre-defined & fixed per unit area, i.e. in acre.
 The criteria for covering Loanee Farmers  is as below :
 Those farmers eligible for availing loans within the seasonality defined (Refer Table encl in Annexure II:) and
as per their loan proposal the respective notified crop should be taken in to account for collection of premium .The premium
should be debited to the account of the eligible farmer without obtaining any consent from the farmer. In other words the premium
should be an  additionality to the loan, debited in advance (even before sowing/ availing loan). Since these loanee farmers
cannot be covered under NAIS there is a possibility that they may miss their opportunity of availing coverage under WBCIS
also. As per GOI direction all eligible credit limit (sanctioned) should be taken in to consideration for compulsory coverage
in to the scheme.
 The criteria for covering Loanee Farmers is based on the availability of ‘Sanctioned Credit Limit’ as on the cut-off
date prescribed for the purpose. The sum insured is to be arrived at on the basis of the acreage of Paddy II crop declared
by the farmer in the loan application (based on which the credit limit has been sanctioned).
 As an illustration, a farmer has a sanctioned credit limit of Rs. 30,000 as on 15th July 2011  for Tapioca Crop.. Since
the farmer has sanctioned credit limit, he / she is eligible for compulsory insurance. Assuming,  the farmer’s loan application
reveals that he / she had declared 4 acres under Tapioca, the sum insured, therefore, is Rs. 12,000 (per acre pre-defined
sum insured) multiplied by the no. of acres declared in the loan application, i.e. 4 acres = Rs. 48,000.  The premium for
Loanee is based on acreage i.e. for  Tapioca Crop 4 acres multiplied by Rs.720/- = 2880.00 must be compulsorily remitted
for Insurance by banks.
 Agricultural Jewel Loans (AJLs) are eligible for compulsory coverage at par with other / pure agricultural loans.It should
be  ensured that all eligible AJLs sanctioned / disbursed as on the cut-off date prescribed for the purpose are insured under
the pilot WBCIS, based on acreage declared, multiplied by Acreage Sum Insured.
 In case of Non Loanee farmers, the sum insured is arrived at by multiplying the pre-defined sum insured for example
Groundnut Crop (Rs.10,000) (Refer Annexure III) with the no. of acres for  the  farmer is proposing (in the proposal form)
for insurance. An illustration: A non-loanee farmer wishes to buy insurance for 5 acres (through proposal form), and in which
case the total sum insured is  for Rs. 10,000 multiplied by 5 acres = Rs. 50,000.  The premium for Groundnut Crop payable
by the farmer is Rs. 350 per acre. For 5 acres the premium payable by the farmer is Rs. 350 *5 = Rs.1750/-  The remaining
portion of premium Rs. 625*5 =3125/-  on behalf of the farmer is shared equally by both State and the Central Govts.
 The sum insured per acre is broadly distributed under different crop phases for notified crops under WBCIS Rabi
2010-11. The details of risk period and distribution of Sum insured Premium rates for various period is provided under
Annexure-III. The actuarial premium for Cereal, Millets, Pulses and Oil seeds is 9.75 % of sum insured. The actuarial premium
for Commercial Horticultural Crops is 11.75 % of sum insured. However, the actual premium payable by the  farmer is what
he / she is paying under NAIS, for Cereal, Millets and Oil seeds  and for Commercial Horticultural Crops is 6% of sum insured
only. The difference between the actuarial premium and the actual premium payable by the farmer is borne by the State and
Central Government on 50:50 basis. The details premium payable is appended as Annexure-III.
 Pay outs (claims) under the scheme are decided on ‘area approach’ basis based on the required data recorded at
the ‘Reference Weather Station (RWS)’ during the risk period. If the RWS data be unavailable for any day during the risk
period, the data of ‘Back-up Weather Station (BWS)’ would be used for the purpose. The details of the RWS and BWS are
appended as Annexure-IV
 A copy of the ‘Non-Loanee Declaration format’ (Annexure-V) and ‘Loanee Declaration format’ (Annexure-VI) is appended
with this communication.
 All payouts under the scheme are settled by Agriculture Insurance Company of India Limited (AIC) and Other private
Insurance company viz., IFFCO-TOKIO, ICICI- LOMBARD, CHOLAMANDALAM-MS,HDFC-ERGO General Insurance company.
and to the extent possible within few days from the end of the risk / observation period as per the time of availability of
the data.
 State Government would continue to conduct the ‘Crop Cutting Experiments (CCEs)’ for all the above notified crops
which is also under NAIS  in the pilot areas for the purpose of comparing the results and review. However, the payouts under
the pilot WBCIS would be made strictly based on the reference weather station data.

VENUE: Agricultural Production Commissioner and Principal Secretary to Government Chamber, Secretariat, Chennai-9.
Date: 31-5-2011 (Tuesday)
Time: 12.00 A.M.
The meeting of State Level Co-Ordination Committee on Crop Insurance for Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme was
held for Kharif 2011-2012 on 31-5-2011. The meeting was chaired by the Agricultural Production Commissioner and Principal
Secretary to Government, Agriculture Department.
The list of participants is appended.
The Agricultural Production Commissioner and Principal Secretary to Government Chairman of the State Level Co-ordination
Committee on Crop Insurance welcomed the Members of the committee.
The Chairman presided over the meeting and took up the agenda points for discussion.
AGENDA POINTS
1. Insurance Products Discussion and negotiation of Premium rates for Agricultural and Horticultural crops-Kharif 2011
In the State Level Technical Committee on Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme meeting, held on 02-03-2011 the products
submitted by the Insurance Companies were discussed in detail.  The committee has recommended to the State Level
Co-ordination Committee on Crop Insurance to accept and notify the products as furnished in annexure - I.
The State Level Coordination Committee on Crop Insurance in its meeting held on 31-5-2011, decided to accept the proposal
to cover the crops mentioned on the annexure in the districts indicated against them. The crops that are not included will
be covered by Agricultural Insurance Company of India Limited under National Agricultural Insurance Scheme.
(ACTION: COA/All Insurance Companies)
2. Inclusion of Central and Western Districts under Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme
The Agricultural Production Commissioner and Principal Secretary to Government has informed to form a committee to
study the performance of the scheme.  Based on the report the additional districts may be included from Rabi 2011-2012.
The Agricultural Production Commissioner and Principal Secretary to Government has also requested the Commissioner of
Agriculture to send the proposals to Government to form the committee.
(ACTION: COA)
3. Inclusion of Oil palm under Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme
The State Level Co-ordination Committee on Crop Insurance has accept the proposals of State Level Technical Committee
to implement the scheme. (ACTION: COA/All Insurance Companies)
4. Pending  issue of Government Order permission for the Implementation of  Weather Based Crop Insurance
Scheme in  2011-2012
All the Agenda has already been approved, it is decided not to pursue this agenda.
