WHEREAS on considering the prevailing Covid-19 pandemic scenario and as per the directives of Government of 
India, Ministry of Home Aﬀ airs, State-wide lockdown was enforced throughout the State with eﬀ ect from 4.00 A.M. of 
10.05.2021 to 6.00 A.M. of 09.08.2021, in order to control the spread of COVID-19. 
 (2) The Hon’ble Chief Minister conducted review meeting to evaluate the Corona prevention activities on 06.08.2021 
with the Medical experts. Based on the prevailing pandemic situation, Government hereby order under Disaster Management 
Act, 2005 to extend the lockdown from 09.08.2021 to 06.00 A.M. on 23.08.2021 with the following instructions.
(A) In order to avoid public gathering at the same place and at the same time, all the religious places and 
places of worship shall remain closed on Friday, Saturday and Sunday.
(B) In view of the local restrictions enforced in the market and other crowded places by the District Collectors/
Corporation Commissioners and Police Departments the spread of the disease is under control. Therefore, the District 
Collectors and Police Oﬃ  cers should continue taking strict action to enforce the Government guidelines.
(C) The Medical Experts have unanimously emphasized the importance of reopening the schools considering the 
facts, viz depression among the school going children, huge learning gap in the society and non-availability of facilities 
for attending online classes. Hence, classes for 9th, 10th, 11th and 12th standards shall be permitted to be conducted 
in all the schools with eﬀ ect from 01.09.2021 with 50% of students at a time by duly following the appropriate standard 
operating procedures. The School Education Department shall take necessary preparatory action and prescribe appropriate 
standard operating procedures for conducting classes regularly.
(D) All Medical Colleges, Nursing and para medical colleges shall function from 16.08.2021 as the Students, 
Professors and Staﬀ  have already been vaccinated. The Health and Family welfare Department shall issue appropriate 
standard operating procedure in this regard.

(i) 
Local administrations shall ensure that meat and ﬁ sh markets shall function as separate shops in open spaces

(ii) 
Shops permitted to function shall follow the following important Standard Operating Procedures.

Shops shall have mandatory hand hygiene (sanitizer dispenser) at the entrance and thermal screening shall 
also be done.

Management shall ensure that the workers and customers wear face masks compulsorily.

All shops and establishments using air conditioning facility shall ensure that the doors and windows are kept 
open for cross ventilation. In order to ensure social distancing norms, more number of persons shall not be 
allowed at a time inside the shops.

Speciﬁ c markings with suﬃ  cient distance may be made at the entrance of the shops to manage the general 
public standing in the queue.

Action will taken against the commercial/other organizations which violate the above guidelines and 
prescribed limit of customers. 
(iii) Test-Track-Treat-Vaccination-Covid-19 Appropriate Behaviour principle shall be strictly adhered to.
(iv) In order to contain the spread of Covid-19, the Containment Zones shall be demarcated upto Micro Level 
and the containment measures shall be initiated by the District Collectors and the Local Bodies as per the Standard 
Operating Procedures.
(v) Within the demarcated Containment Zones, containment measures shall be scrupulously followed, as under:

Only essential activities shall be allowed in the Containment Zones.

No activity, except medical emergencies and supply of essential goods and services shall be permitted.

There shall be intensive house-to-house surveillance by surveillance teams formed for the purpose.

The respective Departments shall strictly ensure the enforcement of the Standard Operating Procedures 
issued for various permitted activities.
(3) The District Collectors and local administrations concerned shall make special eﬀ orts for Information, 
Education and Communication (IEC) activities by creating awareness and self-responsibility among the public 
to contain the spread of the disease. Everybody has to make a commitment and follow it in word and action 
to prevent the spread of Corona. 
(4) In-order to control the spread of COVID-19, Public are requested to maintain discipline and Covid appropriate 
behaviour by avoiding unnecessarily coming out of the house and gathering in Public places. The public are also 
requested to seek medical advice of the nearest hospital as soon as symptoms of infection appear.
(5) The Government also orders that no activities shall be permitted in the Containment Zones. 
(6) The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/ District Collectors and Commissioners of Police/Superintendents 
of Police concerned shall take all necessary measures for the strict enforcement of National Directives for Covid-19 
management viz., wearing of face masks, hand hygiene and social distancing, screening & hygiene, frequent 
sanitization of entire workplace etc., and adherence of the Standard Operating Procedures issued for the permitted 
activities. The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation/ District Collectors concerned may initiate penal action for 
imposing ﬁ nes on persons violating the National Directives.
(7) Any person violating these measures will be liable to be proceeded against as per the provisions of Section 
51 to 60 of the Disaster Management Act, 2005, besides legal action under Section 188 of the IPC, and other legal 
provisions as applicable.
