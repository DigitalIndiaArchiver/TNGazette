MUNICIPAL ADMINISTRATION AND WATER SUPPLY DEPARTMENT
AMENDMENTS TO THE TAMIL NADU MUNICIPAL ENGINEERING SERVICE  RULES, 1997.
[G.O. Ms. No. 134, Municipal Administration and Water Supply (ME.3), 7th September 2015.]
No. SRO A-12(b)/2015.
In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of Section 303, read with Section 77-A of the Tamil Nadu District Municipalities
Act, 1920 (Tamil Nadu   Act V of 1920), the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the Tamil Nadu
Municipal Engineering Service Rules, 1997:-
AMENDMENTS
In the said Rules, (1) in rule 2;
(a)  for the expression “Executive Engineer / Regional Executive Engineer”, the expression “Executive  Engineer”
shall be substituted;
(b) the following provisos shall be added, namely:-
“Provided that the Executive Engineers who are working in the Regional Municipal Offices shall be designated as Executive
Engineer (Regional) and the Executive Engineers who are working in the Municipalities shall be designated as Executive Engineer
(Municipal):
Provided further that the Assistant Executive Engineers who are working in the Municipalities shall be designated as
Assistant Executive Engineer (Municipal):
Provided that the Assistant Engineers who are working in Municipalities shall be designated as Assistant Engineer
(Municipal).”;

(2) in rule 3, in the tabular column;
           (a) for the entry “II.Executive Engineer including Regional Executive Engineer” In Column (1) and the corresponding
entries in column (2) thereof, the following entries shall, respectively, be substituted, namely:-
“Executive Engineer”
By promotion from category III:
(ii)  Provided that if no qualified persons are available for promotion, then by deputation from Corporation
     Service or other Government Departments or Statutory Boards.”;
(b)  in column (2), for the entries against the expression “III. Assistant Executive Engineer” in column (1), the following
entries, shall be substituted, namely:-
“(i) By promotion from category IV Assistant Engineer and by recruitment by transfer from the post of Junior Engineer in the
Tamil Nadu Municipal Engineering Subordinate Service in the ratio of 3:1.
(ii) Every 20th vacancy in the post shall be filled up by recruitment by transfer from the post of Electrical Superintendent
Grade I in the Tamil Nadu Municipal Engineering Subordinate Service.”; and
(c)  in column (2), for the entries against the expression “IV. Assistant Engineer” in column (1), the following
entries shall be substituted, namely:-
“(i) By Direct Recruitment and by recruitment by transfer from the post of Junior Engineer in the Tamil Nadu Municipal
Engineering Subordinate Service in the ratio of 3:1.”:
(3) in rule 5;
(A) in the tabular column;
(a) in column (1), for the expression “Executive Engineer / Regional Executive Engineer”,  the expression “Executive
Engineer” shall be substituted;
(b) in column (3), against the entry “4. Assistant Engineer” in column (1);
(i)  the existing provision shall be numbered as item (i);
(ii)  after item (i) as so numbered, the following items shall be added, namely:-
 “(ii) The Junior Engineers of the Tamil Nadu Municipal Engineering Subordinate Service, shall, on acquiring the qualification
of B.E. Degree in Civil or Mechanical or Electrical Engineering, be qualified from the date of acquiring such qualification and become
eligible only for consideration to the post of Assistant Engineer by recruitment by transfer.
(iii) the option  exercised by the Junior Engineers for appointment to the post of Assistant Engineer by recruitment by
transfer, after acquiring the qualification of B.E. Degree shall be final and they shall be placed at the bottom of the seniority list of the
post of Assistant Engineer on their recruitment by transfer to that post.  Subsequently, they cannot exercise another option to the
post of Assistant Executive Engineer from the category of Junior Engineer.”;
(B) the heading “Note” and the clauses thereunder shall be omitted;
(4) after rule 5, the following rule shall be inserted, namely:-
“5-A. Test:  A probationer in the post of Assistant Engineer shall pass the Account Test for Public Works Department
Officers, Part – I and II within the period of probation. If he fails to pass the said tests, he shall not be deemed to have
completed his probation satisfactorily and he shall not be entitled for appointment as full member of the service and for
annual increments in the time scale of pay applicable to him until he has passed the said tests.  If such probationer does
not pass the said Tests within the period of five years from the date of his appointment, he shall be discharged from the
Service”.
