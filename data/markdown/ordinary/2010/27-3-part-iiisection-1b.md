HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE DEPARTMENT
Amendments to Ad hoc rules relating to temporary post of Lecturer in Bio-Physics in the Department of
Bio-Physics in the Government Medical Colleges under Tamil Nadu Medical Service.
[G.O. Ms. No. 122, Health and Family Welfare (AA1), 3rd May 2010, Chithirai 20, Thiruvalluvar Aandu-2041.]
No. SRO B-50/2010.—In exercise of the powers conferred by the proviso to Article 309 of the Constitution of India,
the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following amendments to the rules published with the Health and Family
Planning Department Notification No. SRO A-1006/70, published at pages 1035-1036 in Part V of the Tamil Nadu
Government Gazette, dated the 11th November 1970, as subsequently amended.
2. The amendments hereby made shall come into force on the 3rd May, 2010.
AMENDMENTS
In the said Rules,—
1. for rule 1, the following rule shall be substituted, namely:—
“The General and the Special Rules applicable to the holders of permanent posts in Class III in Branch I—
Medical of the Tamil Nadu Medical Service shall apply to the holders of the temporary post of Lecturer in Bio-Physics
in the Department of Bio-Physics in the Government Medical Colleges, subject to the modifications specified in the
following rules:—";
2. for rule 3, the following rule shall be substituted, namely:—
“3. Appointment.—Appointment to the post shall be made by direct recruitment”;
3. in rule 4, for sub-rule (b), the following sub-rule shall be substituted, namely:—
“(b) Other qualifications.—No person shall be eligible for appointment to the post, unless he possesses M.Sc
in Bio-Physics or Medical Bio-Chemistry:
Provided 
that 
preference 
shall 
be 
given 
to 
the 
person 
who 
possess 
Ph.D. 
in
Bio-Physics or Medical Bio-Chemistry”;
4. after rule 5A, the following rule shall be inserted, namely:—
“5B. Test.—Every person appointed to the post shall within the period of his probation, pass the Account Test
for Executive Officers and the Departmental Test in the Tamil Nadu Medical Code, failing which, his increments in the
time scale shall be stopped until he passes the Tests, but such stoppage of increments shall not have the effect of
postponing his future increment after he passes the Tests”.
V.K. SUBBURAJ,
Principal Secretary to Government.
